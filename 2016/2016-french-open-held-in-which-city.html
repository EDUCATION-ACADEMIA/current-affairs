2016 French Open held in which city

(A) Nice

(B) Toulouse

(C) Lyon

(D) ** Paris

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 2016 French Open was the 115th edition of the French Open and was held at Stade Roland Garros in Paris.

Concept note-2: -Novak Djokovic defeated Andy Murray in the final, 3–6, 6–1, 6–2, 6–4 to win the men’s singles tennis title at the 2016 French Open. It was his first French Open title and twelfth major title overall, completing the career Grand Slam and achieving a non-calendar year Grand Slam.

Concept note-3: -The French Open (French: Internationaux de France de tennis), also known as Roland-Garros (French: [ʁɔlɑ̃ ɡaʁos]), is a major tennis tournament held over two weeks at the Stade Roland Garros in Paris, France, beginning in late May each year.

Concept note-4: -Garbiñe Muguruza defeated the defending champion Serena Williams in the final, 7–5, 6–4 to win the women’s singles tennis title at the 2016 French Open.

Concept note-5: -Stade Roland Garros is located at the western side of Paris, at the southern boundary of the Bois de Boulogne in Paris’s 16th arrondissement.
