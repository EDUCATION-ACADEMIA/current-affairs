How many Indian car model has failed the crash tests conducted by the Global New Car Assessment Programme (Global NCAP) on 17 May 2016

(A) 4

(B) ** 5

(C) 3

(D) 7

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Five Indian car models have got zero star ratings in crash tests conducted by the Global New Car Assessment Programme (Global NCAP). They are the Renault Kwid, Maruti Suzuki’s Celerio and Eeco, Mahindra Scorpio and Hyundai Eon.

Concept note-2: -Top 20 safest cars in India with Global NCAP rating 2023 Of the 20 models listed here, 5-star rating cars in India include Skoda Kushaq, Volkswagen Taigun, Mahindra Scorpio-N, Tata Punch, Mahindra XUV300, Tata Altroz, Tata Nexon and Mahindra XUV700.

Concept note-3: -The individual NCAPs follow their own protocol to test and score cars. The Euro NCAP tests side impact, side pole, full frontal and front offset, while the Global NCAP tests front offset crash. To conduct the front offset crash test, the car is driven at 64 kmph and with 40 percent overlap into a deformable barrier.

Concept note-4: -About 97 percent of all new vehicles sold are crash-test rated by one or both of the independent organizations.

Concept note-5: -What are the safest cars of 2021 in India? Based on the safety ratings by Global NCAP, the top 5 safest cars are: Mahindra XUV300, Tata Altroz, Tata Nexon, Mahindra Marazzo, and Tata Tiago/Tigor.
