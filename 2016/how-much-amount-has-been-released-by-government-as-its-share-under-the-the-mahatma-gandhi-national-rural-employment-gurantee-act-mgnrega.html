How much amount has been released by government as its share under the the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Gurantee Act (MGNREGA)

(A) Rs 15, 700 crore

(B) Rs 12, 230 crore

(C) ** Rs 50, 130 Lakhs

(D) Rs 20, 500 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In the last budget presented by Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman, the MGNREGA allocation saw a 25% cut to ₹73, 000 crore.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is 75 : 25. Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) is an employment guarantee scheme implemented in India, enacted by legislation on 2 October 2005.

Concept note-3: -The Government of India passed the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005 in September, 2005. The Act gives legal guarantee of a hundred days of wage employment in a financial year to adult members of a rural household who demand employment and are willing to do unskilled manual work.

Concept note-4: -The mandate of the MGNREGA is to provide at least 100 days of guaranteed wage employment in a financial year to every rural household whose adult members volunteer to do unskilled manual work.
