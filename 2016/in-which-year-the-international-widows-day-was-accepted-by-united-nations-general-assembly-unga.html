In which year the International Widows Day was accepted by United Nations General Assembly (UNGA)

(A) 2006

(B) ** 2011

(C) 2015

(D) 1997

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In December 2010, the UN General Assembly formally adopted June 23rd as International Widows’ Day. The first official UN International Widows Day was held on June 23rd, 2011.

Concept note-2: -In 2022, the theme for the International Widows’ Day is “Invisible Women, Invisible Problems”. The theme is an attempt to showcase how the identity of a woman in a society is attached to her partner and after his death, the problems faced by the widow are ignored by policymakers.

Concept note-3: -International Widows’ Day is observed across the world on 23 June. The day aims to gather support for widows and to spread awareness about their situation. International Widows’ Day is observed across the world on 23 June.

Concept note-4: -International Widows Day, celebrated every year on June 23, is a special day established by the United Nations dedicated to addressing the poverty and injustice faced by millions of widows and their dependents. Did you know that widowhood is more common than you think?

Concept note-5: -International Widows Day was established by The Loomba Foundation to raise awareness of the issue of widowhood. The significance of 23 June is that it was on that day in 1954 that Shrimati Pushpa Wati Loomba, mother of the foundation’s founder, Lord Loomba, became a widow.
