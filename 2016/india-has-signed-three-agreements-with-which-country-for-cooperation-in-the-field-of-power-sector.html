India has signed three agreements with which country for Cooperation in the field of Power Sector

(A) Bangladesh

(B) Russia

(C) Bhutan

(D) ** Myanmar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2016, the EU and India established the EU-India Clean Energy and Climate Partnership (CECP), which promotes access to and dissemination of clean energy and climate-friendly technologies, and encourages research and the development of innovative solutions.

Concept note-2: -The Comptroller & Auditor General of India and the Comptroller General of the Republic of Chile signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to strengthen institutional and professional capacities and learn from each other’s best practices.

Concept note-3: -1 | U.S.-India Partnership to Advance Clean Energy (PACE) The United States is committed to enhancing energy cooperation with India in light of the central role that energy plays in a wide range of priority issues – from climate change and energy security to energy access, economic growth, trade and investment.

Concept note-4: -India could attract close to $10 bn in renewable energy investment in 2023. India’s non-fossil fuel based cumulative electric installed capacity stands at 174.53 Giga Watts, which is 42.5% of the installed capacity. India’s target is to produce 5 MT of green hydrogen by 2030.
