India’s first LIGO laboratory will set up in which of the following state

(A) Tamil Nadu

(B) Andhra Pradesh

(C) Karnataka

(D) ** Maharashtra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Maharashtra. First Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory (LIGO) laboratory will be installed in Maharashtra.

Concept note-2: -The observatory, which will cost 12.6 billion rupees (US$177 million) and is scheduled for completion in 2024, will be built in the Hingoli District of Maharashtra state in western India.

Concept note-3: -India’s Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory (LIGO) will be set up at Aundha Nagnath, Hingoli District in Maharashtra.

Concept note-4: -Hingoli income division handed around 225 hectares of land right in Maharashtra to the authorities of Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory (LIGO) venture.

Concept note-5: -As of July 2021, the LIGO-India project was still waiting for ‘full-approval’ from the Cabinet of the Government of India. The ‘full-approval’ from the Cabinet is needed for any construction to begin at the site.
