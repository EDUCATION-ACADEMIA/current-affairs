India’s first war of Independence started in which year

(A) ** 1857

(B) 1850

(C) 1917

(D) 1947

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The year 1857 witnessed the first war of independence, which is perhaps one of the defining moments of Indian freedom struggle.

Concept note-2: -The year 1857 witnessed the first war of independence, which is perhaps one of the defining moments of Indian freedom struggle.

Concept note-3: -Indian Mutiny, also called Sepoy Mutiny or First War of Independence, widespread but unsuccessful rebellion against British rule in India in 1857–59. Begun in Meerut by Indian troops (sepoys) in the service of the British East India Company, it spread to Delhi, Agra, Kanpur, and Lucknow.

Concept note-4: -Immediate Causes of Revolt of 1857 On March 29th, sepoy Mangal Pandey of the 34th Bengal Native Infantry (BNI) declared rebellion against his commanders and fired the first shot at a British officer.

Concept note-5: -The revolt began on May 10, 1857, at Meerut as a sepoy mutiny. It was initiated by sepoys in the Bengal Presidency against the British officers. This war of Independence marked the end of rule by the British East India company.
