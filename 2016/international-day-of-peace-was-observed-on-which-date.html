International Day of Peace was observed on which date

(A) ** 21 September

(B) 8 November

(C) 12 August

(D) 24 December

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each year the International Day of Peace is observed around the world on 21 September. The UN General Assembly has declared this as a day devoted to strengthening the ideals of peace, through observing 24 hours of non-violence and cease-fire. But achieving true peace entails much more than laying down arms.

Concept note-2: -Each year the International Day of Peace is observed around the world on 21 September. The UN General Assembly has declared this as a day devoted to strengthening the ideals of peace, through observing 24 hours of non-violence and cease-fire.

Concept note-3: -The day was first celebrated in 1981 and is kept by many nations, political groups, military groups, and people.

Concept note-4: -The International Day of Peace is celebrated every year on 21 September across the world. The day is devoted to building the ideals of peace among all countries and peoples.

Concept note-5: -Quick Facts. The United Nations’ (UN) International Day of Peace is celebrated on September 21 each year to recognize the efforts of those who have worked hard to end conflict and promote peace. The International Day of Peace is also a day of ceasefire – personal or political.
