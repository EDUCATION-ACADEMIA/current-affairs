Name the festival of Ladakh, that also called as the Kumbh Mela of the Himalayas.

(A) Sprow Festival

(B) Karow Festival

(C) Maestro Festival

(D) ** Naropa Festival

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Naropa Festival is organised in the Hemis Monastery every once in 12 years.

Concept note-2: -Known as the Kumbh of the Himalayas, the Naropa Festival, the largest Buddhist festival in the Himalayan region, is held every 12 years at the Hemis Monastery.

Concept note-3: -Festivals in Ladakh Hemis Festival, Losar Festival, Sindhu Darshan, Phyang Tsedup Festival, Dosmoche Festival, Saka Dawa Festival, Tak Tok Festival, Matho Nagrang Festival, Ladakh Festival and many more.

Concept note-4: -Naropa is Annual festival of Ladakh. It is also known as the “Kumbh of Himalayas". Naropa Festivals takes place every 12 years in the Tibetan calendar. The Shandol Dance was performed by 408 women artists in traditional dresses.

Concept note-5: -Ladakh will celebrate its annual Buddhist festival, Hemis, on July 8 and 9, 2022. This cultural extravagance is all set to welcome tourists from all across the world to enjoy the fanfare and the rich culture of the Tibetan community in Ladakh.
