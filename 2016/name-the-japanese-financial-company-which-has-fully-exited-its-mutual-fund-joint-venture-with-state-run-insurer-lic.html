Name the Japanese financial company which has fully exited its mutual fund joint venture with state-run insurer LIC

(A) Softbank

(B) Mizuho Financial

(C) ** Nomura

(D) Hitachi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Japanese financial company Nomura has fully exited its mutual fund joint venture with state-run insurer LIC, the latter announced in a press release today. As a result, LIC Nomura Mutual Fund has now been renamed LIC Mutual Fund.

Concept note-2: -The first company that dealt in mutual funds was the Unit Trust of India. It was set up in 1963 as a joint venture of the Reserve Bank of India and the Government of India.

Concept note-3: -In accordance with the Securities & Exchange Board of India (Mutual Fund) LIC Mutual Fund was set up as a separate Trust by the Life Insurance Corporation of India (who is the Sponsor).

Concept note-4: -Nippon India Mutual Fund (NIMF) was earlier known as Reliance Mutual Fund.
