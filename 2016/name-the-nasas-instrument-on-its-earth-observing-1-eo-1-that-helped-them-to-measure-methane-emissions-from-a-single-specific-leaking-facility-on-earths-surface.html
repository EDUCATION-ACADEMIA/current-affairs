Name the NASA’s instrument on its Earth Observing-1 (EO-1) that helped them to measure methane emissions from a single, specific leaking facility on Earth’s surface

(A) Mongoose-V microprocessor

(B) ** Hyperion spectrometer

(C) Lockheed L-1011

(D) Beacon Monitor

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The three primary instruments on the EO-1 spacecraft are the Advanced Land Imager (ALI), the Hyperion, and the Linear Etalon Imaging Spectrometer Array (LEISA) Atmospheric Corrector (LAC).

Concept note-2: -Methane Source Finder (MSF) is a data system developed by NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory which streamlines the visualization of airborne methane remote sensing data products. The MSF portal allows public access to the data collected during these airborne surveys, and view the surrounding source.

Concept note-3: -Methane absorbs infrared light in a unique pattern – called a spectral fingerprint – that EMIT’s imaging spectrometer can discern with high accuracy and precision. The instrument can also measure carbon dioxide.

Concept note-4: -SuperCam Measures the Air Scientists can learn about how atmospheric molecules, water ice, and dust absorb or reflect solar radiation.
