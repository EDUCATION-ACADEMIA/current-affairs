RBI’s new debt recast norms are credit positive for banks and will reduce gross NPA by how many basis points following one year

(A) 10-100

(B) 20-100

(C) ** 30-100

(D) 40-100

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition: A non performing asset (NPA) is a loan or advance for which the principal or interest payment remained overdue for a period of 90 days.

Concept note-2: -For the above category of banks, an account would be classified as Non Performing Asset if the : (i) Interest and/or installment of principal remain overdue for a period of more than 180 days in respect of a Term Loan.

Concept note-3: -3. Paragraph 10 of the Circular stipulates that loan accounts classified as NPAs may be upgraded as ‘standard’ asset only if entire arrears of interest and principal are paid by the borrower. NBFCs shall have time till September 30, 2022 to put in place the necessary systems to implement this provision.

Concept note-4: -b) Doubtful Assets An asset is classified as a doubtful asset if it remained as an NPA for more than 12 months.
