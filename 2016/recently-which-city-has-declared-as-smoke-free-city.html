Recently which city has declared as ’smoke-free city’

(A) Aizawl

(B) Shillong

(C) Imphal

(D) ** Kohima

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Howrah was officially declared smoke-free in 2017.

Concept note-2: -Chandigarh on 15th July 2007, was declared a smoke-free city. It was claimed to be India’s first city, with a ban on smoking in public places. Smoking in public places was banned with the implementation of the provisions of the Central Tobacco Control Act in all parts of the city.

Concept note-3: -A survey conducted by the International Institute of Population Science and the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, reveals that 56.6% of people in Kolkata smoke, the highest rate in the country. 82% of men and 23.5% of women smoke in Kolkata The highest number of beedi smokers are in Uttarakhand.

Concept note-4: -Chandigarh was the first city in India to fully implement the government’s smoke-free laws.In 2007, the Chandigarh administration declared its smoke-free city status, making indoor places smoke-free as well as prohibiting smoking in outdoor spaces like parks, markets and other public areas.

Concept note-5: -Inspired locals turn their village into the country’s first tobacco-free area. For most smokers, the statutory warning on cigarette packs means only puff and nonsense. But the 2, 200 villagers of Koolimadu, 25 km from Kozhikode town, have taken it very seriously.
