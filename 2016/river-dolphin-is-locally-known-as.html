River Dolphin is locally known as

(A) ** Sihu

(B) Sisu

(C) Siha

(D) Kihu

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Common or local names: South Asian river dolphin, Ganga dolphin, Gangetic dolphin.

Concept note-2: -Also called boto, bufeo, and pink dolphin, it is common in the turbid waters of the Amazon and Orinoco river basins.

Concept note-3: -Status. The Ganges river dolphin-known locally as susu, which refers to the noise the dolphin is said to make when it breathes-inhabits parts of the Ganges, Meghna, and Brahmaputra river systems in India, Nepal, and Bangladesh.

Concept note-4: -Ganges River dolphins, commonly known as susu, Platanista gangetica gangetica, are distributed throughout the Ganges–Brahmaputra–Meghna and Karnaphuli–Sangu river systems of Nepal, India, Bangladesh, and potentially Bhutan (Mohan et al.

Concept note-5: -The Ganges River Dolphin or also called ‘Susu, ’ is the National Aquatic Animal of India. It is one of the National Symbols of India. Gangetic Dolphins are found in the river systems of Ganga, Brahmaputra, Meghna, and Karnaphuli-Sangu in Nepal, India, and Bangladesh.
