[SOURCES]
Scientists have discovered two small worlds far outside the orbit of which planet

(A) Saturn

(B) Venus

(C) ** Neptune

(D) Pluto

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -WASHINGTON: Scientists have discovered two small ‘worlds’ far outside the orbit of Neptune in the deepest survey ever conducted to search out distant solar system objects.

Concept note-2: -Pluto was the last planet discovered, although that distinction returned to Neptune when Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet. Pluto was discovered in 1930 by the astronomer Clyde Tombaugh.

Concept note-3: -Dwarf planet Haumea is a member of a group of objects that orbit in a disc-like zone beyond the orbit of Neptune called the Kuiper Belt. This distant realm is populated with thousands of miniature icy worlds which formed early in the history of our solar system about 4.5 billion years ago.

Concept note-4: -What is beyond Pluto? There are at least eight more dwarf planets beyond Pluto and Neptune. They include Eris, a little bigger than Pluto, which has its own small moon. There is Haumea, Sedna, Orcus, Quaoar, Varuna, and Makemake.

Concept note-5: -In the outer Solar System, well beyond the orbit of Neptune and Pluto, a ninth planet may be waiting to be discovered. This ‘ghost planet’, nicknamed Planet 9 or Planet X, has never been observed directly, but peculiar goings-on in the space beyond Neptune hint tantalisingly at its existence.
