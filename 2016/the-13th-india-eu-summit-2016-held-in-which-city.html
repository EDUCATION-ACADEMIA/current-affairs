The 13th India-EU Summit 2016 held in which city

(A) New Delhi

(B) ** Brussels

(C) Flanders

(D) Goa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2016, at the 13th India-EU Summit in Brussels, leaders endorsed the India-EU Agenda for Action 2020 (a common roadmap to jointly guide and strengthen the partnership) and established the India-EU Common Agenda for Migration and Mobility (CAMM) as a framework for cooperation on migration management.

Concept note-2: -On 30 March 2016, the EU hosted in Brussels the 13th EU-India summit. Discussions focused on trade and investment, energy and climate, water, migration and foreign and security policy. Leaders at the summit adopted a joint statement, reaffirming their commitment to strengthen the EU-India strategic partnership.

Concept note-3: -The first India-EU Summit took place on 28 June 2000 in Lisbon and it marked a watershed in the evolution of the ties. The relationship was upgraded to a ‘Strategic Partnership’ during the 5th India-EU Summit held in 2004 in The Hague.

Concept note-4: -Statement of the Euro Summit, 16 December 2021 The Euro Summit, meeting in an inclusive format, adopted a statement on 16 December 2021.

Concept note-5: -The European Union ( EU ) is an economic and political union of 27 countries.
