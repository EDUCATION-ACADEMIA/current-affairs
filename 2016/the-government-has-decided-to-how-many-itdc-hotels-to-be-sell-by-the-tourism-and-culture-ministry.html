The government has decided to how many ITDC hotels to be sell by the Tourism and Culture Ministry

(A) 10

(B) ** 14

(C) 18

(D) 7

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NEW DELHI: Fourteen out of 16 perennially loss-making hotels owned by state-run ITDC will be sold off and the process to privatize them has already started, Union tourism and culture minister Mahesh Sharma said on Sunday.

Concept note-2: -Established in 1966, it owns over 17 properties under the Ashok Group of Hotels brand, across India.

Concept note-3: -The Corporation is running hotels, restaurants at various places for tourists, besides providing transport facilities. In addition, the Corporation is engaged in production, distribution and sale of tourist publicity literature and providing entertainment and duty free shopping facilities to the tourists.

Concept note-4: -(ITDC) came into existence in October 1966 with the sole objective of developing and expending tourism infrastructure in the country and thereby promoting India as a tourist destination.

Concept note-5: -Corporation (ITDC) ITDC came into existence in October 1966 and has been the prime mover in the progressive development, promotion and expansion of tourism in the country.
