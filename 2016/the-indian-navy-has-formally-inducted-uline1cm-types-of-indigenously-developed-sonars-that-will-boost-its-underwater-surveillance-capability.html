The Indian Navy has formally inducted _________ types of indigenously developed sonars that will boost its underwater surveillance capability.

(A) 3

(B) 7

(C) ** 4

(D) 2

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The systems included Abhay-compact hull mounted sonar for shallow water crafts, Humsa UG-upgrade for the Humsa sonar system, NACS-Near-field Acoustic Characterisation System, and AIDSS-Advanced Indigenous Distress sonar system for submarines.

Concept note-2: -USHUS is used for detecting and tracking enemy submarines, surface vessels, and torpedoes and can be used for underwater communication and avoiding obstacles.

Concept note-3: -Passive sonar systems are used primarily to detect noise from marine objects (such as submarines or ships) and marine animals like whales.

Concept note-4: -Mid-frequency active sonar (1kHz-10kHz) is the Navy’s primary tactical sonar and its main tool to combat the threat posed by the world-wide proliferation of ultra-quiet diesel submarines.

Concept note-5: -In response to devastating Allied shipping and human losses from U-boat attacks during World War II, the Navy began using sonar.
