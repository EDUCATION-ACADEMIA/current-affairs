The Supreme Court of India has banned all diesel cabs in NCR-Delhi from

(A) ** May 1

(B) May 2

(C) May 3

(D) May 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On 16 December 2015, the apex court barred the registration of diesel vehicles with engines bigger than 2, 000cc in Delhi-NCR until 31 March.

Concept note-2: -BS 3 petrol and BS 4 diesel cars have been temporarily banned in Delhi due to rise in air pollution. The Delhi government took the decision to bam these cars on Monday based on the directions provided under Stage-III of the revised GRAP and under Section 115 of Motor Vehicle Act.

Concept note-3: -10 year old diesel cars still banned To ensure that this rule is strictly followed, all the registering authorities and RTO offices in New Delhi can issue a no-objection certificate (NOC) for the transfer of such older vehicles to other states, where this rule is not in effect as of now. This ban is still applicable.

Concept note-4: -Chaitanya Singh. The Supreme Court on Tuesday imposed costs of ₹50, 000 on two petitioners who moved the Court challenging the ban in Delhi on diesel vehicles older than 10 years and petrol vehicles older than 15 years [Anurag Saxena and Anr. v. Union of India].

Concept note-5: -In its order, the top court of the country mandated the Transport Department of Delhi for a complete ban on plying of 10-year old diesel vehicles and 15-year old petrol vehicles on the roads within Delhi and the National Capital Region.
