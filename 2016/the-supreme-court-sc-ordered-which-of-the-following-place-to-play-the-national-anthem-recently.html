The Supreme Court (SC) ordered which of the following place to play the National Anthem recently

(A) Hospitals

(B) Bank

(C) ** Cinema Theatres

(D) Private Offices

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Supreme Court of India ordered the playing of the National Anthem in all cinema halls across the country before the screening of a movie. A bench headed by Chief Justice of India Dipak Misra order had made the playing of the national anthem in cinema halls mandatory on November 30, 2016.

Concept note-2: -The Supreme Court of India passed a judgment on 11 August 1986 in which it held that the three students were not guilty of disrespecting the National Anthem just because they refused to sing it. Moreover, they did stand in respect whenever the National Anthem was being sung.

Concept note-3: -Answer. Answer: The National Anthem is not played in the cinema halls these days because it was found that the people used to leave the hall midway, laughing, shouting and creating a racket.

Concept note-4: -Ans. It can be played at any time of the day, playing the anthem during the night doesn’t violate any rules. But while the anthem is played every one should stand up and maintain proper decorum.

Concept note-5: -In November 2016, a two-judge Supreme Court bench consisting of Justices Dipak Misra and Amitava Roy ordered that the national anthem be played in all cinemas, allowing people to “voluntarily” become patriotic.
