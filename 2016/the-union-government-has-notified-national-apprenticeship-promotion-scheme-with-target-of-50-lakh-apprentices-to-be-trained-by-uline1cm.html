[SOURCES]

The Union Government has notified National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme with target of 50 Lakh apprentices to be trained by _________

(A) 2016-17

(B) 2019-25

(C) ** 2019-20

(D) 2018-30

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Government of India has approved ‘National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme’ (NAPS) with target to provide apprenticeship training to 50 lakh youth by 2020.

Concept note-2: -The National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme (NAPS), launched in August 2016, aims to promote apprenticeship training in the country by providing stipend support to the apprentices, undertake capacity building of the apprenticeship ecosystem and provide advocacy assistance to support rapid growth.

Concept note-3: -Eligibility of Apprentice Trainees passed out from ITI courses. Trainees under dual-learning mode from ITIs. Trainees who have completed PMKVY/MES courses. Candidates who possess minimum educational qualification required for a trade and have not undergone any formal trade training (Fresher apprentices)

Concept note-4: -About NATS It is a 1 year programme equipping technically qualified youth with practical knowledge and skills required in their field of work. The Apprentices are imparted training by the organizations at their place of work.

Concept note-5: -The National Apprenticeship Training Scheme in India is a one-year program for all students.
