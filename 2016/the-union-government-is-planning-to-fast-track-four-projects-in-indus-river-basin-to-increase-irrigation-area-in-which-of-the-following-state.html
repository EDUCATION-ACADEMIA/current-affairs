The Union Government is planning to fast-track four projects in Indus river basin to increase irrigation area in which of the following state

(A) Punjab

(B) Gujarat

(C) Rajasthan

(D) ** Jammu and Kashmir

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Indus Basin irrigation system Surface-water resources in Pakistan are based on the flows of the Indus River and its tributaries (Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Sutlej, and Beas to the east and the Kabul River to the west).

Concept note-2: -IBIS, which stands for Indus Basin Irrigation System, is the largest irrigation system on the planet. It services Afghanistan, China, India, and Pakistan, providing water to more than 1.6 million square kilometres of agricultural land in Pakistan.

Concept note-3: -The Indus Basin Project is a water control project that resulted from a treaty, Indus Waters Treaty, signed between India and Pakistan in 1960 that guaranteed that Pakistan would receive water from the Indus River independent from upstream control by India.

Concept note-4: -In the mountainous region the principal waterways west of the Indus are the Swat Canals, which flow from the Swat River, a tributary of the Kābul River. Those canals provide irrigation for the two chief crops of the area, sugarcane and wheat.
