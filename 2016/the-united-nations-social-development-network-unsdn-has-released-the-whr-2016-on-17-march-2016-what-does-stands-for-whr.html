The United Nations Social Development Network (UNSDN) has released the WHR 2016 on 17 March, 2016. What does stands for WHR

(A) ** World Happiness Report

(B) World Human Report

(C) World Health Report

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Finland is the ‘happiest’ country in the world, followed by Denmark, Switzerland, Iceland, and Norway. The data comes from the Gallup World Poll, based entirely on survey scores and answers to the main life evaluation question asked in the poll.

Concept note-2: -According to the United Nation’s World Happiness Report that was released on March 20 to mark the World Happiness Day, India fell seven spots in the ranking in 2019 to claim the 140th position in a list of 156 nations. India ranked 132nd in 2018, 122nd in 2017 and 118th in 2016.

Concept note-3: -United Nations Social Development Network (UNSDN)

Concept note-4: -It is measured through surveys that require participants to rank their level of happiness based on an assortment of quality-of-life factors. Bhutan’s gross national happiness index is an economic metric that is used quite frequently by people who study happiness economics.
