The Universal Children’s Day was observed on which date

(A) 12 July

(B) 6 September

(C) 16 October

(D) ** 20 November

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Children’s Day was first established in 1954 as Universal Children’s Day and is celebrated on 20 November each year to promote international togetherness, awareness among children worldwide, and improving children’s welfare.

Concept note-2: -World Children’s Day was first established in 1954 as Universal Children’s Day and is celebrated on 20 November each year to promote international togetherness, awareness among children worldwide, and improving children’s welfare.

Concept note-3: -Every year on November 20th, Universal Children’s Day is observed. This day is primarily intended to promote international cooperation, raise awareness among children worldwide, and improve children’s welfare. On November 20, 1954, Universal Children’s Day was established.

Concept note-4: -Universal Children’s Day, as we know it today, was first proclaimed in 1925, at the World Conference of Child Welfare in Geneva, Switzerland.

Concept note-5: -Universal Children’s Day was first announced by the General Assembly of the United Nations in 1954.
