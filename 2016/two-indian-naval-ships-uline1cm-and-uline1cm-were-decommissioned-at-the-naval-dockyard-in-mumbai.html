Two Indian Naval Ships _________ and _________ were decommissioned at the Naval Dockyard in Mumbai.

(A) Tabar and Satbura

(B) ** Veer and Nipat

(C) Shardul and Magar

(D) Karmuk and Kirpan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is INS Nishank. The Indian Navy will bid farewell to its two ships INS Nishank and INS Akshay, at Naval Dockyard in Mumbai on 3 June 2022. Both the ships will be decommissioned at Naval Dockyard after 32 years of being at the forefront.

Concept note-2: -The decommissioning of INS Ajay was conducted at Naval Dockyard, Mumbai in the traditional manner.

Concept note-3: -Indian Navy decommissions warships INS Nirbhik and INS Nirghat. The warships were decommissioned at a solemn ceremony at the Naval Dockyard yesterday, a Defence spokesperson said today.

Concept note-4: -Coronado is the third Littoral Combat Ship the Navy has decommissioned, with the sea service aiming to deactivate another nine as part of the Fiscal Year 2023 budget. USS Independence (LCS-2), the first of its class, was decommissioned in July 2021 after 11 years. Coronado served eight years.

Concept note-5: -INS Ajay was decommissioned on 19 September 2022 after rendering 32 years of glorious service to the nation.
