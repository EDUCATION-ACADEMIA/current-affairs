What is an alternative to PET plastic, PEF’s full form according to researchers

(A) polyethyne furandicarboxylate

(B) ** polyethylene furandicarboxylate

(C) polyethyne furandicarboxylin

(D) polyethylene furandicarboxlate

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is PEF-Polyethylene Furanoate? Polyethylene Furanoate or PEF is a 100% recyclable, bio-based polymer produced using renewable raw materials (sugars) derived from plants.

Concept note-2: -Polyethylene Furanoate or PEF is a 100% recyclable, bio-based polymer produced using renewable raw materials. It is referred to as the next generation polyester which exhibits great potential to replace polyethylene terephthalate (PET).

Concept note-3: -Bagasse: Compostable, eco-friendly bagasse can replace plastic needing as disposable plates, cups or takeout boxes. Bagasse is made from the pulp that is leftover when the juice is extracted from sugarcane or beets.

Concept note-4: -Environmentally, bio-based PET (30 per cent bio-based content) and PLA were both found to be better than petroleum PET, though neither were as beneficial as recycled PET. They do, however, have widespread commercial production and availability.
