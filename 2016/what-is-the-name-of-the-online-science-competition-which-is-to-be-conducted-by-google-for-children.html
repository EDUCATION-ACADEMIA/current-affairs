What is the name of the online science competition which is to be conducted by Google for children

(A) Computer Science

(B) Formal Sciences

(C) ** Google Science Fair

(D) Natural Sciences

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The students need to submit a project in which science is used to help change the world. You need to submit one (1) Entry either as an individual or as part of a group (“Team”) composed of up to three (3) persons.

Concept note-2: -Doodle for Google is an annual art contest open to students in grades K-12. Students are invited to create their own Google Doodle for the chance to have it featured on Google.com, as well as win some great scholarships and tech packages for their schools.

Concept note-3: -The competition is open to 13-to 18-year-old students around the globe, who formulate a hypothesis, perform an experiment, and present their results. All students must have an internet connection and a free Google Account to participate, and the projects must be in English, German, Italian, Spanish, or French.

Concept note-4: -For his efforts to remove microplastics from our water, Cork native Fionn Ferreira has won the grand prize at the 2019 Google Science Fair.

Concept note-5: -All the student aging between 13 years to 18 years of age who are able to create a theory, perform an experiment from all over the world can participate in the Google Science Fair 2023 contest.
