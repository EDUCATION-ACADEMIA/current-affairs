Which country has discovered the World’s oldest ground-edge stone axe

(A) ** Australia

(B) China

(C) India

(D) Indonesia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Archaeologists from The Australian National University (ANU) have unearthed fragments from the edge of the world’s oldest-known ground-edge axe, found in the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

Concept note-2: -A tiny stone fragment from north-western Australia is a remnant of the earliest known axe with a handle, archaeologists have claimed. The fingernail-sized sliver of basalt is ground smooth at one end and appears to date from 44 to 49, 000 years ago.

Concept note-3: -Early humans were using stone hand axes as far back as 1.8 million years ago.

Concept note-4: -The axe fragment was found in the same layer of sediment as a charcoal sample that was radiocarbon dated to 48, 875-43, 941 years old.
