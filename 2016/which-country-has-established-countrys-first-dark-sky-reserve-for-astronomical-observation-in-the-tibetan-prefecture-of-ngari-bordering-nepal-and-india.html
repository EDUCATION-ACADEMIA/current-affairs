Which country has established country’s first dark sky reserve for astronomical observation in the Tibetan prefecture of Ngari, bordering Nepal and India

(A) Mongolia

(B) ** China

(C) North Korea

(D) Bangladesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -China has established country’s first dark sky reserve for astronomical observation in the Tibetan prefecture of Ngari, bordering Nepal and India.

Concept note-2: -China launched its first “dark sky reserve” for astronomical observation in the Tibetan prefecture of Ngari, bordering Nepal and India, officials said on Thursday.

Concept note-3: -The administration of the Union Territory of Ladakh has officially identified a 1, 073 sqkm area in Ladakh’s Hanle as India’s first dark sky reserve and will be known as the Hanle Dark Sky Reserve (HDSR).

Concept note-4: -India’s first ever Dark Sky Reserve have been set up at Hanle in Ladakh as a part of Changthang Wildlife Sanctuary.

Concept note-5: -Ladakh is now home to India’s first Dark Sky Reserve, Hanle Dark Sky Reserve. Hanle is a small village in the Changthang region, approximately 270 km south-east of Leh.
