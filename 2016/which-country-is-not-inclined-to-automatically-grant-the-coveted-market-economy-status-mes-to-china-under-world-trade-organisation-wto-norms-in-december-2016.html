Which country is not inclined to automatically grant the coveted ‘Market Economy Status’ (MES) to China under World Trade Organisation (WTO) norms in December 2016.

(A) Japan

(B) US

(C) Russia

(D) ** India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -China is a member of WTO but has the status of a non-market economy.

Concept note-2: -China became a member of the World Trade Organization (WTO) on 11 December 2001, after the agreement of the Ministerial Conference. The admission of China to the WTO was preceded by a lengthy process of negotiations and required significant changes to the Chinese economy.

Concept note-3: -After four decades of reform, China today falls far short of a free-market economy-but it has also come a long way from central planning where the government controls prices and production. The reforms have gone furthest in the manufacturing sector, where AD cases are the most relevant.

Concept note-4: -Market Economy status is a status conferred on the country exporting the goods. • The prices of the goods on which they are exported by the country are not questioned and it is. accepted as the benchmark. • Prior to this status, country is considered as a Non Market Economy (NME).
