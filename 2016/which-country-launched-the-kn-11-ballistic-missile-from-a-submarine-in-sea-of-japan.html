Which country launched the KN-11 ballistic missile from a submarine in Sea of Japan

(A) Japan

(B) ** North Korea

(C) China

(D) South Korea

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Pukguksong-1 or Pukkŭksŏng-1, Bukgeukseong-1 (Hangul: 1, Hanja: 1, literally Polaris-1), alternatively KN-11 in intelligence communities outside North Korea, is a North Korean, two-stage submarine-launched ballistic missile (SLBM) that was successfully flight tested on 24 August 2016.

Concept note-2: -India is now the sixth country, alongside China, France, Russia, the United Kingdom and the United States to have nuclear-powered submarines armed with ballistic missiles, the Times of India reported on Friday.

Concept note-3: -North Korea has fired three short-range ballistic missiles towards the Sea of Japan, the latest launches in a year that has seen an unprecedented barrage of missile tests by an increasingly belligerent Pyongyang.

Concept note-4: -Trident II D5 is one of the most advanced long-range submarine-launched nuclear missiles, and is the primary U.S. sea-based nuclear ballistic missile.
