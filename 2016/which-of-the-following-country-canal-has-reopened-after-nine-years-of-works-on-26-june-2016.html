Which of the following country canal has reopened after nine years of works on 26 June 2016

(A) Suez Canal

(B) Kennet and Avon Canal

(C) ** Panama Canal

(D) Corinth Canal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Following additional difficulties including seepage from the new locks, the expansion was opened on 26 June 2016. The expansion doubled the Canal’s capacity. On March 2, 2018, the Panama Canal Authority announced that 3, 000 New Panamax ships had crossed the canal expansion during its first 20 months of operation.

Concept note-2: -The Panama Canal was first developed following the failure of a French construction team in the 1880s, when the United States commenced building a canal across a 50-mile stretch of the narrow Panama isthmus in 1904.

Concept note-3: -The United States spent almost $500 million (roughly equivalent to $13.5 billion in 2021) to finish the project. This was by far the largest American engineering project to date. The canal was formally opened on August 15, 1914, with the passage of the cargo ship SS Ancon.

Concept note-4: -A French company headed by Ferdinand, viscount de Lesseps, started to build a canal in 1881 but failed by 1889.

Concept note-5: -Panama, country of Central America located on the Isthmus of Panama, the narrow bridge of land that connects North and South America. Embracing the isthmus and more than 1, 600 islands off its Atlantic and Pacific coasts, the tropical nation is renowned as the site of the Panama Canal, which cuts through its midsection.
