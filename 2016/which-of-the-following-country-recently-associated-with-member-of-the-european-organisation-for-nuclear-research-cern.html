Which of the following country recently associated with member of the European Organisation for Nuclear Research (CERN)

(A) Ecuador

(B) Iraq

(C) Canada

(D) ** India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1] The CERN convention was signed in 1953 by 12 founding states. 2] Currently, CERN has 22 member states. 3] Besides India, Turkey, Pakistan, Ukraine are Associate members and Serbia and Cyprus are associate members.

Concept note-2: -Founded in 1954, the CERN laboratory sits astride the Franco-Swiss border near Geneva. It was one of Europe’s first joint ventures and now has 22 member states.

Concept note-3: -Cyprus, Estonia and Slovenia are Associate Member States in the pre-stage to Membership. Croatia, India, Latvia, Lithuania, Pakistan, Türkiye and Ukraine are Associate Member States.

Concept note-4: -India was granted Observer status to the CERN Council in 2002. The CERN convention was signed in 1953 by the 12 founding state. Currently, CERN has 22 member states. Besides India, Turkey, Pakistan, Ukraine are Associate members and Serbia and Cyprus are associate members in the pre-stage to membership.

Concept note-5: -In 1991, DAE had signed a formal agreement with CERN, which continues till today. In recognition of most significant contributions, in 2003, India was awarded the Observer status of CERN, and subsequently invited to join CERN as an Associate Member.
