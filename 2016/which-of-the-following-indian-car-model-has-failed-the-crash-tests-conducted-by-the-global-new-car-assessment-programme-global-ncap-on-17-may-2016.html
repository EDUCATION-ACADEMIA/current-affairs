Which of the following Indian car model has failed the crash tests conducted by the Global New Car Assessment Programme (Global NCAP) on 17 May 2016

(A) Fiat Punto

(B) Chevrolet Cruze

(C) Mercedes-benz

(D) ** Renault Kwid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hyundai Eon, Maruti Suzuki Eeco, Maruti Celerio, Mahindra Scorpio and three models of Renault Kwid failed in crash tests carried by Global New Car Assessment Programme.

Concept note-2: -Top 20 safest cars in India with Global NCAP rating 2023 Of the 20 models listed here, 5-star rating cars in India include Skoda Kushaq, Volkswagen Taigun, Mahindra Scorpio-N, Tata Punch, Mahindra XUV300, Tata Altroz, Tata Nexon and Mahindra XUV700.

Concept note-3: -According to Global NCAP findings, the compact SUV did not provide acceptable child safety (up to 3 years old). There were excessive forward movements, which might result in head and chest damage for the child. However, it provides enough head and chest protection for the 1.5-year old.

Concept note-4: -The India-spec Nissan Magnite has finally been crash tested by Global NCAP. It was the standard variant with just two front airbags and scored four stars in adult safety along with a stable rating for the bodyshell integrity. The Magnite scored 11.85 out of 17 in terms of adult occupant protection.

Concept note-5: -The Skoda Kushaq and Volkswagen Taigun platform siblings delivered a stellar performance in Global NCAP’s crash tests, with full five stars for adult and child occupant protection. The two compact SUVs come with ESC and dual airbags as standard.
