Which of the following Indian stock exchange is introducing ’Paperless SIP’ (systematic investment plans) that would allow mutual fund investors to make transactions through various modes of payments.

(A) NSE

(B) KSE

(C) CSE

(D) ** BSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) has introduced digital (paperless) Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) for mutual fund investors.

Concept note-2: -Notes: Mutual funds in India are permitted to invest in Securities, Gold and Real Estate. A mutual fund is prohibited from investing in any unlisted security or a security issued through private placement by an associate or a group company of the sponsor.

Concept note-3: -SIP facility allows an investor to invest a fixed amount of money at pre-defined intervals in the selected mutual fund scheme. The fixed amount of money can be as low as Rs. 500, while the pre-defined SIP intervals can be on a weekly/monthly/quarterly/semi-annually or annual basis.

Concept note-4: -The units of close-ended schemes are mandatorily listed to provide exit route before maturity and can be sold/traded on the stock exchanges. Interval schemes allow purchase and redemption during specified transaction periods (intervals).
