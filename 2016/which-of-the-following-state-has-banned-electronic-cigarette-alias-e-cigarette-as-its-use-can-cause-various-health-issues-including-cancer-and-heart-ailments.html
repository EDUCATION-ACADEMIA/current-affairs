Which of the following state has banned ‘electronic cigarette’ alias ‘e-cigarette’ as its use can cause various health issues, including cancer and heart ailments

(A) Bihar

(B) Punjab

(C) Andhra Pradesh

(D) ** Kerala

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kerala government has decided to ban ‘electronic cigarette’ in view of studies claiming that its use can cause various health issues, including cancer and heart ailments.

Concept note-2: -States of Punjab, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Kerala, Bihar, Uttar Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Puducherry and Jharkhand have taken steps to ban the use of ENDS, officials in the union health ministry have said.

Concept note-3: -But the eastern Indian state of Jharkhand finally took the plunge and announced that it is banning the sale and usage of e-cigarettes. “E-cigarettes are the new method of nicotine use.

Concept note-4: -Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman in 2019 introduced the ban on the sale, storage, and manufacture of e-cigarettes following recommendations of a Group of Ministers (GoM), headed by her.

Concept note-5: -Also, e-cigarettes are banned in the country and possession can invite one-year imprisonment and a fine up to Rs 1 lakh.
