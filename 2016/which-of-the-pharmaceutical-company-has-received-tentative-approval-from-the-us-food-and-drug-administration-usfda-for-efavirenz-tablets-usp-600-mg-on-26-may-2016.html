Which of the Pharmaceutical company has received tentative approval from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (USFDA) for Efavirenz tablets USP, 600 mg on 26 May 2016

(A) Cipla

(B) Aurobindo Pharma

(C) ** Strides Shasun

(D) Cadila Healthcare

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tentative Approval FDA delays final approval of the generic drug product until all patent or exclusivity issues have been resolved. A tentative approval does not allow the applicant to market the generic drug product.

Concept note-2: -Efavirenz (brand names Sustiva® and Stocrin®) is a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI) and is used as part of highly active antiretroviral therapy (HAART) for the treatment of a human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) type 1.

Concept note-3: -Efavirenz is in a class of medications called non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NNRTIs). It works by decreasing the amount of HIV in the blood.

Concept note-4: -Efavirenz is used in combination with other medicines for the treatment of the infection caused by the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV). HIV is the virus that causes acquired immune deficiency syndrome (AIDS). Efavirenz is a non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor (NNRTI).
