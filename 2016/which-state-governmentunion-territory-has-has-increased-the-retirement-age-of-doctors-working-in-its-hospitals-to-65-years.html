Which state Government/Union Territory has has increased the retirement age of doctors working in its hospitals to 65 years.

(A) ** New Delhi

(B) Goa

(C) Puducherry

(D) Madhya Pradesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -New Delhi: Andhra Pradesh government has good news for its employees. In fact, the state employees have two reasons to celebrate as per the latest 7th pay commission news. The state has decided to increase the age of retirement from 60 to 62 years. Along with that, the salary will also be hiked by 23.39 per cent.

Concept note-2: -Andhra Pradesh announces increase in retirement age of State govt employees from 60 years to 62 year. Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister Y.S . Jagan Mohan Reddy has announced an increase in the retirement age of state government employees from 60 years to 62 years.

Concept note-3: -The retirement age for central government employees was last revised in May 1998, when it was moved up from 58 to 60 years. The corresponding age in the private sector has largely been in the 58 to 62 year-band.

Concept note-4: -The Assam government has raised the retirement age of doctors in government medical colleges of the State from 65 to 70 years.
