Who among the following is not part the of the Indian World War I heroes who became part of Britain’s new digital archive

(A) Risaldar Badlu Singh

(B) Sepoy Chatta Singh

(C) Lance Naik Lala

(D) ** Bhagat Singh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Military technology of the time included important innovations in machine guns, grenades, and artillery, along with essentially new weapons such as submarines, poison gas, warplanes and tanks.

Concept note-2: -During the conflict, Germany, Austria-Hungary, Bulgaria and the Ottoman Empire (the Central Powers) fought against Great Britain, France, Russia, Italy, Romania, Canada, Japan and the United States (the Allied Powers).

Concept note-3: -In March 1915, Indian troops provided half the attacking force at the Battle of Neuve Chapelle, which was the costliest in terms of lives. By 1918, India had sent over 1 million soldiers to fight in the War, not including Imperial Service Troops from the Princely States, Sailors and Indian Labour Corps.
