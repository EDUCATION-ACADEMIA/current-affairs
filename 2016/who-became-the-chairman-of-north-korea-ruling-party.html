Who became the chairman of North Korea ruling party

(A) Kim Yong-Nam

(B) ** Kim Jong-Un

(C) Kim Jong-Il

(D) Kim Tong-Un

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kim Jong-un (English: /ˌkɪm dʒɒŋˈʊn, -ˈuːn/; Korean: , Korean: [kim.dʑɔŋ.ɯːn]; born 8 January 1982) is a North Korean politician who has been Supreme Leader of North Korea since 2011 and the leader of the Workers’ Party of Korea (WPK ) since 2012.

Concept note-2: -The Workers’ Party of Korea (WPK) is the founding and sole ruling party of the Democratic People’s Republic of Korea, commonly known as North Korea.

Concept note-3: -EXECUTIVE SUMMARY. The Democratic People’s Republic of Korea (DPRK or North Korea) is an authoritarian state led by the Kim family for 70 years. Shortly after Kim Jong Il’s death in late 2011, his son Kim Jong Un was named marshal of the DPRK and supreme commander of the Korean People’s Army.
