Who became the first Indian wicket keeper and fourth in the world to complete 350 dismissals in One-Day International (ODI) cricket on 15 June 2016

(A) Robin Uthappa

(B) ** Mahendra Singh Dhoni

(C) Dinesh Karthik

(D) Ravindra Jadeja

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -M.S. Dhoni holds the record for the most dismissals in ODIs by an Indian wicket-keeper. He has the third-highest dismissals in the world in ODIs after Kumar Sangakkara (501 dismissals), and Adam Gilchrist (472 dismissals).

Concept note-2: -Under Dhoni’s captaincy, India won the ODI World Cup after 28 years. They beat Sri Lanka in the final at a jam-packed Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai with Dhoni leading from the front with an unbeaten 91.
