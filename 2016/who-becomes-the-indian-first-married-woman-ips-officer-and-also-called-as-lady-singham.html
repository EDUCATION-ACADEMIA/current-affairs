Who becomes the Indian first married woman IPS officer and also called as Lady Singham

(A) Jyoti Priya

(B) ** Manzil Saini

(C) Kim Sharma

(D) Meera Chadda

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During her tenure in Karauli, many dacoits had surrendered. She fearlessly went out into the ravines of Chambal. She also saved many girls from prostitution in Bundi and these are some of the many reasons why she is called Lady Singham.

Concept note-2: -Confirmed! Deepika Padukone is Lady Singham in Rohit Shetty’s next copverse. Watch.

Concept note-3: -The first woman IPS officer in India was Kiran Bedi. She qualified for the UPSC exam in 1972. As Director-General of the Bureau of Police Research and Development, she stayed in the workforce for 35 years before taking voluntary retirement in 2007. The Bharatiya Janata Party later welcomed her in January 2015.

Concept note-4: -Lipi Singh, a resident of the Nalanda district of Bihar, is a 2016 batch IPS officer and is always in news for her good work.

Concept note-5: -Kanchan Chaudhary Bhattacharya (c. 1947-26 August 2019) was the second woman officer in Indian Police Service (IPS) in India, the first being Kiran Bedi. A 1973 batch IPS officer, she was the first woman to become Director General of Police of a state and retired on 31 October 2007 after 33 years of service.
