Who has made the fastest century in cricket history

(A) AB de Villiers

(B) Corey Anderson

(C) ** Iraq Thomas

(D) Shahid Afridi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -South Africa’s AB de Villiers holds the record for the fastest one-day international (ODI) century of all time-he took just 31 balls to reach the milestone in a match against the West Indies in Johannesburg in 2015.

Concept note-2: -In One day International cricket (ODI), the fastest century is held by South African batsman AB De Villiers. De Villiers’ century came up in just 31 balls against the West Indies in the 2nd ODI at Johannesburg on 18 January 2015. De Villiers’ hundred included 8 fours and 16 sixes.

Concept note-3: -South Africa’s David Miller holds the joint record for the fastest international Twenty20 century of all time-he took just 35 balls to reach the milestone in a match against the Bangladesh in Potchefstroom in 2017.

Concept note-4: -AB de Villiers – South Africa vs West Indies (31 balls) This great batsman from South Africa is first in the list of top 10 fastest century in ODI.

Concept note-5: -AB de Villiers holds the record for the fastest 50 in ODI cricket. He took just 16 balls to reach 50 against the West Indies at Johannesburg, 2015.
