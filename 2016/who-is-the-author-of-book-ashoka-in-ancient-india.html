Who is the author of book "Ashoka in Ancient India"

(A) Vishakhadatta

(B) Jhumpa Lahiri

(C) Anurag Mathur

(D) ** Nayanjot Lahiri

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ashoka was the third emperor of the Mauryan dynasty, grandson of its founder Chandragupta and son of the second emperor, Bindusara. Upon Bindusara’s death, Ashoka and his brothers engaged in a war of succession, and Ashoka emerged victorious after several years of conflict.

Concept note-2: -The trilogy was written between 1937-1947 by a Dutch scholar Wytze Keuning in Groningen in the Netherlands. These three volumes Ashoka: The Wild Prince, Book I, Ashoka: The Wise Ruler, Book II and Ashoka: The World’s Great Teacher, Book III are now brought together and presented in this single volume Ashoka The Great.

Concept note-3: -James Mill wrote the book “History of India". He was a historian from Scotland. He never visited India even though he wrote six volumes of “History of India".
