Who was appointed as the acting president of pakistan Sikh Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee

(A) Baba Amarjit Singh

(B) Sham Singh

(C) Sardar Sahib Singh

(D) ** Tara Singh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sundar Singh Majithia was appointed first President of the committee. This post got legal status after the passing of Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925 by legislation in British India. After passing of this act Baba Kharak Singh became the President.

Concept note-2: -Custody of historic Sikh shrines would pass to the Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee, a Sikh-led committee. The SGPC, formed in 1920, was defined as consisting of 120 practicing Sikhs, the heads of the Panj Takht (five primary Sikh gurdwaras), 12 appointees from the Princely States, and “14 co-opted members".

Concept note-3: -Gurdwara Kartarpur Sahib is located in the Shakargarh Tehsil of the Narowal District in Punjab, Pakistan. It is a top tourist attraction for people visiting Pakistan. The shrine is located five kilometres from the Indo-Pakistani border.
