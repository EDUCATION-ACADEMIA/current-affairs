Who won the 2016 Reporters without Borders (RSF)-TV5 Monde Freedom Prize

(A) Najiba Ayubi

(B) Jineth Bedoya

(C) Zaheena Rasheed

(D) ** Hadi Abdullah

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hyderabad: India’s ranking fell from 133 in 2016 to 142 in 2021, of 180 countries, on the World Press Freedom Index that highlights the degree of freedom that journalists, news organisations and netizens have in each country, and the government’s efforts to respect such freedom.

Concept note-2: -Government Rejects World Press Freedom Index 2022 Where India Ranked 150th, Says ‘Methodology Questionable, Non-transparent’

Concept note-3: -India is ranked 150 out of 180 countries when it comes to freedom of the press, according to the 2022 World Press Freedom Index published by Reporters Without Borders.

Concept note-4: -The 2022 edition of the World Press Freedom Index, which assesses the state of journalism in 180 countries and territories, highlights the disastrous effects of news and information chaos – the effects of a globalised and unregulated online information space that encourages fake news and propaganda.
