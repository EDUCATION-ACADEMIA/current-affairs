Who won the 2016 Turner Prize

(A) Michael Dean

(B) Anthea Hamilton

(C) Josephine Pryde

(D) ** Helen Marten

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The four shortlisted artists for the Turner Prize 2016 are: Michael Dean. Anthea Hamilton. Helen Marten – winner.

Concept note-2: -Tate announced today that Helen Marten has won its 2016 Turner Prize. The young London-based sculptor will now receive £25, 000, or about $31, 800. Earlier this month, Marten also won the Hepworth Wakefield museum’s inaugural Hepworth Prize for Sculpture.

Concept note-3: -The four shortlisted artists for the Turner Prize 2016 are: Michael Dean. Anthea Hamilton. Helen Marten – winner.

Concept note-4: -The Turner Prize 2022 has been awarded to Veronica Ryan it was announced this evening at a ceremony at St George’s Hall, Liverpool. The £25, 000 prize was presented by musician Holly Johnson during a live broadcast on the BBC. A further £10, 000 is awarded to each of the other shortlisted artists.
