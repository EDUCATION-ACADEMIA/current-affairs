Who won the gold in the 100-metre final in Rio Olympics 2016

(A) Justin Gatlin

(B) Andre De Grasse

(C) ** Usain Bolt

(D) Heinz Weis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Usain Bolt of Jamaica proves he’s still invincible, winning the Olympic 100m final in 9.81 to claim gold for a third straight Games; American Justin Gatlin and Canadian Andre De Grasse take silver and bronze.

Concept note-2: -Olympic Track and Field 2016: Men’s 100M Medal Winners, Times and Results. Usain Bolt reaffirmed his status as the world’s fastest man Sunday night, winning his third straight gold medal in the 100-meter dash with a blistering run of 9.81 seconds.

Concept note-3: -The event was won by Derek Drouin of Canada, the nation’s first victory in the men’s high jump since 1932 (and second overall). Drouin was the ninth man to win multiple medals in the high jump, after his 2012 bronze.
