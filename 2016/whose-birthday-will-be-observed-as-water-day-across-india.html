Whose birthday will be observed as “Water Day” across India.

(A) Rajendra Prasad

(B) Lala Lajpat Rai

(C) ** Dr BR Ambedkar

(D) Jawaharlal Nehru

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ambedkar in the field of water management and way forward for ensuring inclusive growth of all sections of the society was discussed.

Concept note-2: -What is World Water Day? Every year since 1993, World Water Day (22 March) raises awareness and inspires action to tackle the water and sanitation crisis. It is about taking action to tackle the global water crisis of the 2.2 billion people living without access to safe water.

Concept note-3: -Rajendra Singh (born 6 August 1959) is an Indian water conservationist and environmentalist from Alwar district, Rajasthan in India. Also known as “waterman of India", he won the Magsaysay Award in 2001 and Stockholm Water Prize in 2015.

Concept note-4: -In December 1992, the United Nations General Assembly adopted resolution A/RES/47/193 by which 22 March of each year was declared World Day for Water.

Concept note-5: -According to the UN, World Water Day celebrates water and raises awareness of the 2.2 billion people living without access to safe water. To focus on the importance of freshwater, the United Nations marks March 22 every year as World Water Day.
