Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL) has successfully commissioned India’s first 800 MW coal-based supercritical thermal power plant in

(A) ** Raichur, Karnataka

(B) Trichy, Tamil Nadu

(C) Haridwar, Uttarakhand

(D) Hyderabad, Telangana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited (BHEL) has successfully commissioned the first 800 MW Supercritical thermal unit in Karnataka, which is also the highest rating unit in the state. The unit has been commissioned at the 2x800 MW Yeramarus Thermal Power Station (TPS) located in Raichur district of Karnataka.

Concept note-2: -Adani Enterprise Ltd run Adani Power Ltd (APL) said on Thursday that it has synchronised the country’s first supercritical unit of 660 MW power plant, taking its coal power generation capacity to 1980 MW.

Concept note-3: -Ramagundam Super Thermal power station, one of the biggest thermal power stations in India, is a coal based power station situated at ramagundam in Karimnagar District. The station started power generation in 1983. The station generates about 2600 MW of power annually.

Concept note-4: -All the initial preparatory work was carried out from Bhopal till November 1964. Government decided to create a separate corporation for setting-up and managing these three units. Thus Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited was born and formally incorporated on 13th November, 1964.

Concept note-5: -BHEL is one of the leading players in the hydro power segment with a portfolio of over 500 hydroelectric sets, and a cumulative capacity of more than 31, 000 MW globally.
