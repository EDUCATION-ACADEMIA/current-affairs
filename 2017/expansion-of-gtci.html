Expansion of GTCI

(A) Global of Talent Competitiveness and Index

(B) ** Global Talent Competitiveness Index

(C) Global of Talent and Competitiveness Index

(D) Global Talent and Competitiveness of Index

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Global Talent Competitiveness Index (GTCI 2020): 8th place for Luxembourg.

Concept note-2: -It is a comprehensive annual benchmarking report that measures how countries and cities grow, attract and retain talent. It provides a unique resource for decision makers to understand the global talent competitiveness picture and develop strategies to boost their economies.

Concept note-3: -Highlights of the WCI Index 2022 Denmark has toped the 2022 ranking featuring 63-nations list from the third position last year. It is followed by Switzerland (2nd) slipped from the top ranking in last year and Singapore regained the third spot from fifth.

Concept note-4: -The index is released by INSEAD (Graduate Business School) in partnership with Adecco Group (Human Resource Group) and Google. GTCI was first launched in 2013. At present, 132 countries and 155 cities are ranked in this Global Talent Competitiveness Index.
