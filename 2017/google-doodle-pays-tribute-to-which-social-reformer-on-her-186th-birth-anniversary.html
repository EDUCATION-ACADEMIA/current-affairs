Google Doodle pays tribute to which social reformer on her 186th birth anniversary

(A) Annie Besant

(B) Mother Teresa

(C) Aruna Roy

(D) ** Savitribai Phule

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This year’s Doodle for Google judging panel included actor, filmmaker, producer and TV personality Neena Gupta; Editor-in-Chief at Tinkle Comics, Kuriakose Vaisian; YouTube Creators Slayypoint; and, artist and entrepreneur Alika Bhat, and the Google Doodle team.

Concept note-2: -Doodles are the fun, surprising, and sometimes spontaneous changes that are made to the Google logo to celebrate holidays, anniversaries, and the lives of famous artists, pioneers, and scientists.

Concept note-3: -Who is Fatima Sheikh? Born on 9 January 1831 in Pune, India Fatima Sheikh was a Indian educator and social reformer who played a crucial role in women’s education. Fatima Sheikh co-founded the Indigenous Library in 1984 alongside Indian activist Jyotirao and Savitribai Phule in 1848.

Concept note-4: -Jyotirao Govindrao Phule, also known as Mahatma Jyotiba Phule (11 April 1827 – 28 November 1890) was an Indian social activist, thinker, anti-caste social reformer and writer from Maharashtra.
