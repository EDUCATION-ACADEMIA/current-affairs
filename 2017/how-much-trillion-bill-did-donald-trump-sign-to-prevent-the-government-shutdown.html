How much trillion bill did Donald Trump sign to prevent the government Shutdown?

(A) ** $ 1.2

(B) $ 3

(C) $ 5

(D) $ 6

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The United States federal government shut down from midnight EST on December 22, 2018, until January 25, 2019 (35 days) was the longest government shutdown in history and the second and final federal government shutdown involving furloughs during the presidency of Donald Trump.

Concept note-2: -When there is a government shutdown, federal agencies are required to classify their employees as either “essential” or “non-essential.” The employees classified as “essential” continue to work during the shutdown. However, the employees classified as “non-essential” are put on unpaid furlough.
