Identify the veteran Indian-origin South African anti-apartheid activist, who was one of Nelson Mandela’s closest aides, died recently.

(A) Walter Sisulu

(B) ** Ahmed Kathrada

(C) Steve Biko

(D) Mathews Phosa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mr Kathrada, affectionately known as Kathy, was not only one of Mr Mandela’s closest friends, but also a human rights activist in his own right who had a long history in the struggle against discrimination and apartheid, says the BBC’s Milton Nkosi.

Concept note-2: -Ahmed Kathrada was born on 21 August 1929 in the small country town of Schweizer-Reneke in the Western Transvaal, the fourth of six children in a Gujarati Bohra family of South African Indian immigrant parents from Surat, Gujarat.

Concept note-3: -Nelson Mandela was an important person among the many that were anti apartheid.

Concept note-4: -Former South African president and civil rights advocate Nelson Mandela dedicated his life to fighting for equality-and ultimately helped topple South Africa’s racist system of apartheid. His accomplishments are now celebrated each year on July 18, Nelson Mandela International Day.

Concept note-5: -This was on 16 June 1976. The African National Congress, a group of people who led the struggle against apartheid, and their most well known leader, Nelson Mandela fought the apartheid system for several years.
