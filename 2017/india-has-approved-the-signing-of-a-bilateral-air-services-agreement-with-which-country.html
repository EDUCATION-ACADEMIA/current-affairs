India has approved the signing of a bilateral Air Services Agreement with which country

(A) Colombia

(B) Ghana

(C) Argentina

(D) ** Rwanda

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Context: India has signed a bilateral air service agreement with 116 countries including neighbouring Bangladesh, Afghanistan, and Pakistan along with the US, UK, UAE etc.

Concept note-2: -India has signed Bilateral Air Service Agreement with 116 countries | IBEF.

Concept note-3: -Bilateral agreements are air service agreements between two countries which provide different degrees of freedom of air, which are a set of commercial aviation rights granting a country’s airlines the privilege to enter another country’s airspace.

Concept note-4: -A bilateral air service agreement is concluded between two contracting countries and liberalizes commercial civil aviation services between those countries.

Concept note-5: -India has recently signed an ‘open skies agreement’ with Greece. The agreement aims to allow an unlimited number of flights from India to Greece whereas Greece is allowed to make unlimited flights to 6 metro cities in India.
