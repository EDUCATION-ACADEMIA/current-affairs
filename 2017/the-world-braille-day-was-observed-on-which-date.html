The World Braille Day was observed on which date

(A) 21 September

(B) 6 October

(C) 19 December

(D) ** 4 January

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -We celebrate World Braille Day every year on January 4th because it’s Louis Braille’s birthday. He’s the inventor of braille! Louis was born in 1809 in France and became blind after a childhood accident.

Concept note-2: -January 4th commemorates the birthdate of Louis Braille. The UN celebrated the first official World Braille Day in 2019.

Concept note-3: -World Braille Day is an international day on 4 January and celebrates awareness of the importance of braille as a means of communication in the full realization of the human rights for blind and visually impaired people.

Concept note-4: -World Braille Day, celebrated since 2019, is observed to raise awareness of the importance of Braille as a means of communication in the full realization of the human rights for blind and partially sighted people.

Concept note-5: -Significance of World Braille Day 2023 The method was generally neglected until Braille died in misery on January 6, 1852, in Paris, from illness. The United Nations declared January 4th as World Braille Day in a proclamation issued in 2018.
