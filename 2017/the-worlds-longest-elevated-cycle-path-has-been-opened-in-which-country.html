The world’s longest elevated cycle path has been opened in which country

(A) India

(B) Japan

(C) Japan

(D) ** China

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The longest elevated bike path in the world has been opened in the Chinese city of Xiamen. Dubbed “the winding viaduct” by Chinese news agency Xinhau, the route is predicted to spur a cycling infrastructure revolution across the country.

Concept note-2: -The longest continuous cycle path in the world laid in Qatar | Guinness World Records.

Concept note-3: -North Sea Cycle Route, Europe This route is the longest signposted cycling trail in the world. It offers 6, 000km of waymarked cycling along the coastline of the North Sea and travels through seven countries, including the UK, the Netherlands, Germany, Denmark, Sweden and Norway.

Concept note-4: -While the Netherlands is the country with the most cyclists per capita, the city with the most cyclists is actually Copenhagen, Denmark. Up to 62% of Copenhagen’s population use a bicycle for their daily commute to work or school, and they cycle an average of 894, 000 miles every day.
