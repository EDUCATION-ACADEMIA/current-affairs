What is the Currency of Iceland?

(A) Pound

(B) ** krona

(C) Euro

(D) Ruble

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is always best to pay using Icelandic króna or using your credit or debit card where possible. As with travelling in any location, we’d recommend always carrying your credit or debit card in addition to an adequate amount of local currency in cash.

Concept note-2: -1 ISK = 0.578384 INR Feb 10, 2023 23:44 UTC Check the currency rates against all the world currencies here. The currency converter below is easy to use and the currency rates are updated frequently.

Concept note-3: -Using dollars or euros Although the official currency is Icelandic Kroner, some of the larger hotels and stores do accept euros or dollars. However, they set their exchange rate, so you would end up paying above the odds.

Concept note-4: -Money. Currency: The currency in Iceland is the Icelandic króna (ISK). Many places (restaurants, bars, tourist attractions) will take US dollars, Canadian dollars, Euros, Norwegian, Swedish and Danish currencies. Payment: Icelanders usually pay for everything by credit or debit card.
