What scheme is launched by the Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corp (IRCTC)?

(A) VIKALP scheme

(B) Tatkal Scheme

(C) E Wallet Scheme

(D) ** Book Now, Pay Later Scheme

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under the Tatkal scheme, passengers who plan their journey at short notice can book their tickets for almost all Mail/Express trains through the Indian railway’s internet portal.

Concept note-2: -The Ministry of Railways has launched a new scheme ‘Amrit Bharat Station scheme’ for the modernisation of the railway stations across the country.

Concept note-3: -IRCTC manages the entire on-board catering services of Indian Railways in more than 460 passenger trains with pantry cars that includes Rajdhani trains, Shatabdi trains, Duronto Trains, Gatimaan Express and Mail/Express trains and also in newly introduced passenger trains such as Vande Bharat Express and Tejas Trains.

Concept note-4: -It allows any operator or service provider, or virtually anyone, to lease trains from Indian Railways to run on a theme-based circuit as a special tourism package. Private players and tour operators can procure trains from railways on lease and operate them on any circuit of their choice.

Concept note-5: -The Bharat Gaurav trains were launched by the Railways Ministry to be operated in places having touristic importance and promote theme-based tourism.
