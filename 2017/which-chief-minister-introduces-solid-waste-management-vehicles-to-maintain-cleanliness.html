[SOURCES]

Which Chief Minister introduces Solid Waste Management vehicles to maintain cleanliness?

(A) Shri Narayanswamy

(B) Shri Edappadi Palaniswamy

(C) Shri Yogi Adiyanda

(D) ** Shri Chandrababu Naidu

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Government of India has undertaken multiple large-scale national initiatives such as ‘Swachh Bharat Mission’, ‘National Water Mission’ and ‘Waste to Wealth Mission” as a part of its commitment to effective waste & pollution management in India.

Concept note-2: -At the national policy level, the ministry of environment and forests has legislated the Municipal Waste Management and Handling Rules 2000. This law details the practices to be followed by the various municipalities for managing urban waste.

Concept note-3: -Under which rule of Government, guidelines for solid waste management are followed today? Explanation: At present, we have to follow the Solid Waste Management Rules, 2016. This new rule was notified on 8th April 2016 and it supersedes the Municipal Solid Waste Rules, 2000.

Concept note-4: -The government’s environmental policy focusses on the following areas: (i) The check degradation of land and water through wasteland management and restorations of river water quality programmes. (iii) To make laws and acts for environment protection and to initiate penal measures against those who violate these laws.
