Which city does the Light Utility Helicopter completed its first flight recently?

(A) Chennai

(B) Mumbai

(C) Kolkata

(D) ** Bengaluru

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Testing at sea-level altitudes was completed at Chennai in 2018 and at Puducherry in 2019.

Concept note-2: -On 19 November 2021, Prime Minister Narendra Modi formally handed over the LCH to IAF Air Chief Marshal Vivek Ram Chaudhari, clearing way for full scale induction. On 3 October 2022, the LCH was formally inducted into the IAF and was officially named “Prachand".

Concept note-3: -Facts about “Prachand" 1. The only attack helicopter in the world that can land and take off at an altitude of 5, 000 metres. 2. LCH Prachand is fitted with a 5.8-tonne twin-engine named Shakti engine, primarily designed for deployment in high-altitude areas.

Concept note-4: -The LCH is a 5.5-tonne class dedicated combat helicopter designed and developed by Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd (HAL).

Concept note-5: -The HAL Dhruv is a utility helicopter designed and developed by Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL) in November 1984.
