Which city India’s First Centre for Animal Law set up?

(A) Chennai

(B) ** Hyderabad

(C) Kolkata

(D) Mumbai

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Union Minister Smt. Maneka Sanjay Gandhi inaugurates centre in Hyderabad. NALSAR University of Law, ranked amongst the top three national law universities in India, has established India’s first Centre for Animal Law.

Concept note-2: -Hyderabad gets India’s first Centre for Animal Law.

Concept note-3: -The Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960. The basic cruelty law of India is contained in the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act 1960. The objective of the Act is to prevent the infliction of unnecessary pain or suffering on animals and to amend the laws relating to the prevention of cruelty to animals.

Concept note-4: -Constitution of Animal Welfare Board of India 59 of 1960). Shrimati Rukmini Devi Arundale pioneered the setting up of the Board, with its Headquaters at Chennai.

Concept note-5: -India Code: Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960. Long Title: An Act to prevent the infliction of unnecessary pain or suffering on animals and for that purpose to amend the law relating to the prevention of cruelty to animals.
