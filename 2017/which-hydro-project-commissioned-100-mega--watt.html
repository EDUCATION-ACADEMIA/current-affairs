(A) Tehri Dam Project

(B) Dehar Dam Project

(C) ** Sainj Hydropower Project

(D) Mulshi Dam

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Iceland. The land of fire and ice has unique access to a range of renewable resources. A combination of hydropower and geothermal power provide almost 100% of Iceland’s electricity needs.

Concept note-2: -The Koyna Hydroelectric Project is the largest hydroelectric power plant in India. It is a complex project with four dams including the largest dam on the Koyna River, Maharashtra hence the name Koyna Hydroelectric Project.

Concept note-3: -The Three Gorges Dam on the Yangtze River in China is the world’s biggest hydroelectric facility.

Concept note-4: -The largest hydroelectric dam in the world, China’s Three Gorges Dam can generate the most amount of power. Image: Visual Capitalist. At the top of the list is China’s Three Gorges Dam, which opened in 2003. It has an installed capacity of 22.5 gigawatts (GW), which is close to double the second-place Itaipu Dam.
