Which month the Zero Hunger Programme to be launched?

(A) March

(B) June

(C) ** October

(D) August

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Zero Hunger Program was launched by the government of India on the occasion of World Food Day i.e. 16th OCT 2017.

Concept note-2: -Zero Hunger Programme – Details The programme was initiated by the ICAR (Indian Council of Agricultural Research) along with the ICMR (Indian Council of Medical Research) and the M.S. Swaminathan Research Foundation with BIRAC (Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council).

Concept note-3: -Sustainable Development Goal 2 is about creating a world free of hunger by 2030. In 2020, between 720 million and 811 million persons worldwide were suffering from hunger, roughly 161 million more than in 2019.

Concept note-4: -The Zero Hunger Programme aims to: Decrease child stunting for children 2 years and younger. Ensure access to food all year round. Create stable food systems.

Concept note-5: -Thus, UN Zero Hunger aim is to end hunger and all forms of malnutrition, improve nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture by the year 2030. In order to achieve the goals, the focus should be on increasing access to safe, nutritious food to poor persons, women, children and old people all year round.
