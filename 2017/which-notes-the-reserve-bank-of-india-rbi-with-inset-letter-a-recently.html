(A) Rs 2000

(B) Rs 100

(C) ** Rs 500

(D) Rs 50

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -British India Issues commence with the Paper Currency Act of 1861 which gave the Government the monopoly of note issue in India.

Concept note-2: -The Reserve Bank of India has also issued ₹10 denomination banknotes in the Mahatma Gandhi New Series. The new denomination has a motif of the Sun Temple, Konark on the reverse, depicting the country’s cultural heritage. The base colour of the note is Chocolate brown.
