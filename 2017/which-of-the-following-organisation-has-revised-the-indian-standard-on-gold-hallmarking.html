Which of the following Organisation has revised the Indian Standard on gold hallmarking

(A) Reserve Bank of India

(B) Ministry of Finance

(C) ** Bureau of Indian Standards

(D) Indian Standard Gold Organisation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The BIS Hallmark is a hallmarking system for gold as well as silver jewellery sold in India certifying the purity of the metal. It certifies that the piece of jewellery conforms to a set of standards laid by the Bureau of Indian Standards, the national standards organization of India.

Concept note-2: -Indian Standard “IS 15820:2009 General Requirements for Establishment and Operation of Assaying and Hallmarking Centres” is the basis for recognition of Assaying and Hallmarking Centres.

Concept note-3: -For this, the Bureau of Indian Standards, (BIS) certifies the purity of gold by a process called hallmarking. According to BIS, there are four components that one should look for on the hallmarked gold jewellery to ensure its purity.
