Which Railway Station becomes India’s “First” Energy Efficient Railway Station?

(A) Mysore Railway Station

(B) Chennai Central Railway Station

(C) ** Kacheguda railway station

(D) Old Delhi Railway Station

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kacheguda railway station in Hyderabad has become India’s first Energy-Efficient ‘A1 Category’ Railway Station. It is operated by the South Central Railway (SCR) zone of Indian Railways. The station achieved the feat by replacing 1, 312 conventional lights with Light-Emitting Diode (LED).

Concept note-2: -Since the original structures of Bombay and Thane stations no longer exist, Royapuram station remains the oldest railway station in the entire subcontinent.

Concept note-3: -Gujarat’s Kevadiya railway station is India’s first railway station with a green building certification.

Concept note-4: -Constructed in 1916, by the Nizam of Hyderabad Asaf Jah VII, this Station was the Headquarters of the then Nizam’s Guaranteed State Railway. It is believed that name Kachiguda Railway Station was given because of the Kachhi Community which has a good population residing close to the station.
