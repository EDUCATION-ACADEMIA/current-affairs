Which state government made compulsory the bengali language?

(A) ** West Bengal

(B) Bihar

(C) Kerala

(D) Goa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bengali linguists Suniti Kumar Chatterji and Sukumar Sen suggested that Bengali had its origin in the 10th century ce, deriving from Magahi Prakrit (a spoken language) through Magahi Apabhramsha (its written counterpart).

Concept note-2: -Within India, Bengali is the official language of the states of West Bengal, Tripura and the Barak Valley region of the state of Assam. It is also the second official language of the Indian state of Jharkhand since September 2011.

Concept note-3: -Mostly during you 10+2 you need to take language subjects but for state boards now you can take English A and English B and for ICSE and CBSE boards you can skip Bengali if you want. Further in higher studies it is totally upto you whether you want to opt for Bengali Hons or Bengali as a combination.

Concept note-4: -"From now on, it will be mandatory for students to learn Bengali in schools. The schools affiliated to other boards (ICSE/CBSE) will have to make Bengali as an optional subject from Class 1, giving students the option to choose it either as second or third language, ” Mr Chatterjee said.
