Which state India’s Largest Floating Solar Power Plant Opens?

(A) ** Kerala

(B) Andhra Pradesh

(C) Madhya Pradesh

(D) West Bengal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s largest floating Solar Power Project is now fully operational. NTPC declared Commercial Operation of the final part capacity of 20 MW out of 100 MW Ramagundam Floating Solar PV Project at Ramagundam, Telangana with effect from 1 July.

Concept note-2: -Union Territory Administrator, Banwari Lal Purohit has inaugurated northern India’s largest floating solar power project of 2000kWp worth Rs 11.70 crore at waterworks, Sector 39, Chandigarh. The inauguration was held in the presence of MP Kirron Kher.

Concept note-3: -The correct answer is Madhya Pradesh. The world’s largest floating solar energy project of 600 MW is started in Khandwa district of Madhya Pradesh. Will begin power generation by the year 2022-23.

Concept note-4: -The Kayamkulam plant is the second-largest floating solar power project of the NTPC after the 100-MW plant at Ramagundam in Telangana.
