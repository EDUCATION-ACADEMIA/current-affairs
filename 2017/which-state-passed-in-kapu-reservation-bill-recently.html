Which state Passed in Kapu Reservation Bill recently?

(A) Kerala

(B) ** Andhra Pradesh

(C) Madhya Pradesh

(D) West Bengal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Andhra Pradesh Kapu (Reservation of Seats in Educational Institutions and of Appointments or Posts in the Services under State) Bill, 2017, was reserved by the Hon’ble Governor of Andhra Pradesh, for assent of Hon’ble President of India.

Concept note-2: -Andhra Pradesh state percentage of reservation is around 50%. Including the overall 1/3 reservation for women, 66.66% of seats are reserved in Andhra Pradesh in Education and Government jobs. Andhra Pradesh BC quota has sub-quotas for castes, classified in A, B, C and D.

Concept note-3: -The Kapus are primarily an agrarian community based in the Andhra-Telangana region. It is believed that they migrated from the Gangetic plains, probably from Kampilya (near Ayodhya) thousands of years ago.

Concept note-4: -The Kapu are considered to be a Sat-Shudra community in the traditional Hindu ritual ranking system known as varna.

Concept note-5: -Actually, both the Kamma and the Reddy were originally from the same Kapu caste. It would have been logical if that Kapu caste was recognized as Kshatriya Varna. Then the division wouldn’t have happened. The Kapu are not as economically strong and united as the other two castes.
