Who created the world record by becoming Fastest Batsman to Score 8000 runs in One Day International (ODI)?

(A) ** Virat Kohli

(B) Suresh Raina

(C) Gautam Gambhir

(D) Parthiv Patel

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is AB de Villiers. AB de Villiers set the record for the fastest 8000 runs in one-day cricket in August 2015. AB de Villiers is the only batsman to score 8000 ODI runs in less than 200 innings.

Concept note-2: -Well, only one man can be at the top of the list, and that is former Indian skipper Virat Kohli. The former skipper caught the eyes of everyone since his debut in international cricket and has been one of the most consistent players ever. Virat completed 8000 ODI runs in just 175 innings during his career.

Concept note-3: -South Africa’s AB de Villiers holds the record for the fastest one-day international (ODI) century of all time-he took just 31 balls to reach the milestone in a match against the West Indies in Johannesburg in 2015.

Concept note-4: -Nothing explains this better than this piece of statistic: Afridi has taken 367 innings to reach 8000–the slowest by any batsman for this milestone, eclipsing Mahela Jayawardene’s feat of reaching the milestone in 278 innings!

Concept note-5: -Virat Kohli (India) – 205 ODI innings Virat is a living legend and is the fastest batter to reach 10, 000 runs. He took 205 innings to do so. In his career, Virat has scored 12355 runs in 253 innings, including 43 centuries and 64 fifties.
