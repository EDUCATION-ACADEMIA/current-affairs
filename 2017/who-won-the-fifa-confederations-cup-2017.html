Who won the FIFA Confederations Cup 2017?

(A) ** Germany

(B) Russia

(C) Chile

(D) Portugal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Germany won the final 1–0 via a 20th-minute goal from Lars Stindl to claim their first and only FIFA Confederations Cup title.

Concept note-2: -Germany won the final 1–0 via a 20th-minute goal from Lars Stindl to claim their first and only FIFA Confederations Cup title. It was the last FIFA Confederations Cup match.

Concept note-3: -The competition was last staged in 2017, ahead of the 2018 World Cup in Russia, with a revision to the international calendar made prior to Qatar 2022. FIFA took the decision to do away with the Confederations Cup as they instead look to expand the Club World Cup.

Concept note-4: -With four titles, Brazil was the most successful team. The other former champions were France, with two titles, and Argentina, Denmark, Mexico and Germany with one title each. The competition was cancelled in 2019 and replaced with an expanded FIFA Club World Cup.

Concept note-5: -Real Madrid won the match 1–0 via a goal from Cristiano Ronaldo for their third FIFA Club World Cup title. This was the first time a team had successfully defended the FIFA Club World Cup title, after Real won the previous year. The victory also marked the tenth time a UEFA team had won the Club World Cup.
