A 10-year-old swimmer named Clark Kent has broken a record that was held by Olympian _________ for 23 years.

(A) ** Michael Phelps

(B) Mark Spitz

(C) Matt Biondi

(D) Ryan Lochte

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -10-year-old named Clark Kent breaks record held by Michael Phelps for 23 years. SALINAS, Calif. - Clark Kent may be Superman, but a 10-year-old boy who shares his name has the powers of Aquaman. At the Far West International Swimming Championship Sunday, Clark Kent Apuada placed first in all seven events he swam in.

Concept note-2: -The boy from Monterey Country in the USA is known as aqua Superman after breaking Michael Phelps’ 100 metre butterfly record, set at the same meet in 1995.

Concept note-3: -A 10-year-old named Clark Kent beat Michael Phelps’ 100-meter butterfly record, which he set in 1995. Turns out, Superman might actually exist. A 10-year-old whose real name is Clark Kent Apuada beat Michael Phelps’ 100-meter butterfly record over the weekend, CNN reports.

Concept note-4: -Michael Phelps, arguably the best swimmer in history, is regarded as one of the greatest athletes of all time, not just due to his record-breaking feats, but also due to the longevity of his career. Born on June 30, 1985, in Baltimore, Maryland, Phelps took to swimming at the age of seven.
