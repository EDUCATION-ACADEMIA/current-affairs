A book titled ’Gandhi: The years that changed the world (1914-1948)’ has been written by

(A) Nikesh Shukla

(B) Chandrahas Choudhury

(C) Sujata Massey

(D) ** Ramachandra Guha

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ramachandra Guha is the author of a number of major history books, including INDIA AFTER GANDHI. He lives in Bangalore.

Concept note-2: -A brilliant and scholarly work by Ram Guha. We have heard or read about Gandhi over the years and was always fascinated or sometimes provided us with intrigue. If we want to understand Gandhi fully and dispassionately one must read this book.

Concept note-3: -Gandhi: The Years That Changed the World, 1914-1948 is a non-fiction book by Indian historian Ramachandra Guha (born 1958) published by Penguin Random House in September 2018.

Concept note-4: -The Story of My Experiments with Truth (Gujarati: Satya Na Prayogo athva Atmakatha, lit. “Experiments with Truth or Autobiography") is Mohandas K. Gandhi’s autobiography, which spans his life from childhood to 1921.

Concept note-5: -The famous book written by Mahatma Gandhi in the year 1909 is ‘Hind Swaraj’.
