According to Oxfam International’s report, India ranks _________th among 157 countries.

(A) 145

(B) 146

(C) ** 147

(D) 148

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Oxfam report based on the index said India features among the lowest performers on health spending again. The index showed that it has dropped a further two places in the rankings, to 157th, the 5th lowest in the world.

Concept note-2: -India improves its ranks to 123 on the Commitment to Reducing Inequality Index 2022. India has improved its ranking by 6 places in the recently released Commitment to Reducing Inequality Index (CRII) 2022.

Concept note-3: -India’s top 1% owned more than 40.5% of its total wealth in 2021, according to a new report by Oxfam. In 2022, the number of billionaires in the country increased to 166 from from 102 in 2020, the report said. Meanwhile, it added that the poor in India “are unable to afford even basic necessities to survive".

Concept note-4: -According to the recent Commitment to Reducing Inequality Index (CRII) 2022, India has ranked 123 out of 161 countries in reducing inequality.

Concept note-5: -Oxfam India’s report on inequality in India released on Monday finds that just 5 per cent of Indians own more than 60 per cent of the country’s wealth, while the bottom 50 per cent of the population possess only 3 per cent of the wealth.
