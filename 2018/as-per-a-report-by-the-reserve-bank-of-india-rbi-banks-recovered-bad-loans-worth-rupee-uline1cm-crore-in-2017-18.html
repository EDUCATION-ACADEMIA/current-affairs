[SOURCES]

As per a report by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), Banks recovered bad loans worth Rupee. _________ crore in 2017-18.

(A) ** 40, 400

(B) 35, 300

(C) 20, 200

(D) 55, 500

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Gross NPAs were 11.2 per cent in 2017-18. Significantly, banks were able to recover only Rs 1, 32, 036 crore from the written off loans in the last five years, according to the RTI reply.

Concept note-2: -In the last five financial years, the PSBs have made an aggregate recovery of ₹4, 80, 111 crore from NPA accounts and upgradation of NPAs of ₹1, 45, 356 crore, ” said the minister.

Concept note-3: -Data shared by the ministry of finance in the Lok Sabha provides details of written-off loans recovered by our public sector banks over the last five years. It amounts to ₹1.03 trillion.

Concept note-4: -Recovery rate, commonly used in credit risk management, refers to the amount recovered when a loan defaults. In other words, the recovery rate is the amount, expressed as a percentage, recovered from a loan when the borrower is unable to settle the full outstanding amount.
