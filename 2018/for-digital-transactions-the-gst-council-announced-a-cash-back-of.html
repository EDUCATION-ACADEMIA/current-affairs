For digital transactions the GST Council announced a cash back of

(A) ** 20 %

(B) 15 %

(C) 45 %

(D) 35 %

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The cashback is 20% of the GST component with a cap of Rs 100 and this will be credited to the Bank account of the consumer linked to the card or User ID.

Concept note-2: -Gift, cash-back vouchers taxable as goods, to be liable for 18% GST: AAR | Mint.

Concept note-3: -The government has been charging a tax on digital payments while promoting digital payments. It is high time that GST on payments is removed as it is tantamount to a tax on a tax paid purchase and inhibits the growth of digital payments.

Concept note-4: -Union Budget 2022 | 30% Tax On All Digital Assets + 1% TDS: All You Want To Know.
