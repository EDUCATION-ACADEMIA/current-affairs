For which year Tokyo Unveils Mascots For Olympics And Paralympics?

(A) ** 2020

(B) 2019

(C) 2025

(D) 2021

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“Shuey Rhon Rhon,” a Chinese lantern child, will serve as the mascot for the 2022 Winter Paralympics. Also designed by Cao Xue, Shuey is made from traditional Chinese paper cutting and Ruyi ornaments. Shuey’s heart releases a glow, symbolizing the friendship and courage of Paralympic athletes.

Concept note-2: -The mascots of Paris 2024 are designed like the red Phrygian cap and are called Les Phryges (pronounced free-jes). “We wanted mascots that would embody our vision and be able to share it with the French people and the world…

Concept note-3: -The endangered palm cockatoo has been nominated by Birds Queensland as the mascot of the 2032 Games.

Concept note-4: -The Olympic mascots are fictional characters, usually an animal native to the area or human figures, who represent the cultural heritage of the place where the Olympic and Paralympic Games are taking place. Ever since the 1932 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles, California, the Olympic Games have always had a mascot.
