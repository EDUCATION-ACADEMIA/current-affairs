General elections were held in Pakistan on 25 July 2018 to elect the members of the _________ National Assembly.

(A) 12th

(B) 18th

(C) ** 15th

(D) 10th

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -106 million people were registered to vote for members of the National Assembly of Pakistan and four Provincial Assemblies. Likewise for elections to provincial assemblies, Punjab has 297 constituencies, Sindh 130, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa 99 and Balochistan 51.

Concept note-2: -The National Assembly consists of 342 members.

Concept note-3: -As a result of the election, the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) became the single largest party, though without an overall majority. PTI won 149 seats in the National Assembly. Pakistan Muslim League (N) (PML-N) and Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP) secured 82 and 53 seats, respectively.

Concept note-4: -(1) There shall be [three hundred and thirty-six] seats for members in the National Assembly, including seats reserved for women and non-Muslims. Article 51.

Concept note-5: -The result was a victory for the Awami League, which gained an absolute majority, winning 160 of the 162 general seats and all seven women’s seats in East Pakistan.
