Goa’s ban on importing of fish is to last for _________

(A) ** 6 months

(B) 45 days

(C) 2 months

(D) 12 months

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The annual ban on mechanized fishing has commenced in the state of Goa from today, June 1, 2022, and will go on till August 1, 2022. The fishing ban allows sufficient time for the fish to breed along the coastal belt of Goa.

Concept note-2: -There’s much to choose from Goa’s fresh catch: the flat lepo (sole fish), chonak (giant sea perch), xinaneo (mussels), visvonn (kingfish), tisryo (clams), kalvam (oysters), black and white pomfrets and more. The simplest way to enjoy this fish is fried (with a generous coating of rava).

Concept note-3: -The annual average per capita consumption of fish in Goa is 15-17 kg.
