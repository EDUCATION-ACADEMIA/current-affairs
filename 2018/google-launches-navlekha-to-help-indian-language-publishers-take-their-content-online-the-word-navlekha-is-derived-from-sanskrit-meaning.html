Google launches ‘Navlekha’ to help Indian language publishers take their content online. The word Navlekha is derived from Sanskrit meaning

(A) An Easy way to access online

(B) A Perfect way to publish

(C) A Fast way to digitise

(D) ** A New way to write

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Google launches Navlekha, a new platform for Indic language publishers.

Concept note-2: -Hindi is a descendant of Sanskrit, which is an ancient Indian language. It has been influenced by languages such as Arabic, Persian, Turkish, Portuguese and English, and it is part of the language family known as the Indo-European language family.

Concept note-3: -There is no national language in India. However, article 343(1) of the Indian constitution specifically mentions that “The official language of the Union shall be Hindi in Devanagari script.

Concept note-4: -The Sanskrit language has been spoken since 5,000 years before Christ. Sanskrit is still the official language of India.
