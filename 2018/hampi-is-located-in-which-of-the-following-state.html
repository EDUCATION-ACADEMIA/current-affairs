Hampi is located in which of the following state?

(A) Maharashtra

(B) Jharkhand

(C) Odisha

(D) ** Karnataka

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hampi is a town in the Vijayanagara district in the Indian state of Karnataka. Located along the Tungabhadra River in the east and center part of the state, Hampi is near the city of Hosapete .

Concept note-2: -Hampi or Hampe, also referred to as the Group of Monuments at Hampi, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Hampi town, Vijayanagara district, east-central Karnataka, India.

Concept note-3: -The present-day small village of Hampi was once the grand and prosperous capital city of the Vijayangar empire. Historians believe that at the zenith of its glory, in 1500, Hampi-Vijayanagara was the second-largest medieval-era city in the world after Beijing and probably the richest in India.

Concept note-4: -Hampi is a historical site located in Karnataka, India.

Concept note-5: -The Historic city ‘Hampi’ is located on the banks of the Tungabhadra River. The Tungabhadra River is formed by the confluence of the Tunga River and the Bhadra River at Koodli, Karnataka. The Tunga and the Bhadra rise at Gangamoola, in Varaha Parvatha in the Chikmagalur district in Karnataka.
