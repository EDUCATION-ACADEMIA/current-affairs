How many concrete structured watch towers are being built for the Border Security Force (BSF)?

(A) ** 350

(B) 450

(C) 500

(D) 650

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sources told TOI that 3 pylon shaped towers are coming up in the 4, 050 sq km Sir Creek area and 5 such concrete towers will be built in the 900 sq km Harami nalla area.

Concept note-2: -At present BSF is holding 192 (including 03 NDRF) Bns and 07 BSF Arty Regiments guarding International Border with Pakistan and Bangladesh.

Concept note-3: -The full form of BSF is the Border Security Force. BSF is India’s paramilitary force with a duty to protect India and maintain peace.

Concept note-4: -Therefore, the India’s border with Pakistan is protected by the Border Security Forces. Q. The is a Border Guarding Force of India that guards India’s borders with Pakistan and Bangladesh.

Concept note-5: -The BSF is recognized as an Armed Police Force, in contrast to the Indian Army, which was a Military Force. The Department of Home Affairs coordinates and supervises the Border Security Force. The BSF headquarters are located in New Delhi.
