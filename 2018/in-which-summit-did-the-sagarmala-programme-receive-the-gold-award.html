In which summit did the Sagarmala Programme receive the ‘Gold Award’?

(A) 51st Skoch Summit 2018

(B) ** 52nd Skoch Summit 2018

(C) 52nd Skoch Summit 2017

(D) 53rd Skoch Summit 2017

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sagarmala Programme was approved by the Union Cabinet in March 2015 and a National Perspective Plan was prepared by the Ministry of Shipping which was released on 14th April 2016. As of now, 500 projects have been identified at an estimated infrastructure investment of Rs. 3.55 Lac Crore across all the pillars.

Concept note-2: -The Sagarmala Projects are being undertaken in the states of Gujarat, West Bengal, Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, Kerala, Goa, Karnataka, Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu.

Concept note-3: -What is Sagarmala Project? About: The Sagarmala Programme was approved by the Union Cabinet in 2015 which aims at holistic port infrastructure development along the 7, 516-km long coastline through modernisation, mechanisation and computerisation.

Concept note-4: -It is a flagship programme of the Ministry of Shipping. The Sagarmala Programme was originally started by the National Democratic Alliance (India) government under Atal Bihari Vajpayee in 2003. Under Sagarmala Programme there is an estimated investment of approximately ₹8.570500 lakh crore.
