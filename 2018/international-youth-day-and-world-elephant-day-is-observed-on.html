[SOURCES]

International Youth Day and World Elephant Day is observed on

(A) August 10

(B) August 11

(C) ** August 12

(D) August 13

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This day was observed for the first time on 12 August 2012. And since then, World Elephant Day has been celebrated every year.

Concept note-2: -On August 12, 2012, the inaugural World Elephant Day was launched to bring attention to the urgent plight of Asian and African elephants. The elephant is loved, revered and respected by people and cultures around the world, yet we balance on the brink of seeing the last of this magnificent creature.

Concept note-3: -World Elephant Day was co-founded on August 12, 2012, by Canadian Patricia Sims and the Elephant Reintroduction Foundation of Thailand, an initiative of HM Queen Sirikit of Thailand.

Concept note-4: -World Elephant Day is celebrated to bring attention to the urgent plight of Asian and African Elephants. Act Now! “All tourist attractions that breed elephants for commercial purposes and promote direct contact with elephants, including rides, shows or tricks, should be avoided."

Concept note-5: -Every year theme is alloted to raise awareness about the peotection of elephants. World Elephant Day 2022 Theme is “Personifying prehistoric beauty, theological relevance, and environmental importance”.
