On Ganesh Chaturthi, 580-kg laddu known as ‘maha prasadam’ was prepared in

(A) Pune

(B) Bengaluru

(C) Mumbai

(D) ** Hyderabad

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The presented laddu, which weighed 62 kg in 2016, reached 1,100 kg in 2021.

Concept note-2: -Modaks, also known as sweet dumplings, are considered to be Ganpati’s favourite sweet dish and hence it is difficult to find a house celebrating the festival without modaks at their house. Devotees offer Lord Ganesha ‘bhog’ of modaks on the first day of the festival.

Concept note-3: -Hyderabad: A gigantic laddu, weighing around 1,500 kilogram, was offered to eco-friendly Khairatabad Ganesh by SRK Srikanth, a resident of Tolichowki and who operates an air-cooler manufacturing unit, on Wednesday.

Concept note-4: -Modaks are Lord Ganesha’s favourite foods so much so that he is also known as ‘Modapriya’. There are several stories that mention his love for the sweet dumplings. Made with rice flour, coconut, jaggery and steamed to perfection, devotees prepare several types of Modak and offer them as a prasad.
