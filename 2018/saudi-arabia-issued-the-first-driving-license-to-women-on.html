Saudi Arabia issued the first driving license to women on

(A) ** June 4

(B) June 24

(C) June 20

(D) June 9

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Saudi women will be allowed to start driving in the kingdom from June 24, the General Department of Traffic Director General Mohammed al-Bassami had then said.

Concept note-2: -Saudi Arabia was the last country in the world to repeal its ban on women drivers. In June 2018, women were allowed to drive again in the Kingdom following a 30-year prohibition.

Concept note-3: -W Series driver Abbi Pulling and Saudi Arabian racer Aseel Al Hamad have become the first women to drive a Formula 1 car in Saudi Arabia as part of a demonstration held by Alpine.

Concept note-4: -There are many driving schools for ladies and females in Saudi Arabia and you need to get admission to them through your Absher account. Muroor appointment with the purpose “ – (30) ”. Fee: around SR 2,500.

Concept note-5: -In 1900, Anne Rainsford French Bush was the first woman in America to earn her driver’s license.
