The Indian government has approved to set up two more underground crude oil storages. What are the two places?

(A) ** Chandikhol, Odisha and Padur, Karnataka

(B) Rajkot, Gujarat and Bikaner, Rajasthan

(C) Mangalore and Visakhapatnam

(D) Udupi, Karnataka and Chandikhol, Odisha

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The country’s strategic oil stockpile will boost India’s energy security. The Union Cabinet have given “In Principle approval for establishing 6.5MMT Strategic Petroleum Reserves at two locations Chandikhol (4 MMT) in Odisha and at Padur (2.5 MMT) Karnataka including dedicated SPM’s for the two SPR’s.

Concept note-2: -Strategic crude oil storages are at three underground locations in Mangalore, Visakhapatnam and Padur (Udupi, Karnataka). All these are located on the east and west coasts of India which are readily accessible to the refineries.

Concept note-3: -The 3Ps stand for proven, probable, and possible reserves. The oil industry breaks unproven reserves into two segments: those based on geological and engineering estimates from established sources (probable) and those that are less likely to be extracted due to financial or technical difficulties (possible).

Concept note-4: -Indian Strategic Petroleum Reserve Limited (ISPRL), a Government of India Special Purpose Vehicle, has established Strategic Petroleum Reserves (SPR) facilities with total capacity of 5.33 Million Metric Tonnes (MMT) at 3 locations, namely (i) Vishakhapatnam, (ii) Mangaluru and (iii) Padur.

Concept note-5: -Saudi Arabia is a desert country. It has the highest crude oil reserves. Saudi Arab is second largest crude oil reserve in the world and 1/5th of the total oil reserve in the world.
