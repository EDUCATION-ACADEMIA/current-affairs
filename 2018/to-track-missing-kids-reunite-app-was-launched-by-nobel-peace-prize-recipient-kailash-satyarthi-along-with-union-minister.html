To track missing kids, ’ReUnite’ app was launched by Nobel Peace Prize recipient Kailash Satyarthi, along with union minister

(A) ** Suresh Prabhu

(B) Sushma Swaraj

(C) Maneka Gandhi

(D) Arun Jaitley

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The app, named ReUnite which works on the technology of facial recognition software will help to report, search and identify abandoned children on the streets. The app was launched by child rights activist and Noble Peace Laureate Mr. Kailash Satyarthi and Union Minister for Commerce and Industry Mr. Suresh Prabhu.

Concept note-2: -In 2014 Yousafzai and Kailash Satyarthi were jointly awarded the Nobel Prize for Peace in recognition of their efforts on behalf of children’s rights.

Concept note-3: -As a grass-roots activist, Kailash and the grassroot movement founded by him, Bachpan Bachao Andolan (English: Save Childhood Movement), have liberated more than 83, 000 children from exploitation and developed a successful model for their education, rehabilitation and reintegration into the mainstream society.

Concept note-4: -Wich of these apps have we developed to assist in finding missing children? 1 positive parenting. 2 oneplanet. 3 reunite

Concept note-5: -Since 1980, Kailash Satyarthi has freed and rehabilitated more than one lakh children from the shackles of child labour.
