What is the capital of China?

(A) ** Beijing

(B) Japan

(C) Shingkai

(D) Shillong

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Beijing is the capital of the People’s Republic of China and one of the most populous cities in the world. The metropolis, located in north China, is governed as one of the four municipality directly under the central government. The city is located 39.56 degrees north latitude and 116.2 degrees east longitude.

Concept note-2: -Beijing is the capital of the People’s Republic of China and one of the most populous cities in the world. The metropolis, located in north China, is governed as one of the four municipality directly under the central government.

Concept note-3: -With but few interruptions, Beijing has been the capital of China for some eight centuries, and in number of years as the imperial capital it is exceeded only by Xi’an (Chang’an) in Shaanxi province and Luoyang in Henan province.

Concept note-4: -There are traditionally four major historical capitals of China referred to as the “Four Great Ancient Capitals of China” (simplified Chinese: ; traditional Chinese: ; pinyin: Zhōngguó Sì Dà Gǔ Dū). The four are Beijing, Nanjing, Luoyang and Xi’an (Chang’an).

Concept note-5: -Beijing, Wade-Giles romanization Pei-ching, conventional Peking, city, province-level shi (municipality), and capital of the People’s Republic of China.
