What is the causative agent of Tuberculosis?

(A) Staphylococcus

(B) Clostridium

(C) Vibriobacterium tuberculosis

(D) ** Mycobacterium tuberculosis

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tuberculosis (TB) is caused by a bacterium called Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Concept note-2: -Mycobacterium tuberculosis is a pathogenic bacterial species in the family Mycobacteriaceae and the causative agent of tuberculosis (TB).

Concept note-3: -Tuberculosis (TB) is caused by a type of bacterium called Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It’s spread when a person with active TB disease in their lungs coughs or sneezes and someone else inhales the expelled droplets, which contain TB bacteria.

Concept note-4: -The causative agent of TB is Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Acute tuberculous pneumonia (TP) is an acute form of pulmonary TB. However, acute TP and non-tuberculous community-acquired pneumonia can be easily confused, resulting in deterioration of TP due to delayed treatment.
