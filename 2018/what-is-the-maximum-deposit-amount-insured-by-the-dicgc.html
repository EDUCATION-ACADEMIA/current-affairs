What is the maximum deposit amount insured by the DICGC?

(A) Rs 2, 00, 000

(B) ** Rs 1, 00, 000

(C) Rs 3, 00, 000

(D) No limit

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Each depositor in a bank is insured upto a maximum of 5,00,000 (Rupees Five Lakhs) for both principal and interest amount held by him in the same right and same capacity as on the date of liquidation/cancellation of bank’s licence or the date on which the scheme of amalgamation/merger/reconstruction comes into force.

Concept note-2: -The DICGC insures principal and interest upto a maximum amount of five lakhs. For example, if an individual had an account with a principal amount of 4,95,000 plus accrued interest of 4,000, the total amount insured by the DICGC would be 4,99,000.

Concept note-3: -If a bank goes into liquidation, DICGC is liable to pay to the liquidator the claim amount of each depositor upto Rupees five lakhs within two months from the date of receipt of claim list from the liquidator.

Concept note-4: -Deposit insurance is one of the significant benefits of having an account at an FDIC-insured bank-it’s how the FDIC protects your money in the unlikely event of a bank failure. The standard insurance amount is $250,000 per depositor, per insured bank, for each account ownership category.

Concept note-5: -The PDIC provides a maximum deposit insurance coverage of PhP500,000 per depositor per bank. It covers all types of bank deposits in banks. For insurance purposes, all deposit accounts of a depositor in a closed bank maintained in the same right and capacity shall be added together.
