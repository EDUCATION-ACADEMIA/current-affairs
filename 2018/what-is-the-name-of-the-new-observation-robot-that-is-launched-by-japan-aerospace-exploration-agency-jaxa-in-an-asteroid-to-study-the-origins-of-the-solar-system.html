What is the name of the new observation robot that is launched by Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA) in an asteroid to study the origins of the solar system?

(A) ** MASCOT

(B) RYUGU

(C) HRP-4C

(D) KIROBO

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -JAXA stands for Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency.

Concept note-2: -Hayabusa2 (Japanese: 2, lit. ‘Peregrine falcon 2’) is an asteroid sample-return mission operated by the Japanese state space agency JAXA. It is a successor to the Hayabusa mission, which returned asteroid samples for the first time in June 2010.

Concept note-3: -Mission Highlights The Hayabusa spacecraft touches down on the surface of asteroid Itokawa, marking only the second time in history that a spacecraft has descended to the surface of an asteroid. The Hayabusa spacecraft departs asteroid Itokawa and begins its return trip to Earth 3 months later.
