Which country recently approved ’diverse’ as a third gender option on birth certificates, other official records?

(A) Canada

(B) India

(C) ** Germany

(D) New Zealand

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Intersex people in Germany can now legally identify themselves as such under a new law adopted in December. People who do not fit the biological definition of male or female can now choose the category “diverse” on official documents.

Concept note-2: -Germany’s third gender law introduced the gender “diverse” (German: divers) as a third option in alternative to “female” and “male” in the German civil status register.

Concept note-3: -On January 1, Germany will become the first country in the European Union to offer a “third gender” option on birth certificates. Intersex people – and parents of intersex babies – will be able to register as “divers, ” or miscellaneous, on birth certificates, instead of having to choose between male or female.

Concept note-4: -There are 3 noun genders in German: masculine, feminine, and neuter.
