Which festival Became Europe’s Capital of Culture recently?

(A) Classical Art

(B) Prehistoric Architecture

(C) Capitals of Culture

(D) ** Malta’s Valletta

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Novi Sad was awarded the European Capital of Culture in 2022 title, alongside the Luxembourg town of Esch-sur-Alzette and Lithuania’s second-largest city, Kaunas.

Concept note-2: -In 2023, there are 3 European Capitals of Culture: Elefsina (Greece) Veszprém (Hungary) Timisoara (Romania)

Concept note-3: -The decision came into force on 1 January 2021, and Novi Sad joined Kaunas (Lithuania) and Esch-sur-Alzette (Luxembourg), as the European Capital of Culture in 2022.

Concept note-4: -The European City of Culture programme was launched in the summer of 1985 with Athens being the first title-holder. In 1999, the European City of Culture program was renamed to European Capital of Culture.

Concept note-5: -The three European Capitals of Culture for 2023 are Elefsina in Greece, Timișoara in Romania and Veszprém in Hungary.
