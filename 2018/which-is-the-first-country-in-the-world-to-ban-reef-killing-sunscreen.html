Which is the first Country in the World To Ban Reef-Killing Sunscreen?

(A) ** Palau

(B) Angola

(C) China

(D) Finland

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Palau became the first country in the world to ban ‘Coral reef toxic’ sunscreens. Hence the statement 1 is correct. It came into effect on January 1, 2020, and is part of the new Responsible Tourism Education Act of Palau.

Concept note-2: -Palau Sunscreen Ban Palau is the first country that banned sunscreen nationwide.

Concept note-3: -The Pacific nation of Palau has become the first country to ban sun cream that is harmful to corals and sea life. From Wednesday, sun cream that includes common ingredients, including oxybenzone, is not allowed to be worn or sold in the country.

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution. Thailand has banned sunscreens containing chemicals that damage coral from all of its marine national parks. Lotions tourists use for sun protection are harming slow-growing corals.

Concept note-5: -Palau sunscreen ban: Pacific island becomes first nation to prohibit varieties considered toxic. The tiny Pacific island nation of Palau has become the first country in the world to ban sunscreen considered toxic to coral reefs.
