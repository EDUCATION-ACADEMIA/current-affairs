Which ministry launches Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP)?

(A) ** Ministry of Health Affairs

(B) Ministry of Defence

(C) Ministry of External affairs

(D) Ministry of Finance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Integrated Disease Surveillance Project (IDSP) was launched by Hon’ble Union Minister of Health & Family Welfare in November 2004 for a period upto March 2010. The project was restructured and extended up to March 2012.

Concept note-2: -Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme (IDSP) was launched with World Bank assistance in November 2004 to detect and respond to disease outbreaks quickly.

Concept note-3: -4.2 Definition of Presumptive Surveillance: Under IDSP, the Medical Officers at PHCs, CHCs, district hospitals, private hospitals at the district or sub-district level and private medical practitioners will conduct syndromic surveillance.

Concept note-4: -Integrated Disease Surveillance and Response (IDSR) is a strategy adopted by countries in the WHO African Region for implementing comprehensive public health surveillance and response systems for priority diseases, conditions and events at all levels of health systems.

Concept note-5: -The Integrated Health Information Platform (IHIP) was designed and developed for the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India (MoHFW) under the leadership of Smt. Preeti Sudan, Secretary of Health and Family Welfare and Mr Lav Agarwal, Joint Secretary for Public Health and International Health.
