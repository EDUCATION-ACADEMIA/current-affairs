Which of the following Airways bans smart luggage on flights from January 15?

(A) GoAir

(B) Air India

(C) IndiGo

(D) ** Jet Airways

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Jet Airways disallows smart luggage on flights from January 15.

Concept note-2: -The Smart Bag Ban The new policy primarily targets the lithium batteries that power most popular smart bags. It points towards numerous fires and explosions that have happened involving other lithium battery-powered devices.

Concept note-3: -Most airlines, including easyJet, allow you to disconnect your smart luggage, check it in and then carry the removable battery pack onboard or alternatively take disconnected smart luggage into the cabin.

Concept note-4: -Smart luggage refers to suitcases with tech capabilities - typically USB ports to connect portable chargers for easy access. Some styles include their own portable chargers, while others require you to provide your own. Either way, you’ll never have a dead phone while traveling.

Concept note-5: -Suitcases with chargers are allowed on planes as long as the battery is removable. For example, if your luggage comes with an external battery bank, you’ll want to eject it before turning the baggage in.
