Which of the following countries is planning for a trilateral air exercise?

(A) ** India, Japan, the U.S

(B) India, the U.S, Russia

(C) China, Russia, Afghanistan

(D) Isreal, the U.S., Syria

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The exercise ‘Veer Guardian-2023’ involving the Indian Air Force (IAF) and Japan Air Self Defence Force (JASDF) will be held at Japan’s Hyakuri air base. The IAF said on Saturday that its deployment at the exercise will include four Su-30 MKI jets, two C-17 aircraft and one IL-78 plane.

Concept note-2: -What is the Garuda exercise? Ans. Garuda is a joint exercise between India and France Air Force that will provide a platform for both countries to enhance operational capability and interoperability, while also sharing best practices.

Concept note-3: -The militaries of the Quad-Australia, India, Japan, and the United States-conducted the joint Malabar exercise off the coast of Japan in November.

Concept note-4: -Malabar Exercise started in 1992 as a bilateral naval exercise between the navies of India and the US. Japan joined the Malabar exercise in 2015, making it a trilateral exercise.
