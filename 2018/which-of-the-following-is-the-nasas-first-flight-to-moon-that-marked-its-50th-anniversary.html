Which of the following is the NASA’s first flight to moon that marked its 50th anniversary?

(A) Apollo 6

(B) Apollo 7A

(C) ** Apollo 8

(D) Apollo 11

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NASA’s 1st Flight to Moon, Apollo 8, Marks 50th Anniversary.

Concept note-2: -Apollo 50th Anniversary Event Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex continues to celebrate the Apollo Program’s 50th anniversaries on December 7th, the anniversary of the launch of Apollo 17.

Concept note-3: -Apollo 11, the first space mission to put people on the Moon, was launched on July 16, 1969. Almost every major aspect of the flight of Apollo 11 was witnessed via television by hundreds of millions of people in nearly every part of the globe, until splashdown in the Pacific Ocean on July 24.

Concept note-4: -On July 20, 1969, Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first men to walk on the Moon.

Concept note-5: -For 60 years, NASA Johnson Space Center has been the epicenter for human spaceflight. On May 25, 1961 President John F. Kennedy committed the nation to achieve the goal of “landing a man on the moon and returning him safely to the earth” before the end of the decade.
