Which of the following man-portable air defence systems does India plan to buy from Russia?

(A) ** Igla-S missile system

(B) A-135 anti-ballistic missile system

(C) S-400 missile system

(D) 50R6 Vityaz missile system

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Long-Sought Requirement. India has been looking to procure a VSHORAD for over a decade to replace its Russian Igla-M air defense system inducted in the 1980s.

Concept note-2: -Meant to kill low altitude aerial threats at short ranges, VSHORADS is a man portable Air Defence System (MANPAD) designed and developed indigenously by DRDO’s Research Centre Imarat (RCI), Hyderabad, in collaboration with other DRDO laboratories and Indian Industry Partners.

Concept note-3: -Russia has deployed Pantsir-S1 air defense systems atop two government buildings in Moscow, including the Ministry of Defense on Frunzenskaya Embankment, and a district education ministry building on Teterinsky Lane, according to independent Russian-language media.

Concept note-4: -Also called the Medium Range Surface-to-Air Missile System (MR-SAM) meant for the Indian Air Force, the Barak-8 was originally co-developed with Israel for the Indian Navy under the designation Long Range Surface-to-Air Missile System (LR-SAM).

Concept note-5: -The Akash missile system can target aircraft up to 50 km (31 mi) away. It has the capability to neutralise aerial targets like fighter jets, cruise missiles and air-to-surface missiles as well as ballistic missiles. It is in operational service with the Indian Army and the Indian Air Force. Akash.
