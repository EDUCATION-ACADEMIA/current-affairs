Which of the following state has now become the third state in India to ban hookah bars through law after Gujarat and Maharashtra?

(A) Uttar Pradesh

(B) West Bengal

(C) Assam

(D) ** Punjab

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which other states have banned hookah? Gujarat, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Chhattisgarh, and Punjab have all banned hookah bars. The West Bengal government, citing public health, banned the practice in Kolkata on 2 December.

Concept note-2: -The Gujarat Cabinet on Thursday cleared an ordinance which seeks to impose a total ban on hookah bars across the state, Gujarat CM Vijay Rupani said. Once the law comes into effect, those found violating it could face prison up to 3 years and a fine up to ₹50, 000, Gujarat MoS for Home Pradeepsinh Jadeja said.

Concept note-3: -UPSC Mains. Q. Which of the following state governments has legally banned hookah parlours after Gujarat and Maharashtra? Notes: After Maharashtra and Gujarat, Punjab has become the India’s third state to legally ban hookah bars or lounges.

Concept note-4: -The Telangana High Court permitted hookah parlours to run in the state and said there is no need to obtain a separate license to run them. However, in a restaurant with over 30 seats, the owners can allow people to smoke in a separate corner. This should be informed so the activities can be checked, the court said.
