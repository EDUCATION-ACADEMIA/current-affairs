Which state Govt Decides To Invest Rs 107 Cr To Check Riverbank Erosion?

(A) ** West Bengal

(B) Bihar

(C) Tamil Nadu

(D) Kerala

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The course of the Ganges along the Northern river-front of West Bengal has been fast changing due to unabated bank erosion, especially over the last few decades.

Concept note-2: -But at the time of lowering water level, cohesive banks are most susceptible to erosion because of its low water permeability.

Concept note-3: -Riverbank erosion is one of the major and unpredictable problems worldwide [1] which occurs both naturally and through human impact. The erosion involves the wearing away of soil found along the river bed and banks that will lead to the accumulation of sediment which in turn will increase the river pollution problem.

Concept note-4: -River-bank erosion creates land loss at a large scale. Several hectares of land, including homesteads, fertile lands, and mango gardens/orchards, are washed away by the river due to river-bank erosion. In the gram panchayats such as Gopalpur, Dharampur, Hiranandapur, some people have lost more than half of their land.
