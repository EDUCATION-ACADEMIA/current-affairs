Who acquires GIF Search Platform Tenor recently?

(A) ** Google

(B) Flipkart

(C) Amazon

(D) Snapdeal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Google has acquired GIF platform Tenor, adding a library of animated images to its vast internet search engine.

Concept note-2: -Tenor is an online GIF search engine and database owned by Google. Its main product is the GIF Keyboard, which is available on Android, iOS, and macOS.

Concept note-3: -Terms of the deal were not disclosed by the companies. The deal was estimated to be worth “just north of $100 million plus employee retention packages, although another source says that’s a bit too low, ” Axios reported.

Concept note-4: -The Google acquisition will help Tenor roll out more improvements for users, API partners, and advertisers, Tenor CEO David McIntosh said in a blog post(Opens in a new window).

Concept note-5: -Three years ago, when Apple started supporting third-party keyboards, McIntosh and Hachenburg debuted Tenor’s iOS GIF keyboard (Tenor changed its name from Riffsy a year ago in a push to improve branding). It was downloaded more than a million times in its first week.
