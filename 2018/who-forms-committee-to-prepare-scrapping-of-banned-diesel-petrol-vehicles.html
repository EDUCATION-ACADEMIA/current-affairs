Who forms committee to prepare scrapping of banned diesel, petrol vehicles?

(A) ** National Green Tribunal (NGT)

(B) Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited (BSNL)

(C) Reliance Communications

(D) National Thermal Power Corporation Limited

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Delhi bans BS-III petrol, BS-IV diesel vehicles from Tuesday to combat air pollution. NEW DELHI: The Delhi government on Monday announced that it would no longer allow vehicles with BS-III petrol and BS-IV diesel engines to be driven in the city starting Tuesday in view of the worsening air quality.

Concept note-2: -10-year diesels permanently banned The rule of the National Green Tribunal states that the use of 10-year old diesel-powered and 15-year old petrol-powered cars is not allowed in New Delhi.

Concept note-3: -The Delhi government order said any BS 3 petrol and BS 4 diesel cars found plying till Thursday will be ‘prosecuted under 194 (1) of Motor Vehicles Act which provides a fine of ₹20, 000’.

Concept note-4: -Delhi’s air quality worsened to the severe category on Monday owning. New Delhi: The Delhi government has decided to impose a temporary ban on using BS-III petrol and BS-IV diesel four-wheelers in the national capital from Tuesday in view of the worsening air quality.
