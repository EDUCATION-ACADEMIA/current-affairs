Who is appointed as CIA’s First Female Director recently?

(A) ** Gina Haspel

(B) Dana Priest

(C) Valerie Plame

(D) Diane Western

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -She was the agency’s deputy director from 2017 to 2018 to Mike Pompeo, and became acting director on April 26, 2018, after Pompeo became U.S. secretary of state. She was later nominated and confirmed to the role, making her first woman to become CIA director on a permanent basis.

Concept note-2: -Bill Burns was officially sworn in as Director of the Central Intelligence Agency on March 19, 2021, making him the first career diplomat to serve as Director.

Concept note-3: -Hillenkoetter, 1947–1950. Rear Admiral Roscoe H. Hillenkoetter was the third Director of Central Intelligence, but the first who served as the Director of the Central Intelligence Agency.

Concept note-4: -In January 2021, President Joe Biden nominated Burns to become the director of the Central Intelligence Agency. He was unanimously confirmed by voice vote on March 18, 2021, sworn in officially as director on March 19, 2021, and ceremonially sworn in by Vice President Kamala Harris on March 23, 2021.

Concept note-5: -The CIA is headed by a director and deputy director, only one of whom may be a military officer. The director of central intelligence (DCI) serves as the chief intelligence adviser to the president of the United States.
