Who is liable to pay tax excise duty?

(A) Consumer

(B) Retailer

(C) ** Manufacturer

(D) Manufacturer and Retailer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -6. Who is liable to pay Excise Duty? Ans: The manufacturer or the producer of goods is liable to pay Excise Duty. 7.

Concept note-2: -Today, excise duty applies only on petroleum and liquor. Excise duty was levied on manufactured goods and levied at the time of removal of goods, while GST is levied on the supply of goods and services.

Concept note-3: -Excise duty is a form of indirect tax that is levied by the Central Government of India for the production, sale, or license of certain goods. Excise duty charges are also collected by state governments for alcohol and narcotics.

Concept note-4: -Yes, excise duty has been subsumed by the Goods and Service Tax (GST) along with other indirect taxes. However, excise duty is still levied on certain items like petroleum and liquor.
