Who Launches Google Pay To Take On Apple Pay?

(A) ** Google

(B) Samsung

(C) Verizon

(D) Huawei

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Google launched its latest answer to Apple Pay on Tuesday. It’s called Google Pay and replaces Android Pay, a previous solution that let Android users buy goods with their smartphones. It’s also Google’s answer to Apple Pay Cash.

Concept note-2: -Apple Pay came out in late 20141, and Google Pay (formerly Google Wallet™ and Android Pay™) launched in 2018. Although they are rival payment options, Apple Pay and Google Pay share a lot in common: Both allow users to upload credit card details into their mobile devices.

Concept note-3: -The service was announced at Apple’s iPhone 6 event on September 9, 2014.

Concept note-4: -What to Know. Google Pay on iPhone requires the app from Google and a working Google account. Add debit, credit, and bank accounts from inside the Google Pay app. Google Pay on iPhone can’t use tap-to-pay, but you can send and receive money and pay for online purchases.

Concept note-5: -Google Basically Pays Apple to Stay Out of the Search Engine Business, Class Action Lawsuit Alleges. Apple has an agreement with Google that it won’t develop its own internet search engine so long as Google pays it to remain the default option in Safari, a new class action alleges.
