Who Launches India’s 4th Data Centre In Bhubaneswar recently?

(A) ** NIC

(B) BSNL

(C) NHAI

(D) VIT

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -About: Yotta D1, built at a cost of Rs 5, 000 crore, is the country’s biggest and UP’s first data centre. It is spread over an area of 3 lakh square feet at the upcoming Data Centre Park in Greater Noida, Uttar Pradesh.

Concept note-2: -“The Indian data centre industry is expected to add 681 MW capacity by the end of 2024 leading to a doubling of capacity to 1, 318 MW which will need 7.8 million sq ft of real estate space. Mumbai is expected to account for 57 percent of the new supply followed by Chennai at 25 percent.

Concept note-3: -India has several important intercontinental submarine cables connecting it to Asia, Africa and Europe. There are 145 Indian data centers, the majority of these colocation facilities are located in and around Mumbai.

Concept note-4: -The first Data Centre was launched in Hyderabad in 2008, followed by NDC Pune in 2010, NDC Delhi in 2011 and NDC Bhubaneshwar in 2018. National Data Centre (NDC) at Bhubaneswar is a Cloud-enabled Data Centre which has been offering cloud services to Government Departments since its inception.
