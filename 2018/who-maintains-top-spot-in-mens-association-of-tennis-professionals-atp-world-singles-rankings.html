Who maintains Top Spot in men’s Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP) world singles rankings?

(A) Novak Djokovic

(B) ** Rafael Nadal

(C) Roger Federer

(D) Grigor Dimitrov

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Novak Djokovic, the current men’s singles world No. 1.

Concept note-2: -ATP/WTA Rankings lists (in singles and doubles) are generally released each Monday, except rankings are not produced in the middle week of the four Grand Slams or the ATP and WTA events in Miami and Indian Wells.

Concept note-3: -Tennis rankings work based on a points system that adds up players’ 18 best results in the previous 52 weeks. In order to earn ranking points, tennis players must participate in events hosted by the International Tennis Federation (ITF), the ATP, and the WTA.

Concept note-4: -The rankings are updated every Monday, and points are dropped 52 weeks after being awarded (with the exception of the ATP Finals, from which points are dropped on the Monday following the last ATP Tour event of the following year). Novak Djokovic is the current world No. 1.
