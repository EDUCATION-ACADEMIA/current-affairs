Who won the Wildlife Photographer of the Year award for Under Category by UK’s Natural History Museum?

(A) Dabboo Ratnani

(B) Atil Kasbekar

(C) ** Arshdeep Singh

(D) Lala Deen Dayal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An invited guest shares their insight on an image from Wildlife Photographer of the Year. The good life taken by Daniël Nelson.

Concept note-2: -American photographer Karine Aigner has won the Grand Title in this year’s Wildlife Photographer of the Year Competition with a stunning image of a ball of cactus bees vying for a mate.

Concept note-3: -Katanyou Wuttichaitanakorn won the Young Grand Title Winner 2022. The WPY is one of the most prestigious competitions of its type in world photography. It is organised by London’s Natural History Museum. This year, the competition attracted 38, 575 entries from 93 countries.

Concept note-4: -Sascha Fonseca’s striking image World of the Snow Leopard has won the Wildlife Photographer of the Year 58 People’s Choice Award. The photo was taken during a three-year, bait-free, camera-trap project in the Indian Himalayas.
