World Mosquito Day is observed annually on

(A) 22 August

(B) 19 August

(C) 21 August

(D) ** 20 August

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Mosquito Day, observed annually on 20 August, is a commemoration of British doctor Sir Ronald Ross’s discovery in 1897 that female anopheline mosquitoes transmit malaria between humans.

Concept note-2: -World Mosquito Day is observed on August 20 every year. The Theme of World Mosquito Day 2022 is ‘Harness innovation to reduce the malaria disease burden and save lives.

Concept note-3: -World Mosquito Day is recognised in honour of Sir Ronald Ross, who in 1897 identified the connection between mosquitoes and the spread of malaria. His research revealed that female mosquitoes spread malaria among people.

Concept note-4: -World Mosquito Day is celebrated every year on August 20 to commemorate the discovery of Ronald Ross in 1897 which established that the Anopheles mosquitoes transmit the malaria parasite to humans.
