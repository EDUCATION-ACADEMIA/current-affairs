World’s most experienced space woman who recently retired as astronaut:-

(A) Kathleen Rubins

(B) ** Peggy Whitson

(C) Sandra Magnus

(D) Stephanie Wilson

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NASA astronaut Anne McClain became the first woman to live aboard the space station as part of two different crews with other women: Serena Auñón-Chancellor in 2018 and Christina Koch in 2019. NASA astronauts Jessica Meir and Christina Koch completed the first all-female spacewalk in Oct. 2019.

Concept note-2: -Peggy Whitson, America’s most experienced astronaut, and veteran GT3-class race car driver John Shoffner, a long-time pilot, will anchor a four-person crew launching to the International Space Station aboard Axiom Space’s second commercial trip to orbit, the company announced Tuesday.

Concept note-3: -The first class of NASA astronauts to include females was selected in 1978. While Sally Ride eventually became the first American woman to fly to space, her classmates including Shannon Lucid, Judith Resnik, and Anna Lee Fisher (who became the first mother in space) also went on to make their mark.
