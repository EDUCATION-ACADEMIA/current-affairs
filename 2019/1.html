Which government has approved over $9 million for relief and rehabilitation work in cyclone-hit Odisha.

(A) Sydney

(B) ** Hong Kong

(C) Thailand

(D) Seoul

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Beijing: The Hong Kong government has approved over USD 900,000 for relief and rehabilitation work in cyclone-hit Odisha that could benefit around 45,100 victims, China’s official media reported on Friday.

Concept note-2: -A total of 200 concrete homes were constructed, as well as two concrete schools and two village tube-wells. The project was finally completed in May 2002, two and a half years after the cyclone hit.

Concept note-3: -The developing cyclonic storm in the Bay of Bengal - Cyclone Sitrang - will bypass Odisha and make landfall near West Bengal-Bangladesh coasts on October 25, 2022, said the India Meteorological Department (IMD).

Concept note-4: -Other designations that have been used earlier included Ogni, Helen and Fani from Bangladesh; and Laila, Nargis and Bulbul from Pakistan.

Concept note-5: -Thousands of livestock were killed, paddy and other crop fields were submerged and hundred thousands of people were affected by the disaster. Life in the coastal Orissa was brought to a standstill.
