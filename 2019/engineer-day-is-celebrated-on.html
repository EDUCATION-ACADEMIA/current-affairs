Engineer day is celebrated on

(A) 12th September

(B) 14th September

(C) 13th September

(D) ** 15th September

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -National Engineer’s Day is observed every year on September 15 on the birth anniversary of the legendary engineer Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya . The day is observed every year to pay tribute to Visvesvaraya and also to commemorate the contributions of the engineers to the nation.

Concept note-2: -In India, National Engineers Day is celebrated on 15th September, on the birth anniversary of the famous engineer Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya. Engineers Day was first celebrated in 1968 in India to acknowledge and appreciate the efforts of countless engineers in the country.

Concept note-3: -Engineers Day, which is celebrated on 15 September every year in India, is also celebrated in Sri Lanka and Tanzania.In these countries also, Engineer’s Day is celebrated in the memory of Sir Dr. Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya.

Concept note-4: -Every year on the 15th of September, India celebrates National Engineer’s Day to recognise and honour the achievements of the great engineer Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya. The day is observed to commemorate the great work of engineers and to encourage them to improve and innovate.

Concept note-5: -India observes Engineer’s Day on September 15 as a mark of remembrance and tribute to Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya, who is considered as one of the greatest engineers in the country.
