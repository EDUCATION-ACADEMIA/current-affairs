[SOURCES]

For which state, the world bank funded $57 million for the pro-poor tourism (PPT) development projects?

(A) Meghalaya

(B) ** Uttar Pradesh

(C) Kerala

(D) Gujarat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Bank to fund pro-poor tourism development projects in India. In the top tourist hotspots of Agra and Braj-Mathura regions in Uttar Pradesh World Bank will be funding ‘pro-poor toursim’ (PPT) development projects investing more than $57 million.

Concept note-2: -World Bank aided Uttar Pradesh Pro-Poor Tourism Development Project is an innovative project. In this project under two major destinations i.e., Agra and Braj region of state, project for poverty eradication and employment creation will be operated through Tourism Development related activities.

Concept note-3: -Pro-poor tourism (PPT) is defined as tourism that generates net benefits for the poor. Benefits may be economic, but they may also be social, environmental or cultural. Pro-poor tourism is not a specific product or sector of tourism, but an approach to the industry.

Concept note-4: -The loan agreement was signed by Sameer Kumar Khare, Additional Secretary, Department of Economic Affairs, on behalf of the Government of India; Girijesh Kumar Tyagi, Special Secretary, PWD, on behalf of the Government of Uttar Pradesh; and Junaid Ahmad, Country Director, India on behalf of the World Bank.
