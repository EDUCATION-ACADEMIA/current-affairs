How many cricketer’s have been inducted in the International Cricket Council’s Hall of Fame?

(A) 43

(B) 66

(C) 72

(D) ** 89

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Out of 109 inductees in the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame, 82 are from England , Australia and the West Indies, while the other 27 inductees are from the remaining Test playing nations, India, New Zealand, Pakistan , South Africa , Sri Lanka and Zimbabwe. There are eleven women in the Hall of Fame.

Concept note-2: -Three greats of the game have become the latest people to be inducted into the ICC Cricket Hall of Fame. The ICC announced a trio of additions to the ICC Hall of Fame on Tuesday, with Pakistan legend Abdul Qadir, England trailblazer Charlotte Edwards and West Indies great Shivnarine Chanderpaul the latest inductees.

Concept note-3: -Bishan Singh Bedi (2009), Kapil Dev (2009), Sunil Gavaskar (2009), Anil Kumble (2015), Rahul Dravid (2018) have been inducted in the ICC Hall of Fame from India. To be inducted in the ICC Hall of Fame, it is important to have retired from international cricket for 5 years.

Concept note-4: -Charlotte Edwards, Shivnarine Chanderpaul and Abdul Qadir – the three new ICC Hall of Fame inductees were felicitated ahead of the first ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2022 semi-final between Pakistan and New Zealand.

Concept note-5: -Rahul Dravid is an Indian former International cricketer and a batsman.
