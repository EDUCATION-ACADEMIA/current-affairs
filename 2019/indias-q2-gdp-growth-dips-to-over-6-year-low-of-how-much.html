India’s Q2 GDP growth dips to over 6-year low of how much?

(A) ** 4.5%

(B) 4%

(C) 4.8%

(D) 4.2%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The government released the GDP for the second quarter (July-September) of the current financial year.

Concept note-2: -Nominal GDP or GDP at Current Prices in Q2 2022-23 is estimated at ₹65.31 lakh crore, as against ₹56.20 lakh crore in Q2 2021-22, showing a growth of 16.2 percent as compared to 19.0 percent in Q2 2021-22. 5.

Concept note-3: -India GDP Q2 Growth "Real GDP or GDP at Constant (2011-12) Prices in Q2 2022-23 is estimated at Rs 38.17 lakh crore, as against Rs 35.89 lakh crore in Q2 2021-22, showing a growth of 6.3 percent as compared to 8.4 percent in Q2 2021-22,” stated govt’s press release on GPD numbers.

Concept note-4: -Current‑dollar GDP increased 8.4 percent at an annual rate, or $496.2 billion, in the second quarter to a level of $24.88 trillion (tables 1 and 3), an upward revision of $31.1 billion from the previous estimate.
