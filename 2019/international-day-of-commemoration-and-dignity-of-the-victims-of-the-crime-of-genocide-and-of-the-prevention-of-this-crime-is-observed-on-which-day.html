International Day of Commemoration and Dignity of the Victims of the Crime of Genocide and of the Prevention of this Crime is observed on which day?

(A) 4th December

(B) 5th December

(C) ** 9th December

(D) 15th December

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Day of Commemoration and Dignity of the Victims of the Crime of Genocide and of the Prevention of this Crime – December 9, 2023. International Day of Commemoration and Dignity of the Victims of the Crime of Genocide and of the Prevention of this Crime takes place on December 9 of every year.

Concept note-2: -The Genocide Convention was the first human rights treaty adopted by the General Assembly of the United Nations on 9 December 1948 and signified the international community’s commitment to ‘never again’ after the atrocities committed during the Second World War.

Concept note-3: -The Contracting Parties undertake to enact, in accordance with their respective Constitutions, the necessary legislation to give effect to the provisions of the present Convention, and, in particular, to provide effective penalties for persons guilty of genocide or any of the other acts enumerated in article III.

Concept note-4: -The Assembly also designated 9 December as International Anti-Corruption Day, to raise awareness of corruption and of the role of the Convention in combating and preventing it.
