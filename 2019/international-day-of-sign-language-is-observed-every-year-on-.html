[SOURCES]

International Day of Sign language is observed every year on ____

(A) 22nd September

(B) ** 23rd September

(C) 24th September

(D) 25th September

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On 19 December 2017, the UN General Assembly declared 23 September as International Day of Sign Languages (IDSL).

Concept note-2: -September 23 was chosen as it marked the anniversary of the World Federation of the Deaf’s formation in 1951. The day was first commemorated in 2018 as part of the International Week of the Deaf. There are approximately 70 million deaf people in the world, according to data from the World Federation of the Deaf.

Concept note-3: -The UN General Assembly has proclaimed September 23 as the International Day of Sign Languages to help raise awareness about sign language and the rights of deaf people.

Concept note-4: -It’s National Hunting and Fishing Day, Za’atar Day, Education Technology Day, International Day of Sign Languages, International Rabbit Day… and much more!

Concept note-5: -National ASL Day takes place on April 15 every year to celebrate American Sign Language.
