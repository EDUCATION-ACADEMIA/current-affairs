International Mountain Day is observed on which of the following days?

(A) 16th December

(B) 4th December

(C) ** 11th December

(D) 8th December

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Mountain Day is celebrated annually on December 11 to increase public awareness of the value of mountains to both life and climate. The United Nations General Assembly (UNGA) established International Mountain Day to recognise the importance of mountains.

Concept note-2: -International Mountain Day 2022: Theme Women play a key role in environmental protection and social and economic development in mountain areas. So, the theme for International Mountain Day 2022 is ‘ Women Move Mountains ‘.

Concept note-3: -International Mountain Day is celebrated on 11 December every year to educate children and people about the role that mountains play in providing fresh water, clean energy, food, and recreation. The theme of 2021 is “Sustainable mountain tourism". It is observed on 11 December by the United Nations.

Concept note-4: -The UN General Assembly declared 2002 the UN International Year of Mountains, and on this occasion, it designated 11 December, from 2003 onwards, as International Mountain Day.

Concept note-5: -Ain’t no mountain high enough to keep people from celebrating Mountain Day in Japan. The day is a natural fit because of Japan’s hilly and mountainous terrain. This fairly new holiday which takes place annually on August 11, reminds us to see mountains as natural sanctuaries of peace.
