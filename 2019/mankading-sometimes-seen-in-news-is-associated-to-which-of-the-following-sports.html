Mankading, sometimes seen in news, is associated to which of the following sports?

(A) Basketball

(B) ** Cricket

(C) Football

(D) Hockey

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -But as Harsh Mankad pointed out, there is some element of pride and prestige in being part of a popularly used cricketing term.

Concept note-2: -To put it in simple terms, Mankading refers to the act of dismissing a non striker batter before a ball is bowled by running him out. This dismissal comes into the picture when the non-striker is taking an illegitimate start before the ball is bowled.

Concept note-3: -When the name Vinoo Mankad comes to mind, we remember the famous mode of dismissal, ‘mankading’. It is a controversial way of getting the batter out and for over 70 years has been the source of debate. But let’s not forget, Vinoo Mankad was one of our celebrated all-rounders in the 1950s.

Concept note-4: -’Mankading’ no more ‘against spirit of the game’ as MCC introduces new laws in cricket. The new laws announced by the MCC will come into effect from 1 October 2022. Cricket is an ever-evolving game.

Concept note-5: -The Australian Press invented the word ‘Mankading.” This term is related to Cricket. This term was named after Indian bowler Vinoo Mankad. He had dismissed Bill Brown, not once but twice by removing the bails when he was outside the crease during India’s tour of Australia in 1947.
