Motihari-Amlekhgunj petroleum pipeline project is connected to India & which country?

(A) Pakistan

(B) Myanmar

(C) ** Nepal

(D) Bangladesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Motihari -Amlekhgunj pipeline is a 69 km (43 mi) long trans-border petrolium pipeline between Amlekhgunj Oil Depot in Parsa of Nepal and Motihari of India. This pipeline transports petrolium from Indian Oil Corporation (IOC) to Nepal Oil Corporation (NOC).

Concept note-2: -Ans : The Motihari-Amlekhgunj Pipeline was proposed in the year 1996, but due to several delays and the different problems faced by the pipeline transportation the project got started in the year 2018 and was completed by the year 2019.

Concept note-3: -Kandla–Bhatinda Oil Pipeline (KBPL) was started in 1993 and it was commissioned in 1996. It is the largest oil pipeline in India.

Concept note-4: -1. Hajira-Bijapur-Jagdishpur (HBJ) Gas Pipeline is the world’s largest and the longest underground pipeline. 2. The Salaya-Mathura Pipeline (SMPL) and Paradip-Haldia-Barauni Pipeline are the crude oil pipelines in India.

Concept note-5: -1. Naharkatia-Nunmati-Barauni Pipeline: This was the first pipeline constructed in India to bring crude oil from Naharkatia oilfield to Nunmati.
