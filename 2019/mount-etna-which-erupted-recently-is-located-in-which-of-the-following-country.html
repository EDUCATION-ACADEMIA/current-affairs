Mount Etna which erupted recently is located in which of the following country?

(A) Japan

(B) Spain

(C) ** Italy

(D) South America

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is the highest peak in Italy south of the Alps. Etna covers an area of 1,190 km2 with a basal circumference of 140 km. This makes it by far the largest of the three active volcanoes in Italy, being about two and a half times the height of the next largest, Mount Vesuvius.

Concept note-2: -Etna is located on the island of Sicily, Italy, and has had documented records of eruptions dating back to 1500 BCE.

Concept note-3: -On 29 May 2022 a sudden collapse of the Southeast Crater created a fracture on its northern flank at an elevation of circa 2,800 m (9,186 ft). A small lava flow emerged and headed in the direction of the Leone Valley, just above the much larger Bove Valley.

Concept note-4: -Dust briefly clouded the skies over Sicily in early December 2022, yet the Italian island’s iconic volcano still managed to put on a show.

Concept note-5: -The final major eruptions of the 20th century occurred in 1986 and in 1999. In the early 21st century a major eruption began in July 2001 and lasted several weeks. Other significant early 21st-century volcanic activity included the Strombolian eruptions of 2002–03, 2007, 2015, 2017, 2019, and 2020.
