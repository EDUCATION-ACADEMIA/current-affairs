Name the Helicopter launched anti-tank missile, that is test fired in Balasore, Odisha.

(A) Pralay

(B) ** Helina

(C) Shaurya

(D) Prahaar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. Solution: The correct answer is option 3 i.e. 3rd. Dhruvastra is a third-generation fire and forget class anti-tank guided missile that has been launched from a helicopter.

Concept note-2: -Further, telemetry systems recorded the satisfactory flight performance of the missiles. #DRDOUpdates | Indigenously developed laser-guided ATGMs successfully test fired today.

Concept note-3: -APPLICATION: HELINA is an abbreviation for Helicopter launched NAG and is an Air to Surface Missile system. HELINA is launched from Advanced Light Helicopter (ALH). The ALH is equipped with 2 Twin launchers, one on either side, carrying a total of 8 missiles.

Concept note-4: -The Nag missile (IAST: Nāga; en: Cobra), also called “Prospina” for the land-attack version, is an Indian third-generation, all-weather, fire-and-forget, lock-on after launch, anti-tank guided missile (ATGM) with an operational range of 500 m to 20 km depending on variant.
