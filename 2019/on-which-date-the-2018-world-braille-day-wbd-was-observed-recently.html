On which date, the 2018 World Braille Day (WBD) was observed recently?

(A) January 3

(B) ** January 4

(C) January 2

(D) January 5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2018, the UN General Assembly decided to make it an official observance by proclaiming January 4th as World Braille Day. January 4th commemorates the birthdate of Louis Braille.

Concept note-2: -World Braille Day is annually celebrated on January 4, the birthday of Braille inventor, Louis Braille. The day recognizes the contributions of Louis Braille in helping blind and visually impaired people to read and write. Braille - a code used by the visually impaired worldwide.

Concept note-3: -The UN General Assembly proclaimed January 4 to be World Braille Day in November 2018.

Concept note-4: -World Braille Day, celebrated since 2019, is observed to raise awareness of the importance of Braille as a means of communication in the full realization of the human rights for blind and partially sighted people.

Concept note-5: -We celebrate World Braille Day every year on January 4th because it’s Louis Braille’s birthday. He’s the inventor of braille! Louis was born in 1809 in France and became blind after a childhood accident.
