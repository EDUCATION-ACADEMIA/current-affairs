PM Modi unveils world’s largest Bhagavad Gita at ISKCON temple in south Delhi. It was weighted upto;

(A) ** 800 Kg

(B) 780 Kg

(C) 630 Kg

(D) 500 Kg

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A giant Bhagavad Gita, running into 670 pages and weighing about 800 kgs, was unveiled Tuesday at Delhi’s ISKCON temple by Prime Minister Narendra Modi.

Concept note-2: -As per ISCKON, the holy book measuring 2.8 meters by 2 meters has 670 pages and weights at 800 kg. The Prime Minister inaugurated the world’s largest and heaviest Bhagavad Gita at the Delhi ISKCON temple. As per ISCKON, the holy book measuring 2.8 meters by 2 meters has 670 pages and weights at 800 kg.

Concept note-3: -Gita at Delhi’s ISKCON TEMPLE It is the holy Bhagavad Gita. It contains over 2.8 meters and over 800 kg. The ‘Astounding Bhagavad Gita’ is one of the descriptions in the world. It has the verses of Bhagavad Gita forward with an explanation.

Concept note-4: -The Manuscript, published by Dharmarth Trust, is written with extraordinary variety and nuance of Indian calligraphy ranging from Shankar Bhashya to Bhasanuvada. Speaking on the occasion, Prime Minister Modi said that India is moving forward towards its path of development along with Gita’s mantra.

Concept note-5: -The Bhagavad Gita is a poem written in the Sanskrit language. Its 700 verses are structured into several ancient Indian poetic meters, with the principal being the shloka (Anushtubh chanda). It has 18 chapters in total. Each shloka consists of a couplet, thus the entire text consists of 1,400 lines.
