RBI relaxed leverage ratio for banks to ____

(A) 4%

(B) 4.8%

(C) ** 3.5%

(D) 3.8%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) relaxed the Leverage Ratio (LR) for banks to 3.5%. This is done to help them in order to expand their lending activities.

Concept note-2: -2019 where in it was decided that Non - Domestic Systemically Important Banks (DSIBs) have to maintain a leverage ratio of 3.5% w.e.f 01.10. 2020. Bank’s Leverage Ratio as of 31.12. 2020 is 3.55 % as against 3.50 % as stipulated by RBI.

Concept note-3: -A ratio above 5% is deemed to be an indicator of strong financial footing for a bank.

Concept note-4: -The leverage ratio is a measure of the bank’s core capital to its total assets. The ratio uses tier 1 capital to judge how leveraged a bank is in relation to its consolidated assets whereas the tier 1 capital adequacy ratio measures the bank’s core capital against its risk-weighted assets.

Concept note-5: -Basel III introduced a minimum “leverage ratio". The leverage ratio was calculated by dividing Tier 1 capital by the bank’s average total consolidated assets; the banks were expected to maintain a leverage ratio in excess of 3% under Basel III.
