Sushil Kumar, who recently dies at 79 was the former chief of which Indian froce?

(A) ** Indian Navy

(B) Indian Army

(C) Indian Air Force

(D) Indian Coast Guard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Passing Away of Former Navy Chief Admiral Sushil Kumar. Former Chief of the Naval Staff Admiral Sushil Kumar, PVSM, UYSM, AVSM, NM, passed away in the early hours on 27 November 2019 at Army Hospital (Research & Referral), New Delhi, after a prolonged illness.

Concept note-2: -Sushil Kumar assumed charge of the Indian Navy, as the 16th Chief of Naval Staff, on 30 December 1998. During his tenure as CNS, He was the highest-decorated serving officer in the Indian Navy, and he retired on 29 December 2001.

Concept note-3: -The current Chief of the Naval Staff (CNS) and only full admiral in the Indian Armed Forces is Admiral R. Hari Kumar.

Concept note-4: -Kumar started training in pehlwani at the Chhatrasal Stadium’s akhada at the age of 14. He was trained at the akhada by Yashvir and Ramphal, later by Arjuna awardee Satpal and then at the Indian Railways camp by Gyan Singh and Rajkumar Baisla Gurjar.
