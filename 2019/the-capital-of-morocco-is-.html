The capital of Morocco is ____

(A) Pretoria

(B) Addis Ababa

(C) ** Rabat

(D) Algiers

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rabat, Arabic Ribāṭ, city and capital of Morocco. One of the country’s four imperial cities, it is located on the Atlantic coast at the mouth of the Wadi Bou Regreg, opposite the city of Salé .

Concept note-2: -The Imperial Cities of Morocco are the four historical capital cities of Morocco: Fez, Marrakesh, Meknes and Rabat. Rabat is the current capital of Morocco.

Concept note-3: -Fez was the capital of Morocco for over four centuries, and it still remains the cultural center of the country, as well as one of the most important religious centers.

Concept note-4: -The Kingdom of Morocco is a Muslim country in western North Africa, with coastlines on the Atlantic Ocean and Mediterranean Sea. Just an hour ferry ride from Spain, the country has a unique mix of Arab, Berber, African and European cultural influences.

Concept note-5: -1 Rabat (Arabic: , Berber: ) – the capital of Morocco; very relaxed and hassle-free, highlights include a 12th-century tower and minaret.
