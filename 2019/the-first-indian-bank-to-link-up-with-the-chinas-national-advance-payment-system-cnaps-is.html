The first Indian bank to link up with the China’s National Advance Payment System (CNAPS) is;

(A) Bank of Baroda

(B) Union Bank Of India

(C) Exim Bank

(D) ** SBI, Shanghai

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The SBI’s Shanghai branch is now connected to China’s National Advance Payment System (CNAPS), becoming the first Indian Bank to operationalise it. The CNAPS was launched in 2008 by the People’s Bank of China (PBOC).

Concept note-2: -ICICI Bank, India’s largest private sector bank has launched its first branch in China.

Concept note-3: -The Imperial Bank of China (), China’s first modern bank, opened for business in 1897. The bank was organised as a joint-stock firm.

Concept note-4: -What is CNAPS ? China National Advanced Payment System (CNAPS) is an electronic inter-bank clearing system in China for processing Yuan payments. CNAPS number is a 12-digit number used as a clearing code by Chinese banks and is equivalent of a UK sort code.

Concept note-5: -1 Introduction ICICI was the first bank to champion its usage and introduced internet banking to its customers in 1996.
