The Indigenous Faith Day 2019 was celebrated in which state?

(A) Nagaland

(B) Assam

(C) ** Arunachal Pradesh

(D) Tripura

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Indigenous Faith Day in Arunachal Pradesh – December 1, 2023. Indigenous Faith Day is celebrated every year in the Indian state of Arunachal Pradesh on December 1 to safeguard and promote all indigenous faiths across the state.

Concept note-2: -Indigenous Faith Day is celebrated on December 1 in Arunachal Pradesh, India. It is a day to honor and promote the indigenous religions of the state, which include Buddhism, Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Jainism, and Zorastrianism.

Concept note-3: -However, as 1 December happens to be the birth anniversary of Golgi Bote Talom Rukbo, who is regarded as the father of the indigenous faith movement, the day is celebrated on this day since 2013.

Concept note-4: -Indigenous religions are the ancestral religions of peoples who are native to particular landscapes. Their religions help them achieve the goal of living successfully in those places. Thus, indigenous religions vary, just as the places their practitioners inhabit vary.

Concept note-5: -National Faith Day is celebrated across the world on January 9. It is a common Christian name for girls and has been popular for many years. The meaning of faith is a deep and steadfast belief. Women who are named Faith are loved by everyone for being devoted, steadfast, and loyal. They are a joy to all who know them.
