The longest highway in India is ____

(A) NH 5

(B) NH 10

(C) NH 7

(D) ** NH 44

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NH 44 covers the North-South Corridor of NHDP and it is officially listed as running over 4,112 km (2,555 mi) from Srinagar to Kanyakumari. It is the longest national highway in India.

Concept note-2: -National Highway-7 is the longest national highway in India, which traverses between Varanasi and Kanya Kumari. Identify the places en route from North to South. Q.

Concept note-3: -Currently the longest highway in India is NH-44, starting from Kanyakumari in Tamil Nadu to Srinagar of Jammu and Kashmir.

Concept note-4: -Longest Road in the World 1: Pan-American Highway The Pan-American Highway is a network of highways that stretches across the entire Americas, from its Northern Tip in Prudhoe Bay, Alaska, to Ushuaia in Argentina.

Concept note-5: -National Highway 27 is the second longest national highway of India and is 3,507 Km long. It smoothly connects the eastern part of India with the western part. It is also considered as the financial pulse of India as it connects 7 states and 47 cities with each other.
