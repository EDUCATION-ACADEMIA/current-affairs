The new Android version launched by Google on August 22, 2019 is;

(A) Android Pie

(B) ** Android 10

(C) Android Coco

(D) Android 9A

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -One August 22, 2019, there was an announcement that Android Q would now be called Android 10, officially and that ended the practice of naming after deserts. The logo suggests the Android robot logo resting inside the number 0.

Concept note-2: -As of November 2022 Android 12 is the most popular version of Android, with 30.06% of devices having the operating system installed. The first phones to have Android 12 were the Google Pixel 6 and the Pixel 6 Pro.

Concept note-3: -Android 13 (internally codenamed Tiramisu) was announced in an Android blog posted on February 10, 2022, and the first Developer Preview was immediately released for the Google Pixel series (from Pixel 4 to Pixel 6, dropping support for the Pixel 3 and Pixel 3a).

Concept note-4: -Android 11 (internally codenamed Red Velvet Cake) was intended for three monthly developer preview builds to be released before the first beta release, initially due in May, with a total of three monthly beta releases before the actual release.

Concept note-5: -Android 10 (codenamed Android Q during development) is the tenth major release and the 17th version of the Android mobile operating system. It was first released as a developer preview on March 13, 2019, and was released publicly on September 3, 2019.
