The Orchha town was recently added to the tentative list of World Heritage Sites of UNESCO. It belongs to which state?

(A) Andhra Pradesh

(B) ** Madhya Pradesh

(C) Uttar Pradesh

(D) Himachal Pradesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Situated on the banks of the river Betwa, Orchha is located in Niwari district of Bundelkhand region of Madhya Pradesh.

Concept note-2: -Dholavira and Ramappa Temple are the latest addition to the list under the ‘Cultural’ category. There are two more categories - Natural and Mixed. Latest Update about UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India: 27th July 2021 – Dholavira, Harappan City in the Rann of Kutchh, becomes India’s 40th UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Concept note-3: -Orchha means ‘hidden palace’, as its name suggests it is popular for its temples and palaces and was the capital of the Bundela kingdom in the 16th century. The famous spots in the town are Raj Mahal, Jehangir Mahal, Ramraja Temple, Rai Praveen Mahal, and Laxminarayan Mandir.

Concept note-4: -The historical fort cities of Gwalior and Orchha in Madhya Pradesh have been included in the list of UNESCOs world heritage cities under its urban landscape city programme, according to the state government.

Concept note-5: -First and foremost, we wish to clarify that the two cities, Orchha and Gwalior, have not been inscribed on the list of UNESCO World Heritage sites, which currently includes 38 Indian sites.
