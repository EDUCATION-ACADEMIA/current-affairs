The world’s first CNG port terminal will be opened at which port?

(A) Chennai

(B) Surat

(C) ** Bhavnagar

(D) Mumbai

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Context. Prime Minister Narendra Modi laid the foundation stone for the “world’s first CNG (Compressed Natural Gas) terminal” at Bhavnagar in Gujarat recently.

Concept note-2: -The Prime Minister, Shri Narendra Modi inaugurated and laid the foundation stone of projects worth over ₹5200 crores in Bhavnagar. The Prime Minister laid the foundation stone of the World’s First CNG Terminal and of brownfield port at Bhavnagar.

Concept note-3: -In simple words, CNG is produced by compressing natural gas to less than 1% of its volume at standard atmospheric pressure. In response to high fuel prices and environmental concerns, CNG has been used in auto rickshaws, pickup trucks, transit and school buses, and trains.

Concept note-4: -Overview: The Port of Bhavnagar, also known as the Port of Nagar, is a seaport in the Sorashtra region of Gujarat State in western India. Since the beginning of the 18th century, Nagar has had trade links with Southeast Asia, Africa, Arabia and the Red Sea ports.
