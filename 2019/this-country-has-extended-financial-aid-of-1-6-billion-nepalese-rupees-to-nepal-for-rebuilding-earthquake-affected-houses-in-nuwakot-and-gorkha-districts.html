[SOURCES]

This country has extended financial aid of 1. 6 billion Nepalese rupees to Nepal for rebuilding earthquake affected houses in Nuwakot and Gorkha districts.

(A) Japan

(B) Bangladesh

(C) UK

(D) ** India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India on Monday extended a financial aid of 1.6 billion Nepalese rupees to Nepal to help 50,000 people in Nuwakot and Gorkha districts rebuild their houses damaged in the devastating earthquake in 2015.

Concept note-2: -India has provided 1.54 billion Nepalese Rupees (INR nearly 96 crore) to Nepal as part of its commitment towards assistance and rehabilitation after the 2015 earthquake which saw the death of over 9,000 people in the country, the Indian Embassy in Kathmandu said on Thursday.

Concept note-3: -India was the largest aid donor to Nepal after the earthquake by donating one billion dollars and other non-monetary reliefs. Many countries and organizations donated aid, including China, the United Kingdom, and the United States, all of which provided or funded helicopters as requested by the Nepalese government.

Concept note-4: -The US Geological Service puts reconstruction at $10 billion (€8.9 billion) – or half of GDP. Many Nepalis are still sleeping in the open, and the United Nations believes that 600,000 houses have been destroyed or damaged.

Concept note-5: -India provides Rs 96 crore to Nepal as post-earthquake assistance: Indian Embassy.
