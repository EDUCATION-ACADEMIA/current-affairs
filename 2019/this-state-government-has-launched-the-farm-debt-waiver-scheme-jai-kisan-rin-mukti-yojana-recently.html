This state government has launched the farm debt-waiver scheme ’Jai Kisan Rin Mukti Yojana, recently.

(A) Karnataka

(B) Tamil Nadu

(C) ** Madhya Pradesh

(D) Gujarat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Madhya Pradesh Chief Minister Kamal Nath Tuesday launched his government’s Rs 50,000-crore farm debt-waiver scheme named ‘Jai Kisan Rin Mukti Yojana’.

Concept note-2: -In a farm loan waiver scheme, the Centre or the state Government repays the loan to the banks on behalf of the farmers, simply by using public money collected in the form of taxes. When there is a poor monsoon or natural calamity, farmers cannot repay their loans.

Concept note-3: -EXTENSION OF PERIOD OF PAYMENT OF 75% SHARE BY “OTHER FARMERS” from 01.07. 09 TO 31.12. 09.

Concept note-4: -The first nationwide farm-loan waiver in independent India was implemented in 1990 by the VP Singh-led government. It cost the exchequer Rs 10,000 crore. In 2008, the Agricultural Debt Waiver and Debt Relief Scheme, implemented by the UPA government, involved an outgo of Rs 71,680 crore.

Concept note-5: -Kumaraswamy’s government waived off around Rs 15,000 crore of farm loans in 2018 from cooperative and commercial banks for around 20 lakh farmers. Each farmer was eligible to seek waiver of up to Rs 2 lakh.
