West Bengal celebrated Bengali New Year on 15th April. It is known as;

(A) Pana Sankranti

(B) Vishu

(C) ** Pahela Baishakh

(D) Bohag Bihu

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Bengali new year is also referred to as Pohela Boishakh or Nobo Borso.

Concept note-2: -Pohela Boishakh (Bengali: ) is the first day of the Bengali calendar which is also the official calendar of Bangladesh.

Concept note-3: -Poila Baishakh or Bengali New Year is celebrated with much splendour among the Bengali communities spread all over the world. The day is observed on the first day of the Bengali calendar, which usually falls either on the 14th or 15th of April.

Concept note-4: -Boishakh (Bengali: , Nepali: , Bôishakh, Baishakh) is the first month in the Assamese calendar, Bengali calendar and Nepali calendar. This month lies between the second half of April and the first half of May.

Concept note-5: -Bengali New Year is referred to in Bengali as “New Year” (Bengali: Nôbobôrsho, from Sanskrit Nava(new) Barṣha(year) or “First of Boishakh” (Bengali: Pôhela Boishakh. Nobo means new and Borsho means year. In Bengali, Pohela stands for ‘first’ and Baishakh is first month of Bengali calendar.
