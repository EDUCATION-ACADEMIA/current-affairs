What is the currency of China?

(A) ** Yuan

(B) Yen

(C) Chinese Dollar

(D) Real

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -renminbi, (Chinese: “people’s money”) also called yuan, monetary unit of China. One renminbi (yuan) is divided into 100 fen or 10 jiao.

Concept note-2: -In Standard (Mandarin) Chinese, yuán literally means a “round object” or “round coin". During the Qing Dynasty, the yuan was a round coin made of silver. In informal contexts, the word is written with the simplified Chinese character , that literally means “beginning".

Concept note-3: -Currency exchange rate Chinese yuan to U.S. dollar by month December 2022. In December 2022, the exchange rate of yuan to U.S. dollar was 6.96. In the past decades, the yuan had undergone a slow liberalization being more and more exposed to the international money market.
