What is the maximum gap between two parliamentary sessions?

(A) 3 months

(B) ** 6 months

(C) 8 months

(D) 4 months

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The maximum gap between two sessions of Parliament cannot be more than six months. In other words, the Parliament should meet at least twice a year.

Concept note-2: -Complete answer : The maximum gap allowed between two sessions of the Parliament is 6 months. Hence the parliament must meet at least two times in a year. In India, the parliament conducts three types of sessions each year.

Concept note-3: -The minimum gap between two sessions of Parliament is 90 days, whereas the maximum gap between two sessions of the Parliament cannot be more than six months. To put in other words, the Parliament should meet at least twice a year.

Concept note-4: -The maximum gap between two sessions can be 6 months, which means that Parliament should meet at least twice a year. Was this answer helpful?
