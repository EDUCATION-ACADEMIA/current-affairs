When is the World Wide Web Day celebrated?

(A) ** 1st August

(B) 2nd August

(C) 3rd August

(D) 4th August

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Wide Web Day is observed on 1st of August every year. The day is marked in honour of the ability of people to browse information freely using the Web. The Web is the tool that billions across the globe use every moment to interact with the global system of interconnected computer systems that is the Internet.

Concept note-2: -In 1989, Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web, On 1st August 1989, is World Wide Web Day, marking the invention by Tim Berners-Lee of an internet-based communication system that changed the world. In 1991, the World Wide Web browser was made available on the public internet.

Concept note-3: -Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist, invented the World Wide Web (WWW) in 1989, while working at CERN.

Concept note-4: -Such a collection of useful, related resources, interconnected via hypertext links is dubbed a web of information. Publication on the Internet created what Tim Berners-Lee first called the WorldWideWeb (in its original CamelCase, which was subsequently discarded) in November 1990.
