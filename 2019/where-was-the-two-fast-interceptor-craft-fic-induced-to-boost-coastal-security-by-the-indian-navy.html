Where was the two Fast Interceptor Craft (FIC) induced to boost coastal security by the Indian Navy?

(A) Cuttack, Odisha

(B) Kochi, Kerala

(C) Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh

(D) ** Nagapattinam, Tamil Nadu

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Two new fast interceptor crafts will be inducted in the Indian Navy’s Bengal area, taking its total fleet strength at Kolkata to eight.

Concept note-2: -Currently, the coastal security of India is governed by a three-tiered structure. The Indian Navy patrols the International Maritime Boundary Line (IMBL), while the Indian Coast Guard is mandated to do patrolling and surveillance up to 200 nautical miles (i.e., EEZ).

Concept note-3: -On 12 February 1944 the Japanese submarine RO-110 was depth charged and sunk east-south-east off Visakhapatnam, India by the Indian sloop HMIS Jumna and the Australian minesweepers HMAS Launceston and HMAS Ipswich (J186).

Concept note-4: -HMIS Indus was sunk by a Japanese aircraft during the Burma Campaign on 6 April 1942.

Concept note-5: -The Indian Navy It is headed by the Chief of the Naval Staff or the CNS. He is of the rank of Admiral and has his headquarters at New Delhi. The Southern Naval Command is the Training Command at Kochi.
