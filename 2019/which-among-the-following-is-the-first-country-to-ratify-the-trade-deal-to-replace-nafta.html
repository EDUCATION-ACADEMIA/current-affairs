Which among the following is the first country to ratify the trade deal to replace NAFTA?

(A) Argentina

(B) Brazil

(C) Canada

(D) ** Mexico

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Overview. The U.S. – Mexico – Canada Agreement (USMCA) is a trade agreement between the named parties. The USMCA replaced the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA).

Concept note-2: -Preliminary agreement on the pact was reached in August 1992, and it was signed by the three leaders on December 17. NAFTA was ratified by the three countries’ national legislatures in 1993 and went into effect on January 1, 1994.

Concept note-3: -The North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA /ˈnæftə/; Spanish: Tratado de Libre Comercio de América del Norte, TLCAN; French: Accord de libre-échange nord-américain, ALÉNA) was an agreement signed by Canada, Mexico, and the United States that created a trilateral trade bloc in North America.

Concept note-4: -Canada, the United States and Mexico signed a new North American trade agreement to replace NAFTA on November 30, 2018. The CUSMA (or USMCA in the U.S.) came into force in Canada in July 2020.
