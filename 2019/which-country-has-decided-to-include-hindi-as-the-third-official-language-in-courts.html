Which country has decided to include Hindi as the third official language in courts?

(A) Iran

(B) ** UAE

(C) Saudi Arabia

(D) Oman

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which country has decided to include Hindi as the third official language in courts? Notes: The Abu Dhabi Judicial Department (ADJD) has decided to include Hindi as the third official language used in its courts, alongside Arabic and English, as part of a move designed to improve access to justice.

Concept note-2: -United Arab Emirates. Hindi is adopted as the third official court language in the Emirate of Abu Dhabi.

Concept note-3: -Hindi became the official language of the Union of India in 1950. The Constitution of India provides for the use of Hindi in the Devanagari script as the official language of the Union. According to Article 343, “The official language of the Union shall be Hindi in the Devanagari script.

Concept note-4: -The Abu Dhabi Judicial Department (ADJD) on Sunday acknowledged Hindi language as its third official language after Arabic and English.
