Which country’s citizen should have a visa to enter India via Pakistan, China, Hong Kong and Macau?

(A) Japan

(B) Bhutan

(C) Maldives

(D) ** Nepal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(C) A citizen of Nepal or Bhutan must have a visa for India if he/she is entering India from China, Macau, Hong Kong, Pakistan and Maldives.

Concept note-2: -Hong Kong passport holders are required to apply for an Indian eVisa before departure. Depending on the purpose of visit, it is possible to apply for the India tourist visa for Hong Kongers, which is valid for 1 year, offers double entry, and supports stays of 90 days at a time for touristic purposes only.

Concept note-3: -Since this is no visa as Hong Kong offers a visa-free stay to Indian citizens visiting for a short period of no more than 14 days for the purposes of business, tourism, transit or to meet family and friends, you do not have to pay any visa fee to the Hong Kong high commission.

Concept note-4: -You’ll probably need a visa to go to India Citizens of 164 countries – including most nations in the EU and EEA, the UK, the US, Australia and New Zealand, and many countries in Asia, Africa, the Middle East and Latin America – can apply for an e-Visa before traveling via a simple online form.

Concept note-5: -Is there a Visa on Arrival/E-Visa Available for Indians visiting Hong Kong? No, Hong Kong does not offer the facility of visa on arrival for Indian passport holders. Indians can gain entry into the country only if they have completed Pre-Arrival Registration formalities.
