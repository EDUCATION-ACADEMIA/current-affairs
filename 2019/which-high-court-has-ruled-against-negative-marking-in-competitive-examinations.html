Which high court has ruled against negative marking in competitive examinations?

(A) Delhi High Court

(B) Bombay High Court

(C) Calcutta High Court

(D) ** Madras High Court

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Notes: In a huge relief to a number of students who fail to qualify to enter into prestigious institutions like IITs due to negative marking, the Madras High Court ruled against negative marking in competitive examinations saying they are bad in law.

Concept note-2: -Sometimes when candidates get an easy question and mark it quickly without reading the question properly. But in that hurry, the candidates might mark the wrong answer which results in negative marking. So candidates should read the questions and options properly before marking them.

Concept note-3: -Go on attempting all the questions to which you know the answers for sure. It is only when you are not sure about the one correct answer that you should leave that question to be re-read and re-thought later on.
