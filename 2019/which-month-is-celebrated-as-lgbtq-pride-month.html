Which month is celebrated as LGBTQ Pride month?

(A) ** June

(B) July

(C) August

(D) December

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -LGBT Pride Month is a month, typically in June, dedicated to celebration and commemoration of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) pride. Pride Month began after the Stonewall riots , a series of gay liberation protests in 1969, and has since spread outside of the United States.

Concept note-2: -Pride Month has evolved into a national fixture each June, but October marks a perhaps lesser-known celebration – LGBT History Month. Pride Month has been acknowledged on Chapman University’s campus, so some may wonder if LGBT History Month is also recognized.

Concept note-3: -So when did Pride month start? “Pride” is the designated name or slogan that commemorates the anniversary of the Stonewall Inn Riots. It started in 1970, a year after the Stonewall Inn Riots took place, as a peaceful march with no dress restrictions where people could express themselves and their sexuality freely.
