Which of the following became the fourth Indian Airline to have 100 aircraft fleet?

(A) GoAir

(B) ** SpiceJet

(C) Air Asia

(D) Air India Express

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -IndiGo placed an order for 100 Airbus A320-200 aircraft worth US$6 billion in June 2005 during the Paris Air Show with plans to commence operations in mid-2006. The airline received its first A320 in July 2006 and planned to induct 100 aircraft by 2015–2016.

Concept note-2: –Company has changed its name from Royal Airways Ltd. to Spicejet Ltd.

Concept note-3: -SpiceJet operates a fleet of Boeing and Q-400s that allow for greater efficiency in maintenance and to support its low-cost structure. The new-generation Boeing 737-700s, 737-800s and 737-900ERs with winglets have been a conscious choice.

Concept note-4: -There are seven domestic airlines operating in India. Here is the list of airlines in India: AirAsia India, Air India, Air India Express, Go First, IndiGo, SpiceJet and Vistara.
