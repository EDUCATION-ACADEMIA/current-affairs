Who clinched the men’s title at the 2019 Italian Open Tennis tournament?

(A) Novak Djokovic

(B) ** Rafael Nadal

(C) Roger Federer

(D) Andy Murray

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Defending champion Rafael Nadal defeated Novak Djokovic in the final, 6–0, 4–6, 6–1 to win the men’s singles tennis title at the 2019 Italian Open.

Concept note-2: -In Tennis, defending champion Rafael Nadal of Spain has clinched the Italian Open 2019 title after beating Novak Djokovic. This is Nadal’s ninth Italian Open title. It is the 81st title of Nadal’s career.

Concept note-3: -Defending champions Nikola Mektić and Mate Pavić defeated John Isner and Diego Schwartzman in the final, 6–2, 6–7, [12–10] to win the men’s doubles tennis title at the 2022 Italian Open. The pair saved a championship point en route to their tenth career title as a team.

Concept note-4: -Djokovic has won it! Djokovic has won his first title in over six months as he claims a 38th Masters 1000 title of his career, beating Stefanos Tsitsipas 6-0 7-6(5).

Concept note-5: -Elina Svitolina was the two-time defending champion, but lost in the second round to Victoria Azarenka. Karolína Plíšková won the title, defeating Johanna Konta in the final, 6–3, 6–4.
