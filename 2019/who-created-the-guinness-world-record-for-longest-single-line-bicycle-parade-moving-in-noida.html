Who created the Guinness World Record for ’longest single line bicycle parade (moving)’ in Noida?

(A) NDRF

(B) ** CISF

(C) Indian Coast Guard

(D) National Security Guard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Central Industrial Security Force (CISF) created the Guinness World Record for ‘longest single line bicycle parade (moving)’ in Noida on Sunday. CISF personnel rode 1,327 bicycles non-stop in a single line, maintaining a uniform distance between the bicycles.

Concept note-2: -The record was created at the Yamuna Expressway in Noida when personnel of the central force rode a total of 1,327 bicycles “non-stop with uniform distance between bicycles in a single line,” the official said. “The bicycle parade covered 3.2-km distance non-stop.

Concept note-3: -UCI men’s record: Daniel Bigham. , 2022, 55.548 kilometres (34.516 mi) UCI women’s record: Ellen van Dijk. , 2022, 49.254 kilometres (30.605 mi)

Concept note-4: -“Lt Col Sriram has set a new Guinness world record for the ‘Fastest Solo Cycling – (Men)’ from Leh to Manali (in Himachal Pradesh) on September 26. The total distance covered was 472 kilometers with a total elevation gain of approximately 8,000 metres,” the spokesman said.

Concept note-5: -A Delhi man travelled 61,445 kilometres in 86 days by train and bus to break the Guinness World Record for the longest journey by scheduled public transport in a single country.
