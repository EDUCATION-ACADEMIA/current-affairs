Who has named 2019 FWA footballer of the year?

(A) Harry Kane

(B) ** Raheem Sterling

(C) Kevin De Bruyne

(D) Leroy Sane

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Liverpool star Mo Salah was voted Footballer of the Year for the second time, having first won the award in 2018. The Egyptian received 48% of the vote beating Kevin De Bruyne (Manchester City) and Declan Rice (West Ham) to first place. He said: “This feels great.

Concept note-2: -Meanwhile, Fran Kirby has won the most FWA Women’s Footballer of the Year awards with two to her name. After winning the inaugural trophy in 2017-18, the Chelsea star claimed the award in the 2020-21 season.

Concept note-3: -Mo Salah crowned Footballer of the Year 2022 The good news just keeps coming. Salah previously won the Football Writers’ award in 2018. Salah has been crowned alongside Chelsea striker Sam Kerr who is the Women’s Footballer of the Year.

Concept note-4: -The Football Writers’ Association (FWA) is an association of football journalists and correspondents writing for English newspapers and agencies. It presents the Footballer of the Year Award, the oldest and most distinguished award given in the domestic game.
