Who partners with SonyLIV to provide mobile inflight entertainment?

(A) Air India

(B) ** IndiGo

(C) Air Asia

(D) Kingfisher

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -IndiGo flight: IndiGo partners with SonyLIV to provide mobile inflight entertainment.

Concept note-2: -As it is a weekly subscription, once availed, the flyer can watch it on his or her device even when he or she is not in flight. IndiGo has 47 per cent share in the domestic air passenger market, as per data of aviation regulator DGCA, making it the leading airline in India.

Concept note-3: -Enjoy exclusive in-flight entertainment with our partner, SonyLIV. Pick from blockbuster movies, award-wining tv-shows, and more at just ₹25 for 7 days.

Concept note-4: -Many airplanes are equipped with Wi-Fi that will let customers use their own personal device to stream from a catalog of movies and shows. People who want to watch from their smartphone or tablet must have the American Airlines app downloaded before takeoff to be able to access the content.

Concept note-5: -In-flight movies Personal on-demand videos are stored in an aircraft’s main in-flight entertainment system, whence they can be viewed on demand by a passenger over the aircraft’s built in media server and wireless broadcast system.
