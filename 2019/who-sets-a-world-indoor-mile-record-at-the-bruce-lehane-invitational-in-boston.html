Who sets a world indoor mile record at the Bruce Lehane Invitational in Boston?

(A) Jakob Ingebrigtsen

(B) Paul Chelimo

(C) Selemon Barega

(D) ** Yomif Kejelcha

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Jamaica’s Aisha Praught-Leer breaks 18-year-old national indoor mile record at Scarlet and White Invitational. Jamaica’s Aisha Praught-Leer broke Kenya Sinclair’s 18-year-old national indoor mile record to finish fourth at the 2023 Bruce Lehane Scarlet and White Invitational at Boston University on Saturday.

Concept note-2: -Elite marathon runners average a mile in around 4 to 5 minutes. The current world record for one mile is 3:43.13, set by Hicham El Guerrouj of Morocco in 1999.

Concept note-3: -In this study, 16 year olds were able to reach a maximum running speed of 26 kilometers per hour (3:45 per mile), while two year-olds and 10 year-olds were able to reach six (16:20 per mile) and 20 kilometers per hour (4:50 per mile), respectively.
