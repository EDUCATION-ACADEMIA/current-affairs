Who unveiled a mobile app to aid visually impaired persons in identifying Indian currency notes, recently?

(A) Ministry of Finance

(B) SBI

(C) ** RBI

(D) Central Bank of India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has recently updated its Mobile Aided Note Identifier (MANI) app that helps the visually impaired people to identify banknotes to include 11 more Indian languages.

Concept note-2: -The MANI App was launched on January 01, 2020 for aiding visually impaired persons to identify the denomination of Indian Banknotes.

Concept note-3: -Users can download the app, called ‘MANI’, which is an acronym for Mobile Aided Note Identifier, and will have to scan the notes using the camera. With an eye to aid the differently-abled, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on Wednesday launched a mobile app to identify currency notes.

Concept note-4: -(b) The MANI app can also identify the denomination of currency notes through an audio notification in Hindi/English. (c) For accessing the application features, one can easily navigate the ‘MANI’ app via voice controls.

Concept note-5: -Which IIT has launched an app ‘Roshni’ to help blind identify notes? Notes: IIT Ropar has launched an android app ‘Roshni’ to help the blind in recognizing the denomination of the currency notes, using image processing and analytics.
