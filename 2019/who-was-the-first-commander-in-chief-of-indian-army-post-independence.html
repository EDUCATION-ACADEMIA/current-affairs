Who was the first Commander-in-Chief of Indian Army post Independence?

(A) ** K M Carriappa

(B) Sam Manekshaw

(C) K S Thimayya

(D) P P Kumaramangalam

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In January 1949 Cariappa was named the first Indian commander in chief of the Indian army, replacing the British commanding general, Sir Roy Bucher. As army chief, Cariappa had a mandate to transform the army left by the British into a national military force.

Concept note-2: -Field Marshal Kodandera Madappa Cariappa OBE (28 January 1899 – 15 May 1993) was the first Indian Commander-in-Chief (C-in-C) of the Indian Army. He led Indian forces on the Western Front during the Indo-Pakistani War of 1947.

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Gen KM Cariappa. On 15th January 1949, Field Marshal Kodandera M. Cariappa, who was then a Lieutenant General, took over as the first Indian Commander-in-Chief of the Indian Army from General Sir Francis Butcher, the last British person to hold that post.
