Who was the last Governor General of India?

(A) Lord Mountbatten

(B) Lord Clive

(C) Warren Hastings

(D) ** Rajagopalachari

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lord Mountbatten was the last Viceroy of India.

Concept note-2: -The answer is Chakravarti Rajagopalachari. After independence, Chakravarti Rajagopalachari (1878-1972) became the only Indian and last governor-general of India.

Concept note-3: -William Bentinck became the first governor general of India in 1833. Warren Hastings was the first governor general of Bengal.. This system of overlordship ended with the last Viceroy, Lord Mountbatten. Q.

Concept note-4: -Lord Mountbatten, Last British Governor General of India Leaves for UK, 1948.
