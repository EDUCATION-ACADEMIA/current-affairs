World Day to Combat Desertification and Drought was observed on ____

(A) June 16

(B) ** June 17

(C) June 18

(D) June 19

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The United Nations General Assembly declared 17 June to be “World Day to Combat Desertification and Drought” by its resolution A/RES/49/115 adopted in December 1994.

Concept note-2: -The United Nations General Assembly declared 17 June to be “World Day to Combat Desertification and Drought” by its resolution A/RES/49/115 adopted in December 1994.

Concept note-3: -This year, the theme of the International Day Against Desertification, and Drought “Rising up from drought together", emphasises the need of an early action to avoid disastrous consequences for humanity and the planetary ecosystems.

Concept note-4: -Desertification is the degradation process by which a fertile land changes itself into a desert by losing its flora and fauna, this can be caused by drought, deforestation, climate change, human activities or improper agriculture. Desertification is a process of degradation of the land.

Concept note-5: -Drought and its impacts can be exacerbated by human activities that are not adapted to the local climate and/or soil type. Land degradation is the process of turning fertile land into less or non-productive land. In extreme cases, in drylands, this is called desertification.
