[SOURCES]
Zero Discrimination Day observed on 1st March. The symbol of the event is;

(A) Pigeons

(B) ** Butterfly

(C) Finches

(D) Peacock

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The symbol for Zero Discrimination Day is a butterfly.

Concept note-2: -On Zero Discrimination Day, 1 March 2022, WHO is highlighting the right of all people to live healthy, full and productive lives with dignity regardless of their HIV, viral hepatitis or sexually transmitted infections status.

Concept note-3: -On Zero Discrimination Day, 1 March, we celebrate the right of everyone to live a full and productive life-and live it with dignity and free from discrimination. When you see something that is not right, not fair, not just, you have to speak up.

Concept note-4: -The 2022 edition of the International Day focuses on the theme “VOICES FOR ACTION AGAINST RACISM”.

Concept note-5: -Zero Discrimination Day: History The day was launched and first celebrated on March 1, 2014 by UNAIDS, the organisation that has championed the cause of dismantling systems of oppression against people with HIV/AIDS in UN member countries. It started out as a campaign by the same name in December 2013.
