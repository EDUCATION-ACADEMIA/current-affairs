According to a new study Saturn’s largest moon Titan is now expanding from Saturn at how many times faster than scientists had previously predicted?

(A) ** 100

(B) 50

(C) 150

(D) 60

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Saturnian moon’s orbit is now expanding away from Saturn at a rate 100 times faster than scientists had previously predicted.

Concept note-2: -It would take roughly seven years to get there, and then the same to get back, though well-timed gravity-assists either on the way there or back could reduce the total flight time to just 10 years.

Concept note-3: -Titan takes 15 days and 22 hours to complete a full orbit of Saturn. Titan is also tidally locked in synchronous rotation with Saturn, meaning that, like Earth’s Moon, Titan always shows the same face to the planet as it orbits.

Concept note-4: -Its rotational period is just 5.5 hours, the fastest known rotation of all of Saturn’s moons. Hati is a member of the Norse group of moons.

Concept note-5: -Dutch astronomer Christiaan Huygens discovered Saturn’s largest moon, Titan, on March 25, 1655.
