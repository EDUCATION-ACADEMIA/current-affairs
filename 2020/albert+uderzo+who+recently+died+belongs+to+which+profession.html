Albert Uderzo, who recently died, belongs to which profession?

(A) ** Comic book artist

(B) Director

(C) Politician

(D) Sportsman

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Alberto Aleandro Uderzo (Italian: [alˈbɛrto aleˈandro uˈdɛrtso]; 25 April 1927 – 24 March 2020), better known as Albert Uderzo (French: [albɛʁ ydɛʁzo]), was a French comic book artist and scriptwriter. He is best known as the co-creator and illustrator of the Astérix series in collaboration with René Goscinny .

Concept note-2: -1938. The family moves a second time, from Clichy-sous-Bois to the Rue de Montreuil, in the 11th district in Paris.

Concept note-3: -Asterix was created by writer René Goscinny and illustrator Albert Uderzo and debuted in 1959 in the French comic magazine Pilote.

Concept note-4: -The Creators: Two Frenchmen, writer René Goscinny and illustrator Albert Uderzo, created Asterix as a “typically French hero” for a series fi rst published in 1959 for the comic magazine, Pilote, popular with readers in France and Belgium.

Concept note-5: -The adventures of Asterix, Obelix and friends have been translated into over 100 languages. Reading translations of them is a fun way to practice a language you’re learning.
