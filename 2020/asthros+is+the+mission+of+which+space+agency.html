ASTHROS is the mission of which space agency?

(A) China National Space Administration (CNSA)

(B) Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency (JAXA)

(C) Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)

(D) ** National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -ASTHROS - NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory.

Concept note-2: -June 29, 2022. NASA’s Astrophysics Stratospheric Telescope for High Spectral Resolution Observations at Submillimeter-wavelengths (ASTHROS), seen in this illustration, is a high-altitude balloon mission for studying astrophysical phenomena.

Concept note-3: -We’re working on missions to investigate the surface and interior of Venus and to study the ocean deep below the icy crust of Jupiter’s moon Europa. JPL is taking part in missions to map Earth’s surface and to chart minute movements of the planet’s ice and land-covered surfaces.

Concept note-4: -NATIONAL AERONAUTICS AND SPACE ADMINISTRATION (NASA) | U.S. Department of the Interior.

Concept note-5: -NASA Leadership NASA is led by Administrator Bill Nelson, who became the agency’s 14th administrator on May 3, 2021. Nelson served in the U.S. Senate for 18 years from Florida and as a payload specialist on space shuttle mission 61-C in 1986.
