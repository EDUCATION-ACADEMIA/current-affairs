Central Excise Day is celebrated on which date?

(A) 22nd February

(B) 23rd February

(C) ** 24th February

(D) 25th February

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Central Excise Day is celebrated on 24th February every year to honour the efforts of the Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC). This day is about acknowledging the work of the CBEC members and motivating them to perform their duties with greater efficiency.

Concept note-2: -Central Excise Day 2022: Significance The jobs of the members of the Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs in preventing corruption among the manufacturing industries are worth appreciation and encouragement.

Concept note-3: -Central Excise Day of India is celebrated every year on February 24.

Concept note-4: -(a)a duty of excise to be called the Central Value Added Tax (CENVAT)] on all excisable goods (excluding goods produced or manufactured in special economic zones) which are produced or manufactured in India as, and at the rates, set forth in the First Schedule to the Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 (5 of 1986);
