Chitetsu Watanabe, the world’s oldest man died at the age of how many years?

(A) 108

(B) 110

(C) ** 112

(D) 114

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The world’s oldest living man, Chitetsu Watanabe, has passed away in Japan at the age of 112, the Niigata prefecture government said in a statement Tuesday.

Concept note-2: -In 2010, Sogen Kato was thought to be Tokyo’s oldest living man. On his 111th birthday, officials went to his house to congratulate him for living so long. However, when they went inside his house to meet in, they came across a shocking truth. Sogen Kato was dead for the last 30 years.

Concept note-3: -On February 12, representatives of Guinness World Records visited Chitetsu Watanabe’s nursing home in Niigata, Japan, to present him with a certificate verifying his status as the world’s oldest man.

Concept note-4: -The oldest person ever whose age has been verified is Jeanne Calment (1875-1997) of France, who lived to the age of 122.
