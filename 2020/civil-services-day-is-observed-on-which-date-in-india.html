Civil Services Day is observed on which date in India?

(A) ** 21 April

(B) 12 April

(C) 20 April

(D) 19 April

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Government of India celebrates April 21 every year as ‘Civil Services day’ as an occasion for the civil servants to rededicate themselves to the cause of citizen and renew their commitments to public service and excellence in work.

Concept note-2: -Why is 21st April celebrated as Civil Service Day in India? National Civil Service Day is celebrated on 21st April to commemorate the date on which Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel addressed the probationers of Administrative Services Officers and referred to civil servants as the ‘steel frame of India’.

Concept note-3: -Civil Service Day. The Government of India observes the 21st of April every year as ‘Civil Services Day’. This is an occasion for the civil servants to rededicate themselves to the cause of citizens and renew their commitment to public service and excellence in work.

Concept note-4: -Every year on April 21, the Government of India commemorates ‘Civil Services Day’ as an opportunity for civil officials to recommit themselves to the cause of citizens and renew their vows to public service and work excellence.

Concept note-5: -Going by history, on April 21, 1947, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, the first Home Minister of Independent India addressed the first batch of Administrative Services at Metcalf House, Delhi.
