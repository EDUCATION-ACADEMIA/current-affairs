India witnessed how many people move out of multidimensional poverty between 2005-2006 and 2015-2016, as per UN report on Global Multidimensional Poverty Index?

(A) 373 million

(B) 473 million

(C) ** 273 million

(D) 173 million

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Of the nearly 415 million people who exited poverty in India in the 15 years prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, roughly 275 million did so between 2005/2006 and 2015/2016 and 140 million did so between 2015/2016 and 2019/2021. The country’s MPI value and incidence of poverty were both more than halved.

Concept note-2: -Some 25.01 per cent of the population in India is multi-dimensionally poor, with a Multi-Dimensional Poverty Index (MPI) score of 0.118, said the minister of state for health and family welfare Bharati Pravin Pawar, citing data released by NITI Aayog in 2021.

Concept note-3: -UNDP report says 415 million people lifted out of poverty in India: Centre.

Concept note-4: -In India, poverty at $1.25 a day in 2005 prices increased from 420 million people in 1981 to 455 million in 2005, while the poverty rate as a share of the total population went from 60% in 1981 to 42% in 2005.
