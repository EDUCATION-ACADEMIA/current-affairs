Indian Railways’ Central Railway zone has installed an Automated Ticket Checking & Managing Access machine, 1st of its kind at which station?

(A) Mahalaxmi station

(B) Chinchpokli station

(C) ** Nagpur station

(D) Juinagar station

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. In a first, Indian Railways’ Central Railway zone has installed a newly innovated ATMA – Automated Ticket Checking & Managing Access machine at the Nagpur railway station .

Concept note-2: -Indian Railways Central Railway zone has installed an automated ticket kiosk called ATMA Automated Ticket Checking & Managing Access machine.

Concept note-3: -In 1990, first self printing ticket machine was introduced. In June 2001, WDP-4 was started and 10 of them were provided by General Motors, operating out of Hubli.

Concept note-4: -Kavach alerts when a loco pilot jumps a signal (Signal Passed at Danger -SPAD), which is the leading cause of train collisions. The system can alert the loco pilot and take control of the brakes and bring train movement to a halt automatically when it notices another train on the same line within a prescribed distance.

Concept note-5: -A ticket collector is the supervisor of in-train queries and management activities related to passengers.
