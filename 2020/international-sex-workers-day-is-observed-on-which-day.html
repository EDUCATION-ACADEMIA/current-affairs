International Sex Workers Day is observed on which day?

(A) ** 2 June

(B) 1 June

(C) 4 June

(D) 3 June

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Sex Workers’ Day is observed on 2 June every year across the world. This day is celebrated to highlight the rampant exploitation of sex workers and the terrible conditions they live in. It aims to raise awareness about the abuse and victimisation of sex workers.

Concept note-2: -November 30, 2023 - Thursday Every year, November 30th is International Day of Consent. The holiday was established in 2018 by Jenny Wilson of Irregular Arts and consentculture.co.uk. More than a simple yes or no, consent symbolizes the way we connect to one another, ourselves, and the world around us.

Concept note-3: -The International Day to End Violence Against Sex Workers is held annually on 17 December by sex workers, their advocates, friends, families, and allies.

Concept note-4: -The International Day to End Violence Against Sex Workers is annually observed across the world on 17th December to focus attention on the hate crimes committed against sex workers across the globe. The day also aims to empower sex workers to come together against discrimination and remember the victims of violence.
