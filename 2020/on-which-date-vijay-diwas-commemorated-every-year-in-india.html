On which date Vijay Diwas commemorated every year in India?

(A) ** December 16

(B) December12

(C) December 26

(D) December 13

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Vijay Diwas on December 16 is when the Indian Armed Forces, including veterans, citizens, and students, pay tribute to and remember the sacrifices of their soldiers.

Concept note-2: -December 16 as a day forms a very significant part of modern Indian history. It is a reminder of India’s emerging as a celebrated country in the 1971 war against Pakistan. Vijay Diwas, on December 16, celebrates the fearless whim and vigour of our armed forces.

Concept note-3: -Vijay Diwas is the national holiday that marks the moment that cemented India’s victory against Pakistan in the 1971 war. Vijay Diwas is being celebrated across the nation on December 16 to commemorate India’s victory against Pakistan in the 1971 war.

Concept note-4: -Vijay Diwas or Victory Day is commemorated on December 16, marking the end of the 1971 Indo-Pak War and the liberation of Bangladesh. India declared victory on this day 51 years ago after Pakistan signed the Instrument of Surrender. So, what led to the war, which was found over 13 days?

Concept note-5: -Thirteen days after the war started, India achieved a clear upper hand, and the Eastern Command of the Pakistan military signed the instrument of surrender on 16 December 1971 in Dhaka, marking the formation of East Pakistan as the new nation of Bangladesh.
