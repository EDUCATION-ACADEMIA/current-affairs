Padma Shri awardee Sonam Tshering Lepcha died at 92 in Kalimpong, West Bengal belongs to which field?

(A) Politician

(B) Dancer

(C) ** Folk Musician

(D) Architect

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Sonam Tshering Lepcha (3 January 1928 – 30 July 2020) was an Indian folk musician, composer and lyricist.

Concept note-2: -In 1960, Sonam Tshering became the first ever artist from the Lepcha community to be heard on All India Radio. He compiled and composed more than two hundred folk songs and ten dance-dramas based on Lepcha folklore. In 1963, Tshering staged his first musical dance-drama ‘Teesta Rangeet.

Concept note-3: -Among his collection include a special flute which is used to imitate birdcalls and there is a violin like satsang. In addition to the rare musical instruments like the banjo like tumbok, the museum also contains traditional Lepcha items like a coiled rope ladder for reaching bee hives and honey.

Concept note-4: -Mickma Tshering Lepcha grew up in West Sikkim, in the village of Nehrdhang Busty surrounded by music: his father, a shaman, performed the ceremonial chanting that many believe to be the basis of Lepcha music, and Mickma’s sisters and brothers would often sing as they did their daily chores.

Concept note-5: -Answer: a special bamboo flute, called panthong, in sikkim.
