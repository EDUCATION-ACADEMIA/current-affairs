RBI conducted the simultaneous purchase of Government Securities maturing between 2027 and 2033, and sale of 182 day and 364 day treasury bills under Open Market Operations for how much worth each?

(A) Rs 20, 000 crore

(B) ** Rs 10, 000 crore

(C) Rs 30, 000 crore

(D) Rs 40, 000 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -1.3 Treasury bills or T-bills, which are money market instruments, are short term debt instruments issued by the Government of India and are presently issued in three tenors, namely, 91 day, 182 day and 364 day.

Concept note-2: -Treasury bills are usually sold in denominations of $1, 000. However, some can reach a maximum denomination of $5 million in non-competitive bids. These securities are widely regarded as low-risk and secure investments.

Concept note-3: -Those that mature in less than 91 days are called Cash Management Bills (e.g. 35-day, 42-day). T-Bills do not bear interest but are rather issued and sold at a discount from face value (they can’t be traded at a premium) and are redeemed at maturity for the full face value of the instrument.

Concept note-4: -Treasury bills, also called T-bills, are short term government securities with a maturity period of less than one year issued by the central government of India. Several financial instruments pay interest to you on your investment; treasury bills do not pay interest because they are also called zero-coupon securities.
