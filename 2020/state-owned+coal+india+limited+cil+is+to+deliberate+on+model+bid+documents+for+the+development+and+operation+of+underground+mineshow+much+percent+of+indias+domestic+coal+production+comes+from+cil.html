State-owned Coal India Limited (CIL) is to deliberate on model bid documents for the development and operation of underground mines.How much percent of India’s domestic coal production comes from CIL?

(A) ** 80%

(B) 20%

(C) 25%

(D) 65%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Produces around 83% of India’s overall coal production in India where approximately 57% of primary commercial energy is coal dependent, CIL alone meets to the tune of 40% of primary commercial energy requirement.

Concept note-2: -Currently, there are 36 captive and commercial coal mines operating in India that produced 27.7Mt of coal in Q2 2022 - reporting a 79% y-o-y growth.

Concept note-3: -In September, 1975 CIL was formed as a Holding Company with five subsidiaries namely Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL), Central Coalfields Limited (CCL ), Eastern Coalfields Limited ( ECL ), Western Coalfields Limited ( WCL ) and Central Mine Planning and Design Institute ( CMPDI ).

Concept note-4: -Coal India (CIL), which accounts for over 80 per cent of domestic coal production, is eyeing an output of 700 million tonnes (MT) for FY’23.
