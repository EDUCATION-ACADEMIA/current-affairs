Tata Power Company has started its 178 megawatt (MW) hydropower project (HPP) in which country?

(A) Russia

(B) ** Georgia

(C) Norway

(D) Japan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Adjaristsqali Georgia LLC, a Joint Venture between Tata Power, Norway’s Clean Energy Invest (CEI), and International Financial Corporation (IFC) today announced the start of commercial operation of the 178 MW Shuakhevi Hydro Power Project (HPP) located in southwest Georgia.

Concept note-2: -Tata Power’s Bhivpuri Hydro Power Plant completes 100 years of clean energy generation.

Concept note-3: -Tata Power and Norway-based SN Power entered into an exclusive partnership to develop hydro power projects in India and Nepal. Tata Power has a JV with the Royal Government of Bhutan under which it has commissioned a 126 MW Dagachhu Hydro Project with Druk Green Power Company in March 2015.

Concept note-4: -Pioneering hydropower generation in India in 1915 with the first hydro station at Khopoli in 1915, Tata Power today has 30% of clean energy generation in its portfolio including solar and wind generation.
