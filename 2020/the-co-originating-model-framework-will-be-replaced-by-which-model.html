The ‘co-originating model’ framework will be replaced by which model?

(A) Co-depositing model

(B) Co-creating model

(C) Co-debt model

(D) ** Co-lending model

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Co-origination, also known as co-lending, is a setup where banks and non-banks enter into an arrangement for the joint contribution of credit for priority sector lending.

Concept note-2: -The future of co-lending in India In order to support the MSMEs, the RBI will continue to amend the regulations in response to the needs of different stakeholders. The co-lending market could reach a total volume of roughly Rs 25–30 thousand crores in 2022-2023; the potential for expansion from here is quite high.

Concept note-3: -Prompt Corrective Action (PCA)is a framework introduced by the Reserve Bank of India in 2022. The Prompt Corrective Action framework is a structured early-intervention mechanism for banks with weak financial records due to loss of profitability or poor asset quality.

Concept note-4: -“Co-Lending Model” (CLM) helps the borrower get loans at a very affordable and competitive rate. Can banks lend to NBFCs? Yes, banks can provide credit to NBFCs for the purpose of on-lending to certain priority sectors.
