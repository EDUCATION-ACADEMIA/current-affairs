[SOURCES]

To improve the performance of the Goods and Service Tax Network, government of India has partnered with which company?

(A) TCS

(B) Wipro

(C) ** Infosys

(D) Cognizant

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The GSTN has also been approved for a non-recurring grant of Rs 315 crores. The contract for developing this vast technological backend was awarded to Infosys in September 2015.

Concept note-2: -The government has paid Rs 164.5 crore to Infosys between January 2019 to June 2021 for developing the portal. In 2015, Infosys had won the Rs 1, 380 crore-contract to build the technology platform for Goods and Services Tax (GST).

Concept note-3: -In India, there are four different types of GST: Integrated Goods and Services Tax, State Goods and Services Tax, and Central Goods and Services Tax. Is GST charged on goods and services in India? GST is the single tax that applied to the supply of goods and services from the manufacturer to the customer.

Concept note-4: -GSTN, short for Goods and Service Tax Network, is a non-profit non-government company. It provides shared IT infrastructure and service to both central and state governments including taxpayers and other stakeholders. The registration Front end services, Returns, and payments to all taxpayers will be provided by GSTN.
