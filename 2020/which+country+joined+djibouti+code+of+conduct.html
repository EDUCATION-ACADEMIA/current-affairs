Which country joined Djibouti Code of Conduct?

(A) China

(B) Iran

(C) ** India

(D) USA

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is International Maritime grouping against piracy. India joined the Djibouti Code of Conduct (DCOC) as an observer as part of efforts aimed at enhancing maritime security in the Indian Ocean region.

Concept note-2: -Under the Code, which became effective from the date it was signed, signatories declared their intention to co-operate to the fullest possible extent in the repression of transnational organized crime in the maritime domain, maritime terrorism, and other illegal activities at sea.

Concept note-3: -The Djibouti Code of Conduct Trust Fund is a multi-donor voluntary fund. Financial contributions may be made by Member States of the United Nations or the IMO, organizations, institutions or private individuals to support transnational maritime crime capacity building.

Concept note-4: -The International Maritime Organization Djibouti Code of Conduct (DCoC), adopted in 2009, was originally an agreement on cooperation between East African and Southwest Asian states to counter piracy.
