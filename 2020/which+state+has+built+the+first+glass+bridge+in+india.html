Which state has built the First Glass Bridge in India?

(A) ** Bihar

(B) Sikkim

(C) West Bengal

(D) Gujarat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Notes: The country’s first glass floor suspension bridge is to be built across the river Ganga in Rishikesh, Uttarakhand. The state government has recently approved the design of the glass-floor bridge.

Concept note-2: -The glass bridge, India’s first, is being built amid picturesque five hills in Rajgir, Bihar, designed to enhance the number of domestic and international tourists to the state.

Concept note-3: -Chikhaldara Skywalk project | World’s longest skywalk with glass top to come up in India Melghat tiger reserve.

Concept note-4: -The Glass Skywalk is India’s first ever skywalk, set in the picturesque town of Pelling in Northeast Sikkim. Built at an astounding height of 7200 feet, this colossal structure allows tourists to look down several thousand feet in the air, from the peak to the very bottom of mountains.

Concept note-5: -Now, the second skywalk has been inaugurated in Bihar at par with China and it’s honestly something you mustn’t miss. The glass skywalk is present at Rajgir in Nalanda district of Bihar.
