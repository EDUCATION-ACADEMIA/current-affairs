Which bank said that a long moratorium exceeding six months can impact the credit behaviour of borrowers and increase the risks of delinquencies post resumption of scheduled payments?

(A) SEBI

(B) SIDBI

(C) SBI

(D) ** RBI

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As per the RBI website, some of the banks that are currently under moratorium are Garha Co-operative Bank Ltd., Guna, Madhya Pradesh, Deccan Urban Co-operative Bank Limited, Vijayapura, Karnataka, Independence Co-operative Bank Ltd, Nashik, Maharashtra etc.

Concept note-2: -One of the most important benefits of a loan moratorium is that it doesn’t impact your credit score negatively. Simply put, the non-payment of loans through regular instalments does not impact a borrower’s credit score in an adverse manner.

Concept note-3: -1) The government will now have to compensate banks for not charging an interest on interest on all loans during the moratorium period. As per rating agency ICRA, the interest on interest for a period of six months is expected to be ₹13, 500-14, 000 crore.

Concept note-4: -Moratorium just deferments your credit card dues, it isn’t a waiver for your repayment obligation. Interest will keep accruing, and you will have to pay the entire outstanding amount (inclusive of the interest charges) on the due date immediately after the moratorium period completes.
