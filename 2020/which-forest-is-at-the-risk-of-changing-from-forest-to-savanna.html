Which forest is at the risk of changing from Forest to Savanna?

(A) Black Forest

(B) ** Amazon Forest

(C) Baikunthapur Forest

(D) Otzarreta Forest

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A team of Europe based scientists have recently found that the tropical forests especially the Amazon forest are slowly changing into Savannah like environment.

Concept note-2: -The Amazon Rainforest Is at Risk of Becoming Savanna.

Concept note-3: -For decades, deforestation, drought, fires, and climate change have ravaged the Amazon, worrying scientists that the sprawling South American rainforest could eventually hit a critical tipping point and irreversibly transition into a drier, savanna-like ecosystem.

Concept note-4: -Despite large uncertainty in most regions we find that some forests are consistently at higher risk, including southern boreal forests and those in western North America and parts of the Amazon.

Concept note-5: -Savannas can result from either climate, soil conditions, animal behavior, or agricultural practices, which limit the occurrence of trees. Humans create savannas by burning the grasslands and felling the trees in order to plant crops.
