Who is to become the Goodwill Ambassador of the Indian contingent at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics?

(A) ** Sourav Ganguly

(B) Sachin Tendulkar

(C) Sunil Gavaskar

(D) Virat Kohli

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NEW DELHI: The Indian Olympic Association has invited BCCI President Sourav Ganguly to become the Goodwill Ambassador of the Indian contingent at the Tokyo Olympics this year.

Concept note-2: -In a letter to Ganguly, a former India cricket team captain, IOA secretary general Rajeev Mehta said, “The IOA extends the honour to you to be the Goodwill Ambassador of Team India to the Tokyo Olympic Games 2020. We hope you will generously extend your support for Team India.”

Concept note-3: -IOA invited Sourav Ganguly to become the Goodwill Ambassador of the Indian contingent at the Tokyo Olympics.

Concept note-4: -By India Today Web Desk: Boxing great MC Mary Kom and Indian men’s hockey team captain Manpreet Singh led the Indian contingent out as they marched through Tokyo’s National Stadium during the opening ceremony of the 2020 Olympics.

Concept note-5: -The nation sent its largest-ever contingent of 126 competitors to the 2020 Games. To date, the 2020 Summer Olympics are the most successful Games for India since its first regular Olympics appearance in 1920, with Indian Olympians winning 7 medals (1 gold, 2 silver and 4 bronze).
