Who sworn in as Afghanistan President for 2nd term?

(A) Abdullah Abdullah

(B) Hamid Karzai

(C) ** Ashraf Ghani

(D) Abdul Rashid Dostum

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -After his second term ended in 2014, Ashraf Ghani was elected as the next Afghan president.

Concept note-2: -Mohammad Ashraf Ghani Ahmadzai (born 19 May 1949) is an Afghan politician, academic, and economist who served as the president of Afghanistan from September 2014 until August 2021, when his government was overthrown by the Taliban.

Concept note-3: -The current supreme leader is Hibatullah Akhundzada, who assumed office in exile on 25 May 2016, upon being chosen by the Leadership Council, and came to power on 15 August 2021 with the Taliban’s victory over Western-backed forces in the 2001–2021 war.

Concept note-4: -President Hamid Karzai became the first ever democratically elected head of state of Afghanistan on 7 December 2004. His successor, Ashraf Ghani, was in power from 29 September 2014 to 15 August 2021, when he fled the country as Kabul fell to the Taliban following its 2021 offensive.
