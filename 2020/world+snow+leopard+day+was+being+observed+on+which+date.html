World Snow Leopard Day was being observed on which date?

(A) 21 October

(B) 22 October

(C) ** 23 October

(D) 24 October

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Every year, October 23 is celebrated globally as International Snow Leopard Day with the aim to raise awareness about conservation and preservation of these splendid animals. The day was chosen to celebrate the anniversary of the first Global Snow Leopard Forum held in Bishek, Kyrgyzstan in 2013.

Concept note-2: -Snow Leopard Day - March 5, 2022: Sweat in the name of wildlife conservation.

Concept note-3: -Every year on the 3rd May is the celebration of International Leopard Day. In an ongoing attempt to increase awareness of the importance of this species, this is a day that celebrates leopards and attempts to increase the global awareness of their status as well as the threats that leopard populations are faced with.

Concept note-4: -Are snow leopards endangered? The snow leopard is categorised as ‘Vulnerable’ on the International Union for the Conservation of Nature (IUCN) Red List of Threatened Species.

Concept note-5: -Snow leopards also have many names in the east where they live. In Nepal, snow leopards are called Heung chituwa; in Tibet they are Sah or Shen; in Russia and Mongolia they are Irbis, and in Urdu, one of the languages of central Asia, they are Barfani chita.
