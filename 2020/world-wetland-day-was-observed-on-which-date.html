World Wetland Day was observed on which date?

(A) February 4

(B) ** February 2

(C) February 3

(D) February 1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Wetlands Day is recognised as a United Nations International Day of Importance, celebrated around the world each year on 2 February. It marks the anniversary of the signing of the Convention on Wetlands of International Importance (Ramsar Convention) in Ramsar, Iran, on 2 February 1971.

Concept note-2: -World Wetlands Day Find more information about the yearly theme and activities below: 2023 | It’s time for wetland restoration. 2022 | Wetlands Action for People and Nature. 2021 | Wetlands and Water.

Concept note-3: -The World Wetlands Day is observed on 2nd February every year all over the world to commemorate the signing of Ramsar Convention on Wetlands of International Importance in 1971.

Concept note-4: -World Wetlands Day is annually held on February 2 to celebrate how wetlands, which are lands saturated by water, help maintain biodiversity on Earth.
