As per S & P Global Ratings, what is the GDP growth projection of India in fiscal 2021-22?

(A) ** 10%

(B) 11%

(C) 12%

(D) 9%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India’s output will expand 7 per cent in fiscal year 2022-2023 and 6 per cent in next fiscal year, ” S&P Global Ratings Asia-Pacific chief economist Louis Kuijs said. The Indian economy grew 8.5 per cent in 2021.

Concept note-2: -S&P had in September projected the Indian economy to grow 7.3% in 2022-23 and 6.5% in next fiscal year (2023-24).

Concept note-3: -S&P Lowers India GDP Growth Forecast by 30 bps to 7% For 2022-23. S&P Global Ratings cut India’s economic growth forecast for the current fiscal year ending March to 7 per cent as against 7.3 per cent projected earlier.

Concept note-4: -Real GDP growth is estimated at 7.0 per cent in 2022-23, according to the first advance estimate of the National Statistical Office (NSO).

Concept note-5: -India GDP Growth Slows to 6.3% in Q3 The Indian economy expanded 6.3% yoy in Q3 2022, slightly higher than forecasts of 6.2%, but well below a 13.5% growth in Q2, as distortions caused by COVID lockdowns faded, high prices and rising interest rates weighed on demand and slowing global demand started to impact exports.
