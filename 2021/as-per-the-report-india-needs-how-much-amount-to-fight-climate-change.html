As per the report, India needs how much amount to fight climate change?

(A) 150 billion

(B) 250 billion

(C) ** 400 billion

(D) 350 billion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under the Paris Agreement 2015, India said it needs at least $2.5trn for climate actions till 2030.

Concept note-2: -The allocation to the Climate Change Action Plan under the MoEFCC reduced from Rs 40 crore in 2020-21 to Rs 30 crore in 2021-22.

Concept note-3: -The report, commissioned by Britain and Egypt as the past and current hosts of the UN summit, said that developing countries alone need a combined $1trn a year in external funding to meet the goals set out in their Nationally Determined Contributions, or NDCs (the climate action plan set out in the Paris Agreement).

Concept note-4: -The Government of India launched National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC) on 30thJune, 2008 outlining eight National Missions on climate change. These include: National Solar Mission. National Mission for Enhanced Energy Efficiency.

Concept note-5: -The overall climate finance requirement by 2050 to achieve net zero emissions by 2070 will be in the order of tens of billions of dollars and around ₹85.6 trillion by 2030 for India’s adaptation needs, the document that runs into over 100 pages said.
