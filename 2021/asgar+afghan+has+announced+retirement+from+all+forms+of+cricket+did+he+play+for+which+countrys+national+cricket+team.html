Asgar Afghan has announced retirement from all forms of cricket. Did he play for which country’s national cricket team?

(A) Pakistan

(B) Bangladesh

(C) ** Afghanistan

(D) England

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mohammad Asghar Afghan (Pashto: ), is a former Afghan cricketer and former captain of the Afghanistan national cricket team.

Concept note-2: -Former Afghanistan captain Asghar Afghan has explained why he has decided to retire from international cricket during the ICC Men’s T20 World Cup 2021. Afghan played his final game for his country on Sunday in Afghanistan’s 62-run win over Namibia.

Concept note-3: -A teary-eyed Asghar Afghan said his decision to retire in the middle of the ongoing T20 World Cup was triggered by the pain that he and his teammates endured after Afghanistan’s heart-breaking loss to Pakistan in their previous match.

Concept note-4: -The Afghanistan national cricket team earned official One Day International status in April 2009. The U19 team earned a spot in the ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup 2012.

Concept note-5: -They continued to play cricket on their return to their home country in late 2001. Like all sports, cricket was originally banned by the Taliban, but it became an exception in 2000 and the Afghanistan Cricket Federation was elected as an affiliate member of the ICC the following year.
