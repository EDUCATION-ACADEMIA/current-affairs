Based on the UN report, Cyclone Amphan 2020 has resulted in how much economic loss in India?

(A) $10 Billion

(B) ** $14 Billion

(C) $22 Billion

(D) $9 Billion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -“Cyclone Amphan, which made landfall on May 20 near the IndiaBangladesh border in the eastern Bay of Bengal, was the costliest tropical cyclone on record for the North Indian Ocean, with reported economic losses in India of approximately USD 14 billion, ” it said.

Concept note-2: -They often cause large-scale damage to property and infrastructure and disrupt economic activity. The impact of cyclones on the national economy can be significant. In recent years, the impact of cyclones has been felt particularly acutely by the agricultural sector.

Concept note-3: -Within India, the storm killed 98 people, and caused $13.8 billion (2020 USD).

Concept note-4: -Hot on ‘Tauktae’ trail Economic losses were assessed at around $3 billion, almost double those due to Tauktae. More than 1.2 million people living in low-lying areas have had to leave their homes. In neighbouring Odisha, more than 10, 000 villages were damaged.

Concept note-5: -Impact of Cyclones in India Torrential rainfall leads to unprecedented floods and damages to houses and buildings. Due to storm surge, seawater levels rise, and the coastal areas are exposed to floods. The rise in seawater level also erodes beaches and embankments.
