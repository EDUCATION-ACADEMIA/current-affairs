Cabinet approves how much FDI in PSU refiners to aid BPCL sale?

(A) ** 100%

(B) 50%

(C) 49%

(D) 23%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Union Cabinet on Thursday approved a proposal to allow 100 per cent foreign direct investment (FDI) in public sector refiners, expanding the scope for FDI in the privatisation of Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd (BPCL).

Concept note-2: -The Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) said in a Press Note that up to 100% FDI under the automatic route for petroleum refiners and in those without any disinvestment or dilution of domestic equity in the existing PSUs at 49%.

Concept note-3: -Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited BPCL, a Maharatna PSU, aims to efficiently meet the energy needs of our nation with a combined refining capacity of over 40 MMT through refineries in Mumbai, Kochi, Numaligarh and Bina, which are strategically located.

Concept note-4: -The FDI limit will be 49 per cent, via automatic route, for ‘petroleum refining by the PSUs without any disinvestment or dilution of domestic equity in the existing PSUs’.
