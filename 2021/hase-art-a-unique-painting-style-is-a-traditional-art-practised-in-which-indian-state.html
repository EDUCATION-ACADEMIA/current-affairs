Hase art, a unique painting style is a traditional art practised in which Indian state?

(A) West Bengal

(B) Kerala

(C) ** Karnataka

(D) Andhra Pradesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hase art is a unique painting style practised by the Devaru community (subcaste of Ediga) residing in Sagar, Soraba Taluks of Shivamogga district and Sirsi and Siddapur Taluks of Uttara Kannada in Karnataka.

Concept note-2: -Notes: Hase art is a unique painting style practised by the Devaru community residing in Shivamogga district and Uttara Kannada in Karnataka. The Hase Chittara painting depicts rural life, traditional farming, harvesting, among other aspects of daily lives.

Concept note-3: -Hase Chittara is a folk art form, which is alive even today in the villages in Sagar, Sirsi, Siddapur and Hosanagar belt of Malnad. The art form is mainly nurtured by the Divaru community. The Hase Chittara form of painting uses natural colours and is classified into three types based on the colours used.

Concept note-4: -Madhubani paintings are the most celebrated style of folk painting from India; it is a form of wall art that arises in the Mithila region of Bihar. This eye-catching art style never fails to amaze one by its beautiful illustrations on the exposed interior walls of the houses in Bihar.

Concept note-5: -Pattachitra or Patachitra, another traditional art form of India, is a common term for contemporary, cloth-based scroll painting based in the East Indian states of Odisha and West Bengal. The name Pattachitra is adapted from the Sanskrit language, patta, implying canvas, and Chitra, meaning image.
