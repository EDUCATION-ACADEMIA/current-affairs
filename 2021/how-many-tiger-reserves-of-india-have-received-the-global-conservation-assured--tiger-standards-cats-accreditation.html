How many Tiger Reserves of India have received the Global Conservation Assured | Tiger Standards (CA|TS) accreditation?

(A) 12

(B) 20

(C) 15

(D) ** 14

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a proud moment, 14 tiger reserves in India are awarded the Global Conservation Assured/Tiger Standards (CA|TS) status for impressive tiger conservation.

Concept note-2: -Until 2018, 50 protected areas have been designated tiger reserves. In 2022, 53rd tiger reserve in India was declared in Ranipur Wildlife Sanctuary, Uttar Pradesh, and the State’s fourth tiger reserve. India is home to 80 percent of tigers in the world.

Concept note-3: -Ranipur Wildlife Sanctuary will be the 54th tiger reserve in India.

Concept note-4: -Conservation Assured Tiger Standards (CA|TS) is a worldwide accreditation standard for the conservation of tigers. It comprises 7 pillars, which contain 17 elements that assure successful restoration of the tiger population within a protected natural spot.

Concept note-5: -Mudumalai and Anamalai Tiger Reserves, Tamil Nadu. Bandipur Tiger Reserve, Karnataka. Parambikulam Tiger Reserve, Kerala. Sunderbans Tiger Reserve, West Bengal.
