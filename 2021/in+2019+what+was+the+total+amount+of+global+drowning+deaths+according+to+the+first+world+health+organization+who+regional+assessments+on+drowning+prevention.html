In 2019, what was the total amount of global drowning deaths, according to the first World Health Organization (WHO) regional assessments on drowning prevention?

(A) 34%

(B) ** 61%

(C) 78%

(D) 37%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are an estimated 236, 000 annual drowning deaths worldwide.

Concept note-2: -Global Drowning Research and Prevention. Drowning is the third leading cause of unintentional injury death worldwide, claiming an estimated 236, 000 lives each year.

Concept note-3: -The first-ever World Drowning Prevention Day was commemorated in 2021 on July 25. Since last year, this day is observed annually across the globe.

Concept note-4: -Drowning deaths totaled 756 in 2019 among children aged 0–17 years. Rates shown are crude rates (deaths per 100, 000). Access data table for Figure 1 . SOURCE: National Center for Health Statistics, National Vital Statistics System, Mortality.

Concept note-5: -Every year in the United States there are an estimated: 4, 000* fatal unintentional drownings-that is an average of 11 drowning deaths per day.
