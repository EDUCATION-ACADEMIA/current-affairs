India’s first-ever emergency landing strip at National Highway for the Indian Air force Planes has been launched in which state?

(A) Uttar Pradesh

(B) ** Rajasthan

(C) Andhra Pradesh

(D) Gujarat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Defence minister Rajnath Singh and Gadkari last year inaugurated the country’s first emergency landing strip on a national highway at Gandhav-Bhakasar section on NH-925 in Barmer, Rajasthan.

Concept note-2: -Ans : The first emergency landing facility in India is located on NH 925, which is in Barmer of the state of Rajasthan. It is a 3 kilometres section constructed by the national highway authority of India for the aircraft of the Indian air force.

Concept note-3: -The National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) has developed a 3-km section of Satta-Gandhav stretch of NH-925A, as an emergency landing facility (ELF) for the IAF.

Concept note-4: -IAF identifies 28 emergency landing strips on highways.

Concept note-5: -The country’s first emergency airstrip being built on the border of Barmer-Jalore district on the Indo-Pak border will be inaugurated on Thursday. As a rehearsal, three fighter planes were landed here on Wednesday.
