Jagannath Rath Yatra is observed in which state?

(A) Uttar Pradesh

(B) Kerala

(C) ** Odisha

(D) West Bengal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ratha Yatra (Read as: Ratha Jatra) (Odia: ) (/ˈrʌə ˈjɑːtrɑː/, Odia pronunciation: [ɾɔt̪ʰɔ dʒat̪ɾa]; lit. ‘Car Festival or Chariot Festival’) is a Hindu festival associated with Lord Jagannath held at Shri Khetra Puri Dham in the state of Odisha, India.

Concept note-2: -The Ratha Yatra, also known as the Chariot Celebration of Lord Jagannath, Devi Subhadra, and Lord Balabhadra, is the most prominent Hindu festival in the Puri city of Odisha. The holy city of Odisha’s Puri is all set to celebrate the famous Rath Yatra festival of Lord Jagannath today.

Concept note-3: -People come from across the world to participate in Jagannath Puri Rath Yatra. This festival is observed on the Shukla Paksha Dwitiya Tithi in the month of Aashadha. Jagannath Rath Yatra was celebrated in Odisha’ Puri on Friday, 1st July 2022.

Concept note-4: -The Ratha Yatra, also known as the Chariot Celebration of Lord Jagannath, Devi Subhadra, and Lord Balabhadra, is the most prominent Hindu festival in the Puri city of Odisha. This festival takes place every year on the second day of the Shukla Paksha in the months of June or July.

Concept note-5: -The Jagannath Rath Yatra is one of the important festivals of Hinduism. Every year, a majestic procession carrying the statues of Lord Krishna, Balaram, and Subhadra is pulled by the devotees to reach the Jagannath temple.
