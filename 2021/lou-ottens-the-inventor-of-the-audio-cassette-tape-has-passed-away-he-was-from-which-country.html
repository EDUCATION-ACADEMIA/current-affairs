Lou Ottens, the inventor of the audio cassette tape has passed away. He was from which country?

(A) Finland

(B) Ireland

(C) ** Netherlands

(D) Sweden

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Lodewijk Frederik Ottens (21 June 1926 – 6 March 2021) was a Dutch engineer and inventor, best known as the inventor of the cassette tape, and for his work in helping to develop the compact disc.

Concept note-2: -The Cassette Tape, or Compact Cassette, was first developed by the Philips company in 1962 in Belgium. Philips released the invention to Europe at the Berlin Radio Show on August 30, 1963; the invention was released in the United States in November of next year.

Concept note-3: -Magnetic tape recording as we know it today was developed in Germany during the 1930s at BASF (then part of the chemical giant IG Farben) and AEG in cooperation with the state radio RRG. This was based on Fritz Pfleumer’s 1928 invention of paper tape with oxide powder lacquered onto it.
