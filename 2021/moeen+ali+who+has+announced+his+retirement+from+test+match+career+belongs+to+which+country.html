Moeen Ali, who has announced his retirement from Test match career, belongs to which country?

(A) India

(B) Pakistan

(C) UAE

(D) ** England

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Moeen Ali will remain in Test retirement after turning down another offer from England men’s head coach Brendon McCullum to reverse his decision. The allrounder stepped away from the longest format in September 2021 but admitted in the summer he would “love to play” under the Ben Stokes-McCullum axis.

Concept note-2: -“I want to enjoy my cricket and it wouldn’t be fair to reverse my decision and then struggle to give it my all. It’s time to close the door on that side of my career. To play 64 tests for England has been a privilege and a dream fulfilled”, he added.

Concept note-3: -England all-rounder Moeen Ali retired from Test cricket in September last year, but recently in June he hinted at a possible return to the red-ball format.

Concept note-4: -Moeen Munir Ali is an English cricketer who has been a part of England’s national Men’s cricket team since 2014. He was born in Birmingham on 18 June 1987. Ali was part of England’s 2019 World Cup squad which lifted the title for the first time.
