NASA’s Curiosity rover sent a stunning video of Earth-like clouds passing over which planet?

(A) Jupiter

(B) Mercury

(C) Venus

(D) ** Mars

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Since landing on Mars in August 2012, NASA’s Curiosity rover has been exploring 3-mile-high Mt. Sharp in Gale Crater. A recap of the rover’s decade on Mars includes some of its most exciting science, as we mark the 10th anniversary of its Aug.

Concept note-2: -The Curiosity rover found that ancient Mars had the right chemistry to support living microbes. Curiosity found sulfur, nitrogen, oxygen, phosphorus and carbon– key ingredients necessary for life–in the powder sample drilled from the “Sheepbed” mudstone in Yellowknife Bay.

Concept note-3: -Mars Science Laboratory arrived at Mars through technological innovations that tested a completely new landing method. The spacecraft descended on a parachute, then during the final seconds before landing, the landing system fired rockets to allow it to hover while a tether lowered Curiosity to the surface.

Concept note-4: -First among the mission’s scientific goals was to search for and characterize a wide range of rocks and soils for clues to past water activity on Mars. The rovers were targeted to sites on opposite sides of Mars that looked like they were affected by liquid water in the past.
