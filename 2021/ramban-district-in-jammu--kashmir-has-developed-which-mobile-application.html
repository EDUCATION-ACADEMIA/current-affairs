Ramban district in Jammu & Kashmir has developed which mobile application?

(A) Mahakavacha

(B) Chatboat

(C) Healing Bridge

(D) ** e-Suvidha

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In Jammu and Kashmir, the District Administration, Ramban has developed a mobile application named e-Suvidha.

Concept note-2: -This place is famous for the sulphur spring whose water is believed to have miraculous healing powers.

Concept note-3: -Ramban district is 1, 156 metres (3, 792 feet) above sea level (on average). The boundary lines of Ramban district encompass hill station Patnitop as its southernmost point, Assar on its eastern edge, Gool to the west, and Banihal to the north. The terrain of district Ramban is tough and hilly.

Concept note-4: -Before the formation of the J&K State in 1846, there was a small village consisting of 15 houses on the right bank of Chenab river known as Nashband (later ‘Ramban’).

Concept note-5: -Ramban is a town in Ramban district of Jammu and Kashmir, India, which is the district headquarters of Ramban district.
