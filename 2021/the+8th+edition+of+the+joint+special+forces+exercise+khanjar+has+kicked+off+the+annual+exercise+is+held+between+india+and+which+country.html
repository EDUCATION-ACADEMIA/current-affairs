The 8th edition of the Joint Special Forces Exercise ‘Khanjar’ has kicked off. The annual exercise is held between India and which country?

(A) Bahrain

(B) Azerbaijan

(C) Afghanistan

(D) ** Kyrgyzstan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The 8th Indo-Kyrgyz Joint Special Forces Exercise ‘Khanjar’ was launched at the Special Forces Brigade of the Kyrgyz Republic’s National Guards in Bishkek, the host country’s capital.

Concept note-2: -The eighth edition of special forces exercise, ‘Khanjar’ was inaugurated with a focus on counter-terror drills. Initiated in 2011, this is the eighth edition of special forces exercise. This year, the focus is on high-altitude warfare. In 2015, India and Kyrgyzstan signed an agreement on bolstering defence cooperation.

Concept note-3: -Khanjar (English: dagger) is the annual bilateral training exercise of the Indian Army and the Kyrgyz Armed Forces. It is held at a high altitude terrain in the city of Sarsenke, Naryn Province in Kyrgyzstan. The exercise, which was started in 2011, enhances military relations between India and Kyrgyzstan.

Concept note-4: -’Khanjar’ is joint military exercise between India and Kyrgyztan. Khanjar exercise includes insertion of troops by going stealthily from helicopters and destroying hiding places of terrorists. Till date there have been 5 joint military exercises between India and Kyrgyzstan.

Concept note-5: -Exercise “SURYA KIRAN” is conducted annually between India and Nepal with the aim to enhance interoperability in jungle warfare & counter terrorism operations in mountainous terrain and HADR under UN mandate.
