The International Chefs Day is observed annually on which date?

(A) ** October 20

(B) October 17

(C) October 18

(D) October 19

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Chefs Day is celebrated annually on October 20. The day was established in 2004 by the World Association of Cooks Societies (WACS) to recognize the contributions that chefs make to society. It is also a day for chefs to celebrate their profession and to promote culinary education and training.

Concept note-2: -International Chefs Day is observed every year on October 20. It was the late chef, Dr. Bill Gallagher who introduced this day in 2004. To honour the contributions of cooks all throughout the world, the day is observed.

Concept note-3: -International Chefs Day was initiated in 2004. It was created by the late Dr. Billy Gallagher, a renowned chef and businessman, who was the President of the South African Chefs Association for 21 years. Dr Gallagher also became the President of the World Association of Chefs Societies (WACS) in 1996.

Concept note-4: -Visit a fine restaurant, get gourmet in your own kitchen, thank a chef in your life and encourage others to get crafty with food on International Chef’s Day. Going out to restaurants one of people’s favorite activities because they get the chance to taste the best cuisine from the chefs that work behind the scenes.

Concept note-5: -1. Alain Ducasse. Alain Ducasse is a well-known French chef who is considered among the best chefs in the world with 21 Michelin stars. He started working as chef in 1980 and made his way through the top rated chefs earning 21 Michelin stars in 2012, second highest after Joel Robuchon who had 31 stars.
