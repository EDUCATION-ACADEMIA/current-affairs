The International Day for the Total Elimination of nuclear weapons is held every year on which day?

(A) 23 September

(B) 24 September

(C) ** 26 September

(D) 25 September

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The United Nations observes 26 September every year as the International Day for the Total Elimination of Nuclear Weapons. The aim of the day is to enhance public awareness about the threat posed to humanity by nuclear weapons and the necessity for their total elimination.

Concept note-2: -The General Assembly, through its resolution 68/32, declared that 26 September will be the International Day for the Total Elimination of Nuclear Weapons.

Concept note-3: -However, the treaty has been extended to 2026. As of 2022, nine countries have nuclear weapons and a combined stockpile of 12, 700 warheads. Russia and the United States control over 90% of them.
