The International Everest Day is marked on which day?

(A) ** 29 May

(B) 28 May

(C) 27 May

(D) 26 May

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Everest Day is celebrated every year on May 29 in memory of the first summit of Mount Everest on this day by New Zealand’s Sir Edmund Hillary and Nepal’s Tenzing Norgay Sherpa in 1953. They battled snow storms and extreme weather conditions to reach the summit of the world’s highest mountain.

Concept note-2: -Tenzing Norgay was the first person to reach at the highest point of the earth and hoist the Indian National Flag on Mt. Everest on 29 May 1953.

Concept note-3: -International Day of United Nations Peacekeepers. Jerusalem Day - May 29, 2022. Learn About Composting Day.

Concept note-4: -How tall is Mount Everest? At 29, 031.69 feet (8, 848 meters), often rounded to 29, 032, feet high Mount Everest stands as the tallest peak above sea level.
