The first-ever automated, driverless train in the world has been unveiled in which country?

(A) Japan

(B) ** Germany

(C) Finland

(D) Switzerland

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -UPSC Mains. Q. Which country has launched the world’s first automated, driverless train? Notes: German rail operator Deutsche Bahn and industrial group Siemens unveiled the world’s first automated, driverless train in the city of Hamburg.

Concept note-2: -German rail operator Deutsche Bahn and industrial group Siemens on Monday unveiled the world’s first automated, driverless train in the city of Hamburg, billing it as more punctual and energy efficient than traditional trains.

Concept note-3: -This project is being developed by ‘Siemens and Deutsche Bahn’. It is being dubbed as “world first”. The project is part of a 60-million-euro modernisation of rapid urban rail system of Hamburg.

Concept note-4: -The world’s first fully automated driverless railway opened in Japan’s Kobe in 1981.

Concept note-5: -Cities like Paris, Tokyo, Barcelona and Dubai have already embraced driverless trains.
