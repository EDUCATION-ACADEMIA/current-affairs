The Google meet has put how much time limit on group video calls with 3 or more participants?

(A) 40 minutes

(B) ** 60 minutes

(C) 50 minutes

(D) 80 minutes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Anyone with a Google Account can create a video meeting, invite up to 100 participants, and meet for up to 60 minutes per meeting at no cost.

Concept note-2: -On a computer, you can host: One-on-one calls for up to 24 hours. Calls with 3 or more participants for up to 60 minutes.

Concept note-3: -You can use the Google Meet suite of features and capabilities in the new Meet app on your Android or iOS device or on the web. You are still able to make unlimited video calls on your phone with no time limits.

Concept note-4: -After 60 minutes, Google Meet will disconnect the call automatically. One-on-one video calls can go up to 24 hours. However, the host can upgrade their Google account or subscribe to the paid version of Google Meet to continue the call.

Concept note-5: -Now it seems that in order to retain a sustainable profit model, Google has returned the 1-hour limit to video calls on free user accounts. That’s longer than Zoom’s 40 minute cutoff, and the limit only applies to calls with three or more participants – so those 1-on-1s can still last up to 24 hours.
