The International Day of Reflection on the 1994 Rwanda Genocide is observed on which day?

(A) 5 April

(B) 6 April

(C) ** 7 April

(D) 8 April

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Theme: “Remember-Unite-Renew”. When: Thursday, 7th April 2022, starting from 11:00 a.m.

Concept note-2: -On 7 April, UNESCO commemorates the International Day of Reflection on the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda, which was established by the United Nations General Assembly in 2003. The date marks the beginning of the genocide perpetrated against members of the Tutsi minority by the Hutu extremist-led government.

Concept note-3: -July 4 is Kwibohora, also known as Rwanda Liberation Day. On this day in 1994 the Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) secured the capital of Kigali and ended the 1994 Genocide Against the Tutsi in Rwanda.

Concept note-4: -During the 1994 Rwandan genocide, also known as the genocide against the Tutsi, members of the Hutu ethnic majority in the east-central African nation of Rwanda murdered as many as 800, 000 people, mostly of the Tutsi minority.
