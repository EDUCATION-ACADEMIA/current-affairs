The Jallianwala Bagh Massacre incident is commemorated on which day annually?

(A) 14 April

(B) 11 April

(C) 12 April

(D) ** 13 April

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Jallianwala Bagh massacre, also known as the Amritsar massacre, took place on 13 April 1919. A large peaceful crowd had gathered at the Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar, Punjab, British India, to protest against the Rowlatt Act and arrest of pro-independence activists Saifuddin Kitchlew and Satyapal.

Concept note-2: -A short distance away from the Golden Temple complex is a spacious park, Jallianwalla Bagh, where on April 13, 1919, British colonial government troops fired on a crowd of unarmed Indian protesters, killing 379 of them and wounding many more.

Concept note-3: -It took place on 13 April 1919. The then Acting Brigadier-General Reginald Dyer ordered troops of the British Indian Army to fire their rifles into a crowd of unarmed Indian civilians who got together in Jallianwala Bagh, Amritsar, Punjab, killing at least 379 people and injuring over 1, 200 other people.

Concept note-4: -The 103rd anniversary of the Jallianwala Bagh massacre was observed on 13 April 2022. Hundreds of people protesting peacefully against the Rowlatt Acts, which granted the colonial administration repressive powers, were gunned down by British forces without any provocation, in 1919.

Concept note-5: -On 13 April 1919, thousands of people were gathered at Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar, Punjab.
