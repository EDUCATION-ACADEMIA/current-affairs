The National Security Guard (NSG) force of India marks which edition of the Raising Day 2021?

(A) 30

(B) ** 37

(C) 19

(D) 28

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The National Security Guard (NSG) force, popularly known as Black Cats, observes its Raising Day on October 16, every year. The year 2021 marks the 37th anniversary of the establishment of the NSG.

Concept note-2: -The National Security Guard (NSG), commonly known as Black Cats, is a counter-terrorism unit of India under the Ministry of Home Affairs. It was founded on 16 October 1984, following Operation Blue Star, for combating terrorist activities and protect states against internal disturbances.

Concept note-3: -“Nurture young minds - develop a safety culture” was the National Security Day 2022 theme.

Concept note-4: -Shri M A Ganapathy, 1986 batch Indian Police Service officer of the Uttarakhand cadre took over as the Director General, National Security Guard on 18th March, 2021.

Concept note-5: -The motto of NSG is ‘Sarvatra Sarvottama Suraksha’ (omnipresent omnipotent defense). NSG has 7, 350 active personnel and its annual budget is 816.10 crore. NSG headquarter is in New Delhi.
