The Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty is an agreement between which of the following two countries?

(A) Germany-Russia

(B) Russia-China

(C) India-Russia

(D) ** USA-Russia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Strategic Arms Reduction Talks (START), arms control negotiations between the United States and the Soviet Union (and, later, Russia) that were aimed at reducing those two countries’ arsenals of nuclear warheads and of the missiles and bombers capable of delivering such weapons.

Concept note-2: -The Strategic Arms Limitation Talks (SALT) were a series of bilateral conferences and international treaties signed between the United States and the Soviet Union.

Concept note-3: -The Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (START) I was signed July 31, 1991, by the United States and the Soviet Union. This was the first treaty that required U.S. and Soviet/Russian reductions of strategic nuclear weapons.

Concept note-4: -Bush and Russian President Vladimir Putin signed the Strategic Offensive Reductions Treaty (SORT), also known as the Moscow Treaty, on May 24, 2002. The treaty committed the United States and Russia to reducing their deployed strategic nuclear forces to 1, 700-2, 200 warheads apiece.

Concept note-5: -U.S. President George H. W. Bush and Russian President Boris Yeltsin signed the Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty II (START II) on January 3, 1993. START II established a limit on strategic weapons and required that reductions be implemented in two phases.
