The World Sickle Cell Day is observed every on which day?

(A) 16 June

(B) 18 June

(C) 17 June

(D) ** 19 June

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Sickle Cell Day is celebrated every year on June 19. The day aims to increase awareness of sickle cell disorders at the national and international level. The United Nations General Assembly adopted a resolution recognising sickle cell disease as a public health problem on December 22, 2008.

Concept note-2: -Each year, on June 19, countries all over the world celebrate World Sickle Cell Day, which was initially presented as a resolution by the United Nations in 2008. The goal of this day is to recognize sickle cell anemia is a public health problem and promote education about this genetic condition.

Concept note-3: -Since 2008, World Sickle Cell Day has been held annually, in order to help increase public knowledge and raise awareness of Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) and the struggles sufferers and their families go through.

Concept note-4: -Each year on the 19th of June, the World Sickle Cell Day event is conducted by the United Nations General Assembly to raise awareness of sickle cell disease at a national and international level.
