Under RBI’s new directives, what is the minimum initial capital required to set up a new universal bank?

(A) Rs 500 crore

(B) Rs 700 crore

(C) ** Rs 1000 crore

(D) Rs 800 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -(i) For Universal Banks: The initial paid-up voting equity share capital/ net worth required to set up a new universal bank, may be increased to ₹1000 crore (from present ₹500 crore).

Concept note-2: -The minimum initial capital requirement for licensing new banks should be enhanced from ₹500 crore to ₹1000 crore for universal banks, and from ₹200 crore to ₹300 crore for small finance banks.

Concept note-3: -At the time of establishment, the authorized capital of the Reserve Bank of India was ₹50 million. The government’s share in this was only ₹2-2.2 million.

Concept note-4: -200 Cr. The minimum capital for Small Finance is prescribed at Rs. 200 crore .
