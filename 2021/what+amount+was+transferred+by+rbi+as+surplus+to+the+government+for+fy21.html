What amount was transferred by RBI as surplus to the government for FY21?

(A) Rs 75, 991 crore

(B) Rs 101, 062 crore

(C) ** Rs 99, 122 crore

(D) Rs 89, 145 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) Board approved a sharply lower surplus transfer of ₹30, 307 crore to the government for the accounting year 2021-22, the lowest in 10 years. This is in sharp contrast to the surplus transfer of ₹99, 122 crore for nine months ended March 31, 2021 (July 2020-March 2021).

Concept note-2: -The act mandates that profits made by the central bank from its operations be sent to the Centre. As the manager of its finances, every year the RBI also pays a dividend to the government to help with the finances from its surplus or profit.
