What is the India’s GDP growth rate that has been projected by the ICRA?

(A) ** 10.1 %

(B) 11.2 %

(C) 15 %

(D) 9 %

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -"We have revised down our forecast for real gross domestic product growth to 7 per cent for fiscal 2023 from 7.3 per cent, primarily because of the slowdown in global growth that has started to impact our exports and industrial activity.

Concept note-2: -Reacting to the NSO data, Aditi Nayar, Chief Economist, ICRA Ltd, said the GDP estimate of 7 per cent for 2022-23 factors in 4.5 per cent growth for the second half of the fiscal.

Concept note-3: -The growth in real GDP during 2022-23 is estimated at 7 per cent as compared to 8.7 per cent in 2021-22, it stated.

Concept note-4: -Real GDP growth is estimated at 7.0 per cent in 2022-23, according to the first advance estimate of the National Statistical Office (NSO).

Concept note-5: -Rating agency ICRA has maintained its forecast of 7.2% growth in India’s GDP for the financial year FY23.
