What is the enhanced maximum per transaction limit for online fund transfer under the Indo-Nepal Remittance Facility Scheme, as per RBI?

(A) Rs 5 lakh

(B) Rs 1 lakh

(C) ** Rs 2 lakh

(D) Rs 3 lakh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How much money can I remit under INRF? Ans: One can remit upto ₹2 lakhs per transaction to the beneficiary residing in Nepal; provided the sender maintains account with any NEFT enabled bank branch in India. Walk-in / Non-customer can remit upto ₹50, 000 per transaction to Nepal residing beneficiary. 3.

Concept note-2: -How much money can be transferred abroad annually? The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has a limit of $2, 50, 000 (INR 2, 04, 50, 250) per financial year for foreign remittances. This includes both personal and business-related transactions.

Concept note-3: -Remittance From India This platform is available to resident individuals under Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) of RBI subject to present limit of USD 2, 50, 000 per Financial Year and from NRE account of an NRI.

Concept note-4: -Frequently asked questions. How much money can I send to Nepal? You can send up to $5, 000 per transfer to family and friends in Nepal.
