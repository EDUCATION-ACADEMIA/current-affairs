What is the new maximum temperature that has been recorded in Antarctica?

(A) 13 degrees Celsius

(B) 17 degrees Celsius

(C) ** 18.3 degrees Celsius

(D) 15 degrees Celsius

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Published. GENEVA, 1 July 2021 (WMO) - The World Meteorological Organization (WMO) has recognized a new record high temperature for the Antarctic continent of 18.3° Celsius on 6 February 2020 at the Esperanza station (Argentina).

Concept note-2: -The recent extraordinary heatwave in Antarctica appears to have set a new World Record for the largest temperature excess above normal (+38.5 °C / +69.3 °F) ever measured at an established weather station. It “appears to have set a new World Record for the largest temperature excess above normal …

Concept note-3: -Location of Dome C and Vostok in Antarctica. On March 18th, 2022, a remote site on the Antarctic plateau known as “Dome C”, located at 3, 233 meters above sea level, recorded a daily high temperature of -10.1 °C (+13.8 °F).

Concept note-4: -Minus 12°C would be a frigid afternoon almost anywhere except Antarctica. But when the temperature hit -12.2°C on 18 March at Concordia research station, 1600 kilometres from the South Pole, it was the highest reading recorded at that location.

Concept note-5: -Not only did experts observe that Antarctica’s temperature reached more than 70 degrees Fahrenheit above average, but they also found that parts of the Arctic weighed in at around 50 degrees Fahrenheit (30 degrees Celsius) hotter than average, AP News reported.
