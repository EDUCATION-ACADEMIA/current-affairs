What is the status of “Bryde’s whale”, seen in the news?

(A) Near Threatened

(B) Least Concern

(C) ** Endangered

(D) Extinct

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Q. What is the status of “Bryde’s whale”, seen in the news recently? Notes: Gulf of Mexico Bryde’s whales have been classified as “endangered” under the Endangered Species Act. They are the only known resident baleen whales in the Gulf of Mexico, with less than 100 individuals left in the population.

Concept note-2: -Population estimates conducted in Auckland suggest the Bryde’s whales have a population of around 140 animals. For this reason they have Nationally Critical status in New Zealand. 9. Bryde’s whales are able to feed on three different types of food – fish, krill and plankton.

Concept note-3: -One of the World’s Most Endangered Whales Scientists believe there are fewer than 100 Gulf of Mexico Bryde’s whales, making them one of the most endangered whales in the world.

Concept note-4: -The population may include up to 90, 000–100, 000 animals worldwide, with two-thirds inhabiting the Northern Hemisphere.

Concept note-5: -The carcass of an endangered Bryde’s whale was found near the border of Puri and Ganjam districts of Odisha, recently.
