When is the 2021 World Antimicrobial Awareness Week (WAAW) being observed?

(A) November 15 to 21

(B) ** November 18 to 24

(C) November 17 to 23

(D) November 14 to 20

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Antimicrobial Awareness Week (WAAW) is celebrated from 18-24 November every year. The 2021 theme, Spread Awareness, Stop Resistance, called on One Health stakeholders, policymakers, health care providers, and the general public to be Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) Awareness champions.

Concept note-2: -18 to 24 November is World Antimicrobial Awareness Week A global action plan to tackle the growing problem of resistance to antibiotics and other antimicrobial medicines was endorsed at the Sixty-eighth World Health Assembly in May 2015.

Concept note-3: -World Antimicrobial Awareness Week | 18-24 November 2022. The theme of WAAW 2022 is “Preventing Antimicrobial Resistance together". AMR is a threat to humans, animals, plants and the environment. It affects us all.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is 16 November to 22 November 2015. The First World Antibiotic Awareness Week was observed from 16 November to 22 November 2015. It aims to increase awareness of antibiotic resistance.

Concept note-5: -The slogan of World Antimicrobial Awareness Week remains ‘Antimicrobials: Handle with Care’.
