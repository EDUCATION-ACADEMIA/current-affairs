When is World Tuna Day observed globally?

(A) ** May 2

(B) First Saturday of May

(C) May 1

(D) First Sunday of May

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the 2nd of May, World Tuna Day is observed. This day is celebrated to raise awareness about the Tuna fish.

Concept note-2: -World Tuna Day is observed every year on May 2. This day is established by the United Nations General Assembly to raise awareness about the importance of Tuna Day.

Concept note-3: -Tuna is rich in Omega-3 and it also contains minerals, proteins, and vitamin B12, among other advantages. That is why in December 2016, the United Nations General Assembly voted to officially observe World Tuna Day.

Concept note-4: -Using art, music, dance, writing, video, PNA invites you to share your vision of this year’s theme: “Our Tuna, Our Heritage” as the world gets ready to celebrate World Tuna Day, May 2, 2022.

Concept note-5: -The countries that produce the most in tuna fishing yield are Indonesia, Japan, and the Philippines. Thailand is now the site of the majority of the world’s tuna processing plants, processing approximately 25% of the world’s canned tuna.
