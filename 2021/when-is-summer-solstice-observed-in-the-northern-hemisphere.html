When is Summer Solstice observed in the Northern Hemisphere?

(A) June 20th

(B) ** June 21st

(C) June 22nd

(D) June 23rd

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -summer solstice, the two moments during the year when the path of the Sun in the sky is farthest north in the Northern Hemisphere (June 20 or 21) or farthest south in the Southern Hemisphere (December 21 or 22).

Concept note-2: -Summer officially begins in the Northern Hemisphere on June 21 with the summer solstice. Summer officially begins in the Northern Hemisphere on June 21 with the summer solstice, the day with the greatest amount of possible daylight and the shortest night.

Concept note-3: -The summer solstice occurs during summer. This is the June solstice (usually 20 or 21 June) in the Northern hemisphere and the December solstice (usually 21 or 22 December) in the Southern.

Concept note-4: -The summer solstice occurs at the moment the earth’s tilt toward the sun is at a maximum. Therefore, on the day of the summer solstice, the sun appears at its highest elevation with a noontime position that changes very little for several days before and after the summer solstice.

Concept note-5: -The Solstices (Summer & Winter) The summer solstice occurs at the moment the earth’s tilt toward/from the sun is at a maximum. Therefore, on the day of the summer solstice, the sun appears at its highest elevation with a noontime position that changes very little for several days before and after the summer solstice.
