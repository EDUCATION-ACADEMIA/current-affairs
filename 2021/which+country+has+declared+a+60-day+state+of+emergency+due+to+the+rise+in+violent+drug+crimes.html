Which country has declared a 60-day state of emergency due to the rise in violent drug crimes?

(A) Spain

(B) El Salvador

(C) Peru

(D) ** Ecuador

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ecuador president declares state of emergency over drug violence. QUITO: Ecuador’s President Guillermo Lasso on Monday declared a state of emergency in the country grappling with a surge in drug-related violence, and ordered the mobilization of police and military in the streets.

Concept note-2: -On 30 September a nationwide strike was called by all levels of police, including the military police. If you have plans to travel, you should contact your airline.

Concept note-3: -1, 2022. At least five Ecuadorean police officers were killed on Tuesday in explosive attacks in response to prisoner transfers from overcrowded and violent penitentiaries, prompting President Guillermo Lasso to declare a state of emergency in two provinces.

Concept note-4: -Crime in Ecuador The crime rate in Ecuador is relatively low compared to other countries in South America. However, petty crime is still a problem in some areas, particularly in the larger cities of Quito and Guayaquil. Pickpocketing and bag snatching are the most common crimes that travelers face.

Concept note-5: -Alert: Expanded State of Emergency Date: June 21, 2022 Location: Nationwide Event: The Government of Ecuador issued executive order 459 June 20, which expands the State of Emergency to six provinces: Chimborazo, Tungaruhua, Cotopaxi, Pichincha, Pastaza, and Imbabura provinces.
