Which country has topped the Inclusive Internet Index 2021?

(A) ** Sweden

(B) Australia

(C) Norway

(D) China

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2022, India ranked 50th for overall internet inclusivity. Among the sub-indices, the country performed relatively well in terms of affordability and readiness. Availability ranked lowest that year. Sweden ranked first in the overall ranking, with United States in second place.

Concept note-2: -Sweden ranks first in the Inclusive Internet Index 2021, followed by the United States and Spain. Majority of countries (77 out of 120 countries) showed an overall improvement in Internet Inclusion.

Concept note-3: -Singapore takes the top spot on the Index in 2022 with a performance level of 86.1%, followed by South Korea with a performance level of 84.4%.

Concept note-4: -India has not only improved its ranking, but also improved its score from 49.74 in 2021 to 51.19 in 2022.

Concept note-5: -The index seeks to measure the extent to which the internet is not only accessible and affordable, but also relevant to all, allowing usage that enables positive social and economic outcomes.
