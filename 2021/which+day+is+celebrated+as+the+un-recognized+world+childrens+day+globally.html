Which day is celebrated as the UN-recognized World Children’s Day globally?

(A) ** November 20

(B) November 19

(C) November 18

(D) November 17

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Children’s Day was first established in 1954 as Universal Children’s Day and is celebrated on 20 November each year to promote international togetherness, awareness among children worldwide, and improving children’s welfare.

Concept note-2: -World Children’s Day is celebrated on November 20 annually. The day aims to promote international togetherness and awareness among children as well as to improve their welfare. November 20 marks the anniversary of the UN General Assembly adopted a Declaration and Convention on children’s rights.

Concept note-3: -Children’s day is celebrated every year on the November 14th 2023. It was originally started as a day to promote literacy and raise money for charity. In 2023, the celebration will be themed “Celebrating Creativity – The Power of imaginations”.

Concept note-4: -November 20th is an important date as it is the date in 1959 when the UN General Assembly adopted the Declaration of the Rights of the Child. It is also the date in 1989 when the UN General Assembly adopted the Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Concept note-5: -Every year on November 20th, Universal Children’s Day is observed. This day is primarily intended to promote international cooperation, raise awareness among children worldwide, and improve children’s welfare. On November 20, 1954, Universal Children’s Day was established.
