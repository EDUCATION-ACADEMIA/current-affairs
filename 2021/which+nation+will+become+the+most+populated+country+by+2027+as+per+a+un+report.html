(A) Japan

(B) US

(C) ** India

(D) Pakistan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India is set to become the world’s most populous country by the end of the decade, overtaking China, according to the latest edition of the United Nations’ World Population Prospects.

Concept note-2: -Bloomberg is reporting that this is four years ahead of an earlier estimate forecasting India to surpass China by 2027. China, currently the world’s most populous nation, is expected to experience a sharp decline in its population as early as next year.

Concept note-4: -An earlier report projected India surpassing China by 2027. Lower mortality rates and demographic changes may ensure that central and southern Asia become the world’s most-populous region by 2037.

Concept note-5: -Like China, India also previously explored unsuccessful attempts to curb its population growth.
