Which one is the Indo-Russia joint military exercise?

(A) Surya Kiran

(B) ** INDRA

(C) Hand-in-Hand

(D) Rudra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The military exercise between Russia and India is known as Indira. The INDRA series of exercises began in 2003 and was conducted as a bilateral naval exercise alternately between the two countries.

Concept note-2: -"A joint cross-branch Russian-Indian exercise, INDRA 2022, is planned to be held on the territory of the Southern Military District. Units of ground forces of Russia and India will practice common training objectives at the Prudboi training ground in the Volgograd Region.

Concept note-3: -Russia and India are co-hosting a large-scale Army Exercise named INDRA – 2021. The military exercise INDRA is a joint Exercise between the Russian Army and the Indian Army. The Exercise involved about 10, 000 Soldiers and was a grand event for both the Countries.

Concept note-4: -INDRA NAVY is a joint, biennial military exercise conducted by India and Russia starting in 2003. The exercise is tasked with boosting cooperation and interoperability between the Russian and Indian navies. The word INDRA is a portmanteau of the participants’ respective countries.
