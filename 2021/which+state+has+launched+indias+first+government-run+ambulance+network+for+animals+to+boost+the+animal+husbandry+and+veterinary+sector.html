Which state has launched India’s first government-run ambulance network for animals to boost the animal husbandry and veterinary sector?

(A) ** Andhra Pradesh

(B) Tamil Nadu

(C) Rajasthan

(D) Maharashtra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Andhra Pradesh chief minister YS Jagan Mohan Reddy flagged off Phase 2 of India’s first government-run veterinary ambulance network by flagging off an additional 165 veterinary ambulance units from Tadepalli on Wednesday morning.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Chennai. The Blue Cross of India has launched a “first-of-its-kind” ambulance for street animals in Chennai in collaboration with the international animal welfare organization Four Paw.

Concept note-3: -The state government of the Andhra Pradesh has decided to set up “India’s first government-run ambulance network” for animals. This decision was taken in order to further boost the animal husbandry and veterinary sector in the state.

Concept note-4: -Chennai-based Blue Cross flag off country’s first mobile ambulance for street animals’ treatment.

Concept note-5: -India’s first ambulance for street animals has been launched in Chennai, Tamil Nadu. It has been started by the Blue Cross of India in collaboration with the international animal welfare organization “Four Paw”. Madras High Court Justice Dr Anitha Sumanth launched the ambulance.
