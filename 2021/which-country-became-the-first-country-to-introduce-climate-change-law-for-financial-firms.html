Which country became the first country to introduce climate change law for financial firms?

(A) Australia

(B) Switzerland

(C) Russia

(D) ** New Zealand

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -New Zealand has become the first country to pass laws requiring banks, insurers and investment managers to report the impacts of climate change on their business.

Concept note-2: -Greece passes first climate law.

Concept note-3: -The Montreal Convention (1987) The Montreal Protocol was the first global treaty that dealt with the environment and showed what was possible with global cooperation and proved something of a template for future global environmental treaties.

Concept note-4: -New Zealand becomes first country to force finance companies to act on climate risks. New Zealand has become the first country in the world to pass a law forcing financial institutions to disclose and, the government says, act on climate-related risks and opportunities.

Concept note-5: -The Climate Change Action Plan was announced on October 19, 1993. This plan aimed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions to 1990 levels by 2000. Clinton described this goal as “ambitious but achievable, ” and called for 44 action steps to achieve this goal.
