Which country had withdrawn from the Open Skies Treaty after US?

(A) Japan

(B) ** Russia

(C) India

(D) China

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On 22 November 2020, the United States withdrew from the treaty, and on 15 January 2021, Russia also announced its intention to leave, citing the U.S. withdrawal and the inability of member nations to guarantee that information gathered would not be shared with the U.S. Russia formally withdrew in December 2021.

Concept note-2: -The Soviet Union rejected the proposal because it considered overflights equal to espionage and believed the United States had more to gain than it did.

Concept note-3: -The United States and the European Union signed the EU-US Open Skies Agreement in 2007. This agreement, which took effect in March 2008, liberalized the airline industry in the North Atlantic.
