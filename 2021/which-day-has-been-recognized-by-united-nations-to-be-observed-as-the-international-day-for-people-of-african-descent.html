Which day has been recognized by United Nations to be observed as the International Day for People of African Descent?

(A) August 30

(B) ** August 31

(C) August 29

(D) August 28

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Day for People of African Descent – August 31, 2023. International Day for People of African Descent is observed on August 31 each year. This holiday was created by the United Nations to celebrate the diverse heritage and several contributions of people of African descent.

Concept note-2: -The year 2011 was proclaimed International Year for People of African Descent.

Concept note-3: -In December 2013, the UN General Assembly adopted resolution 68/237, by which it proclaimed 2015 to 2024 to be the International Decade for People of African Descent, with the theme “People of African descent: recognition, justice and development”.

Concept note-4: -Siddis, or Habshis, are a unique tribe that has African ancestry and lives in South Asia. They are mainly found in three Indian states-Gujarat, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh-and according to the latest census, their total population size is about 0.25 million.

Concept note-5: -“The African Diaspora are peoples of African descent and heritage living outside the continent, irrespective of their citizenship and who remain committed to contribute to the development of the continent and the building of the African Union.”
