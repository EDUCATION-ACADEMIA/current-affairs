Which is the Indian Navy’s hydrographic survey ship decommissioned after serving the nation for 40 years?

(A) Darshak

(B) Sarvekshak

(C) ** Sandhayak

(D) Jamuna

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -During her 40 years of illustrious service in the Indian Navy, INS Sandhayak undertook over 200 major hydrographic surveys in Western and Eastern coasts of the Indian peninsula, the Andaman Sea, and surveys in neighbouring countries including Sri Lanka, Myanmar and Bangladesh.

Concept note-2: -Owing to the Covid-19, Indian Navy’s oldest hydrographic survey ship INS Sandhayak was decommissioned on Friday in a low-key event at in Visakhapatnam after 40 years of service.

Concept note-3: -The INS Ajay was in active naval service for more than 32 years during her illustrious journey.

Concept note-4: -UPSC Mains. Q. Which Ship of the Indian Navy, has been decommissioned after 41 years into service? Notes: The Indian Navy’s first destroyer – INS Rajput has been decommissioned from the service after guarding the nation for a term of 41 years.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is INS Nishank. The Indian Navy will bid farewell to its two ships INS Nishank and INS Akshay, at Naval Dockyard in Mumbai on 3 June 2022. Both the ships will be decommissioned at Naval Dockyard after 32 years of being at the forefront.
