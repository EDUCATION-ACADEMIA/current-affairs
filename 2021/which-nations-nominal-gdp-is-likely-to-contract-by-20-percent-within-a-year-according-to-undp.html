Which nation’s nominal GDP is likely to contract by 20 percent within a year, according to UNDP?

(A) Pakistan

(B) ** Afghanistan

(C) North Korea

(D) Iran

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Explanation: Afghanistan’s nominal GDP is likely to contract by 20 percent from USD 20 billion in 2020 to USD 16 billion within just one year, as per United Nations Development Programme (UNDP).

Concept note-2: -U.S. gdp growth rate for 2021 was 5.67%, a 9.08% increase from 2020. U.S. gdp growth rate for 2020 was -3.40%, a 5.69% decline from 2019. U.S. gdp growth rate for 2019 was 2.29%, a 0.63% decline from 2018. U.S. gdp growth rate for 2018 was 2.92%, a 0.66% increase from 2017.

Concept note-3: -United States Nominal GDP reached 6, 533.1 USD bn in Dec 2022, compared with 6, 431.0 USD bn in the previous quarter. Nominal GDP in US is updated quarterly, available from Mar 1947 to Dec 2022, with an average number of 1, 047.3 USD bn.

Concept note-4: -"Nominal GDP for BE 2023-2024 has been projected at Rs 3, 01, 75, 065 crore (Rs 301.75 lakh crore) assuming 10.5 percent growth over the estimated Nominal GDP of Rs 2, 73, 07, 751 crore (Rs 273.08 lakh crore) as per the First Advance Estimates of FY 2022-23, ” the Budget documents said.
