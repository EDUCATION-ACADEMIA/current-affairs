Which state has declared encroachment of temple land as a cognizable and non-bailable offence?

(A) Karnataka

(B) ** Tamil Nadu

(C) Telangana

(D) Kerala

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tamil Nadu makes Temple land grabbing a non-bailable offence - Read Key Facts here for Current affairs exam! - GKToday.

Concept note-2: -Grabbing temple land is a cognizable offence now The Tamil Nadu Assembly on passed a Bill to make the encroachment of properties belonging to religious institutions a cognisable and non-bailable offence, amending the Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959.

Concept note-3: -When eviction proceedings are initiated under the Tamil Nadu Land Encroachment Act, 1905 (Tamil Nadu Act III of 1905), the encroachers invariably move the Civil Courts to thwart the eviction proceedings by obtaining injunction, etc. Timely retrieval of lands has become quite difficult.

Concept note-4: -The Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act XXII of 1959 controls 36, 425 temples, 56 mutts or religious orders (and 47 temples belonging to mutts), 1, 721 specific endowments and 189 trusts.

Concept note-5: -Tamil Nadu govt’s HR&CE dept has taken over 38, 000 Hindu temples, SC seeks response over Indic Collective’s plea.
