Which village of India has been awarded as one of the best Tourism Villages by the UNWTO?

(A) Kongthong, Meghalaya

(B) Lakhimpur, Uttar Pradesh

(C) Ladhpura Khas, Madhya Pradesh

(D) ** Pochampally, Telangana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pochampally is also known as Bhoodan Pochampally to commemorate the Bhoodan Movement that was launched by Acharya Vinobha Bhave from this village on 18th April, 1951. Vinoba Bhabe was the first international figure to receive the Ramon Magsaysay Award in 1958. He was awarded Bharat Ratna posthumously in 1983.

Concept note-2: -In the same line, for its crafts, reforms, and historical significance, the Pochampally village in Telangana has been awarded as one of the best tourism villages in the world by the United Nations World Tourism Organisation.

Concept note-3: -The Ministry of Tourism recommended three villages for the UNWTO Best Tourism Village entry from India. These were Kongthong in Meghalaya, Ladhpura Khas in Madhya Pradesh, and Pochampally in Telangana.

Concept note-4: -India’s Khonoma village, located in Nagaland, is a part of the ‘Upgrade Programme’, which will benefit those villages that “do not fully meet the criteria to receive recognition” The best tourism villages of the world in 2022 are…

Concept note-5: -Hyderabad: Chief Minister K Chandrashekhar Rao formally handed over the identity letter issued by United Nations World Tourism Organisation (UNWTO) recognising Bhoodan Pochampally village in Yadadri Bhongir district as one of the best tourism villages in the world.
