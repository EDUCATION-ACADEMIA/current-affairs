Who is the author of the book ‘Translating Myself and Others’?

(A) Arundhati Roy

(B) ** Jhumpa Lahiri

(C) Sudha Murthy

(D) Anita Desai

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Translating Myself and Others is a collection of candid and disarmingly personal essays by Pulitzer Prize–winning author Jhumpa Lahiri, who reflects on her emerging identity as a translator as well as a writer in two languages.

Concept note-3: -Interpreter of Maladies is a book collection of nine short stories by American author of Indian origin Jhumpa Lahiri published in 1999. It won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction and the Hemingway Foundation/PEN Award in the year 2000 and has sold over 15 million copies worldwide.

Concept note-4: -Pulitzer Prize-winning author Jhumpa Lahiri is celebrated for her depiction of immigrant and Indian-American life, yet her poignant stories also capture universal themes of longing, loneliness and barriers of communication.
