Who has become the 1st Indian Swimmer to qualify for Tokyo Olympics?

(A) ** Sajan Prakash

(B) Sandeep Sejwal

(C) Shamsher Khan

(D) Deepak Kumar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Sajan Prakash. Sajan Prakash has become the first Indian swimmer to qualify for the upcoming Tokyo Olympics. Sajan achieved the feat as he clocked 1 minute 56.38 seconds in the men’s 200m butterfly at the Sette Colli Trophy in Rome.

Concept note-2: -Sajan Prakash will always be remembered as the first Indian swimmer to win a direct qualification to the Olympics. At the 2021 Sette Colli trophy in Rome, Sajan Prakash clocked 1:56.38 seconds - a new national record - in the men’s 200m butterfly event.

Concept note-3: -The First Indian Female Swimmer in Olympics Maana Patel has qualified for the upcoming Tokyo Olympics as the first Indian female swimmer. On July 2, 2021, the Sports Authority of India (SAI) confirmed that she has qualified for the prestigious event through the universality quota.

Concept note-4: -India first participated at the Olympic Games in 1900, with a lone athlete Norman Pritchard winning two medals – both silver – in athletics and became the first Asian nation to win an Olympic medal.
