Who has won the EU’s top Human Rights award-Sakharov Prize for Freedom of Thought?

(A) Dmitry Muratov

(B) ** Alexei Navalny

(C) Ahmad Massoud

(D) Maria Ressa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Sakharov Prize - what it is about Awarded for the first time in 1988 to Nelson Mandela and Anatoli Marchenko, the Sakharov Prize for Freedom of Thought is the highest tribute paid by the European Union to human rights work.

Concept note-2: -The father of the Soviet hydrogen bomb, Andrei Sakharov, was awarded the Peace Prize in 1975 for his opposition to the abuse of power and his work for human rights. The leaders of the Soviet Union reacted with fury, and refused Sakharov permission to travel to Oslo to receive the Prize.

Concept note-3: -The Russian physicist Andrei Dmitrievich Sakharov (1921-1989), who won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1975, first came to prominence as the father of the Soviet hydrogen bomb. Concerned at the implications his work had for the future of humankind, he sought to raise awareness of the dangers of the nuclear arms race.
