Who has won the Women’s Prize for Fiction for the fantasy novel Piranesi?

(A) Sudha Murty

(B) J. K. Rowling

(C) Michelle Obama

(D) ** Susanna Clarke

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Join Susanna Clarke, winner of the 2021 Women’s prize for fiction, for a livestreamed discussion of her novel, Piranesi.

Concept note-2: -We are over the moon to reveal that Ruth Ozeki has won the 2022 Women’s Prize for Fiction with her fourth novel The Book of Form and Emptiness.

Concept note-3: -The story is told through the research notes of the eponymous narrator, who reconstructs the story of his own arrival as he explores this world. Piranesi won the 2021 Women’s Prize for Fiction.

Concept note-4: -The Baileys Women’s Prize for Fiction is awarded annually for the best full novel of the year written by a woman and published in the UK. Any woman writing in English - whatever her nationality, country of residence, age or subject matter - is eligible.

Concept note-5: -19.30, London, 7th June 2017: British author Naomi Alderman has won the 2017 Baileys Women’s Prize for Fiction with her fourth novel The Power (Viking).
