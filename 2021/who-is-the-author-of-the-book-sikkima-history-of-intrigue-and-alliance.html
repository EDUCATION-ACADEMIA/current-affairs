Who is the author of the book “Sikkim:A History of Intrigue and Alliance”?

(A) Triloki Nath Kaul

(B) Arundhati Ghose

(C) Mani Shankar Aiyar

(D) ** Preet Mohan Singh Malik

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Preet Mohan Singh Malik has been a member of the Indian Foreign Service since 1962. He was posted in Gangtok from October 1967 to April 1970 at the political office for India to Sikkim and Bhutan.

Concept note-2: -Sikkim: A History of Intrigue and Alliance by Preet Mohan Singh Malik.

Concept note-3: -On 15 May 1975 the President of India ratified a constitutional amendment that made Sikkim the 22nd state of India. The Kingdom of Sikkim was founded by the Namgyal dynasty in the 17th century. It was ruled by Buddhist priest-kings known as the Chogyal. It became a princely state of British India in 1890.

Concept note-4: -Before Sikkim became an Indian state in 1975, it was a small Buddhist kingdom. The kingdom was founded in the seventeenth century with the establishment of the Namgyal Dynasty as sovereign and its rulers continued to provide Sikkimese kings, called ‘Chogyal’, up until the twentieth century.

Concept note-5: -Independent monarchy A popular vote for Sikkim to join the Indian Union failed and Indian Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru agreed to a special protectorate status for Sikkim. Sikkim was to be a tributary of India, in which India controlled its external defence, diplomacy and communication.
