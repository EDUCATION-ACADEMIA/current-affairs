With which country India exchanged the list of nuclear installations?

(A) Russia

(B) Bangladesh

(C) Nepal

(D) ** Pakistan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -India and Pakistan today exchanged the list of nuclear installations and facilities, covered under the Agreement on the Prohibition of Attack against Nuclear Installations and Facilities between the two countries.

Concept note-2: -It said lists are exchanged annually on January 1. The practice has been in place since 1992. The neighbours have fought three wars and have had a number of military skirmishes in recent years.

Concept note-3: -The Non-nuclear aggression agreement is a bilateral and nuclear weapons control treaty between the two South Asian states, India and Pakistan, on the reduction (or limitation) of nuclear arms and pledged not to attack or assist foreign powers to attack on each’s nuclear installations and facilities.

Concept note-4: -Simla Agreement on Bilateral Relations between India and Pakistan signed by Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, and President of Pakistan, Z. A. Bhutto, in Simla on 2 July 1972.

Concept note-5: -Pokhran-II consisted of five detonations, the first of which was a fusion bomb while the remaining four were fission bombs. The tests were initiated on 11 May 1998, under the assigned code name Operation Shakti, with the detonation of one fusion and two fission bombs.
