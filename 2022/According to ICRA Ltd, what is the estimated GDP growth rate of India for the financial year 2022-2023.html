According to ICRA Ltd, what is the estimated GDP growth rate of India for the financial year 2022-2023?

(A) 10%

(B) ** 9%

(C) 8%

(D) 7%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Rating agency ICRA has maintained its forecast of 7.2% growth inIndia’s GDP for the financial year FY23.

Concept note-2: Growth in India isprojected to slow to 6.9% in FY23, says World Bank. Gross domestic product (GDP)growth in India will slow to 6.6% in the financial year 2023-24 from 6.9% projected inthe ongoing fiscal, according to the World Bank.

Concept note-3: The growth inreal GDP during 2022-23 is estimated at 7 per cent as compared to 8.7 per cent in2021-22, it stated.

Concept note-4: The Economic Survey 2022-23 estimatedIndia’s GDP growth at 6.5 per cent, which is lower than the projected 7 per centgrowth for the ongoing financial year.

Concept note-5: Reacting to the NSOdata, Aditi Nayar, Chief Economist, ICRA Ltd, said the GDP estimate of 7 percent for 2022-23 factors in 4.5 per cent growth for the second half of thefiscal.
