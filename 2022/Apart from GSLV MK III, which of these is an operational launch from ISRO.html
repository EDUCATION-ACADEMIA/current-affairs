Apart from GSLV MK III, which of these is an operational launch from ISRO?

(A) VSLV

(B) ** PSLV

(C) NSLV

(D) MSLV

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: India has three active operational launch vehicles: PolarSatellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV),Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle Mk-III (LVM3).

Concept note-2: Thefirst launch took place on 22 October 2022, injecting 36 satellites for LowEarth Orbit. ISRO changed the name of GSLV Mk3 to LVM 3 after thesuccessful launch of LVM3-M2 mission. The rename was done to removeany ambiguity on the ability of the vehicle to put payloads in a particularorbit.

Concept note-3: GSLV Mk III is a three-stage heavy lift launch vehicledeveloped by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Two massiveboosters with solid propellant constitute the first stage, the core with liquidpropellant form the second stage and the cryogenic engine completes thefinal stage.

Concept note-4: Launch history. As of August 12, 2021,rockets from the GSLV family have made 14 launches, resulting in 8 successes,four failures, and two partial failures. All launches have occurred from theSatish Dhawan Space Centre, known before 2002 as the Sriharikota Range(SHAR).

Concept note-5: GSLV-MkIIIGSLV Mk III is designed to carry 4t classof satellites into Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit (GTO) or about 10t to Low EarthOrbit (LEO), which is about twice the capability of GSLV Mk II. The twostrap-on motors of GSLV Mk III are located on either side of its core liquidbooster.
