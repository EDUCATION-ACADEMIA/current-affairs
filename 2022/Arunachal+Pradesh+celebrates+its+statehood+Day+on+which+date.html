Arunachal Pradesh celebrates its statehood Day on which date?

(A) 18th February

(B) 19th February

(C) ** 20th February

(D) 21st February

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Statehood Day in Arunachal Pradesh – February 20, 2023. Statehood Day in Arunachal Pradesh is a state holiday observed on February 20 in the Northeastern Indian state of Arunachal Pradesh. It is a day to commemorate the granting of statehood to the state which occurred in the year 1987.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Goa. On May 30, Goa celebrated Statehood Day. On this day, in 1987, Goa ceased to be a Union Territory and became the 25th State of the Union of India.

Concept note-3: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Tripura, Meghalaya, and Manipur. 50th Statehood Day of Tripura, Meghalaya, and Manipur was celebrated on 21 January 2022. On this day, the North East Reorganisation Act, 1971 was implemented providing statehood to the three states.

Concept note-4: -Mishra (As of Feb 2022) conferred the ‘Arunachal Ratna’, the highest State Honour to the first Lieutenant Governor of Arunachal Pradesh Late KAA Raja, on the occasion.

Concept note-5: -Both Mizoram and Arunachal Pradesh were given Statehood on this day in 1987. Prime Minister Narendra Modi on February 20 greeted the people of Mizoram and Arunachal Pradesh on their Statehood Day and hailed their contribution.
