As per the Amendment in Aadhar Rules, supporting documents need to be updated once in how many years?

(A) 25

(B) 5

(C) ** 10

(D) 20

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The Government of India has amended Aadhaar rules,specifying that supporting documents associated with an Aadhaar needto be updated “at least once” on completion of 10 years from the date ofenrolment.

Concept note-2: Update Aadhaar every 10 yearsAadhaar numberholders may update their supporting documents in Aadhaar at least once every10 years from the date of enrolment for Aadhaar by submitting Proof ofIdentity (POI) and Proof of Address (POA) documents as stipulated in theAadhaar Enrolment and Update Regulation.

Concept note-3: AadhaarCard Update 2022: Unique Identification Authority of India (UIDAI) hasissued new guidelines to update the Aadhaar card. Now you no longer needsupporting documents to update your Aadhaar card. Aadhaar is a 12-digitunique identity number that serves as the primary identification system inIndia.

Concept note-4: Resident can carry out 3rd time Name update throughexception handling. For the same you can carry out the update at Aadhaar Centreand then approach concerned Regional Office of UIDAI for approval of update underexception.

Concept note-5: Photograph Identity Card / Certificate withPhotograph issued by Central Govt./ State Govt. like Bhamashah, DomicileCertificate, Resident Certificate, Jan- Aadhaar, MGNREGA/ NREGS Job Card,Labour Card etc.
