As per the ‘Living Planet Report’ published by World Wildlife Fund, by what percentage the monitored wildlife population has plunged in the last 50 years?

(A) 46%

(B) 34%

(C) 72%

(D) ** 69%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There has been a 69 per cent decline in the wildlife populations of mammals, birds, amphibians, reptiles and fish, across the globe in the last 50 years, according to the latest Living Planet Report by World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF).

Concept note-2: -A new report reveals monitored populations of wild mammals, fish, reptiles, birds and amphibians have declined by an average of 69% over nearly 50 years, while conservation efforts have failed to tackle the root causes of biodiversity loss.

Concept note-3: -WASHINGTON, D.C. (October 12, 2022) - Monitored populations of vertebrates (mammals, birds, amphibians, reptiles and fish) have seen a devastating 69% drop on average since 1970, according to World Wildlife Fund’s (WWF) Living Planet Report 2022.

Concept note-4: -The 2022 global Living Planet Index shows an average 69% decrease in monitored wildlife populations between 1970 and 2018. Latin America shows the greatest regional decline in average population abundance (94%). Population trends for monitored freshwater species are also falling steeply (83%).

Concept note-5: -Wildlife populations plummet by 69% The Living Planet Report 2022 is a comprehensive study of trends in global biodiversity and the health of the planet. This flagship WWF publication reveals an average decline of 69% in species populations since 1970.
