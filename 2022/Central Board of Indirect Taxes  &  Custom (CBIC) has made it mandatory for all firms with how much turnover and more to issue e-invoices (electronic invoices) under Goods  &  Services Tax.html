Central Board of Indirect Taxes & Custom (CBIC) has made it mandatory for all firms with how much turnover and more to issue e-invoices (electronic invoices) under Goods & Services Tax?

(A) Rs. 30 crore

(B) ** Rs. 20 crore

(C) Rs. 25 crore

(D) Rs. 10 crore

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Therefore, CBIC has clarified that presently, e-invoicing hasbeen made mandatory for registered persons having aggregate turnover more thanRs. 10 crore in any preceding financial year from 2017-18 onwards as per Central TaxNotification 17/2022 dated 01/08/2022.

Concept note-2: The Central Board ofDirect taxes and Customs (CBIC) has made the e-invoicing system mandatory fortaxpayers with a turnover higher than Rs. 50 crore from 1st April 2021. Thegovernment extended the e-invoicing applicability to businesses having more than Rs.20 crore turnover w.e.f 1st April 2022.

Concept note-3: Businesses covered in thesixth phase of e-invoicingThe government had plans to make e invoicingmandatory for businesses with a cumulative yearly turnover of over Rs. 5 crorein any previous financial year from 2017-18 to 2021-22 from 1st January2023.

Concept note-4: The government would seem to decide to make the GSTe-invoicing mandatory for the firms whose turnover is Rs 5 cr or exceeds hencedrawing the limit lower down to Rs 10 cr, as per the government administration. Thenew rule may come in 2023.

Concept note-5: With e-invoicing alreadyimplemented for businesses with a turnover of 50 crores, the current phase isapplicable for businesses with a turnover exceeding 20 crores from 1st April 2022.From 1st October 2022, e-invoicing will be applicable for businesses with a turnoverexceeding 10 crores.
