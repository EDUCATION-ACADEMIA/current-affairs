Chandigarh International Airport will be renamed after whom?

(A) Kartar Singh Sarabha

(B) ** Shaheed Bhagat Singh

(C) Udham Singh

(D) Sukhdev Thapar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Shaheed Bhagat Singh International Airport (IATA: IXC, ICAO: VICG) is a civil enclave customs airport serving the city of Chandigarh, India. The airport is located in the village of Jhiurheri in the Mohali district of Punjab, India.

Concept note-2: -Biju Patnaik International Airport (IATA: BBI, ICAO: VEBS), also known as Bhubaneswar Airport, is situated in the city of Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India. It is the sole international airport in the state. It is named after the former Chief Minister of Odisha, Biju Patnaik, who was also a famed aviator and freedom fighter.

Concept note-3: -Indira Gandhi International Airport (IATA: DEL, ICAO: VIDP) is the primary international airport serving Delhi, the capital of India, and the National Capital Region (NCR).

Concept note-4: -Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Mopa International Airport in Goa, which has been named after former Chief Minister Manohar Parrikar. The new airport, which is about 35 km from capital city Panaji, can handle 44 lakh passengers annually.

Concept note-5: -MOHALI: The international airport in Mohali has been named Shaheed Bhagat Singh International Airport, Chandigarh. Even as it is in Mohali, the airport has got the word Chandigarh as a suffix in it on a demand by . Haryana.
