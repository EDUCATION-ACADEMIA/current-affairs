DGCA has curtailed which airline’s flights to 50 percent?

(A) ** SpiceJet

(B) Air India

(C) Indigo

(D) Vistara

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Download The Economic Times News App to get Daily Market Updates & Live Business News. Indian aviation regulator DGCA has extended the restriction it placed on low cost airline SpiceJet under which the airline was asked to curtail flights by 50 percent.

Concept note-2: -SpiceJet has received the aviation regulator DGCA approval for wet leasing five Boeing 737 Max planes for up to six months. The airline has two of the aircraft operating on different routes, according to sources, reported news agency PTI.

Concept note-3: -Budget air carrier SpiceJet, which courted controversy for a Tweet featuring veteran actor Dharmendra, and describing women flight attendants as “red hot girls", today took down the post from social media.

Concept note-4: -Passengers of a Bengaluru-bound SpiceJet plane had to wait for a long time at the aerobridge at the Delhi airport on Tuesday, with the airline saying the flight was delayed due to a weather disruption that led to the incoming crew exceeding its duty time limit.
