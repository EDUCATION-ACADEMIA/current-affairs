Formula One world champion, who has won the Spanish Grand Prix in a Red Bull?

(A) Charles Leclerc

(B) Sergio Perez

(C) George Russell

(D) ** Max Verstappen

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Formula One world champion Max Verstappen won the Spanish Grand Prix in a Red Bull one-two on Sunday to take over at the top from Ferrari’s luckless Charles Leclerc, who retired with an engine failure while leading.

Concept note-2: -It was the 62nd edition of the Spanish Grand Prix and the sixth round of the 2022 Formula One World Championship. Max Verstappen won the race ahead of teammate Sergio Pérez, while George Russell completed the podium. Charles Leclerc, the pole-sitter and championship leader, was forced to retire due to engine issues.

Concept note-3: -F1: Champion Max Verstappen wins Abu Dhabi Grand Prix for Red Bull – as it happened | Formula One | The Guardian.

Concept note-4: -Max celebrated his first win on his first start for Red Bull Racing. It happened on May 15, 2016 at the Spanish Grand Prix in Barcelona, and MV#33 was 18 years and 228 days young. His success has also earned him the titles of Youngest Rider on the Podium and Youngest GP Leader.
