Google invested $1 billion in which Indian company to expand its hold in India?

(A) Reliance

(B) TCS

(C) ** Airtel

(D) Mahindra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Internet giant Google will invest up to $1 billion in BhartiAirtel, India’s second-largest service provider, by acquiring 1.28% of Airtel’s equityand in other commercial agreements on mutual terms over the next fiveyears.

Concept note-2: As part of this partnership, Google intends to invest upto $1B, as part of its Google for India Digitization Fund, which includes equityinvestment as well as a corpus for potential commercial agreements, to be identifiedand agreed on mutually agreeable terms over the course of the next fiveyears.

Concept note-3: It has invested $4.5 billion in Reliance Industries’ JioPlatforms and led an investment of $145 million in InMobi’s Glance, which alsoowns a short-video platform, Roposo.

Concept note-4: What is in it forAirtel? The funds invested by Google will help Airtel to develop a variety ofconsumer electronics. The electronics will be across different prices to make themaffordable for the users. The funding will further give more resources to thecompany to boost its 5G plan.

Concept note-5: In 2010, Warid Telecomsold a majority 70.90% stake in the company to Bharti Airtel for US$300million.
