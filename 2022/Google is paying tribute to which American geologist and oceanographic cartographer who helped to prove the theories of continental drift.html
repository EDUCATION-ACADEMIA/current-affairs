Google is paying tribute to which American geologist and oceanographic cartographer who helped to prove the theories of continental drift?

(A) Tiara Moore

(B) Caitlyn Larsen

(C) Rebecca Nesel

(D) ** Marie Tharp

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Today’s Doodle celebrates the life of Marie Tharp, anAmerican geologist and oceanographic cartographer who helped prove thetheories of continental drift. She co-published the first world map of the oceanfloors.

Concept note-2: On November 21, an interactive Google Doodlehighlighted the pioneering work of Marie Tharp. Starting in 1948, Tharp worked atthe Lamont Geological Observatory - now the Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory atthe Columbia Climate School. There, she used sonar data to draw topographic mapsof the ocean floor.

Concept note-3: Marie Tharp was a pioneering Americangeologist and oceanographic cartographer who helped create the first scientific map ofthe Atlantic Ocean floor.

Concept note-4: On November 21 (Monday), Googlededicated a doodle to one of the biggest names in cartography. Marie Tharp,the American geologist and oceanographic cartographer, created some ofthe most groundbreaking maps that went on to change the perceptions ofpeople about the world.

Concept note-5: Marie Tharp is credited withproducing one of the world’s first comprehensive maps of the ocean floor.She and research partner Bruce Heezen transformed a once barren and flatlandscape into a dynamic three-dimensional space with mountains, valleys andtrenches.
