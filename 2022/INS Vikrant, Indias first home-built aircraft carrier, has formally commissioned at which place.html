INS Vikrant, India’s first home-built aircraft carrier, has formally commissioned at which place?

(A) Naval Dockyard, Vishakapatnam

(B) Goa Shipyard Limited

(C) Bombay Dockyard

(D) ** Cochin Shipyard Limited

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: INS Vikrant (pronounced [vikrɑːnt̪]) is an aircraft carrierconstructed by the Cochin Shipyard Limited (CSL) at Kerala for the Indian Navy. Itis the first aircraft carrier built in India. It is named Vikrant as a tribute toIndia’s first aircraft carrier, INS Vikrant (1961).

Concept note-2: India’sPrime Minister Narendra Modi addresses an audience before commissioningIndia’s first home-built aircraft carrier INS Vikrant at a state-run shipyard inKochi, India, Sept. 2, 2022. The new aircraft carrier is India’s second – thefirst one was built in the former Soviet Union.

Concept note-3: TheINS Vikrant was launched on September 22, 1945 as Hercules. However, itsconstruction was stalled and was completed when India purchased it fromBritain in 1957. On March 04, 1961 it was commissioned as Vikrant in its firstavatar.

Concept note-4: Aim is to make India an indigenous ship building hub:RM. Indian Naval Ship (INS) Mormugao (D67), second warship of the P15B class ofstealth guided-missile destroyers, was commissioned in the august presence of RakshaMantri Shri Rajnath Singh at Naval Dockyard, Mumbai on December 18,2022.
