Ind-Ra, has lowered the economic growth forecast rate of India’s GDP in FY23 to which percent?

(A) 7.7-7.9%

(B) 7.6%

(C) 7.5%

(D) ** 7-7.2%

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Earlier, the ADB had cut India’s GDP (Gross DomesticProduct) growth forecast by 50 basis points to 7 per cent for Fiscal Year 2022-23while Fitch slashed India’s growth forecast by 80 basis points to 7 per cent. Ind-Raexpects growth at 6.9 per cent while it is 6.8 per cent for SBI EconomicResearch Division.

Concept note-2: India’s GDP to grow at 6.9% inFY23 & will remain one of the fastest-growing major economies, says WorldBank.

Concept note-3: World Bank on India’s real GDPLast month, the WorldBank said it expected India’s real GDP to grow at 6.9 percent in the FY23, up fromits previous projection of 6.5%.

Concept note-4: The Organisation forEconomic Co-operation and Development (OECD) has downgraded India’sGDP growth forecast to 6.6% for FY 2022-23 from 6.9% projected earlier onmoderation in exports and domestic demand and inflation crimping privateconsumption.
