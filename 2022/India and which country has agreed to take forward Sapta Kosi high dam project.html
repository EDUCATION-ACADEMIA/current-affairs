India and which country has agreed to take forward Sapta Kosi high dam project?

(A) Japan

(B) ** Nepal

(C) Sri Lanka

(D) Bangladesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: India and Nepal have agreed to take forward the Sapta Kosihigh dam project through further studies, as senior officials of the two sides methere and comprehensively reviewed the bilateral water-sector cooperation,including the implementation of the Mahakali Treaty and cooperation in areas offlooding and inundation.

Concept note-2: Sapta Kosi High Dam is amultipurpose project proposed to be constructed on the Saptakoshi Riverof Nepal (Known as Kosi River in India). The project is primarily aimedto control floods in south-east Nepal and northern Bihar and to generatehydropower.

Concept note-3: Under the Koshi Agreement, signed between thegovernments of Nepal and India in 1954, the Indian state of Bihar is responsible forpreventing floods in the area affected by the Koshi Project, roughly 150kilometres of embankments built along the river in India and Nepal to preventfloods.

Concept note-4: About Pancheshwar DamPancheshwar Dam is arock-fill dam which is being constructed on the Sarda River (also known asMahakali in Nepal), which forms the international boundary between India andNepal.

Concept note-5: Pancheshwar Multipurpose Project (PMP) is abi-national Multi Purpose Project, primarily aimed at energy production andaugmenting irrigation in India and Nepal.
