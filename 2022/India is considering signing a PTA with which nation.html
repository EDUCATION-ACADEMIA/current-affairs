India is considering signing a PTA with which nation?

(A) Uzbekistan

(B) ** Oman

(C) Israel

(D) Hungary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: India may consider preferential trade agreementwith Oman:Piyush Goyal. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal on Thursday said thatIndia may consider negotiating a preferential trade agreement (PTA) withOman.

Concept note-2: INDIA AND FREE-TRADE AGREEMENTSWithinAsia, India has signed bilateral FTAs with Sri Lanka (1998), Afghanistan (2003),Thailand (2004), Singapore (2005), Bhutan (2006), Nepal (2009), Korea (2009),Malaysia (2011) and Japan (2011).

Concept note-3: The country has recentlysigned three such agreements with Mauritius, the UAE and Australia. Further, Indiais also actively engaged in FTA negotiations with some of its trading partnersincluding the UK, European Union and Canada.

Concept note-4: A preferentialtrade area (also preferential trade agreement, PTA) is a trading bloc thatgives preferential access to certain products from the participating countries.This is done by reducing tariffs but not by abolishing them completely. It isthe first stage of economic integration.

Concept note-5: A FrameworkAgreement to Promote Economic Cooperation between India and Chilewas signed on January 20, 2005. The Framework Agreement envisaged aPreferential Trade Agreement (PTA) between the two countries as a firststep.
