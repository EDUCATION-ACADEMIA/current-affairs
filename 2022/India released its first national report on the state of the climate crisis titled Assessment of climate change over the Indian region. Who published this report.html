India released its first national report on the state of the climate crisis titled “Assessment of climate change over the Indian region”. Who published this report?

(A) MacMillan India Ltd.

(B) ** Spring Nature

(C) Taxman Publications Limited

(D) Hachette India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: In 1896, a seminal paper by Swedish scientist SvanteArrhenius first predicted that changes in atmospheric carbon dioxide levelscould substantially alter the surface temperature through the greenhouseeffect. In 1938, Guy Callendar connected carbon dioxide increases in Earth’satmosphere to global warming.

Concept note-2: The first Assessment ofClimate Change over the Indian Region has been published by the Ministry ofEarth Sciences (MoES). Hence 1 statement is correct. It is India’s first-evernational forecast on the impact of global warming on the subcontinent in thecoming century.

Concept note-3: The global average temperature in thelast century has gone up by 1.1°C, according to the latest estimates by theIPCC.

Concept note-4: India emits about 3 gigatonnes (Gt) CO2eq ofgreenhouse gases each year; about two and a half tons per person, which is less thanthe world average. The country emits 7% of global emissions, despite having 17% ofthe world population.
