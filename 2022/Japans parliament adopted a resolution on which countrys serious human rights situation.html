Japan’s parliament adopted a resolution on which country’s “serious human rights situation”?

(A) Syria

(B) ** China

(C) Afghanistan

(D) Venezuela

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Japan’s parliament has adopted a resolution on theserious human rights situation in China, and asked Prime Minister FumioKishida’s government to take steps to relieve the situation, ahead of BeijingWinter Olympics.

Concept note-2: The Freedom and People’s RightsMovement, Liberty and Civil Right Movement, Free Civil Right Movement (, JiyūMinken Undō) (abbreviated as Freedom Party (, Jiyūtō)) or Popular RightsMovement was a Japanese political and social movement for democracy in the1880s.

Concept note-3: Diet, also called (1889–1947) Imperial Diet, JapaneseKokkai (“National Assembly”), or Teikoku Gikai (“Imperial Assembly”), thenational legislature of Japan.

Concept note-4: The Senkaku Islands dispute, orDiaoyu Islands dispute, is a territorial dispute over a group of uninhabited islandsknown as the Senkaku Islands in Japan, the Diaoyu Islands in the People’s Republicof China (PRC), and Tiaoyutai Islands in the Republic of China (ROC orTaiwan).
