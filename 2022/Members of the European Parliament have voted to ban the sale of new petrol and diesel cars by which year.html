Members of the European Parliament have voted to ban the sale of new petrol and diesel cars by which year?

(A) ** 2035

(B) 2024

(C) 2031

(D) 2033

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Members of the European Parliament have voted to ban the sale of new petrol and diesel cars by 2035. The move aims to step up the fight against climate change through the faster development of electric vehicles.

Concept note-2: -European Union lawmakers and member countries reached a deal to ban the sale of new gasoline and diesel cars and vans by 2035.

Concept note-3: -Q. Which global bloc voted for a ban on the sale of new petrol and diesel cars from 2035? Notes: European Parliament lawmakers recently voted to support an EU ban on the sale of new petrol and diesel cars from 2035. EU lawmakers also endorsed a 55 percent reduction in CO2 from automobiles in 2030 compared with 2021.

Concept note-4: -EU ban on sale of new petrol and diesel cars from 2035 explained | News | European Parliament.

Concept note-5: -To combat the deteriorating climate crisis and secure a safe future, California has become the world’s first government to effectively ban petrol-powered vehicles by 2035.
