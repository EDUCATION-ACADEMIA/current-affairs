NASA and which company has launched Starliner Spacecraft to carry astronauts to ISS?

(A) Embraer

(B) Airbus

(C) SpaceX

(D) ** Boeing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Boeing launched its Starliner spacecraft, designed to ferry astronauts to and from the International Space Station, on an uncrewed test mission toward the orbiting outpost Thursday evening.

Concept note-2: -In 2014, NASA awarded the CCtCap contracts to Boeing and SpaceX through a public-private partnership as part of the agency’s Commercial Crew Program.

Concept note-3: -Boeing developed Starliner under a roughly $4.2 billion fixed-price NASA contract awarded in 2014, which includes six operational Starliner missions. NASA has a similar contract with SpaceX, whose rival Crew Dragon capsule has flown six crewed flights for the agency since 2020.
