CURRENT AFFAIRS-2021
[SOURCES]

NPCI extended the deadline for third-party apps to comply with UPI Market share guidelines by how many years?

(A) 3 Years

(B) ** 2 Years

(C) 5 Years

(D) 10 Years

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The operator of digital payments infrastructure, NationalPayments Corporation of India (NPCI) has extended the deadline for existingthird-party apps to comply with its market share guidelines on the Unified PaymentsInterface (UPI) network by two years.

Concept note-2: “Taking into account thepresent usage and future potential of UPI, and other relevant factors, the timelinesfor compliance of existing TPAPs (third party app providers) who are exceeding thevolume cap, is extended by two years i.e. till December 31, 2024 to comply with thevolume cap,” it said in a circular.

Concept note-3: The extension comes afterNPCI and the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) held consultations with the government,ET had reported previously. One of the reasons why NPCI has chosen to extendthe deadline is that it believes other players haven’t been able to scale upoperations, sources said.

Concept note-4: According to NPCI guidelines, aperson can make a maximum payment of up to Rs 1 Lakh per day throughUPI.

Concept note-5: Catch up quick: The NPCI has set a limit of 30% marketshare on UPI for any digital payments entity. The rule is set to take effect fromJanuary 2023.
