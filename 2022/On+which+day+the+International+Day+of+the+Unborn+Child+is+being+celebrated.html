On which day the International Day of the Unborn Child is being celebrated?

(A) March 23

(B) March 24

(C) March 22

(D) ** March 25

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The International Day of the Unborn Child is observed every year on March 25th. As a day, to celebrate the value of human life, dignity, and the developing child yet to be born. Every country has a different law regarding abortions.

Concept note-2: -International Day of the Unborn Child 2022: Theme It is a day to recall the remarkable journey of life each member of the human family has taken or been prevented from taking by the act of abortion.

Concept note-3: -(d) As used in this section, the term “unborn child” means a child in utero, and the term “child in utero” or “child, who is in utero” means a member of the species homo sapiens, at any stage of development, who is carried in the womb.

Concept note-4: -Based on one of Thailand’s more gruesome true stories, this ripped-from-the-headlines genre effort concerns the scandal of the discovery of 2,002 fetuses in a Bangkok Buddhist temple that had come from illegal abortion clinics.
