Orbital Rail Corridor has been launched in which state of India?

(A) Madhya Pradesh

(B) Gujarat

(C) Uttar Pradesh

(D) ** Haryana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Haryana Orbital Rail Corridor (HORC), with target completion by 31 March 2025, is an under construction twin-track 121.7-kilometre (75.6 mi)-km long Indian gauge railway project in Haryana state of India, which will run around along the Western Peripheral Expressway (WPE) on the western side of Delhi.

Concept note-2: -Haryana Orbital Rail Corridor (HORC) project is a 121.742 km proposed semi high-speed rail line connecting Harsana Kalan (Sonipat) and Asaoti (Palwal) in Haryana through 17 stations along the Kundli – Manesar – Palwal (KMP) / Western Peripheral Expressway with an estimated cost of Rs. 4,822.69 crore.

Concept note-3: -Status of Project The project has been approved by Govt. of Haryana and Ministry of Railways; after clearance of the project by Extended Board for Railways (EBR) and the project is approved by Cabinet Committee of Economic Affairs.

Concept note-4: -The correct answer is Tamil Nadu. Indian Railways launched the country’s first green rail corridor between Rameswaram-Manamadurai on 24 July 2016 in Tamil Nadu. Trains on the 114-km Rameswaram-Manamadurai railway line will be equipped with bio-toilets.

Concept note-5: -HORC is a new electrified Double Broad-gauge (BG) rail line of around 126 km from Palwal to Sonipat, via Sohna, Manesar and Kharkhouda in the State of Haryana, bypassing Delhi and will serve both passenger and freight traffic.
