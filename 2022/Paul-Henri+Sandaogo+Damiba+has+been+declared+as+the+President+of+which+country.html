Paul-Henri Sandaogo Damiba has been declared as the President of which country?

(A) Namibia

(B) Mali

(C) ** Burkina Faso

(D) Zimbabwe

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Paul-Henri Sandaogo Damiba (French: [pɔl ɑ̃ʁi sɑ̃daɔɡɔ damiba]; born January 1981) is a Burkinabé military officer who served as interim president of Burkina Faso from 31 January 2022 to 30 September 2022, when he was removed in a coup d’état, by his own military colleague Ibrahim Traoré.

Concept note-2: -A former French colony, it gained independence as Upper Volta in 1960. The name Burkina Faso, which means “Land of Incorruptible People,” was adopted in 1984.

Concept note-3: -A coup d’état took place in Burkina Faso on 30 September 2022, removing Interim President Paul-Henri Sandaogo Damiba over his alleged inability to deal with the country’s Islamist insurgency. Damiba had come to power in a coup d’état just eight months earlier. Captain Ibrahim Traoré took over as interim leader.
