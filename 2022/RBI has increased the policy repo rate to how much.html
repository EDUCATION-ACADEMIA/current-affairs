RBI has increased the policy repo rate to how much?

(A) ** 4.90

(B) 4.80

(C) 4.50

(D) 4.70

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: In its December monetary policy review, the central bank hadraised the key benchmark interest rate (repo) by 35 basis points (bps) after deliveringthree back-to-back increases of 50 bps.

Concept note-2: Current Repo Rate 2023.The Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) announced in December 2022 that the reporate has been increased by 35 basis points and the rate is now 6.25%. Duringits meeting MPC has decided to keep the reverse repo rate unchanged at3.35%.

Concept note-3: RBI Repo Rate Hike: The Reserve Bank of India(RBI) recently hiked the repo rate by 35 bps, the fifth such increase in 2022.The repo rate currently stands at 6.25 per cent.

Concept note-4: Here’swhy. There is room for at least another 50 bps hike in India’s policy reporate going forward.

Concept note-5: As announced in the MonetaryPolicy Statement 2022-23 dated December 07, 2022, the Bank Rate is revisedupwards by 35 basis points from 6.15 per cent to 6.50 per cent with immediateeffect.
