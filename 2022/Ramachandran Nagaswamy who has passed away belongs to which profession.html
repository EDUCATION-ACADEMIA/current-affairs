Ramachandran Nagaswamy, who has passed away, belongs to which profession?

(A) Author

(B) Filmmaker

(C) ** Archaeologist

(D) Mountaineer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ramachandran Nagaswamy (10 August 1930 – 23 January 2022) was an Indian historian, archaeologist and epigraphist who was known for his work on temple inscriptions and art history of Tamil Nadu.

Concept note-2: -LUCKNOW: Doyen of Indian archaeology and former director general of the Archaeological Survey of India, Prof BB Lal, passed away in Delhi on Saturday. He was 101. A recipient of Padma Vibhushan award (2021), Prof Lal’s contribution to the world of archaeology is often described as “beyond measure” by most.

Concept note-3: -The correct answer is Archaeologist. Renowned archaeologist, art historian, and Padma Bhushan awardee R Nagaswamy passed away in January 2022. He was the first director of the Tamil Nadu government’s Department of Archaeology.

Concept note-4: -The life of renowned archaeologist and Padma Bhushan awardee R Nagaswamy would fit the saying perfectly. At the age of 91, his latest book, ‘Senthamizh Naadum Pandbum’ was published at the beginning of this year.

Concept note-5: -Ramachandran, who retired from the TN Archaeology Department as an epigraphist added, “No wonder Nagaswamy is hailed as the father of Tamil archaeology”. After its formation, Nagaswamy became the first director of the Tamil Nadu government’s Department of Archaeology in 1966. He retired in 1988.
