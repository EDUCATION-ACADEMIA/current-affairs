Rosh Hashanah festival belongs to which community?

(A) Hindu

(B) Jain

(C) Buddhist

(D) ** Jewish

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Rosh Hashanah, or “Head of the Year,” is the Jewish NewYear, starting at sunset and continuing for two days. On Rosh Hashanah,Jews attend religious services and celebratory meals. Rosh Hashanah alsomarks the beginning of the Ten Days of Repentance, which conclude on YomKippur.

Concept note-2: Rosh Hashanah is observed in various forms by Jewishpeople all around the world. Each family has its own traditions for Rosh Hashanah,which usually depends on where they are from.

Concept note-3: AttendSynagogue Services-Gathering together in a synagogue for prayer services isan important part of Rosh Hashanah. Special prayers for this holy serviceand readings from the Torah are read from the Machzor prayer book. Somesynagogues will live stream their services this Rosh Hashanah for those unable toattend.

Concept note-4: Rosh Hashana, (Hebrew: “Beginning of the Year”) ,Hashana also spelled Hashanah or Ha-shanah, also called Day of Judgment or Day ofRemembrance, a major Jewish observance now accepted as inaugurating the religiousNew Year on Tishri 1 (September or October).
