S & P Global Ratings cut India’s growth projection for the current fiscal to what per cent from 7.8 per cent earlier?

(A) ** 7.3

(B) 7.6

(C) 7.4

(D) 7.5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: India’s output will expand 7 per cent in the fiscalyear 2022-2023 (ending in March 2023) and 6 per cent in the next fiscalyear, by our estimates,” S&P Global Ratings Asia-Pacific chief economistLouis Kuijs said.

Concept note-2: S&P had in September projectedthe Indian economy to grow 7.3% in 2022-23 and 6.5% in next fiscal year(2023-24).

Concept note-3: Standard & Poor’s credit rating for Indiastands at BBB- with stable outlook.

Concept note-4: NEW DELHI:India’s gross domestic product (GDP) growth is estimated at 7% for 2022-23on the back of a rebound in services and farm sectors but may face strongheadwinds in the months ahead due to the geopolitical situation and possiblerecession in several countries across the globe.

Concept note-5: TheEconomic Survey 2022-23 estimated India’s GDP growth at 6.5 per cent,which is lower than the projected 7 per cent growth for the ongoing financialyear.
