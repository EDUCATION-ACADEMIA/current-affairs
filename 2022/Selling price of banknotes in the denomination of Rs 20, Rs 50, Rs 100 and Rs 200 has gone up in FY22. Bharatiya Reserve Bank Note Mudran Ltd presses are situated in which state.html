Selling price of banknotes in the denomination of Rs 20, Rs 50, Rs 100 and Rs 200 has gone up in FY22. Bharatiya Reserve Bank Note Mudran Ltd presses are situated in which state?

(A) ** Mysuru

(B) Dewas

(C) Nashik

(D) Hyderabad

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Interestingly, even as RBI spent ₹4,984.8 crore, which is 24 percent more than it did in FY21 (₹4,012.09 crore), the total supply was lower.The overall cost of printing currency in FY22 was the second highest, afteran all-time high of around ₹8,000 crore during the year of demonetisation(2016-17).

Concept note-2: Data compiled from RBI’s annual reports shows thecost of printing currency notes in 2021-22 at ₹4,984.8 crore was almost 1.5 times the₹2,063.16 crore spent in 2008-09.

Concept note-3: Detailed Solution. Thecorrect answer is Rs 100 currency notes. People in India most prefer the Rs100 denomination banknotes, while Rs 2,000 are least preferred. This wasrevealed in a Reserve Bank of India survey of a diverse sample of 11,000respondents revealed.

Concept note-4: RBI is not going to buy any kind of oldcoins.
