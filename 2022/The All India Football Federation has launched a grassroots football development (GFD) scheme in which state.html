The All India Football Federation has launched a grassroots football development (GFD) scheme in which state?

(A) Assam

(B) Bihar

(C) ** Arunachal Pradesh

(D) Bihar

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Explanation: The All India Football Federation haslaunched a grassroots football development (GFD) scheme in ArunachalPradesh.

Concept note-2: The AIFF Grassroots programme pays specialattention to quality and quantity of licensed E-certificate coaches, accreditedacademies, grassroots festivals, Golden Baby Leagues, amateur football tournaments,futsal and anything which doesn’t come under the umbrella of professionalFootball.

Concept note-3: For the record, On the 23 June 1937 at the ArmyHeadquarters, Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, AIFF was formedat a meeting of the representatives of football associations of six regionswhere the game was very popular in those days.

Concept note-4: FormerIndian goalkeeper Kalyan Chaubey was elected as the new president of theAll Indian Football Federation (AIFF). He will become the first ex-playerto hold the top office of India’s football governing body in its 85 years ofexistence.

Concept note-5: Grassroots football is all football that isnon-professional and non-elite. This includes, but is not limited to, children’sfootball, schools and youth football, amateur football, football for disabled players,football for veterans and walking football.
