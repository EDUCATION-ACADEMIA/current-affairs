The European Council recently granted the candidate status to which country?

(A) Georgia

(B) Estonia

(C) ** Republic of Moldova

(D) Latvia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The European Parliament yesterday voted overwhelmingly togrant European Union candidate status to Ukraine and Moldova. EuropeanCouncil President Charles Michel made the announcement after discussionsamong leaders of the EU’s 27 member states. The vote was 529-45, with 14abstentions.

Concept note-2: There are eight recognised candidates formembership of the European Union: Turkey (since 1999), North Macedonia (2005),Montenegro (2010), Serbia (2012), Albania (2014), Moldova (2022), Ukraine (2022),and Bosnia and Herzegovina (2022).

Concept note-3: The European Councilrecently granted the candidate status to which country? Notes: Two countries –Ukraine and Republic of Moldova – were granted the status of candidate country bythe European Council. This paves the way towards them becoming the members ofthe European Bloc.

Concept note-4: Candidate country status is conferred bythe European Council on the basis of an opinion from the European Commission,drawn up following an application for membership of the European Union (EU) bythe country concerned.
