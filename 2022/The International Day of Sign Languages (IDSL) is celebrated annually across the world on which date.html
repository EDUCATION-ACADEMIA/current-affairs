The International Day of Sign Languages (IDSL) is celebrated annually across the world on which date?

(A) September 22

(B) ** September 23

(C) September 24

(D) September 25

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The UN General Assembly has proclaimed 23 September as theInternational Day of Sign Languages in order to raise awareness of the importance ofsign language in the full realization of the human rights of people who aredeaf.

Concept note-2: History of International Day of Sign LanguagesThe daywas established after the United Nations General Assembly declared September 23 asthe International Day of Sign Languages in 2017. The following year, theInternational Day of Sign Languages was celebrated as part of the InternationalWeek of the Deaf.

Concept note-3: September 23 was chosen as it marked theanniversary of the World Federation of the Deaf’s formation in 1951. The day wasfirst commemorated in 2018 as part of the International Week of the Deaf. There areapproximately 70 million deaf people in the world, according to data fromthe World Federation of the Deaf.

Concept note-4: The UN GeneralAssembly has proclaimed September 23 as the International Day of SignLanguages to help raise awareness about sign language and the rights of deafpeople.

Concept note-5: The theme for the 2022 International Day of Signlanguages is “Sign Languages Unite Us!”. Deaf communities, governments and civilsociety organisations maintain their collective efforts in fostering, promoting andrecognising national sign languages as part of their countries’ vibrant and diverselinguistic landscapes.
