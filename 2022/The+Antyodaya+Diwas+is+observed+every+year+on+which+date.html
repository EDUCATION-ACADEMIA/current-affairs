The Antyodaya Diwas is observed every year on which date?

(A) ** September 25

(B) September 24

(C) September 22

(D) September 23

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Every year, Antyodaya Diwas is celebrated in India on September 25 to mark the birth anniversary of Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya and remember his life and legacy. In 2014, Prime Minister Narendra Modi dedicated the day in honour of Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya.

Concept note-2: -Antyodaya means “uplifting the poorest of the poor” or “rise of the last person". The day was declared by the Government of India on September 25, 2014 and is being officially celebrated since the year 2015.

Concept note-3: -Yes, 25 September is celebrated as Antyodaya Diwas in India. This is one of the important days in September because it commemorates the birth anniversary of Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya.

Concept note-4: -Antyodaya Diwas 2022: Theme The importance of this day lies in “reaching out to the last person”.

Concept note-5: -Deen Dayal Antyodaya Yojana - National Livelihoods Mission (NRLM) was launched by the Ministry of Rural Development (MoRD), Government of India in June 2011 as a restructured version of Swarna Jayanti Gram Swarozgar Yojna (SGSY).
