The first-ever World Deaf T20 Cricket Championship in 2023 will be held in which of the following states?

(A) Jammu & Kashmir

(B) Gujarat

(C) ** Kerala

(D) Haryana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Kerala. The All India Sports Council of the Deaf has got approval from the International Committee of Sports for the Deaf (ICSD) to host the first World Deaf T20 Cricket Championship in Kerala. It will be held from January 10 to 20, 2023.

Concept note-2: -Hyderabad: Hyderabad is going to host the inaugural Deaf T20 Cricket World Cup from May 27 to June 7 next year. Telangana State Cricket Association of the Deaf (TSCAD) under the aegis of the All India Cricket Association of the Deaf (AICAD) is going to conduct the tournament in the State.

Concept note-3: -The first World Deaf T20 cricket championship will be held in Thiruvanantha puram, Kerala from January 10 to January 20, 2023.

Concept note-4: -Haryana became champion of the 6th IDCA T20 National Cricket Championship 2022 for the Deaf.

Concept note-5: -The first T20 World Cup, played in South Africa in 2007, was won by India, creating a huge market for T20 on the subcontinent that was quickly exploited by the IPL. Subsequent World Cups were played in various cricket-playing countries, with the West Indies capturing the most titles (two).
