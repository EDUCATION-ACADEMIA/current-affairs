‘Tomato Flu’, which has been seen in the news, is endemic to which state?

(A) Tamil Nadu

(B) Odisha

(C) ** Kerala

(D) Karnataka

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Three months earlier in May, Kerala reported the country’sfirst case of Tomato Flu. And since then it has spread to other parts of the country.The infection is caused by Coxsackievirus A16. Once infected, the virus produces redand painful blisters on the skin, hence referred to as “Tomato Flu” or “TomatoFever”.

Concept note-2: 5 Tomato flu is a self-limiting illness and nospecific drug exists to treat it. The tomato flu was first identified in theKollam district of Kerala on May 6, 2022, and as of July 26, 2022, more than82 children younger than 5 years with the infection have been reported bythe local government hospitals.

Concept note-3: Tomato flu is a rareviral infection that could be a new variant of the viral hand-foot-and-mouthdisease, a common infectious disease targeting mostly children aged one tofive years. Initially, Kerala , Tamil Nadu and Odisha reported tomato flucases.

Concept note-4: Epidemiologists use the term endemic to refer to adisease which has spread in a limited area but has been around for some time.The report attempts to trace the history of the infection and claims that‘tomato flu’ was first identified in the Kollam district of Kerala on May6 this year.

Concept note-5: Cases of tomato flu were first reportedin Kerala in early May and have mostly spread among kids under 5 yearsold.
