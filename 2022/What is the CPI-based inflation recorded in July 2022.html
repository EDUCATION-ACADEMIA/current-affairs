What is the CPI-based inflation recorded in July 2022?

(A) 7.71 %

(B) 5.71 %

(C) 4.71 %

(D) ** 6.71 %

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: As a result, most of the CPI sub-groups recorded inflationrates above 6 per cent during this period (Table 6). In fact, the cumulativeweight of CPI items which recorded inflation in excess of 6 per cent went upsuccessively barring a transitory moderation during May-July 2022 (Chart10).

Concept note-2: Final index for the month of July, 2022 (Base Year:2011-12=100): For the month of July, 2022 the final Wholesale Price Index andinflation rate for ‘All Commodities’ (Base: 2011-12=100) stood at 154.0 and 14.07%respectively.

Concept note-3: The All-India CPI-IW for November, 2022remained stationary at 132.5 (one hundred thirty two point five). On 1-monthpercentage change, it remained static between October, 2022 and November, 2022when compared to an increase of 0.64 per cent recorded between correspondingmonths a year ago.

Concept note-4: The country’s retail inflation, which ismeasured by the consumer price index (CPI), dropped to a 1-year low of5.72% in Dec. 2022 from 5.88% in Nov. 2022.

Concept note-5: India CPIInflation Rate September, IIP Growth August 2022: India’s retail inflation,which is measured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI), rose to a five-monthhigh of 7.41 per cent in the month of September, up from 7.00 per cent inAugust.
