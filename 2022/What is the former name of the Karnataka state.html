What is the former name of the Karnataka state?

(A) Travancore

(B) ** Mysore

(C) Madras

(D) Kannada

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Today marks the golden jubilee of renaming the stateKarnataka from erstwhile Mysore.

Concept note-2: After Independence of Indiafrom the British rule, the Mysore state was created in 1953, wherein all theKannada dominant areas under different dispensations were unified and theenlarged Mysore state carved in 1956 which was later renamed as Karnataka in1973.

Concept note-3: Bettada Chamaraja Wadiyar, the raja of Mysuru rebuiltthe fort of Mysuru and made his headquarters and called the city ‘Mahishura Nagara’meaning the city of Mahishur. Many inscriptions done in the 17th century and laterrefer to Mysuru as ‘Mahishuru’.

Concept note-4: The existing Kannada name,Bengalūru, was declared the official name of the city in 2006. Bangalore issometimes referred to as the “Silicon Valley of India” (or “IT capital ofIndia”) because of its role as the nation’s leading information technology (IT)exporter.
