What is the name of the youngest known magnetar discovered by NASA?

(A) Magne J1818.0-1607

(B) ** Swift J1818.0-1607

(C) Neutr J1818.0-1607

(D) Star J1818.0-1607

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The magnetar J1818. 0-1607 can be seen in this compositeimage. X-rays from Chandra reveal the object and data from the Spitzer SpaceTelescope and Wide-Field Infrared Survey Explorer taken before the discovery showthe wide field of view.

Concept note-2: VT 1137-0337 is a very young neutronstar, and quite possibly as young as 14.

Concept note-3: A magnetar is aneutron star with an unusually strong magnetic field. Astronomers have spottedmagnetars elsewhere in the universe, but they’ve never before seen one beingborn. This time, researchers suspected they’d spotted a newborn magnetarbecause of an unusual pattern of flashing light.

Concept note-4: Magnetarsare a type of neutron star, an incredibly dense object mainly made up oftightly packed neutron, which forms from the collapsed core of a massivestar during a supernova. What sets magnetars apart from other neutronstars is that they also have the most powerful known magnetic fields in theuniverse.
