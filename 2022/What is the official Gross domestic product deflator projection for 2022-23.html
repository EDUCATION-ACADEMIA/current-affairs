What is the official Gross domestic product deflator projection for 2022-23?

(A) 4.5-5 %

(B) ** 3-3.5 %

(C) 1.5-2 %

(D) 10 %

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The growth in real GDP during 2022-23 is estimated at 7.0 percent as compared to 8.7 per cent in 2021-22, according to data released by the NSO,Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation.

Concept note-2: TheEconomic Survey 2022-23 estimated India’s GDP growth at 6.5 per cent,which is lower than the projected 7 per cent growth for the ongoing financialyear.

Concept note-3: The GDP deflator is seen at about 7.9% thisfiscal and wholesale price based inflation, which has a higher weight in thedeflator, will drive the deflator based inflation downwards next year. TheRBI has pegged CPI inflation at 6.7% in 2022-23 with real GDP growth at6.8%.

Concept note-4: Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman said on Thursdaythat the Centre’s gross domestic product (GDP) deflator projection for 2022-23 is 3to 3.5 per cent.

Concept note-5: According to the survey, India is likely towitness GDP growth of 6 to 6.8 per cent in 2023-24, depending on the trajectory ofeconomic and political developments globally. However, many economists believe thatGDP growth is likely to be at the lower end.
