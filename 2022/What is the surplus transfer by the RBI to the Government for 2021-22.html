What is the surplus transfer by the RBI to the Government for 2021-22?

(A) Rs 78784 crores

(B) Rs 99500 crores

(C) ** Rs 30307 crores

(D) Rs 20201 crores

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The board of Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has approved thetransfer of ₹30,307 crore as surplus to the Central Government for the accountingyear 2021-22. The board has also decided to maintain the Contingency RiskBuffer at 5.50% without quantifying the amount.

Concept note-2: TheReserve Bank of India (RBI) will transfer Rs 30,307 crore as surplus tothe government for fiscal ended March 2022 down 69% from the Rs 99,126crore in the year ended March 2021 and lower than the Rs 74,000 crorebudgeted by the government for the current fiscal.

Concept note-3: Datacompiled from RBI’s annual reports shows the cost of printing currency notes in2021-22 at ₹4,984.8 crore was almost 1.5 times the ₹2,063.16 crore spent in2008-09.

Concept note-4: The Reserve Bank acts as an adviser to theGovernment, whenever called upon to do so, on monetary and banking relatedmatters. The Central Government and State Governments may make rules for thereceipt, custody and disbursement of money from the consolidated fund, contingencyfund, and public account.
