What percentage increase in gross direct tax receipts was there in FY22?

(A) 40

(B) ** 30

(C) 10

(D) 20

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The Gross collection of Direct Taxes (before adjusting forrefunds) for the FY 2022-23 stands at Rs. 13,63,649 crore compared to Rs. 10,83,150crore in the corresponding period of the preceding financial year, registering a growthof 25.90% over collections of F.Y. 2021-22.

Concept note-2: 24.58%Increase in Direct Tax Collections up to 10/01/2023 (FY 2022-23): CBDT.The preliminary estimates of total money collected from direct taxes forthe FY 2022-23 (up to January 10, 2023) continue to indicate consistentgrowth.

Concept note-3: Goods and service tax (GST) accounts for 26.8 percent of the central government’s gross tax revenue of Rs 25.2 lakh crore inFY22.

Concept note-4: Government Revenues in India averaged 390881.48 INRTens of Millions from 1997 until 2022, reaching an all time high of 2207634.00 INRTens of Millions in March of 2022 and a record low of 82.00 INR Tens ofMillions in April of 1999.

Concept note-5: Share of direct and indirect taxin total central tax in India FY 2018-2023. As per Indian Union Budgetestimates for financial year 2023, direct taxes accounted for 51.5 percent andindirect taxes accounted for 48.5 percent of total central tax collection inIndia.
