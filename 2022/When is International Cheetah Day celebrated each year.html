When is International Cheetah Day celebrated each year?

(A) December 02

(B) ** December 04

(C) December 03

(D) December 05

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Every year, International Cheetah Day is celebrated on 4thDecember since 2010. Dr. Laurie Marker designated the day as International CheetahDay in remembrance of Khayam, a cheetah she raised from a cub at Wildlife Safari inWinston, Oregon.

Concept note-2: Celebrate the cheetah on December 4th! In2010, Dr. Laurie Marker designated December 4th as International Cheetah Day. Theannual celebration was set in remembrance of a cheetah cub named Khayam, that sheraised at Wildlife Safari in Winston, Oregon.

Concept note-3: On 17September 2022, 8 cheetahs from Namibia arrived in Madhya Pradesh’sKuno National Park, where they were released as part of the programme toreintroduce the feline in India. Out of the 8 cheetahs, 5 are female and 3 aremale.

Concept note-4: Project Cheetah is an initiative by the government torestore the extinct back in the Indian woods. Popular as the World’s firstinter-continental large wild carnivore translocation project, the movement willrevitalize the wildlife.

Concept note-5: The first fossil records of cheetah(Acinonyx) date from approximately 4 million years ago and evidence ofrelated species was retrieved in America, Europe, Asia, and Africa (Chapter3).
