When is International Delegates Day is being celebrated?

(A) April 23

(B) April 26

(C) ** April 25

(D) April 24

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: International Delegate’s Day is observed on 25 April everyyear. It is observed to raise awareness of the role of the representatives anddelegates of the Member States to the United Nations.

Concept note-2: TheInternational Day for Universal Access to Information is celebrated each year aroundthe world on the 28 September. The global conference opening ceremonytaking place in Tashkent, Uzbekistan, will launch the 2022 edition of theDay under the theme “Artificial Intelligence, e-Governance and Access toInformation”.

Concept note-3: There are currently 193 UN MemberStates. Each of the Member States of the United Nations has one seat in theGeneral Assembly. Information about current members, growth of membershipsince 1945, the procedure on how a country becomes a member, and themembership of principal UN organs is provided below.

Concept note-4: TheFirst Continental Congress was a meeting of delegates from 12 of the 13British colonies that became the United States.

Concept note-5: Anexperienced civil servant who has worked in the foreign affairs department ofher government for a number of years, gaining experience and developingdiplomacy skills, may be able to apply to become a UN delegate for hercountry.
