When was UN International Day for National Disaster Reduction is being observed?

(A) October 12

(B) ** October 13

(C) October 15

(D) October 14

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Held every 13 October, the day celebrates how people and communities around the world are reducing their exposure to disasters and raising awareness about the importance of reining in the risks that they face.

Concept note-2: -In 2022, the International Day will focus on Target G: “Substantially increase the availability of and access to multi-hazard early warning systems and disaster risk information and assessments to people by 2030.”

Concept note-3: -International Day for Disaster Reduction began in 1989, after a call by the United Nations General Assembly for a day to promote a global culture of risk-awareness and disaster reduction.

Concept note-4: -October 13 is designated as International Day for Disaster Risk Reduction with a focus on encouraging a global culture of risk awareness and catastrophe preparedness.

Concept note-5: -The 2017 edition continues as part of the “Sendai Seven” campaign, centred on the seven targets of the Sendai Framework. This year the focus is on Target B, of which the aim is to reduce the number of people affected by disasters by 2030.
