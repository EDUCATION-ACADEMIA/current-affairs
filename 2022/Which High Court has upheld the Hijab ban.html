Which High Court has upheld the Hijab ban?

(A) Bombay HC

(B) Madras HC

(C) Delhi HC

(D) ** Karnataka HC

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: On the basis on these findings and an analysis of theKarnataka Education Act of 1983, the court held that an order of the Karnatakagovernment in February upholding the hijab ban was legal. A three-judgebench of the Karnataka High Court reading out the hijab ban order onTuesday.

Concept note-2: The Supreme Court on Thursday delivered asplit verdict on the ban of wearing of hijab in educational institutions inKarnataka – with one judge affirming that the state government is authorised toenforce uniform in schools and the other calling hijab a matter of choicethat cannot be stifled by the state.

Concept note-3: Justice HemantGupta dismissed the 26 appeals filed against the judgment of the KarnatakaHigh Court which held that hijab was not an essential practice of Islamand allowed the ban on wearing headscarf in educational institutions in theState.

Concept note-4: This included hijab, a head-covering worn by someMuslim females. The Karnataka High Court eventually ruled that hijab is not anessential practice of Islam.

Concept note-5: India’s Supreme Court has failed todeliver a verdict on whether Muslim students can wear the hijab in schools andcolleges, with two judges expressing opposing views. One judge upheld aKarnataka high court order from March that said the hijab was not “essential” toIslam.
