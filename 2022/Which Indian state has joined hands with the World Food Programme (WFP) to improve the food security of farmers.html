Which Indian state has joined hands with the World Food Programme (WFP) to improve the food security of farmers?

(A) ** Odisha

(B) Tamil Nadu

(C) Telangana

(D) Assam

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The correct answer is Odisha. The United Nations World FoodProgramme (WFP) and the Odisha government have joined hands. They willimprove the food security of small and marginal farmers by strengthening theirresilience to climate change.

Concept note-2: UPSC Mains. Q. Which Indianstate has joined hands with the World Food Programme (WFP) to improve foodsecurity of farmers? Notes: The United Nations World Food Programme (WFP) andthe Odisha government have joined hands to improve the food security of smalland marginal farmers.

Concept note-3: Chhattisgarh government wasthe first who took the initiative to pass the first food security act in thecountry.

Concept note-4: New Delhi/Jaipur: The United Nations WorldFood Programme (WFP) and the Department of Food and Civil Supplies,Government of Rajasthan, are partnering to improve the State’s Targeted PublicDistribution System (TDPS), which is the world’s largest food safety netscheme.

Concept note-5: Food security has been a major concern in India.According to UN-India, there are nearly 195 million undernourished people in India,which is a quarter of the world’s hunger burden. Also, roughly 43% of children inIndia are chronically undernourished.
