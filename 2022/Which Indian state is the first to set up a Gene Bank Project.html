Which Indian state is the first to set up a Gene Bank Project?

(A) Kerala

(B) ** Maharashtra

(C) West Bengal

(D) Telangana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The Maharashtra Cabinet on Thursday approved the country’sfirst-of-its-kind gene bank programme in the State. The programme is aimed atconserving native biodiversity to ensure that natural resources are passedon to the next generation.

Concept note-2: The National Gene Bankestablished in the year 1996 to preserve the seeds of Plant Genetic Resources(PGR) for future generations, has the capacity to preserve about one milliongermplasm in the form of seeds.

Concept note-3: Recently, MaharashtraCabinet approved the ‘Maharashtra Gene Bank’, a first-of-its-kind project inIndia.

Concept note-4: It is headquartered in New Delhi and has 10 regionalstations.

Concept note-5: The International Crops Research Institute for theSemi-Arid Tropics (ICRISAT) in Hyderabad has set up the world’s first gene bankthat is powered entirely by solar energy. Spread across 4,500 sq metres, the genebank is powered by a 500 kW solar power plant generating 60,000 units permonth.
