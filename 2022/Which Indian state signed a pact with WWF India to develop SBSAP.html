Which Indian state signed a pact with WWF India to develop SBSAP?

(A) ** Arunachal Pradesh

(B) Kerala

(C) Assam

(D) Karnataka

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Q. Which Indian state signed a pact with WWF India todevelop State Biodiversity Strategy and Action Plan (SBSAP)? Notes: ArunachalPradesh signed a pact with WWF India to develop the State Biodiversity Strategyand Action Plan (SBSAP).

Concept note-2: This gave rise to development ofState Biodiversity Strategy and Action Plan (SBSAP) and Local BiodiversityStrategy and Action Plan (LBSAP) at state and local levels.

Concept note-3: Itattempts to promote conservation & sustainable use of the State’s biologicalresources by promoting awareness amongst the masses, cooperation betweenstakeholders and creating mechanisms required to plan for natural resourcemanagement and its long-term conservation.

Concept note-4: The state hasmore than 500 species of fauna and shelters four major cats namely tiger, leopard,clouded leopard, snow leopard and also rare lesser feline species like the Golden Catand Marbled Cat. It also covers an amazingly rich variety of avifauna with over 650bird species.

Concept note-5: The NBAP 2008 identifies threats and constraintsin biodiversity conservation taking into cognizance the existing legislations,implementation mechanisms, strategies, plans and programmes, based on whichaction points have been designed.
