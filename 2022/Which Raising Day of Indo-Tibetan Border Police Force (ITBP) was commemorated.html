Which Raising Day of Indo-Tibetan Border Police Force (ITBP) was commemorated?

(A) ** 61

(B) 50

(C) 70

(D) 100

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Was raised on October 24, 1962 to guard the India-Chinaborder. The 61st Raising Day of Indo-Tibetan Border Police Force (ITBP) wascommemorated with great enthusiasm at the Basic Training Centre in Bhanu nearChandigarh on Monday.

Concept note-2: Indo-Tibetan Border Policewas raised on October 24, 1962 for reorganizing the frontier intelligenceand security set up along the Indo-Tibetan border. Only four Battalionswere sanctioned to begin with. ITBP was initially raised under the CRPFAct.

Concept note-3: Senior IPS officers Anish Dayal Singh and Sujoy LalThaosen on Monday took charge as the new director general of the Indo-TibetanBorder Police and the CRPF respectively.

Concept note-4: Senior IPS officerAnish Dayal Singh on Monday took charge as the new director general (DG) of theIndo-Tibetan Border Police that guards the 3,488-km Line of Actual Control withChina.

Concept note-5: Indo-Tibetan Border Police Constable salary inIndia ranges between ₹ 3.6 Lakhs to ₹ 6.5 Lakhs with an average annualsalary of ₹ 4.8 Lakhs. Salary estimates are based on 13 Indo-Tibetan BorderPolice latest salaries received from various employees of Indo-Tibetan BorderPolice.
