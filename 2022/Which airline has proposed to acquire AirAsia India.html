Which airline has proposed to acquire AirAsia India?

(A) Vistara

(B) SpiceJet

(C) ** Air India

(D) Indigo

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: In a fillip to the integration of airline entities owned byTata Sons, the Competition Commission of Indiaon Tuesday approved AirIndia’s proposal to acquire the entire shareholding of low-cost subsidiaryAirAsia India.

Concept note-2: Tata Sons, via its fully owned subsidiary,Talace Private Limited (“Talace)”, had acquired 100% stake in Air India on27th January 2022.

Concept note-3: Tatas are consolidating their fourairlines - full-service Vistara into Air India and LCC AirAsia India into AIExpressWhile the LCC merger has to be completed within a year, the fullserviceones have to be integrated by March 2024 after getting regulatoryapprovals.

Concept note-4: NEW DELHI : AirAsia Bhd exited the Indianbudget airline that carried its name, with its majority Indian partner, TataGroup’s Air India, agreeing to acquire the 16.67% stake the Malaysian carrierstill owned in AirAsia India for ₹155.65 crore.

Concept note-5: This25.1 per cent stake will be in an enlarged Air India group – it will have AirIndia, Vistara, AirAsia India and Air India Express – and the merger of allairlines is targeted for completion by March 2024, subject to regulatoryapprovals.
