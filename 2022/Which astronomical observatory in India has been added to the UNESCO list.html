Which astronomical observatory in India has been added to the UNESCO list?

(A) Vainu Bappu Astronomical Observatory

(B) Madras astronomical observatory

(C) IUCAA Girawali Observatory

(D) ** Bihar Astronomical Observatory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: 106-year-old astronomical observatory at the LangatSingh College, Muzaffarpur in Bihar has been added to UNESCO Worldheritage observatories list. This observatory is first of its kind in eastern part ofIndia.

Concept note-2: The 106-year-old astronomical observatory of LangatSingh College in Muzaffarpur, was claimed to have been included in UNESCO’s listof endangered institutions. The college setup in 1899 is affiliated with Bhim RaoAmbedkar Bihar University (BRABU). This observatory was established in thecollege in 1916.

Concept note-3: B [Bihar] :The astronomical observatory at theLS College, Muzaffarpur has been included in UNESCO list of ImportantEndangered Heritage Observatories of the World.

Concept note-4: The first sitesto be listed were the Ajanta Caves, Ellora Caves, Agra Fort, and Taj Mahal, all ofwhich were inscribed in the 1983 session of the World Heritage Committee. The mostrecent site listed was Dholavira, in 2021.

Concept note-5: Astrosat.AstroSat is India’s first dedicated Space Astronomy Observatory launched into a650-km, 6° inclination orbit on September 28, 2015, with a lift-off massof 1515 kg, by PSLV-C30 (XL) rocket from Satish Dhawan Space CentreSriharikota.
