Which country had initiated the idea of observing the International Mother Language Day?

(A) ** Bangladesh

(B) Sri Lanka

(C) India

(D) Nepal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The idea to celebrate International Mother Language Day wasthe initiative of Bangladesh. It was approved at the 1999 UNESCO GeneralConference and has been observed throughout the world since 2000. UNESCObelieves in the importance of cultural and linguistic diversity for sustainablesocieties.

Concept note-2: International Mother Language Day is a publicholiday in Bangladesh, where it is also known as Shohid Dibôsh, or ShaheedDay. It is a global observance but not a public holiday in other parts of theworld.

Concept note-3: Bangladesh is the only nation in the world to havefought for and won its independence on the basis of preserving the right tospeak its own language, and this day has a special emotional significanceacross the country.

Concept note-4: February 21st has been recognizedas the “International Mother Language Day” by UNESCO. In India, wealso refer to it as the Matribhasha Diwas. This idea of celebrating motherlanguages was first presented by Bangladesh at the 1999 UNESCO GeneralConference.

Concept note-5: In Bangladesh, 21 February (Ekushey February) isobserved as Language Movement Day, a national holiday. In 1999, UNESCOdeclared 21 February as International Mother Language Day, in tribute to theLanguage Movement and the ethnolinguistic rights of people around theworld.
