Which country has become the second largest oil supplier of India?

(A) Iraq

(B) ** Russia

(C) United Arab emirates

(D) Iran

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Russia now supplies almost 22% of India’s total crude imports,ahead of Iraq’s 20.5 per cent and Saudi Arabia’s 16 per cent.

Concept note-2: Russia,in that month, became India’s second-biggest supplier. Imports dipped marginally inthe following two months.

Concept note-3: Russia has for the first time emergedas top oil supplier to India replacing Iraq as refiners last month snapped up oilfrom Moscow fearing a price cap from Dec.

Concept note-4: Russia isnow India’s second-biggest source of crude oil, after Iraq. Until February,only around 2% (12 million barrels of Urals crude) of India’s total crude oilimports came from Russia.

Concept note-5: Largest global crude oilimporters 2021In 2021, Europe imported more oil and its products thanany other region across the globe, at roughly 13.5 million barrels per day.China followed closely as the second-largest importer, with 12.7 million dailybarrels.
