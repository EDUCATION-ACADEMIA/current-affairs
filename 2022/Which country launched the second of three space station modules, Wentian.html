Which country launched the second of three space station modules, ‘Wentian’?

(A) Russia

(B) ** China

(C) Israel

(D) South Korea

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: China launches second space station module,Wentian.

Concept note-2: China launched on Sunday the second of threemodules needed to complete its new space station, state media reported, the lateststep in Beijing’s ambitious space programme.

Concept note-3: Wentian, whichliterally means “quest for the heavens,” is the second of three modules planned forlaunch by China. A third, named Mengtian, is scheduled to launch in October andwill complete the T-shaped Tiangong space station.

Concept note-4: Chinasuccessfully launched its space station lab module Wentian, the largest spacecraft everdeveloped by the country, into orbit on Sunday afternoon.

Concept note-5: TheWentian module was launched atop a Long March 5B rocket from HainanIsland at 2:22 p.m. local time on Sunday. Wentian, China’s largest everspacecraft weighing 23 metric tons, is equipped with four scientific cabinets toconduct ecological and biotechnological experiments under varying gravityconditions.
