Which country’s museum has signed an agreement with India to repatriate seven stolen artefacts to India in August 2022?

(A) Switzerland

(B) ** Scotland

(C) Canada

(D) Denmark

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The correct answer is Scotland. Museums in the Scottish cityof Glasgow on 19 August 2022 signed an agreement with the Indian govt torepatriate seven stolen artefacts to India. This is Scotland’s “largest-ever”repatriation of objects from a single collection.

Concept note-2: GlasgowMuseums has agreed to return seven stolen artefacts to India. Delegates from theIndian High Commission sealed the move by signing an agreement at the KelvingroveArt Gallery and Museum. It is thought to be the first repatriation to India from aUK museum - with more to follow elsewhere.

Concept note-3: The BritishMuseum, which houses more than 8 million artifacts like the Benin Bronzes and theParthenon Marbles, possesses the most number of stolen goods, human rights lawyerGeoffrey Robertson argued.

Concept note-4: The shrine is in India, but you cansee some of the sculptures, also known as the Amaravati Marbles, at the BritishMuseum. The Amaravati sculptures consist of carved relief panels showingnarrative scenes from the life of the Buddha, as well as Buddhist emblems andsymbols.

Concept note-5: Six of the seven items, including the 11 Century stonedoor jams and 14th century carvings were stolen from shrines and temples in the19th century.
