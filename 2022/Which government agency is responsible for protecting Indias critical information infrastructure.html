Which government agency is responsible for protecting India’s critical information infrastructure?

(A) ICERT

(B) National Cyber Coordination Centre

(C) ** NCIIPC

(D) National Informatics Centre

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Nodal agency: National Critical Information InfrastructureProtection Centre (NCIIPC) is the nodal agency in India that is responsible forprotecting Critical Information Infrastructure. It is part of the NationalTechnical Research Organization (NTRO) and comes under the Prime Minister’sOffice (PMO).

Concept note-2: The Role of the Office of InfrastructureProtectionIP acts on behalf of the Secretary of Homeland Security, implementingthe national critical infrastructure protection responsibilities set forth inPresidential Policy Directive (PPD) 21: Critical Infrastructure Security andResilience.

Concept note-3: The Information Technology Act, 2000 definesCritical Information Infrastructure (CII) as “… those computer resource, theincapacitation or destruction of which, shall have debilitating impact on nationalsecurity, economy, public health or safety".

Concept note-4: Access informationon infrastructure and governance provided by the Ministry of Electronics &Information Technology. Users can get details about the National KnowledgeNetwork (NKN) and e-Infrastructure such as Information Technology InvestmentRegions (ITIR).

Concept note-5: According to section 70B of the InformationTechnology Act, 2000 (IT Act), the Union Government by notification should appointan agency named Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERTIn) to serve asthe national agency for incident response.
