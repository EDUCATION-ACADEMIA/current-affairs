Which institution released the ‘Global Findex database 2021’?

(A) IMF

(B) ** World Bank

(C) RBI

(D) World Economic Forum

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Q. Which institution released the ‘Global Findex database2021’? Notes: The World Bank released the Global Findex Database 2021:Financial Inclusion, Digital Payments, and Resilience in the Age of COVID-19 Itsurveyed how people in 123 economies use financial services throughout2021.

Concept note-2: Collected by Gallup, Inc. during the COVID-19pandemic, the Global Findex 2021 is the world’s most comprehensive databaseon financial inclusion and the only demand-side data source for global andregional cross-country analysis. The 2021 edition follows earlier editionsfrom 2011, 2014 and 2017.

Concept note-3: Adults with an account(%), 2011–2021Worldwide account ownership has reached 76 percent of theglobal population-and 71 percent of people in developing countries. Thegender gap in account ownership across developing economies has fallen to6 percentage points from 9 percentage points, where it hovered for manyyears.

Concept note-4: India is among seven countries home to half the world’s1.4 billion adults without access to formal banking, a recent World Bankreport has flagged. The report also noted that in Sub-Saharan Africa, youngadults (ages 15–24) make up almost 40 per cent of those without access tobanking.

Concept note-5: Nearly eight in 10 adults in China now have a bankaccount, according to the 2014 Global Findex. This represents a 15 percentage pointincrease since 2011.
