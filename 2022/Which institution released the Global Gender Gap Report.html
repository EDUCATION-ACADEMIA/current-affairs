Which institution released the ‘Global Gender Gap Report’?

(A) International Monetary Fund

(B) ** World Economic Forum

(C) World Bank

(D) UN Women

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Notes: The Global Gender Gap Index for 2022 wasrecently released by the World Economic Forum (WEF). Iceland (90.8%) ledthe global ranking in the first place while India is ranked at 135 out of 146countries.

Concept note-2: Ranking: Iceland has retained its position as the world’smost gender-equal country, among 146 nations on the index. Finland, Norway, NewZealand and Sweden are the top five countries on the list respectively. Afghanistan isthe worst-performing country in the report.

Concept note-3: Iceland, Finland,and Norway bagged the top three positions with a score of 90.8 percent, 86 percent,and 84.5 percent respectively. The COVID-19 pandemic has had a lasting impacton gender equality.

Concept note-4: The Global Gender Gap Indexwas first introduced by the World Economic Forum in 2006 as a frameworkfor capturing the magnitude of gender-based disparities and tracking theirprogress over time.

Concept note-5: The Gender Inequality Index is acomposite measure reflecting inequality in achievements between The GII is acomposite measure, reflecting inequality in achievements between women andmen in three dimensions: reproductive health, empowerment and the labourmarket.
