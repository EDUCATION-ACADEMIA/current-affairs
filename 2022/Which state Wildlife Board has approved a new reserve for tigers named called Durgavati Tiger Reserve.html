Which state Wildlife Board has approved a new reserve for tigers named called Durgavati Tiger Reserve?

(A) ** Madhya Pradesh

(B) Uttar Pradesh

(C) Gujarat

(D) Maharashtra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The Madhya Pradesh Wildlife Board recently approved thecreation of the Durgavati Tiger Reserve. It will host tigers of Panna Tiger Reserve(PTR), quarter of which is set to be submerged because of the linking of Ken-Betwarivers.

Concept note-2: The Durgavati Tiger Reserve is a 2,339 square kilometersnew tiger reserve that will spread across Narsinghpur, Damoh, and Sagar districtsapproved by Madhya Pradesh Wildlife Board.

Concept note-3: VeeranganaDurgawati Wildlife Sanctuary is a wildlife sanctuary in Damoh district of MadhyaPradesh, India. Named after Rani Durgavati, a queen of the Gondi people,and covering an area of only 24 sq km, the sanctuary was notified by theGovernment of Madhya Pradesh in 1996.

Concept note-4: The GuruGhasidas National Park and Tamor Pingla Wildlife Sanctuary have beenunited to form India’s newest tiger reserve, which is located in the stateof Chhattisgarh. This will be the fourth tiger reserve in Chhattisgarh andthe 53rd tiger reserve in all of India.

Concept note-5: Until 2018, 50protected areas have been designated tiger reserves. In 2022, 53rd tiger reservein India was declared in Ranipur Wildlife Sanctuary, Uttar Pradesh, andthe State’s fourth tiger reserve. India is home to 80 percent of tigers in theworld.
