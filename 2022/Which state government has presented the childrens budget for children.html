Which state government has presented the children’s budget for children?

(A) Government of Maharashtra

(B) Gujarat government

(C) Government of Kerala

(D) ** Government of Madhya Pradesh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Abhishek Mishra. Explanation: The Madhya Pradesh state government for the first time presented a ‘child budget’ as part of its annual financial plan.

Concept note-2: -A total of ₹1896.35 crs is allocated in the Child Budget in 2021-22 which is 9.2 percent of total State Plan outlay (8.9 percent in 2020- 21).

Concept note-3: -While the size of the Budget grew by 119.78%; from Rs 1,794,892 crore in 2014-15 to Rs 3,944,909 crore in 2022-23, the budget for children grew by only 14.38%, from Rs 81,075 crore in 2014-15 to Rs 92,736.5 crore in 2022-23.

Concept note-4: -The total allocation for children in Union Budget 2023 is Rs. 92,736.5 crore, against an allocation of Rs. 85,712.56 crore in the last Budget. Though this is an increase of 8.19% in absolute terms, it’s not proportionate to the increase in the total expenditure in the Union Budget.

Concept note-5: -"There has been a 0.05 percentage points decline in the share of allocation for child budget to the Union Budget from 2.35 per cent (2022-23 BE) to 2.30 per cent (2023-24 BE).
