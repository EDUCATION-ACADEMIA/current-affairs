Which state has the largest gold reserves in the country?

(A) Chhattisgarh

(B) Jharkhand

(C) ** Bihar

(D) Gujarat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: In certain areas, G2 (general) exploration can also be carriedout, Ms. Bamhrah said. Union Minister of Mines Pralhad Joshi had last yearinformed the Lok Sabha that Bihar holds the highest share of India’s goldreserves.

Concept note-2: Karnataka is the largest producer of gold inIndia.

Concept note-3: Indian gold reserves and resources are concentrated inKarnataka and Rajasthan. The majority of these reserves are located in the state ofKarnataka and account for 88% of the total; a further 12% are situated inAndhra Pradesh and an insignificant amount (less than 0.1t) are found inJharkhand.

Concept note-4: The Jamui district of Bihar has the India’s largestgold reserve for which the state government has accorded permission for exploration.As per a Geological Survey of India (GSI) survey, around 222.88 million tonnes of thegold reserve, including 37.6 tonnes of mineral-rich ore, are present in Jamuidistrict.
