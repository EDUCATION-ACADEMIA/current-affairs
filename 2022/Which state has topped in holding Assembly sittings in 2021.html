Which state has topped in holding Assembly sittings in 2021?

(A) ** Kerala

(B) Maharashtra

(C) Uttar Pradesh

(D) Rajasthan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Q. Which state held the most number of sittings of the State Assembly in 2021? Notes: As per the recent study of the think tank PRS Legislative Research (PRS), Kerala held the most number of sittings of the State Assembly in 2021 at 61 days.

Concept note-2: -The biggest state, Uttar Pradesh, has 403 members in its Assembly. States which have small populations and are small in size have a provision for having an even smaller number of members in the Legislative Assembly.

Concept note-3: -The Constitution of India states that a State Legislative Assembly must have no less than 60 and no more than 500 members however an exception may be granted via an Act of Parliament as is the case in the states of Goa, Sikkim, Mizoram and the union territory of Puducherry which have fewer than 60 members.

Concept note-4: -Length of sessions The longest was the first (76th Congress), which lasted 58 days, meeting (usually every third day) between November 7, 1940 (the day after election day), and January 3, 1941.
