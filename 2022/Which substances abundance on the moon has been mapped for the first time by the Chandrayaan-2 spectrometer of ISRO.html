Which substance’s abundance on the moon has been mapped for the first time by the Chandrayaan-2 spectrometer of ISRO?

(A) ** Sodium

(B) Iron

(C) Nitrogen

(D) Calcium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: The X-ray spectrometer CLASS on the Chandrayaan-2Orbiter has mapped the abundance of sodium on the Moon for the firsttime.

Concept note-2: Chandrayaan-2 Orbiter’s X-ray spectrometer ‘CLASS’ hasmapped an abundance of sodium on the moon for the first time, as per the IndianSpace Research Organisation (ISRO).

Concept note-3: Chandrayaan-1 X-rayFluorescence Spectrometer (C1XS) detected sodium from its characteristic line inX-rays. The X-ray spectrometer ‘CLASS’ on the Chandrayaan-2 Orbiter has mappedan abundance of sodium on the moon for the first time, according to the IndianSpace Research Organisation (ISRO).

Concept note-4: The major discovery ofthe Chandrayaan-1 mission is the detection of water (H2O) and hydroxyl (OH) onthe lunar surface. The data also revealed their enhanced abundance towards thepolar region.

Concept note-5: Scientists used the CLASS instrument(Chandrayaan-2 large area soft X-ray spectrometer) carried by the secondIndian Moon mission, Chandrayaan-2. This is the first effort to provide aglobal-scale measurement of sodium on the lunar surface using X-ray fluorescentspectra.
