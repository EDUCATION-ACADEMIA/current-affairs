Which country banned the import of goods from China’s Xinjiang province to prevent the use of forced labour?

(A) ** USA

(B) Australia

(C) UAE

(D) Ukraine

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tough new US regulations on the import of goods from the Xinjiang region of China have come into effect. Under the rules, firms have to prove imports from the region are not produced using forced labour.

Concept note-2: -After finding out that the Chinese products are produced with forced labour, the US imposed a ban on Xinjiang imports, which hit China’s supply chain.

Concept note-3: -The ban, which is aimed at pressuring Beijing over the forced labor of the minority Uyghur Muslims in Xinjiang, puts the onus on importing companies to ensure none of their goods were even partially made in that region. The blanket measure has threatened to reshape the broader US-China trade relationship.

Concept note-4: -For garment manufacturers in South and Southeast Asia, a sweeping United States ban on all Xinjiang products – including cotton – may initially seem like a windfall, given its smothering effect on China’s role as the world’s top textile maker.

Concept note-5: -UPSC Mains. Q. Which country has banned Chinese goods after naming them products of “Forced Labour’? Notes: The United States has recently banned eight Chinese goods including cotton, hair products, computer components and some textiles from China’s Xinjiang province.
