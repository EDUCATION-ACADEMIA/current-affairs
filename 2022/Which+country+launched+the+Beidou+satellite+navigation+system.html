Which country launched the ‘Beidou’ satellite navigation system?

(A) Israel

(B) Japan

(C) ** China

(D) Russia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -China on November 4, 2022 outlined plans to further expand the global reach of its home-grown Beidou satellite navigation system, billed as its alternative to America’s Global Positioning System (GPS).

Concept note-2: -Beidou is the Chinese version of GPS. But with 45 satellites in orbit, it is the largest network of global positioning satellites ever built. GPS has 11 command and control antennas and 16 monitoring sites around the world, according to the US government. There are no GPS ground stations located in China.

Concept note-3: -China’s BeiDou navigation project was launched in the early 1990s. The system then became operational within China in 2000 and in the Asia-Pacific region in 2012.

Concept note-4: -BeiDou-3 is a new global navigation satellite system being developed by China National Space Administration (CNSA) as an alternative to the global positioning system (GPS) of the US, Russia’s GLONASS, and Europe’s Galileo.

Concept note-5: -About ”Beidou Satellite Navigation System” BeiDou is a global GNSS owned and operated by the People’s Republic of China. BDS was formally commissioned in 2020. The operational system of BeiDou consists of 35 satellites. BDS was previously called Compass.
