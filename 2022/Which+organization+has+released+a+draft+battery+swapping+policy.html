Which organization has released a draft battery swapping policy?

(A) ** NITI Aayog

(B) National Development Council

(C) Small Industries Development Bank of India

(D) Invest India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -UPSC Mains. Q. Which institution has released a draft battery swapping policy for Electric Vehicles? Notes: Government think-tank Niti Aayog has prepared a draft battery swapping policy, to support the adoption of battery-swapping, mainly in electric scooters and three-wheeler electric rickshaw.

Concept note-2: -Keeping in mind the benefits of Electric vehicles, Niti Aayog released a draft policy in April 2022 on battery swaps for two- and three-wheelers. This battery swap policy helps reduce battery costs and promotes affordability. Furthermore, it will boost the sale of electric vehicles.

Concept note-3: -The correct answer is Niti Aayog. Niti Aayog has released a draft battery swapping policy. It is aimed at catalyzing large-scale adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) by making them more accessible, affordable, and efficient.

Concept note-4: -The draft policy is aimed at helping India achieve its net-zero target by 2070. Although efforts are underway to boost the availability of charging infrastructure, battery swapping is an alternative which involves exchanging discharged batteries for charged ones and providing the flexibility to charge them separately.

Concept note-5: -In a nutshell, battery swapping is a take on omitting the charging session wait time and exchanging the discharged battery for a fully charged one. The main goal behind it is to even out the charging experience with the one everyone knows from fuelling up an ICE car.
