Which pandemic PM Care for Children scheme has been unveiled in India?

(A) Swine flu

(B) Plague

(C) Kavil

(D) ** Covid-19

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Children are entitled to receive monthly stipend between the age of 18 and 23 years, by investing the corpus of Rs. 10 lakhs into Monthly Income Scheme of Post Office. They will receive the amount of Rs. 10 lakh on attaining the age of 23 years.

Concept note-2: -The PM CARES for Children Scheme was launched by The Hon’ble Prime Minister of India on 29th May 2021 for the Children.

Concept note-3: -“That the PM CARES Fund is a public charitable trust comprising of voluntary donations made by individuals and institutions and is not a business of the Central Government. Besides, the PM CARES Fund does not receive funds or finances by the Government,” the response adds.

Concept note-4: -The initial purpose of establishing PMNRF (Prime Minister National Relief Fund) was to help the people displaced due to partition of India and Pakistan. The PM CARES fund was established with the objective of helping people affected by COVID-19 pandemic.
