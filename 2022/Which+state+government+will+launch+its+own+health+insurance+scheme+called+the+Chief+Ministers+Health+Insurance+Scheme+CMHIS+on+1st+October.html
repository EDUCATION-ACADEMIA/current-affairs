Which state government will launch its own health insurance scheme called the Chief Minister’s Health Insurance Scheme (CMHIS) on 1st October?

(A) ** Nagaland

(B) Kerala

(C) Haryana

(D) Assam

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Amma Health Insurance scheme or CMCHIS was launched by the Tamil Nadu government to reduce the finial burden for low-income families during any medical scheme. Based on cashless facilities, the health insurance scheme provides extensive coverage for hospitalisation, follow-up treatments and diagnostic treatments.

Concept note-2: -CMHIS is a significant step toward achieving the Nagaland Sustainable Development Goal Vision 2030, which was introduced in August 2021. This goal envisions that by the year 2030, the people of the state of Nagaland will be provided with equitable, affordable, and high-quality healthcare services.

Concept note-3: -Rajasthan became the first state in the country to provide health insurance for all its residents. The state began registration for Mukhyamantri Chiranjeevi Swasthya Bima Yojana under which each family in the state can claim up to Rs five lakh a year for medical expenses.

Concept note-4: -Chiranjeevi Health Insurance Scheme: Rajasthan is a government scheme that provides cashless medical insurance to the permanent residents of Rajasthan, for up to Rs. 5 Lakh. The scheme was made available to all the families of Rajasthan from May 1, 2021.

Concept note-5: -PM-JAY is the world’s largest health insurance/ assurance scheme fully financed by the government. It provides a cover of Rs. 5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization across public and private empanelled hospitals in India.
