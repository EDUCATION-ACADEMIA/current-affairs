Which two countries have applied to join BRICS Grouping?

(A) Tajikistan and Columbia

(B) ** Iran and Argentina

(C) Iraq and Saudi Arabia

(D) Kenya and Chile

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Since South Africa joined the BRIC grouping (now BRICS) in 2010, numerous other countries have expressed interest in joining the bloc, including Argentina and Iran.

Concept note-2: -Concerning a BRICS expansion, Lavrov stated that Algeria, Argentina, and Iran had all applied, while it is already known that Saudi Arabia, Türkiye, Egypt and Afghanistan are interested, along with Indonesia, which is expected to make a formal application to join at the upcoming G20 summit in Bali.

Concept note-3: -Recent news that other mostly non-democratic states – Egypt, Iran, Saudi Arabia, Turkey (and Argentina) – have either applied to join the BRICS, or are considering doing so, is also cause for concern among Western governments.

Concept note-4: -Two new countries - Iran and Argentina - have applied to join the increasingly influential BRICS - Brazi, Russia, India, China and South Africa - grouping, an Iranian official said Monday, according to news agency Reuters. The official said Iran’s inclusion in BRICS would result in ‘added values for both sides’.

Concept note-5: -Iranian official says membership ‘would result in added values for both sides’.
