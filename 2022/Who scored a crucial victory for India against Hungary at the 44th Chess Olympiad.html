Who scored a crucial victory for India against Hungary at the 44th Chess Olympiad?

(A) Koneru Humpy

(B) Harika Dronavalli

(C) Suhani Shah

(D) ** Tania Sachdev

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: Tania Sachdev battled long and hard to rake in a preciouspoint as India A registered a sensational 2.5-1.5 win against Hungary in thefourth-round match of women section at the 44th Chess Olympiad in Mamallapuram,Chennai on Monday.

Concept note-2: It started on 19 July at the Indira GandhiArena in New Delhi, where FIDE President Arkady Dvorkovich handed the torch tothe Indian prime minister Narendra Modi, who passed it to former World ChessChampion Viswanathan Anand.

Concept note-3: D Gukesh and Nihal Sarin wonthe gold medals on the men’s board 1 and board 2, respectively, while Arjun Erigaisibagged the silver medal on board 3.

Concept note-4: Nihal Sarin and RaunakSadhwani scored crucial victories over Matthias Bluebaum and Liviu-DieterNisipeanu respectively in the final round.

Concept note-5: History was made inthe open section of the FIDE Chess Olympiad, which came to an end thismorning in Chennai, India. Uzbekistan, one of the youngest teams on theroster, defeated rating favourites the Netherlands by 2.5-1.5, securing the goldmedal.
