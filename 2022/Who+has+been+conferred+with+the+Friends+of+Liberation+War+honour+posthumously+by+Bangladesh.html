Who has been conferred with the ‘Friends of Liberation War’ honour posthumously by Bangladesh?

(A) ** Edward M Kennedy

(B) Harry Reid

(C) Johnny Isakson

(D) Bob Dole

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bangladesh Prime Minister Sheikh Hasina conferred the prestigious ‘Friends of Liberation War’ honour on former US Senator Edward M Kennedy posthumously on Monday in Dhaka for his contribution to the liberation of Bangladesh. The honour was handed over to his son Edward M Ted Kennedy Junior.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is India and Pakistan. 1971 war for Liberation of Bangladesh was fought between India and Pakistan. it resulted in the independence of the People’s Republic of Bangladesh.

Concept note-3: -The Bangladesh Freedom Honour (Bengali: Bānglādēśa sbādhīnatā sam’mānanā) is the highest state award given by the government of Bangladesh for foreigners or non nationals. The award was posthumously conferred on former Indian prime minister Indira Gandhi on 25 July 2011.

Concept note-4: -Osmani joined the Provisional Government of Bangladesh in 1971 as the commander-in-chief of the nascent Bangladesh Forces. Regarded as the founder of the Bangladesh Armed Forces, Osmani retired as a four-star general from the Bangladesh Army in 1972.

Concept note-5: -The East Bengal Regiment and the East Pakistan Rifles played a crucial role in the resistance. Led by General M. A. G. Osmani and eleven sector commanders, the Bangladesh Forces waged a mass guerrilla war against the Pakistani military. They liberated numerous towns and cities in the initial months of the conflict.
