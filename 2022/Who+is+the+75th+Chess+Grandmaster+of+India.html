Who is the 75th Chess Grandmaster of India?

(A) ** V. Pranav

(B) P. Harikrishna

(C) P. Iniyan

(D) Tania Sachdev

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Chess prodigy V Pranav on Sunday became India’s 75th Grandmaster by winning a tournament in Romania. The Chennai-based Pranav won the Limpedea Open in Baia Mare, Romania to secure his third and final GM norm and attain the Grandmaster title.

Concept note-2: -Pranav Anand became India’s 76th Grandmaster when he beat IM Emin Ohanyan (ARM) in the tenth round of World Youth Under-16 Open Championship 2022.

Concept note-3: -Sixteen-year-old Aditya Mittal has become India’s 77th chess Grandmaster during an ongoing tournament in Spain. The Mumbai player, who had secured three GM norms, crossed 2,500 ELO points mark during the sixth round of the ongoing Ellobregat Open tournament in Spain.

Concept note-4: -India’s 78th GM Koustav Chatterjee has earned a lot of medals in various National and International events.

Concept note-5: -Congratulations Mitrabha Guha : 72nd Grandmaster of India – All India Chess Federation.
