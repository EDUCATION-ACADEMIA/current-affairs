World Maritime Day is celebrated on which date?

(A) ** September 29

(B) July 29

(C) August 29

(D) May 29

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International. World Maritime Day is celebrated on the last Thursday of every September, on September 28 this year, to honor the tireless work of the international maritime industry workers. The word ‘maritime’ comes from the Latin word ‘maritimus,’ which means ‘of the sea.

Concept note-2: -The International Maritime Organization observed World Maritime Day on the last Thursday of September. This year, it will be observed on September 29. The day focuses on drawing public attention to maritime safety and the marine environment.

Concept note-3: -Thank you for celebrating World Maritime Day 2022 with us. Our theme for the year has been “New technologies for greener shipping".

Concept note-4: -World Maritime Day was first held in 1978 to mark the 20th anniversary of the IMO Convention’s entry into force. Celebrations are held throughout the world to focus attention on the importance of shipping safety, maritime security and the marine environment, and to emphasize a particular aspect of IMO’s work.

Concept note-5: -The purpose of the day is to appreciate the importance of maritime industry and to underline the importance of maritime security, maritime environment, safety, and shipping. The day also marks the day of adaptation of International Maritime Organisation (IMO) convention in 1958. It was first observed in 1978.
