A UN panel has recommended that which country’s Great Barrier Reef is to be listed as a World Heritage Site in Danger?

(A) ** Australia

(B) Germany

(C) Hong Kong

(D) Italy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A UN panel recommended the listing of Australia’s Great Barrier Reef as a world heritage site that is “in danger”.

Concept note-2: -UNESCO has issued its second recommendation for the Great Barrier Reef to be placed on the In Danger list. UNESCO has again recommended the Reef is added to its In Danger list, citing the impacts of climate change such as frequent coral bleaching and poor water quality as serious threats to the Reef’s future.

Concept note-3: -The Great Barrier Reef was listed as a World Heritage Site in 1981 when UNESCO recognised its environmental wonder and scientific importance.

Concept note-4: -The reef is located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, in the Coral Sea. The reef, which is large enough to be visible from space, is made up of nearly 3,000 individual reefs. Much of the Great Barrier Reef is a marine protected area, managed by the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Authority of Australia.

Concept note-5: -As the world’s most extensive coral reef ecosystem, the Great Barrier Reef is a globally outstanding and significant entity. Practically the entire ecosystem was inscribed as World Heritage in 1981, covering an area of 348,000 square kilometres and extending across a contiguous latitudinal range of 14o (10oS to 24oS).
