‘Alluri Sitarama Raju’ has a freedom fighter from which present-day state?

(A) ** Andhra Pradesh

(B) Karnataka

(C) Kerala

(D) Tamil Nadu

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Born in present-day Andhra Pradesh, he was involved in opposing the British in response to the 1882 Madras Forest Act, which actively restricted the free movement of Adivasis in their forest habitats, and prevented them from practicing their traditional form of agriculture called podu.

Concept note-2: -Alluri visited areas in the district of Visakhapatnam and became aware of the needs of the indigenous people. He was an Indian nationalist active in the struggle for Indian independence. Raju led the 1922-24 Rampa Revolt, in which a coalition of tribal leaders and other supporters fought against the British Raj.

Concept note-3: -Alluri Sitarama Raju Biography Alluri is best remembered for leading the Rampa Rebellion against the British in which he organised the tribal people of Vishakhapatnam and East Godavari districts to revolt against the foreigners.

Concept note-4: -Alluri Sitaram Raju was born on July 4 1857, in Mogallu village of Palakoderu Mandal in West Godavari district in Andhra Pradesh. He started the Rampa rebellion and was popularly known as “Manyam Veerudu” or Hero of the Forest. The freedom fighter was only eight years old when his father passed away.

Concept note-5: -Rising discontent towards the British led to the Rampa Rebellion/Manyam Rebellion of 1922, in which he played a major part as a leader. The Rampa Rebellion coincided with Mahatma Gandhi’s Non-Cooperation Movement. He persuaded people to wear khadi and give up drinking.
