First-Ever ‘Made in India’ surgical robotic system has been installed at which Institute?

(A) Anand Rishiji Hospital

(B) ** Rajiv Gandhi Cancer Institute

(C) Dharamshila Cancer Hospital

(D) AIIMS, New Delhi

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Rajiv Gandhi Cancer Institute and Research Center, New Delhi (RGCI) have installed the first-ever Made-in-India Surgical Robotic System, “SSI-Mantra”, devised by the new-age Indian med-tech start-up SS Innovations.

Concept note-2: -"SSI Mantra, a brainchild of robotic cardiac surgeon Dr Sudhir P Srivastava, has introduced a new era of surgical procedures in India, making robotic surgery accessible and affordable for the people of our country.

Concept note-3: -After the US FDA approval of the da Vinci system in 2000,[2] India got its first urologic robotic installation at the All India Institute of Medical Sciences, New Delhi, in 2006.

Concept note-4: -Robotic surgery in Indraprastha Apollo Hospitals Delhi By combining computer-assisted technology and their expertise, surgeons may perform extremely delicate and precise minimally invasive surgery (MIS) using the Da Vinci System.

Concept note-5: -1980s. The first records of robotic surgery performed date to the 80s. Specifically, the first surgical robot, PUMA 560, was used in a brain biopsy procedure. This procedure took place in 1985 as robotics started to be implemented to reduce movement due to hand tremors.
