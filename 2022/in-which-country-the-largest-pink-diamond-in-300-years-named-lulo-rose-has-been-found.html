In which country, the largest pink diamond in 300 years named ‘Lulo Rose’, has been found?

(A) ** Angola

(B) Oman

(C) Sudan

(D) Ghana

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This rare pink diamond, weighing 34 grams, is thought to be the largest discovered in the last 300 years. The 170-carat stone has been named the “Lulo Rose", after the mine in Angola where it was found.

Concept note-2: -Miners from Angola in Central Africa have discovered a rare pure pink diamond, which is being claimed to be the largest one ever to be found in 300 years as per a report by Phys.org.

Concept note-3: -The largest known pink diamond is the Daria-i-Noor, discovered in India, and weighs an estimated 182 carats. A rare 170-carat pink rough diamond, weighing 34 grams, has been discovered at Lucapa’s Lulo Mine in Angola. Titled ‘The Lulo Rose’, it is touted to be the largest pink diamond discovered in the last 300 years.

Concept note-4: -A rare pure pink diamond, believed to be the largest found in 300 years, has been unearthed in Angola, a country in Central Africa.

Concept note-5: -Angola’s biggest diamond, dubbed the “4th February Stone,” was recovered from the Lulo mine in February 2016, according to Lucapa. The 404.2-carat stone sold for $16 million.
