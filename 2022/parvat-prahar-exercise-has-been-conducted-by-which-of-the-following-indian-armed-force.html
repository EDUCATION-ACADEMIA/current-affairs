‘Parvat Prahar’ Exercise has been conducted by which of the following Indian Armed Force?

(A) Defence Security Corps

(B) ** Indian Army

(C) Indian Navy

(D) Indian Air Force

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Parvat Prahar Exercise is a 20 day long exercise routine conduced by Indian Army to undertake disengagement from Patrolling Point 15 in eastern Ladakh. This exercise was held at a height of 14,000 feet and it used all newly terrain vehicles transported by Chinook heavy lift helicopters and K9 Vajra howitzers.

Concept note-2: -This exercise named Parvat Prahar is being conducted by the One Strike Corps of the Indian Army.

Concept note-3: -Exercise “SURYA KIRAN” is conducted annually between India and Nepal with the aim to enhance interoperability in jungle warfare & counter terrorism operations in mountainous terrain and HADR under UN mandate.

Concept note-4: -Hand-in-Hand is an annual exercise that takes place as part of India’s and China’s military diplomacy and interaction. The exercise will include tactical operations in an UN-mandated multinational counterinsurgency/counterterrorism environment.
