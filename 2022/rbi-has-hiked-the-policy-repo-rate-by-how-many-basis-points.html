RBI has hiked the policy repo rate by how many basis points?

(A) 30 basis points

(B) 45 basis points

(C) ** 40 basis points

(D) 35 basis points

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Basis points (BPS) are used to show the change in the value or rate of a financial instrument, such as 1% change equals a change of 100 basis points and 0.01% change equals one basis point.

Concept note-2: -RBI Repo Rate Hike: The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) recently hiked the repo rate by 35 bps, the fifth such increase in 2022.

Concept note-3: -Reserve Bank of India (RBI), the central banking institution of India controls the monetary policy of the Indian currency. The key repo rate has been hiked on 7 December 2022 by the Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) by 35 basis point to 6.25%.

Concept note-4: -The rate of interest charged by the central bank on the cash borrowed by commercial banks is called the “Repo Rate”. For example: If the Repo Rate is 10% and the loan amount borrowed by a commercial bank from RBI is Rs 10,000, then the interest paid to the RBI will be Rs 1,000.
