The United States announced hosting which edition of the US-Pacific Island Countries Summit?

(A) 2nd

(B) 3rd

(C) 4th

(D) ** 1st

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The U.S.-Pacific Island Country Summit was a meeting hosted by President of the United States Joe Biden with Pacific Island leaders held on 28–29 September 2022 at the White House in Washington, D.C., which coincided with the week of the 77th session of the United Nations General Assembly.

Concept note-2: -The U.S. Pacific Islands region includes our 50th state, Hawai’i, as well as the Territories of Guam, American Samoa, the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI), the Republic of Palau (RP), the Federated States of Micronesia (FSM), and the Republic of the Marshall Islands (RMI).

Concept note-3: -They are the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, the Federated States of Micronesia, Fiji, French Polynesia, Kiribati, the Marshall Islands, Nauru, New Caledonia, New Zealand, Palau, Solomon Islands, Tonga, Tuvalu, Vanuatu, and Wallis and Futuna.

Concept note-4: -Eleven Pacific Island countries are members of the World Bank and have a combined population of about 2.3 million people, scattered across an area equivalent to 15% of the globe’s surface.
