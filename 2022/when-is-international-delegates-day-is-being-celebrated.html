When is International Delegates Day is being celebrated?

(A) April 23

(B) April 26

(C) ** April 25

(D) April 24

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -International Delegate’s Day is observed on 25 April every year. It is observed to raise awareness of the role of the representatives and delegates of the Member States to the United Nations.

Concept note-2: -The International Day for Universal Access to Information is celebrated each year around the world on the 28 September. The global conference opening ceremony taking place in Tashkent, Uzbekistan, will launch the 2022 edition of the Day under the theme “Artificial Intelligence, e-Governance and Access to Information”.

Concept note-3: -There are currently 193 UN Member States. Each of the Member States of the United Nations has one seat in the General Assembly. Information about current members, growth of membership since 1945, the procedure on how a country becomes a member, and the membership of principal UN organs is provided below.

Concept note-4: -The First Continental Congress was a meeting of delegates from 12 of the 13 British colonies that became the United States.

Concept note-5: -n. The body of diplomatic personnel in residence at a nation’s capital.
