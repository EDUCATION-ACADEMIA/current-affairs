Which bank will manage the IVAC in Bangladesh for another two years?

(A) Bank of Baroda

(B) HDFC

(C) Canara Bank

(D) ** SBI

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Abhishek Mishra. Answer: Option C Explanation: The State Bank of India (SBI) will manage the Indian Visa Application Centre (IVAC) in Bangladesh for two more years.

Concept note-2: -Fifteen Indian Visa Application Centres (IVACs) located across Bangladesh are managed by State Bank of India in Bangladesh since December 2005. IVAC Dhaka at Jamuna Future Park, which started its operations from July 2018, is the world’s biggest Indian Visa Application Centre.

Concept note-3: -There are fifteen Indian Visa Application Centers (IVAC) managed by State Bank of India in Bangladesh.

Concept note-4: -The Bangladesh Tourist Visa is a single-entry or multiple-entry visa that allows the holder to enter Bangladesh for up to 60 days. Bangladesh visas usually have a maximum stay of 30 days and come in single entry or multiple entry types.

Concept note-5: -According to Visa Rules-2006 If any foreigner wants to extend his/her visa in Bangladesh he/she has to apply to Department of Passport and Immigration (DIP) by prescribed form. DIP sends this application form to Special Branch (SB) of Bangladesh Police for Police Report.
