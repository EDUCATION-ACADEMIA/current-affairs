Who has become the first Pakistani female singer to win the Grammy Award?

(A) ** Arooj Aftab

(B) Sajjad Ali

(C) Abida Parveen

(D) mustafa zahid

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Winning Best Global Music Performance for her single “Mohabbat,” Aftab became the first Pakistani woman to ever win a GRAMMY.

Concept note-2: -This year, Arooj Aftab became the first Pakistani artist to win a Grammy for her rendition of a famous ghazal by the legendary Mehdi Hassan.

Concept note-3: -The 1st GRAMMY Awards took place in 1958, and women were among the first crop of recipients. The first female multiple GRAMMY winner was jazz singer Ella Fitzgerald, who took home two statues: Best Vocal Performance, Female and Best Jazz Performance, Individual.

Concept note-4: -Arooj Aftab had a dazzling 2021 and 2022. She released her latest album Vulture Prince and soon found herself on Barack Obama’s summer playlist. ‘Mohabbat’, the track featured, has become the crowning glory of her album, and was in contention for a Grammy under the Best Global Music Performance category.

Concept note-5: -Watch Beyoncé Break The Record For Most GRAMMY Wins In History | 2023 GRAMMYs. It’s official: Beyoncé has made GRAMMY history. Her win for Best Dance/Electronic Album for ‘RENAISSANCE’ put her GRAMMY total at 32, the most of all time.
