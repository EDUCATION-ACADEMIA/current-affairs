Youngsters above what age can now apply in advance to enrol on the Voter’s list?

(A) 14

(B) 16

(C) ** 17

(D) 15

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The voting age was reduced from 21 to 18 in the year 1989. Q. When was the voting age reduced from 21 years to 18 years in India ?

Concept note-2: -Every citizen who is or above 18 years of age has a right to vote without any discrimination of caste, race , religion, sex, literacy, etc. The voting age was reduced from 21 to 18 by the Parliament in the year 1989 by the 61st Constitutional Amendment Act of 1988.

Concept note-3: -The bill came into force on 28th March 1989. The Prime Minister who reduced the voting age from 21 to 18 years - Rajiv Gandhi.

Concept note-4: -Not less than Twenty Five Years of age on the date of scrutiny of nomination papers. (Refer : Article 84 (b) of Constitution of India and Article 173 (b) of the Constitution read with Sec. 36 (2) of the Representation of People Act, 1951.)
