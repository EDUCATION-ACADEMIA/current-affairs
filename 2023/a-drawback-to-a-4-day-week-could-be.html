A drawback to a 4 day week could be;

(A) A longer school year

(B) Not enough class time

(C) Too much homework

(D) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Putting in too much time at work, even for shorter stints, can lead to stress, burnout, disconnect, scheduling conflicts, and bigger bills. In short, four-day work weeks might be sweet, but they can fail employees and organizations. So don’t quit your job yet.

Concept note-2: -Here’s what you need to know: Pros of a 4-day workweek can include cost savings, increased productivity, and employee retention. Some disadvantages, however, can include scheduling challenges, reduced producivity, and added stress.

Concept note-3: -A 4-day work week is a schedule where employees work 4 days per week instead of the more traditional 5 days. There are a few different ways that this schedule can be set up, but the most common is to have employees work Monday-Thursday or Tuesday-Friday.

Concept note-4: -Not only does a 4 day work week increase employee satisfaction, company commitment and teamwork, but it also decreases stress levels. Even better, reducing employees’ work schedules to a 4 day work week doesn’t harm their productivity or company output.
