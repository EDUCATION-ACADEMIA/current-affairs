Clove, the commonly used spice is obtained from

(A) Fruit

(B) Stem

(C) Root

(D) ** Flower bud

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is a Flower Bud. Clove is an aromatic flower bud of the trees belonging to Myrtaceae, Syzygium aromaticum.

Concept note-2: -Cloves are the aromatic flower buds of a tree in the family Myrtaceae, Syzygium aromaticum (/sɪˈzɪdʒiːəm ˌærəˈmætɪkəm/). They are native to the Maluku Islands (or Moluccas) in Indonesia, and are commonly used as a spice, flavoring or fragrance in consumer products, such as toothpaste, soaps, or cosmetics.

Concept note-3: -The correct answer is Clove. Clove spices are obtained from flower buds.

Concept note-4: -Clove is a spice which is obtained from the plant Syzygium aromaticum. It is nothing but the aromatic flower buds of the Clove plant.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is Flower bud. Cloves (Syzygium aromaticum) are aromatic dried flower buds. It relates to the family Myrtaceae.
