Emirati women are famous for?

(A) Stick dancing

(B) ** Hair dancing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Khaleegy, or Khaliji, is a traditional folk dance performed by women in the United Arab Emirates and wider Middle East. The women flip their hair in brightly coloured traditional dress.

Concept note-2: -Al Ayyala has been practised for generations by people across the UAE, including both coastal and inland communities. The dance has such rich significance that it was added to UNESCO’s Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2014.

Concept note-3: -Khaleegy’ or Khaliji (from Arabic ) is a mixture of modern style and traditional folkloric dance from the Persian Gulf countries of Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Oman, Bahrain, Qatar and the United Arab Emirates.

Concept note-4: -Anyone who has spent even a short time in this part of the world will be familiar with what is popularly known as the “hair dance” or more correctly “raqs sha’ar” and performed by younger girls at important celebrations and social occasions.
