Featured in Friday’s episode, what do scientists hope to improve with a melting metal robot?

(A) ** surgery

(B) cars

(C) airplanes

(D) movies

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer. The scientists are mainly hoping to increase the accuracy, precision and success rate of the functioning of the melting metal robot.

Concept note-2: -The self-replicating Xenobot was first “conceived” by an artificial intelligence (AI) program working on UVM’s supercomputer. The AI ran an evolutionary algorithm capable of testing billions of biological body shapes in a simulation. The goal was to discover which configuration of cells is capable of self-replication.

Concept note-3: -The US scientists who created the first living robots say the life forms, known as xenobots, can now reproduce – and in a way not seen in plants and animals. Formed from the stem cells of the African clawed frog (Xenopus laevis) from which it takes its name, xenobots are less than a millimeter (0.04 inches) wide.

Concept note-4: -The T-1000 is a fictional character in the Terminator franchise. A shapeshifting android Terminator assassin, the T-1000 is the main antagonist of Terminator 2: Judgment Day, as well as a minor antagonist in Terminator Genisys and the theme park attraction T2 3-D: Battle Across Time.

Concept note-5: -The shape of a xenobot’s body, and its distribution of skin and heart cells, are automatically designed in simulation to perform a specific task, using a process of trial and error (an evolutionary algorithm).
