For how long was the sacred oil found in the temple expected to last?

(A) The sacred oil found in the temple was expected to only light a candle for 8 days.

(B) ** The sacred oil found in the temple was expected to only light a candle for 1 day.

(C) The sacred oil found in the temple was expected to only light a candle for 7 days.

(D) The sacred oil found in the temple was expected to only light a candle for 3 days.

(E) The sacred oil found in the temple was expected to only light a candle for 5 days.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As the story goes, a small quantity of oil to light the Temple’s menorah miraculously lasted eight days.

Concept note-2: -Hanukkah, the eight-day Jewish festival of lights is all about olive oil. According to tradition, during the rededication of the Holy Temple (the Second Temple) in Jerusalem at the time of the Maccabean revolt, a small quantity of oil used to light the Temple’s menorah somehow miraculously burned for eight days.

Concept note-3: -Miraculously, the one cruse of oil lasted eight nights.

Concept note-4: -Hanukkah is observed for eight nights and days every year. Normally it occurs between late November and December, although the exact dates change every year. This is because Hanukkah is always on the 25th day of Kislev in the Hebrew calendar. 25 Kislev is a date from the Hebrew calendar.

Concept note-5: -In order to rededicate the temple, the Maccabees had to light a menorah that would burn within the temple at all times. However, they only had enough pure olive oil to last for one day. Miraculously, the oil burned for eight days, leaving time to find a fresh supply of oil.
