Full name of C N G fuel used in vehicles

(A) ** Compressed Natural gas

(B) Combined Natural Gas

(C) Combo Natural Gas

(D) Confined Natural Gas

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The full form of CNG is Compressed Natural Gas. CNG is a fuel that can be used in replacement of diesel, petrol, and LPG (liquefied petroleum gas). The burning of CNG creates less toxic gases than the previously mentioned fuels.

Concept note-2: -Two forms of natural gas are currently used in vehicles: compressed natural gas (CNG) and liquefied natural gas (LNG).

Concept note-3: -CNG, also known as compressed natural gas, is an eco-friendly alternative to gasoline. Made by compressing natural gas (methane) down to less than 1% of its volume, CNG fuel is safer than gasoline and diesel because it is non-toxic and does not contaminate ground water.

Concept note-4: -The Full Form of the CNG is Compressed Natural Gas.

Concept note-5: -CNG is the compressed form of Natural Gas, CNG is widely used in Auto rickshaws, Cars & Buses. For use in Automobiles as fuel, it is compressed to a pressure of 200-250 bar to enhance the vehicle on-board storage capacity. CNG or Compressed Natural Gas is a fossil fuel substitute for petrol, diesel, or LPG.
