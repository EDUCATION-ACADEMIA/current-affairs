GARAMPANI SANCTURY IS LOCATED AT

(A) GANGATOK, SIKKIM

(B) KOHIMA, NAGALAND

(C) ** DIPHU, ASSAM

(D) JUNAGARH, GUJRAT

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Garampani Wildlife Sanctuary is a 6.05-square-kilometre (2.34 sq mi) wildlife sanctuary located in Karbi Anglong district, Assam, India.

Concept note-2: -Garampani Wildlife Sanctuary is a 6.05-square-kilometer wildlife sanctuary in Assam’s Diphu (Karbi Anglong district). It is 25 kilometres from Golaghat. It is one of the oldest sanctuaries, with hot springs and waterfalls, and is flanked by Nambor Sanctuary, which has 51 unique orchid species.

Concept note-3: -Garampani Wildlife sanctuary is situated in Karbi Anglong district of Assam. It is 25 km from Golaghat and 65 km from Kaziranga National Park. This area is famous for hot water springs.

Concept note-4: -One of Assam’s oldest sanctuaries, the Garampani Wildlife Sanctuary is located in the Karbi Anglong area. Garampani means ‘hot water’ in the local language, and the sanctuary takes its name from the fact that it is famous for its hot springs and waterfalls.
