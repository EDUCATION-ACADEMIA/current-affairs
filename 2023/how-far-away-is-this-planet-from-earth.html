How far away is this planet from Earth?

(A) 600 miles

(B) 40 light years

(C) 50 miles

(D) ** 640 light years

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A light-year is the distance a beam of light travels in a single Earth year, which equates to approximately 6 trillion miles (9.7 trillion kilometers).

Concept note-2: -Light-year is the distance light travels in one year. Light zips through interstellar space at 186, 000 miles (300, 000 kilometers) per second and 5.88 trillion miles (9.46 trillion kilometers) per year.

Concept note-3: -A light-year, alternatively spelled light year, is a large unit of length used to express astronomical distances and is equivalent to about 9.46 trillion kilometers (9.46×1012 km), or 5.88 trillion miles (5.88×1012 mi).

Concept note-4: -The white dwarf is approximately 200 light years from Earth and is located in the constellation Boötes.
