How many wheels a auto rickshaw has

(A) ** 3

(B) 2

(C) 9

(D) 4

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An auto rickshaw is a motorized version of the pulled rickshaw or cycle rickshaw. Most have three wheels and do not tilt.

Concept note-2: -Answers. Three wheeled cars/rickshaws were cheap to manufacture, at a time when raw materials were scarce, small enough to park easily in cities designed and relatively fuel efficient. They are also much more stable than their two-wheeled cousins.

Concept note-3: -NEW DELHI-They’re one of the most recognizable icons of modern India. The auto rickshaw, a sputtering motorized three-wheeler that’s less than half the cost of a regular taxi, is the backbone of urban transit for millions of Indians.

Concept note-4: -Although a three-wheel design makes sense for many reasons—they tend to be more aerodynamic than boxy cars, they offer greater protection than a motorcycle, low weight plus big engine means lots of fun—the benefits can be outweighed by the instability.
