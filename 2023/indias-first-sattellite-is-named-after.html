INDIAS FIRST SATTELLITE IS NAMED AFTER?

(A) ** AARYABHATTA

(B) BHASKARACHARYA 2

(C) BHASKARACHARYA 1

(D) ALBERT EINSTEIN

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Aryabhata spacecraft, named after the famous Indian astronomer, was India’s first satellite; it was completely designed and fabricated in India and launched by a Soviet Kosmos-3M rocket from Kapustin Yar on April 19, 1975.

Concept note-2: -Space: Fact or Fiction? ISRO’s first satellite, Aryabhata, was launched by the Soviet Union on April 19, 1975. Rohini, the first satellite to be placed in orbit by an Indian-made launch vehicle (the Satellite Launch Vehicle 3), was launched on July 18, 1980.

Concept note-3: -India’s first satellite Aryabhatta was named after the mathematician and astronomer of 5th-century Aryabhatta. It was India’s first satellite and was launched on 19th April 1975 from Kapustin Yar, a Soviet rocket launch and development site in Astrakhan Oblast using a Kosmos-3M launch vehicle.
