International Youth day celebrated on

(A) 19 August

(B) 11 August

(C) ** 12 August

(D) 17 August

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 1998, a resolution proclaiming 12 August as International Youth Day was adopted by the first session of the the World Conference of Ministers Responsible for Youth, which was hosted by the Government of Portugal in cooperation with the United Nations (Lisbon, 8-12 August 1998).

Concept note-2: -Event: International Youth Day 2022 | SDG Knowledge Hub | IISD. International Youth Day is celebrated annually on 12 August to bring youth issues to the attention of the international community and celebrate the potential of youth as partners in today’s global society.

Concept note-3: -In 1999, in its resolution 54/120, the General Assembly endorsed the recommendation made by the World Conference of Ministers Responsible for Youth (Lisbon, 8-12 August 1998) that 12 August be declared International Youth Day.

Concept note-4: -Intergenerational Solidarity: Creating a World for All Ages. the objective of International Youth Day 2022 is to amplify the message that action is needed across all generations to achieve the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and leave no one behind.
