Many older Emirati men prefer traditional Emirati clothes, such as the

(A) Ghutra

(B) ** Kandura

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The kandura is worn with a ghutrah headdress made of cotton. These were traditionally worn to protect the head and face from the desert heat and sand.

Concept note-2: -The traditional dress of Emirati men is a Dishdasha or a Kandura. This is a single-piece ankle-length garment. In their homes, they sometimes wear half-sleeved Kanduras, but formal Kanduras are always full-sleeved.

Concept note-3: -Emirati men choose to wear a kandura, a white tunic with an ankle-length woven from wool or cotton, and Emirati women are worn with a Burqa. ON AVERAGE, a UAE male national will have to change her clothes up to 50 Kanduras to ensure that the dress is clean.

Concept note-4: -The traditional dress of a UAE woman is the abaya. It is important to note that the abaya is not really a dress. Instead it can be more accurately compared to a thin, flowing cloak that covers the body.
