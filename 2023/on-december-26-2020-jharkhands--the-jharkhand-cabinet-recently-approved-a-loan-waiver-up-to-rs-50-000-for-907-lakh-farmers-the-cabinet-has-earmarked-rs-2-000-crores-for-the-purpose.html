On December 26, 2020, Jharkhand’s ____ :The Jharkhand Cabinet recently approved a loan waiver up to Rs 50, 000 for 9.07 lakh farmers. The cabinet has earmarked Rs 2, 000 crores for the purpose.

(A) ** Farm Loan Waiver Scheme

(B) Farmers Loan Waiver Scheme

(C) Farming Loan Waiver Scheme

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A loan waive-off is a benefit offered to borrowers by the government where the borrower is no longer under the burden of paying back the loan amount to the lender as a result of a genuine change in their financial circumstances. Examples of loan waivers are the Agricultural Debt Waiver and Debt Relief Scheme in India.

Concept note-2: -Reputational Consequences: Loan waiver schemes will disrupt credit discipline as farm loan waivers may act as a temporary solution and can prove to be a moral hazard in future. This is because those farmers who can afford to pay their loans might not pay it expecting a waiver.
