‘Operation Meghdoot’ is commemorated as which day on April 13 every year, by the Indian Army?

(A) Meghdoot Day

(B) ** Siachen Day

(C) Kargil Day

(D) Warrior Day

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is 36th. The Indian Army observed the 36th Siachen Day on 13th April. On April 13, 1984, the Indian Army launched ‘’Operation Meghdoot” to secure Bilafond La and other passes on the Saltoro Ridgeline from Pakistani aggression.

Concept note-2: -Indian Army marks Siachen Day on April 13 every year. The day is observed to commemorate the courage of the Indian Army under “Operation Meghdoot”. The Day also honours Siachen Warriors serving their motherland successfully from the enemy.

Concept note-3: -On this day in 1984, Indian troops first unfurled the tri colour at Bilafond La pass launching Operation Meghdoot. This was the codename for the Indian Armed Forces’ operation to seize control of the Siachen Glacier in Kashmir.

Concept note-4: -Siachen Glacier is strategically important to India, Pakistan, and China since it serves as a hub for the Shaksgam Valley, Karakoram Pass, and Aksai Chin. As a result, keeping Siachen is critical for India in order to prevent incursion from Pakistan and China.
