Oxford languages pick ‘Aatmanirbharta’ as the Hindi word of 2020.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -NEW DELHI: ‘Aatmanirbharta’ implying self-reliance has been named by Oxford Languages as its Hindi word of the year 2020 as it “validated the day-to-day achievements of the countless Indians who dealt with and survived the perils of a pandemic".

Concept note-2: -Goblin mode: Oxford Word of the Year 2022-Times of India.

Concept note-3: -Explained: How was ‘Aatmanirbharta’ chosen as the Hindi Word of the Year by Oxford Languages?

Concept note-4: -Few other words that the English dictionary picked are-Bas, Jhuggi, Funda, Nivas, Gully, Natak, Sevak, Sevika, Chup, Surya Namaskar, Desh, Diya, Bada Din among many others.

Concept note-5: -The Oxford Dictionaries Word of the Year 2017 is… youthquake. The noun, youthquake, is defined as ‘a significant cultural, political, or social change arising from the actions or influence of young people’.
