Planets and the Moon do not have light of their own.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A planet is made of rock and metal and produces no light of its own. A planet shines because it reflects the light of the Sun. We see planets in the night sky, such as Venus, the so-called “Evening Star.” Stars have their own light and twinkle in the night, but planets do not.

Concept note-2: -When we look at the Moon, if it does not make its own light, why does it look so bright-where does the Moon get its light? The Moon gets its light from the Sun. In the same way that the Sun illuminates Earth, the Moon reflects the Sun’s light, making it appear bright in our sky.

Concept note-3: -The Earth We live on planet Earth. Earth is part of the Solar System. … The planets are stars that do not have their own light and revolve around the Sun. They are: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune and Pluto.

Concept note-4: -No, the planets do not give their own light. They just reflect the sunlight, that’s how we are able to see them. Q. Planets look bright because they give off visible light of their own.

Concept note-5: -Planets do not have their own light and heat. A planet is an astronomical body orbiting a star or stellar remnant that is massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity, is not massive enough to cause thermonuclear fusion. The inner, rocky planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars.
