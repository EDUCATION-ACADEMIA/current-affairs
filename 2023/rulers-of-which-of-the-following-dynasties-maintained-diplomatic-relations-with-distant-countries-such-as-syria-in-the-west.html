Rulers of which of the following dynasties maintained diplomatic relations with distant countries such as Syria in the West?

(A) ** Maurya

(B) Gupta

(C) Chola

(D) Pallava

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This shows that Mauryan dynasty maintained the diplomatic relations with distant countries like Syria in the West.

Concept note-2: -Answer. Answer: Mauryan Emperor Bindusara had friendly relations with the Greek King Antiochos-I of Syria. Deimachos was a Syrian Ambassador who came in the court of Bindusara.

Concept note-3: -The Mauryas were a dynasty consisting of three important rulers-Chandragupta, his son Bindusara, and Bindusara’s son Ashoka.

Concept note-4: -Chandragupta, also spelled Chandra Gupta, also called Chandragupta Maurya or Maurya, (died c. 297 bce, Shravanbelagola, India), founder of the Mauryan dynasty (reigned c. 321–c. 297 bce) and the first emperor to unify most of India under one administration.

Concept note-5: -Brihadratha was the last ruler of the Mauryan Empire. He was killed by his commander in chief, Pushyamitra Shunga.
