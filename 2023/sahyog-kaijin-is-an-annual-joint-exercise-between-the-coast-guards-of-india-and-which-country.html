‘Sahyog-Kaijin’ is an annual joint exercise between the Coast Guards of India and which country?

(A) ** Japan

(B) Sri Lanka

(C) Thailand

(D) Burma

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ans: Sahyog-Kaijin is conducted annually between India and Japan. The drill Sahyog-Kaijin follows the 2006 Memorandum of Cooperation (MoC) between the Indian Coast Guard and the Japanese Coast Guard.

Concept note-2: -The exercise, Sahyog Kaijin, is between the Indian and Japanese Coast Guard as agreed in 2006. This activity proceeds with personnel of both sides coming together on one of the vessels of either nation and further practising the exercise together.

Concept note-3: -Sahyog-Kaijin-India-Japan joint exercise for Coast Guards.

Concept note-4: -27, 2022. India and Japan are set to hold their maiden air exercise from January 12-26. This is the first time that the Indian Air Force and Japanese Air Self Defense Forces will be exercising together. The exercise, Veer Guardian 2023, will take place at Hyakuri Air Base, Japan.

Concept note-5: -The inaugural edition of the 16-day bilateral air exercise between the Indian Air Force and the Japan Air Self-Defence Force has concluded in Japan. The exercise, ‘Veer Guardian 2023’, involved precise planning and skilful execution by both the air forces, the Indian Air Force said on Friday.
