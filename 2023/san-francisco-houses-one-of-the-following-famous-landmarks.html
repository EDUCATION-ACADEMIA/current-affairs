San Francisco houses one of the following famous landmarks.

(A) ** Golden Gate Bridge

(B) Sydney Opera House

(C) Burj Khalifa

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A quintessential icon of San Francisco, the Golden Gate Bridge was a bridge that almost wasn’t built. Starting off with controversy, the bridge has now become a unifying National Historic Landmark to see in San Francisco. A popular activity to enjoy this iconic landmark is biking or walking across the bridge.

Concept note-2: -Upon its completion in 1937, it was the tallest and longest suspension bridge in the world. The Golden Gate Bridge came to be recognized as a symbol of the power and progress of the United States, and it set a precedent for suspension-bridge design around the world.

Concept note-3: -The 1.7-mile-long Golden Gate Bridge, an icon of the San Francisco Bay region, connects the city of San Francisco to Marin County, California. At its completion in 1937, the suspension bridge was considered an engineering marvel-the longest main suspension bridge span in the world.

Concept note-4: -The Golden Gate Bridge, the symbol of San Francisco, is not on the Interstate System. It carries U.S. 101 across the bay. On June 14, 1846, Captain John Frémont declared California’s independence from Mexico.
