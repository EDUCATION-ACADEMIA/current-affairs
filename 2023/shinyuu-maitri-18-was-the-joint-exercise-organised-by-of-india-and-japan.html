SHINYUU Maitri 18 was the joint exercise organised by of India and japan?

(A) Armies

(B) navies

(C) ** air forces

(D) coast guard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It is a joint military air force exercise between Indian Air Force and the Japanese Air Self Defence Force (JASDF). It was started in 2019. The first edition took place at the Air Force Station, Arjan Singh in Panagarh, West Bengal.

Concept note-2: -Exercise crucial and significant in terms of security challenges faced by both nations in the backdrop of current global situation, says Army. The fourth edition of India-Japan bilateral Army exercise ‘Dharma Guardian’ began at Camp Imazu in Shiga province, Japan on Friday and will go on till March 2.

Concept note-3: -SHINYUU Maitri: It is a bilateral exercise between the Indian Air Force and the Japanese Air Self Defence Force (JASDF). JIMEX: It is a bilateral maritime exercise between India and Japan. Sahayog-Kaijin: Joint bilateral exercise between the Indian Coast Guard and the Japan Coast Guard.

Concept note-4: -27, 2022. India and Japan are set to hold their maiden air exercise from January 12-26. This is the first time that the Indian Air Force and Japanese Air Self Defense Forces will be exercising together. The exercise, Veer Guardian 2023, will take place at Hyakuri Air Base, Japan.

Concept note-5: -IAS Exam Latest Updates Exercise Maitree began in 2006 as a bilateral exercise between the Indian Army and the Royal Thai Army. It is an annual event involving troops from both countries’ armies. The exercise is also known as the Indo-Thailand Joint Exercise.
