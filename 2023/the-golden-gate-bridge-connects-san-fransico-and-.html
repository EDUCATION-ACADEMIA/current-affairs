The Golden Gate Bridge connects San Fransico and ____

(A) ** Marin County

(B) San Jose

(C) Sacramento

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Golden Gate Bridge is an iconic structure connecting the city of San Francisco to Marin County, California. It spans almost two miles across the Golden Gate, the narrow strait where San Francisco Bay opens to meet the Pacific Ocean.

Concept note-2: -What areas of land does the Golden Gate Bridge connect? The bridge connects the San Francisco Peninsula and the Marin Headlands.

Concept note-3: -Golden Gate Bridge, suspension bridge spanning the Golden Gate in California to link San Francisco with Marin county to the north. Upon its completion in 1937, it was the tallest and longest suspension bridge in the world.

Concept note-4: -The Golden Gate Bridge, Highway and Transportation District is a special district of the State of California which operates and maintains the Golden Gate Bridge and two unified public transit systems – Golden Gate Transit and Golden Gate Ferry – connecting the counties of Marin, Sonoma, San Francisco, and Contra Costa.

Concept note-5: -It is the other bridge in San Francisco. Unlike its famous neighbor, the Golden Gate Bridge, the Bay Bridge is decidedly unsexy. It does not gracefully arch over the Pacific Ocean, linking gorgeous headlands.
