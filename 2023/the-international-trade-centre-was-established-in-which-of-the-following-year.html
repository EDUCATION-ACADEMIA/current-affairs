The International Trade Centre was established in which of the following year?

(A) 1944

(B) 1947

(C) ** 1964

(D) 1967

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Originally created by the General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade (GATT) in 1964, ITC has been operated since 1968 under the joint aegis of GATT/WTO and the UN, the latter acting through the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD).

Concept note-2: -The International Trade Centre (ITC) was established on 1 January 1968. The ITC has a joint mandate with the World Trade Organization (WTO) and the United Nations (UN) through the United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD). The ITC is the focal point for trade-related technical assistance.

Concept note-3: -International Trade of India Since 1498, Europeans did trade with the rulers of India using the sea route. The main export items then were spices like pepper, ginger, cinnamon, cardamom, nutmeg, mace, and cloves. From 1947-1991, the Indian economy remained largely as a closed economy.

Concept note-4: -The first United Nations Conference on Trade and Development (UNCTAD) was held in Geneva in 1964.
