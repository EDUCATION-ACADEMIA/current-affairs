This forest in Chhattisgarh was opposed to be opened up for coal mining by the villagers.

(A) Sundarbans

(B) Kukrail reserve forest

(C) ** Hasdeo Arand forest

(D) Baikunthapur forest

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hasdeo Arand is a forest in the Indian state of Chhattisgarh in central India. The forest is 170, 000 hectares in area and is home to a diverse ecology and adivasi communities such as the Gonds. It is on top of the Hasdeo Arand coalfield in the north of Chhattisgarh.

Concept note-2: -A sprawling forest in the northern part of Chhattisgarh, Hasdeo Arand is known for its biodiversity and also its coal deposits. The forest falls under Korba, Sujapur and Sarguja districts with sizeable tribal population. The Hasdeo river, a tributary of Mahanadi, flows through it.

Concept note-3: -Baikunthpur Coalfields: Main coal mines are Charcha, Pandavpara, Katkona under the Baikunthpur coalfields. Jhagrakhand or Hasdev Coalfields: Main coal mines are Jhagrakhand and haldibadi.

Concept note-4: -Raniganj was the very first coalfield in India where coal mining started during the East India Company i.e. in the year 1774.
