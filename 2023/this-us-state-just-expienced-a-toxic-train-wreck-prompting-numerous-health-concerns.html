This U.S. state just expienced a toxic train wreck, prompting numerous health concerns.

(A) Indiana

(B) Pennsylvania

(C) ** Ohio

(D) Kentucky

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On Feb. 10, the EPA sent a letter to Norfolk Southern Railway Company reporting five toxic chemicals found in air, soil, or water surrounding the crash site. They are: vinyl chloride, butyl acrylate, ethylene glycol, isobutylene, and ethylhexyl acrylate.

Concept note-2: -NTSB: Ohio train derailment was ‘100% preventable’ The “preventable” and “traumatic” derailment of a train carrying dangerous chemicals in Ohio can be traced to an overheated wheel bearing, which was 253 degrees hotter than the air temperature, National Transportation Safety Board officials said Thursday.

Concept note-3: -The operators of a Norfolk Southern train carrying hazardous chemicals near East Palestine, Ohio, earlier this month tried to stop the train after a wheel bearing overheated to a dangerous degree, the National Transportation Safety Board said.

Concept note-4: -EAST PALESTINE, Ohio-The chemicals that spilled and were later burned near East Palestine, Ohio, after the Feb. 3 train derailment have the kinds of names that bring back visions of high school chemistry: vinyl chloride, butyl acrylate and isobutylene.
