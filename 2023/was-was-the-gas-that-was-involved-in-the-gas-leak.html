was was the gas that was involved in the gas leak

(A) Sarin

(B) GAs

(C) LPG

(D) ** Styrene

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -At about 3 a.m. on May 7, 2020, styrene monomer vapour had leaked out of a storage tank on the premises of LG Polymers unit located at Venkatapuram village on the outskirts of the Port City. The dangerous vapour, which spread over 3 km, had left 12 persons, who included two children, dead.

Concept note-2: -It is a flammable liquid that is used in the manufacturing of polystyrene plastics, fiberglass, rubber, and latex. According to Tox Town, a website run by the US National Library of Medicine, styrene is also found in vehicle exhaust, cigarette smoke, and in natural foods like fruits and vegetables.

Concept note-3: -Its presence in air is principally due to emissions from industrial processes involving styrene and its polymers and copolymers. Other sources of styrene in the environment include vehicle exhaust, cigarette smoke and other forms of combustion and incineration of styrene polymers (7).

Concept note-4: -Styrene is used to make insulation, pipes, automobile parts, printing cartridges and copy machine toner, food containers, packaging material, carpet backing, luggage, shoes, toys, floor waxes and polishes, according to US National Library of Medicine.

Concept note-5: -Styrene Monomer is a clear, colorless to yellow, oily liquid, with a sweet odor at low concentrations. It is used to make plastics, paints, synthetic rubbers, protective coatings, and resins.
