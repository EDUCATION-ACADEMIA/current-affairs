What country in Southeast Asia has the largest number of cases of COVID-19?

(A) Indonesia

(B) ** Pilipinas

(C) Singapore

(D) Malaysia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Philippines is contending with one of the worst COVID-19 outbreaks in southeast Asia. As of April 18, 2021, there were 926 052 cases of SARS-CoV-2 infection and 15 810 deaths recorded. WHO has warned that the country’s health-care system risks being overwhelmed.

Concept note-2: -Southeast Asia is composed of eleven countries of impressive diversity in religion, culture and history: Brunei, Burma (Myanmar), Cambodia, Timor-Leste, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand and Vietnam.

Concept note-3: -In fact, the GDP of the ASEAN region has been skyrocketing for a few years now, reflecting the region’s thriving economy. The Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) comprises Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, the Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, and Vietnam.

Concept note-4: -With no effective interventions, they could very well become a lost generation. With most economies in the region still reeling from the fallout of the pandemic, fears are mounting over the prospect of the creation of a “lost generation” in Asia-home to more young people than any other region.
