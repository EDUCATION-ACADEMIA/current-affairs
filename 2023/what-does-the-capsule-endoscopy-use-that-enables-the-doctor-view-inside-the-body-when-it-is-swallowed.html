What does the capsule endoscopy use that enables the doctor view inside the body when it is swallowed?

(A) Lasers

(B) ** Wireless Camera

(C) Detectors

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The capsule contains lights to illuminate your digestive system, a camera to take images and an antenna that sends those images to a recorder you wear on a belt. Capsule endoscopy is a procedure that uses a tiny wireless camera to take pictures of your digestive tract.

Concept note-2: -Listen to pronunciation. (WY-er-les KAP-sul EN-doh-SKOPE) A device used to look at the inside of the intestines and other parts of the digestive tract. It is a capsule that is about the size of a large pill, with a lens, a light, a camera, a radio transmitter, and a battery inside.

Concept note-3: -The ScotCap Test is a capsule that you swallow, and it contains 2 tiny cameras inside. The cameras take pictures of the lining of the bowel to look for any problems or signs of disease. This test can be used instead of a colonoscopy. The large bowel, also known as the colon or large intestine.
