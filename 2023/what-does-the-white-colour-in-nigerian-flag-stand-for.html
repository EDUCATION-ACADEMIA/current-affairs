What does the white colour in Nigerian flag stand for?

(A) ** Peace

(B) Energy

(C) Love

(D) Dignity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The winning design was by Michael Taiwo Akinkunmi, a Nigerian student in London. In his flag of equal green-white-green vertical stripes, green stood for agriculture and white for unity and peace.

Concept note-2: -Colours of the Flag In the national flag of India the top band is of Saffron colour, indicating the strength and courage of the country. The white middle band indicates peace and truth with Dharma Chakra. The last band is green in colour shows the fertility, growth and auspiciousness of the land.

Concept note-3: -(1900 – 1914) When Nigeria gained independence in 1960 a new green-white-green tricolour flag was adopted. Designed by Michael Taiwo Akinkunmi, the green stripes represent the wealth in the country and the white band represents peace.

Concept note-4: -It’s a three-line flag with green lines on the sides and white in the middle. Green color stands for: country’s wealth; rich natural resources. While the meaning of the white color is: peace; unity. Read more: The flag was raised at the Independence Day of Nigeria on 1st October 1960 for the first time.

Concept note-5: -The flag of Nigeria was created in 1959 and first flown on October 1, 1960. The flag features alternating green, white, and green vertical bars. The white border symbolizes peace, while the two green buildings stand for Nigeria’s abundant natural resources.
