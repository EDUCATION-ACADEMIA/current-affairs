What happens to families of Eritreans who flee the country?

(A) ** the government punishes the families

(B) the families are given extra care

(C) the families are given money monthly

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a 2009 report, Human Rights Watch documented that families of draft evaders were collectively punished usually by being jailed or forced to pay fines.

Concept note-2: -Regardless of age, Eritreans without a formal release from the mandatory national service (even above 60 years of age) are not granted passports or exit visas to allow them to exit the country legally.

Concept note-3: -Eritrean forces have carried out large-scale massacres, summary executions, widespread sexual violence including rape, gang rape, and sexual slavery, widespread pillage, attacks on refugee camps, and destruction and pillaging of crops.

Concept note-4: -Forced labor and slavery are prohibited, except where authorized by law under Article 16 of the ratified, but suspended, Eritrean Constitution. Proclamation 11/199 prohibits the recruitment of children under 18 years of age into the armed forces.
