What is the meaning of Bahrain?

(A) no rain

(B) ** two seas

(C) king

(D) good father

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Bahrain is an Arabic word meaning “Two Seas” and refers to the two sources of water surrounding the islands, fresh water springs and the surrounding seas. Bahrain is an archipelago of 33 small islands and enjoys a strategic location in the Arabian Gulf midway between the Qatar peninsula and Saudi Arabia.

Concept note-2: -Today, Bahrain’s “two seas” are generally taken to be the bay east and west of the island, the seas north and south of the island, or the salt and fresh water present above and below the ground.

Concept note-3: -Bahrain means “two seas” in Arabic. It refers to the presence of fresh water springs beneath saltwater oceans; in some places, one can actually dive beneath the layer of saltwater and get to the fresh water underneath.

Concept note-4: -Bahrain, small Arab state situated in a bay on the southwestern coast of the Persian Gulf. It is an archipelago consisting of Bahrain Island and some 30 smaller islands. Its name is from the Arabic term al-baḥrayn, meaning “two seas.”
