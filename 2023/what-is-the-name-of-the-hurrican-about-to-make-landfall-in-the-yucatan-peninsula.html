What is the name of the hurrican about to make landfall in the Yucatan peninsula?

(A) ** Delta

(B) Alpha

(C) Dawn

(D) Gamma

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Yucatan Peninsula is far enough from the Caribbean to be missed by most hurricanes. This means that most hurricane don’t reach Cancun, or by the time a hurricane gets near, it’s downgraded to a less severe tropical storm. The last hurricanes to hit Cancun were hurricane Delta in 2020, and hurricane Wilma in 2005.

Concept note-2: -Landfall: The intersection of the surface center of a tropical cyclone with a coastline. Because the strongest winds in a tropical cyclone are not located precisely at the center, it is possible for a cyclone’s strongest winds to be experienced over land even if landfall does not occur.

Concept note-3: -Those four are the Labor Day Hurricane of 1935, Camille in 1969, Andrew in 1992 and Michael in 2018.
