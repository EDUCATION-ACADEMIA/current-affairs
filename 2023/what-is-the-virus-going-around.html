What is the virus going around?

(A) Epstein-barr virus

(B) ** Coronavirus

(C) Adenovirus

(D) Norwalk virus

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Symptoms such as the temporary loss of taste and smell can still happen in some instances, but it has become less common with the Omicron variant and subvariants. Other symptoms may include fever, chills, fatigue, muscle or body aches, sore throat, nausea or vomiting and diarrhea.

Concept note-2: -New Variant BF.7-Symptoms The symptoms of this variant are similar to those of the other subvariants of Omicron. An infected person may show symptoms of fever, cough, sore throat, runny nose, fatigue, vomiting and diarrhoea.

Concept note-3: -COVID-19 is a respiratory disease caused by SARS-CoV-2, a coronavirus discovered in 2019. The virus spreads mainly from person to person through respiratory droplets and small particles produced when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks.

Concept note-4: -Typical symptoms include fever, chills, aches, cough and sore throat. Intestinal symptoms, such as vomiting or diarrhea, are possible but less common. If you have a severe or worsening symptom, such as difficulty breathing, go immediately to a hospital emergency room.
