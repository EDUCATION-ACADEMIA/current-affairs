When did Mexico become independent?

(A) 1979

(B) ** 1980

(C) 1981

(D) 1982

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer: In 1821, Mexico won its independence.

Concept note-2: -Mexico got independence in the year 1821.

Concept note-3: -Iturbide defeated the Royalist forces still opposed to independence, and the new Spanish viceroy, lacking money, provisions, and troops, was forced to accept Mexican independence. On August 24, 1821, O’Donojú signed the Treaty of Córdoba, thus ending New Spain’s dependence on Old Spain.

Concept note-4: -On August 24, 1821, Spanish Viceroy Juan de O’Donojú signed the Treaty of Córdoba, which approves a plan to make Mexico an independent constitutional monarchy. In 1822, as no Bourbon monarch to rule Mexico had been found, Iturbide was proclaimed the emperor of Mexico.
