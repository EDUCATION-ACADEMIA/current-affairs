When did the celebration of “World Earth Day” started across the world on April, 2018?

(A) 1980

(B) 1972

(C) 1975

(D) ** 1970

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Every year on April 22, Earth Day marks the anniversary of the birth of the modern environmental movement in 1970.

Concept note-2: -Earth Day. Earth Day was first observed on April 22, 1970, when an estimated 20 million people nationwide attended the inaugural events at tens of thousands of sites including elementary and secondary schools, universities, and community sites across the United States.

Concept note-3: -The First Earth Day in April 1970.

Concept note-4: -A month later, United States Senator Gaylord Nelson proposed the idea to hold a nationwide environmental teach-in on April 22, 1970. He hired a young activist, Denis Hayes, to be the National Coordinator. Nelson and Hayes renamed the event “Earth Day".

Concept note-5: -In the late 1960s there was an increased awareness of environmental concerns among Americans, and the prominent environmentalist and U.S. senator Gaylord Nelson sought to galvanize the conservation movement through the creation of a national celebration.
