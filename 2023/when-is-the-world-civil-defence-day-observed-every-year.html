When is the ‘World Civil Defence Day’ observed every year?

(A) February 28

(B) March 2

(C) March 3

(D) ** March 1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -World Civil Defence Day is celebrated on March 1st every year to raise awareness about civil defence and emergency management. The day was first observed in 1990, and since then, it has become an essential part of the calendar for emergency management professionals and civil defence organizations around the world.

Concept note-2: -World Civil Defence Day 2023 History On May 28, 1931, French Surgeon-General George Saint-Paul established the Association of “Geneva Zones” in Paris, making him the first to come up with the idea..

Concept note-3: -Every year, World Civil Defence Day is celebrated on March 1. The day was brought into existence by the International Civil Defence Organisation or the ICDO in 1990. We observe this day to honour the people who work for civil defence and make sacrifices to protect their nation.

Concept note-4: -Civil Defence Day is celebrated on 6th December every year to make aware of the citizens and highlight the importance of Civil Defence. The civil Defence provides protection to the civilian population during crisis.
