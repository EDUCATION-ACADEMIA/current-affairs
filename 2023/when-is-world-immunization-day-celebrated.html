When is World Immunization Day celebrated?

(A) 14 March

(B) 15 March

(C) ** 16 March

(D) 17 March

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Government of India observes 16 March as National Vaccination Day to acknowledge and appreciate the hard work of frontline health care workers to ensure the vaccination of every child. India has made remarkable progress in scaling up routine immunization through intensified vaccination drives.

Concept note-2: -World Immunization Day is celebrated on November 10 every year. It is celebrated in order to spread awareness about the benefits of getting vaccinated and how it can prevent a plethora of diseases in the long run.

Concept note-3: -On 16 March every year, National Vaccination Day is observed in India which is also known as National Immunisation Day (IMD). It was first observed on 16 March 1995 when the first dose of Oral Polio Vaccine was given.

Concept note-4: -World Immunization Week 2022: #Long Life For All.

Concept note-5: -World Immunization Week, celebrated in the last week of April, aims to highlight the collective action needed and to promote the use of vaccines to protect people of all ages against disease.
