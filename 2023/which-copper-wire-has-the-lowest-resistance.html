Which copper wire has the lowest resistance?

(A) 1 meter of 1 mm diameter wire

(B) ** 0.5 meter of 1 mm diameter wire

(C) 0.5 meter of 0.5 mm diameter wire

(D) 1 meter of 0.5 mm diameter wire

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Answer and Explanation: A short thick copper wire at 25 degree Celsius has lower resistance.

Concept note-2: -A copper wire has diameter 0.5 mm and resistivity of 1.6×10−8 m.

Concept note-3: -R=85 Q. Determine the resistance of a copper wire having a length of 1km and diameter of 0.5mm. Resisivity of copper =1.7×10−3m.

Concept note-4: -A copper wire has a diameter of 0.5mm and resistivity of 1.6 x 10-8 m. The length of this wire to make its resistance 10 is 122.7 m. The resistance change if the diameter is doubled is 2.5 . As a result, resistance will only be one-fourth as strong when the diameter is doubled.
