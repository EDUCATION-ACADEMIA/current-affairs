Which country has approved a project to build the world’s first energy island in the North Sea?

(A) SWEDEN

(B) ** DENMARK

(C) GERMANY

(D) PAKISTAN

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Danish Parliament has agreed to build the world’s first offshore wind energy hub, designed as an artificial island in the North Sea.

Concept note-2: -An artificial island, with wind power and installations to convert excess energy into fuel, is planned in the North Sea. When completed it may provide electricity for 10 million households.

Concept note-3: -Denmark has recently unveiled a massive 37-billion-euro project to build two islands with wind energy.

Concept note-4: -Denmark’s municipality of the island of Samsø has completely transformed its energy system from fossil fuels to renewable energy, becoming the world’s first renewable energy island.

Concept note-5: -The world’s first energy island will be built in the North Sea and will, when fully operational, provide green offshore wind power to more than 10 million homes in Northern Europe. It will also host facilities for generating green fuels and energy storage.
