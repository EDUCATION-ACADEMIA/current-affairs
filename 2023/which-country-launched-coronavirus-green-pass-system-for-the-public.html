Which country launched Coronavirus ‘Green Pass’ system for the public?

(A) India

(B) Japan

(C) Afghanistan

(D) ** Israel

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Israel has launched a coronavirus “green pass” system, which allows people who have been vaccinated to access public facilities that would otherwise be off limits.

Concept note-2: -In December 2020, the UK became the first country in the world to approve this vaccine and began rolling out an initial 800, 000 doses at the start of the month, and is now in use worldwide.

Concept note-3: -It is no longer required to present a Green Pass when entering places and events.

Concept note-4: -The reinforced Green Pass: a new certificate which differentiates vaccinated from unvaccinated people-UniCamillus. Enrolment. STUDENT AREA.

Concept note-5: -Green Pass No Longer Required for Entry As of June 1, 2022, the Italian government no longer requires a green pass for entry into the country. This means that travelers do not have to show any Covid-19 documentation upon entry (vaccination or recovery certificate or negative Covid test).
