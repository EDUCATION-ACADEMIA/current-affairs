Which country recently allowed women to work on the subway after originally classifying that work as too dangerous and too demanding for women?

(A) ** Russia

(B) Saudi Arabia

(C) China

(D) India

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Russia, from Jan. 1, shortened the list of jobs it bars women from holding because of their physical demands or hazardous nature, allowing them to become metro drivers, train conductors and lorry drivers.

Concept note-2: -Russia’s constitution guarantees men and women equal employment but the country’s labor code states women shouldn’t do hard, physical labor or jobs that entail harmful or dangerous conditions.

Concept note-3: -But the revised list still says women are restricted from working in jobs in the “chemical industry, metallurgy, oil production, coal mining, manufacturing of insulation, and some others owing to the harmful effects of certain compounds on women’s reproductive health.”

Concept note-4: -Russia’s women’s movement originated in the 19th century and gained force in the early 1900s, spearheaded by female educators, doctors, and activists. They championed educational, legal, and economic equality and sought to improve daily life for working-class women.

Concept note-5: -In July 1917, women over 20 were given the right to vote and hold public office. The first opportunity to exercise their newly-won right was during elections for the Constituent Assembly in November 1917.
