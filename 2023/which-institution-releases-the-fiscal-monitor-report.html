Which institution releases the ‘Fiscal Monitor’ Report?

(A) World Bank

(B) RBI

(C) ** IMF

(D) OECD

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is International Monetary Fund.

Concept note-2: -Disclaimer: The Fiscal Monitor is a survey by the IMF staff published twice a year, in the spring and fall. The report analyzes the latest public finance developments, updates medium-term fiscal projections, and assesses policies to put public finances on a sustain-able footing.

Concept note-3: -This Annual Report emphasizes the work of the IMF’s Executive Board, whose guidance and oversight are essential. It also highlights the real-time advice, capacity development, and support the Fund has provided to our members. Working together is the only way we can successfully address the challenges we face.

Concept note-4: -Role and Purposes of the IMF The International Monetary Fund is a cooperative international monetary organization whose members currently include 183 countries of the world.
