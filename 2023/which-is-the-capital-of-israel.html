Which is the capital of Israel?

(A) ** Jerusalem

(B) Jericho

(C) Tel Aviv

(D) Palestenia

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In July 1980, the Knesset passed the Jerusalem Law as part of the country’s Basic Law, which declared Jerusalem the unified capital of Israel.

Concept note-2: -Tel Aviv was the temporary government center of the State of Israel until the government moved to Jerusalem in December 1949.

Concept note-3: -Journey to the Beginning The city of Jerusalem was first built on the hill south of temple mount by the ancient Canaanites in the 18th century b.c.e. David son of Jesse, King of Israel, conquered the small Jebusite (Canaanite) city and made it his capital, which he called-the City of David (2nd Samuel 5).

Concept note-4: -Drawing from the deep well of Jewish connection to Jerusalem and the centrality of the city to the life of the newly established State of Israel, it was only natural that the Knesset, Israel’s parliament, declared Jerusalem as the capital of Israel on 23 January 1950.
