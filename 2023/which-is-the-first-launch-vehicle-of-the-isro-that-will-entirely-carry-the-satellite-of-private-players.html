Which is the first launch vehicle of the ISRO, that will entirely carry the satellite of private players?

(A) PSLV C 31

(B) PSLV G 7

(C) ** PSLV C 51

(D) PSLV Mark II

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Notes: The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is set to launch satellites of private players onboard PSLV C 51. The first of its kind mission would launch an earth observation satellite ‘ANAND’, fully built by a start-up named Pixel India.

Concept note-2: -PSLV-C51/Amazonia-1. India’s Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle PSLV-C51 successfully launched Amazonia-1 along with 18 co-passenger satellites today (February 28, 2021) from Satish Dhawan Space Centre SHAR, Sriharikota.

Concept note-3: -It is a Radar Imaging Satellite designed to provide high quality images under all weather conditions for applications such as Agriculture, Forestry & Plantations, Soil Moisture & Hydrology and Flood mapping. Weighing about 1710 kg, it generates 2280 W power and has a mission life of 10 years.

Concept note-4: -SLV. Satellite Launch Vehicle-3 (SLV-3) was India’s first experimental satellite launch vehicle, which was an all solid, four stage vehicle weighing 17 tonnes with a height of 22m and capable of placing 40 kg class payloads in Low Earth Orbit (LEO (Low Earth Orbit) ).

Concept note-5: -The Indian Space Research Organisation(ISRO) today launched the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle(PSLV)-C52 rocket carrying ISRO’s earth observation satellite EOS-04 and two others off from the first launch pad of Satish Dhawan Space Centre at Sriharikota in Andhra Pradesh.
