Which nation is working to develop first wooden satellites to reduce space junk?

(A) Russia

(B) United States

(C) ** Japan

(D) China

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Japan. Japan’s Sumitomo Forestry company and Kyoto University have decided to collaborate to develop the world’s first wooden satellite by 2023.

Concept note-2: -The correct answer is Japan. Japan has planned a 2023 launch of the world’s first satellite made out of wood to reduce debris. Japan’s Sumitomo Forestry company and Kyoto University aim to launch the world’s first wood-based space satellite. This Satellite will combat the problem of space junk.

Concept note-3: -Notes: WISA Woodsat is a nano satellite, which would be the world’s first wooden satellite to be launched to space by the end of this year from New Zealand. This nanosatellite is a cube shaped structure made of birch plywood with its sensors developed by the European Space Agency (ESA).

Concept note-4: -KYOTO (Kyodo)–A plan is under way in Japan to launch the world’s first “wooden” satellite in 2023, as its development team aims to harness the environmental friendliness and low cost of wood in space development.

Concept note-5: -World’s first wooden satellite to be launched from New Zealand Key Points. The WISA Woodsat satellite will be launched in a Rocket Lab Electron rocket. European Space Agency consists of 22 member states. WISA Woodsat is a nanosatellite and weighs about 2.2lbs.
