Which of the following organizations has approved a loan of $2.9 billion to Sri Lanka?

(A) World Bank

(B) ** International Monetary Fund

(C) Asian Development Bank

(D) New Development Bank

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The IMF in September last year approved Sri Lanka a 2.9 billion dollar bailout package over 4 years pending Sri Lanka’s ability to restructure its debt with creditors-both bilateral and sovereign bond holders.

Concept note-2: -1, 2022. COLOMBO, Sri Lanka-The International Monetary Fund will provide Sri Lanka $2.9 billion over four years to help salvage the country from its worst economic crisis under a preliminary agreement the agency announced Thursday.

Concept note-3: -Instead, the IMF provides financial support to countries hit by crises to create breathing room as they implement policies that restore economic stability and growth. It also provides precautionary financing to help prevent crises. IMF lending is continuously refined to meet countries’ changing needs.

Concept note-4: -The IMF works to achieve sustainable growth and prosperity for all of its 190 member countries. It does so by supporting economic policies that promote financial stability and monetary cooperation, which are essential to increase productivity, job creation, and economic well-being.
