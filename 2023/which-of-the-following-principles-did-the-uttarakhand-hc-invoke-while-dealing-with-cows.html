Which of the following principles did the Uttarakhand HC invoke while dealing with cows?

(A) ** Parens Patriae

(B) Locus Standi

(C) Rex Non Potest Peccare

(D) Caveat Emptor

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The court did this by invoking the ‘parens patriae’ doctrine. Parens patriae in Latin means ‘parent of the country’ and is a doctrine that grants the court inherent power and authority to act as guardian for those who are unable to take care for themselves.

Concept note-2: -There is a lack of uniformity among State laws governing cattle slaughter. The strictest laws are in Delhi, Gujarat, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, Ladakh, Punjab, Rajasthan and Uttarakhand, where the slaughter of cow and its progeny, including bulls and bullocks of all ages, is completely banned.
