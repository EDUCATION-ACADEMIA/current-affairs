Who built the Jama Masjid?

(A) Jahangir

(B) Akbar

(C) Imam Bukhari

(D) ** Shah Jahan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mughal emperor Shah Jahan built the Jama Masjid between 1650 and 1656, at the highest point of Shahjahanabad. The mosque was designed by architect Ustad Khalil, and constructed by approximately 5000 workers. The workforce was diverse, consisting of Indians, Arabs, Persians, Turks, and Europeans.

Concept note-2: -One of the largest mosques in India, Jama Masjid is situated in Old Delhi. Its construction was started in 1644 and completed by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

Concept note-3: -Why was Jama Masjid built? Following the death of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, Shah Jahan decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi and founded the walled city of Shahjahanabad. The Jama Masjid was then commissioned to be the main center of worship for the new city.

Concept note-4: -The Jama Masjid is a fine example of Islamic architecture. The Jama Masjid was constructed in 1656, during the reign of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan.

Concept note-5: -It is one of the important historical monuments of India built during the rule of Mughals in India. It was built with the order of Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan between 1644 and 1656. The mosque consists of three large gates, four towers and 3 minarets. The use of white marble along with red stone to build the structure.
