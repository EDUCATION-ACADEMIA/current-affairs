Who has been sworn in as the 41st Prime Minister of New Zealand?

(A) Carmel Sepuloni

(B) Chris Carlson Cook

(C) Jacinda Ardern

(D) ** Chris Hipkins Chris Hipkins was sworn in as the 41st Prime Minister of New Zealand in Wellington by the country’s governor-general, Cindy Kiro on January 25, 2023. Chris Hipkins stated after formally assuming office that this was the greatest honor and responsibility of his life, and he was looking forward to the challenges ahead. Jacinda Ardern resigned as Prime Minister of New Zealand last week.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The prime minister of New Zealand (Māori: Te pirimia o Aotearoa) is the head of government of New Zealand. The incumbent prime minister, Chris Hipkins, leader of the New Zealand Labour Party, took office on 25 January 2023.

Concept note-2: -The current prime minister is Chris Hipkins, who assumed office on 25 January 2023 following the resignation of Jacinda Ardern.

Concept note-3: -Labour’s Helen Clark became New Zealand’s first elected female prime minister following the general election in November 1999.
