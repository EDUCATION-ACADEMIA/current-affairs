Charles Fuller, who died recently, belonged to which profession?

(A) Doctor

(B) Singer

(C) ** Playwright

(D) Director

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Charles Fuller, the Pulitzer Prize-winning playwright of the searing and acclaimed “A Soldier’s Play” who often explored and exposed how social institutions can perpetuate racism, has died. He was 83. Fuller died of natural causes on Monday in Toronto, said his wife, Claire Prieto-Fuller.

Concept note-2: -Fuller Jr., whose ‘A Soldier’s Play’ won the Pulitzer Prize, was memorialized by a city that admired him. About 150 people attended a memorial service for the North Philly native, who said he drew upon the conflict, poverty and violence he saw while living in public housing to write his plays.

Concept note-3: -A Soldier’s Play, drama in two acts by Charles Fuller, produced and published in 1981 and awarded the Pulitzer Prize for drama in 1982.
