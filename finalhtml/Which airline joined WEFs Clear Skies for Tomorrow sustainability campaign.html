Which airline joined WEF’s “Clear Skies for Tomorrow” sustainability campaign?

(A) Air India

(B) SpiceJet

(C) Vistara

(D) ** IndiGo

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -IndiGo Joins WEF Sustainability Campaign ‘Clear Skies for Tomorrow’

Concept note-2: -The country’s largest carrier IndiGo on Monday said it has joined a sustainable initiative spearheaded by the World Economic Forum (WEF). The airline has become a signatory to the Clear Skies for Tomorrow, India Coalition campaign.

Concept note-3: -Established in 2019, the Clean Skies for Tomorrow initiative (CST) is a global, cross-value-chain coalition working to facilitate the transition to net-zero flying by 2050.

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is British Airways. British Airways will become the first airline to use sustainable aviation fuel produced on a commercial scale in the UK. British Airways and Phillips 66 Limited have signed a multi-year sustainable aviation fuel supply agreement.

Concept note-5: -Notes: IndiGo was the first airline in India to have launched an ESG report.
