Which state government has tied up with the British Council to expand higher education?

(A) Government of Punjab

(B) Government of Kerala

(C) ** Government of Telangana

(D) Government of Maharashtra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Explanation: The government of Telangana has tied up with the British Council to expand higher education and to renew partnerships in English and Arts.

Concept note-2: -The partnership would help teachers and students from the state’s colleges upskill themselves as well as help Karnataka expand collaborations through the British Council initiatives like the UK-India Education Research Initiative (UKIERI) and the Going Global Partnerships, Ashwath Narayan said.

Concept note-3: -The Maharashtra government has signed a MoU with the British Council to strengthen educational and cultural cooperation.

Concept note-4: -The British Council was first established in India in 1948.

Concept note-5: -The structure of Indian Higher Education is three-layered, consisting of Universities, Colleges and Courses. The universities and colleges work in unison with regulatory as well as accreditation bodies to deliver standardized education.
